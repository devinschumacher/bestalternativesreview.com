### What materials are used in Sccy's 9mm holsters?

Sccy offers holsters in both Kydex and leather construction. Kydex models offer exceptional durability and retention while remaining lightweight. Leather holsters provide a natural feel and can develop a personal patina over time. 

The company also produces molded plastic holsters that combine affordability with decent retention, though they may not match the longevity of metal options. 


### How do I choose between a Kydex and leather holster?

If you prioritize durability, easy carry, and precise retention, a Kydex holster may be your best option. These holsters mount directly to the slide, eliminating potential wear points and allowing quick draw. 

Leather holsters, on the other hand, offer a more traditional carry experience with a natural grip. They're ideal for concealed carry where you want the holster to become part of your clothing, though regular cleaning and conditioning are necessary to maintain quality.

### Can I carry a different firearm in my Sccy holster?

While Sccy holsters are designed specifically for their 9mm pistols, many users successfully carry other 9mm variants in compatible models. Always verify that the holster fits your specific firearm and grip style. 

If you're considering cross-carriage, test the holster with your intended firearm to ensure proper fit and function before making a purchase. 


### What retention levels are available in Sccy holsters?

Sccy offers three retention levels in their Kydex holsters: Low (finger retention), Medium (strong retention), and High (locking retention). The standard level is Medium, which provides solid retention without requiring excessive finger pressure. 

Most users find Medium retention adequate, though high retention may be preferred for daily carry or strong-handlers. Low retention is useful for off-duty carry or casual concealed carry.

### How do I properly break in a leather Sccy holster?

To break in a leather Sccy holster, start by cleaning it with mild soap and water. Apply a thin layer of leather conditioner and buff until absorbed. Store the holster with the firearm removed, rotating it weekly for even wear. 

Avoid wearing the holster immediately after conditioning. Allow 24-48 hours for the leather to settle before regular use. Regular conditioning every 6 months will maintain the holster's quality and comfort. 


### What maintenance do Sccy holsters require?

Most Sccy holsters, especially metal models, require minimal maintenance. Clean the holster regularly with mild soap and water, then dry thoroughly. Check for any signs of wear or damage before each use. 

Leather holsters need more attention. Apply a new coat of conditioner every 6 months to maintain suppleness. Check stitching for signs of wear and address any issues immediately to prevent damage.
