## Buyer's Guide

Frontals, or forehead protectors, are essential safety equipment for many sports and activities. These protective devices shield the forehead and face from impact, reducing the risk of serious injury. Understanding the key features and considerations can help you choose the right frontal for your specific needs. 


### Anatomy and Design

Modern frontals feature a curved design that matches the natural contours of the forehead, providing optimal protection while allowing for freedom of movement. The protective shell is typically made from shock-resistant materials like polycarbonate, while the interior padding uses foam or gel to absorb impact and distribute pressure evenly. 

![Frontals-2](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Frontals-2/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Fit and Adjustment

A proper fit is crucial for effective protection. Size ranges vary by manufacturer, so it's important to measure your forehead accurately. Most frontals offer adjustable straps and closures to secure them in place without causing pressure points. Some models provide multiple sizing options or universal fits for added versatility. 


### Visibility and Comfort

For active use, visibility is essential. Clear visors or see-through panels are common in frontals, but some designs incorporate ventilation for added comfort during intense activities. The padding and strap materials should be breathable and moisture-wicking to prevent heat buildup and reduce irritation. 

![Frontals-3](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Frontals-3/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Compatibility and Usage

Many frontals are designed to work with helmets or other headgear, so compatibility is important. Look for frontals with dedicated mounting systems or clips that attach securely to your existing equipment. Consider whether the frontal can be worn with glasses or face masks if needed for your specific use case. 


## FAQ

![Frontals-4](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Frontals-4/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What is a frontal?

A frontal is a type of cosmetic hair piece that covers the scalp in the forehead region, typically extending from ear to ear. It's designed to address hair loss, scalp conditions, or to complete a hairstyle. 

Frontals can be worn alone or under a regular hair system, and they're available in various styles including lace frontals and monofilament frontals, which mimic natural hair growth patterns. 


### What are the main types of frontals?

The primary types of frontals include lace frontals, monofilament frontals, and solid frontals. Lace frontals use a breathable lace grid for a more natural look, monofilament frontals feature individual hair strands for scalp invisibility, while solid frontals are seamless. 

Each type offers different benefits regarding comfort, breathability, and natural appearance. The choice depends on the wearer's specific needs and preferences. 

![Frontals-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Frontals-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### How long do frontals last?

Frontals typically have a lifespan of 1-3 years with proper care, including regular cleaning and avoiding tangling by keeping them styled in a loose bun or ponytail when not in use. 

Regular maintenance and protection from environmental damage, such as heat styling tools and harsh sun exposure, can extend their longevity. 


### What maintenance is required for frontals?

Frontals need daily cleaning with mild soap and water, avoiding harsh brushes that can cause tangling. They should be brushed gently in the direction of hair growth and styled using appropriate hair care products. 

When not in use, frontals should be stored in a cool, dry place and given occasional deep conditioning treatments to maintain their quality and appearance. 

![Frontals-6](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Frontals-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Are frontals suitable for all hair types?

Frontals can be adapted to suit most hair types through proper selection of the base and styling techniques. People with fine hair may benefit from lighter bases, while those with thick hair might prefer more structured styles. 

It's important to consider factors like scalp sensitivity and the specific needs of your hair type when selecting a frontal system. 


### How do I care for my scalp when wearing a frontal?

Regular scalp care is essential when wearing a frontal. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner specifically formulated for sensitive scalp, and consider scalp massaging techniques to maintain blood flow. 

If you experience itching, redness, or other discomfort, consult a dermatologist, as these may indicate an underlying scalp condition that requires medical attention.
