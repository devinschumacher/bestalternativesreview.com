## Buyer's Guide

Eagle Creek backpacks combine innovative design elements with practical features, making them a popular choice for travelers and outdoor enthusiasts. Here's what to consider when selecting an Eagle Creek backpack that meets your needs. 


### Capacity and Size

The right backpack size depends on your intended use. For short trips or everyday carry, consider a 15-20L pack. Longer trips or when carrying heavy gear may require a 30-50L capacity. Always measure your gear before purchasing to ensure the backpack will fit comfortably and provide adequate storage. 


### Design Considerations

Eagle Creek offers both traditional upright and sloped back designs. Upright packs distribute weight more evenly but have a higher profile, while sloped-back models sit lower and hug your body better for hiking. Look for a pack with adjustable hip belts and shoulder straps to optimize fit and comfort. 


### Waterproofing and Organization

Eagle Creek uses waterproof materials like their popular DrySuite technology for critical areas like pockets and zippers. The number and type of pockets vary by model, with many featuring specialized compartments for laptops, documents, and small accessories. The main compartment should have a secure closure system that prevents items from shifting during transport. 


### Maintenance and Durability

Investing in a backpack with quality construction and durable materials pays off over time. Look for packs made from reinforced fabrics like Cordura or ripstop nylon, which resist tearing and wear. The stitching quality and hardware durability are also important indicators of a pack's longevity.
