## FAQ

### What is a TV stand riser?

A TV stand riser is a vertical component that extends the height of a traditional TV stand, allowing you to raise your television off the floor and improve viewing angles. It's particularly useful for smaller TV stands or when you want to minimize cable exposure. 

The riser typically features brackets that secure to the TV stand and provides a surface for the TV to sit on, while also hiding cables and supporting the TV's weight. Most risers are available in various heights to accommodate different TV sizes and stand designs. 


### How do TV stand risers improve viewing?

By raising the TV, risers allow for better line of sight and reduce the need to crane your neck to see the screen, which can be particularly beneficial for rooms with high ceilings or where the TV is placed on a lower furniture piece. 

Additionally, they help to achieve the optimal viewing height, which is typically 1.5 to 3 times the distance from the viewer's eyes to the screen. This can enhance visual comfort and reduce eye strain during viewing sessions. 

![TV-Stand-Riser-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/TV-Stand-Riser-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What materials are TV stand risers made from?

TV stand risers are commonly made from metal, specifically powder-coated steel or aluminum, which provide strength and durability while maintaining a lightweight profile. These materials resist corrosion and provide a sleek appearance. 

Some risers feature premium materials like polished stainless steel for an upscale look, while others incorporate plastic or composite materials for affordability and added weight capacity. 


### How much weight can a TV stand riser support?

Most TV stand risers are designed to support between 50 and 100 pounds, though this varies by manufacturer and model. Heavier TVs may require specialized brackets or additional support structures. 

It's important to check the weight rating for your specific TV stand and television model, as some older or lighter stands may not require additional support, while newer, larger TVs need more substantial mounting solutions.

### What installation considerations should I keep in mind?

Before installing a TV stand riser, ensure that your TV stand has sufficient open space and bracket attachment points. Check the distance between existing mounting holes or consider additional mounting hardware if needed. 

Also, verify that the riser's brackets align properly with the stand's structure and that the combined weight won't exceed the stand's maximum capacity. Proper installation requires both the TV stand and riser to be level and securely fastened. 


### How do I choose the right height for my TV stand riser?

The ideal height depends on your viewing distance and TV size. A general rule is to position the TV's center at eye level when seated. For most viewers, this translates to a riser height between 12 and 20 inches, though this can vary based on specific room dimensions and furniture placement. 

Consider measuring the distance from your seating area to the TV stand and adjusting the riser height accordingly. Test different heights by placing a book or riser prototype on your stand to find the most comfortable viewing position.
