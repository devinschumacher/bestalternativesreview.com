### What's the difference between flat and flush-trim bits?

Flat router bits are the most basic type, featuring a straight cutting surface that can handle a variety of cutting and engraving tasks. They're useful for general woodworking, milling, and shaping projects. 

Flush-trim bits have a specially angled cutting surface designed to remove material flush with a surface like a countertop or piece of trim. They're particularly useful for finishing edges or creating flush-mounted joints without manual sanding.
