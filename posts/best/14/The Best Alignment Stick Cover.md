## FAQ


### What is an alignment stick cover?

Designed for precision work and engineering applications, an alignment stick cover provides a protective sleeve for measurement tools such as calipers, micrometers, and gauges. The cover prevents damage to delicate measuring surfaces while maintaining the tool's accuracy. 

These covers are typically made from durable materials like rubber or plastic, with precise tolerances to ensure accurate measurements when in use. They are available in various lengths and diameters to fit different measuring tools. 


### What measurements can an alignment stick cover accommodate?

Most alignment stick covers are designed to fit standard measurement tools, with typical diameters ranging from 5mm to 25mm. They are available in lengths suitable for both short measurements (10mm to 50mm) and longer spans, depending on the specific measuring tool. 

The inner diameter of the cover is usually slightly smaller than the tool it's protecting, ensuring a snug fit while allowing for smooth, accurate measurements. The outer measurements are standardized to accommodate common tool sizes. 


### What are the benefits of using an alignment stick cover?

These protective covers prevent damage from scratches, impacts, and environmental factors, extending the life of delicate measuring tools. They maintain measurement accuracy by protecting tool surfaces from contamination and wear, which can affect precision readings. 

The covers are reusable, making them a cost-effective solution for protecting expensive measurement tools. They are also practical for maintaining hygiene standards in medical and food processing environments where contamination-free measuring is essential. 


### How do I clean an alignment stick cover?

Most covers can be cleaned with mild soap and water. For stubborn dirt or oil, specialized tool cleaning solutions can be used. It's important to avoid abrasive cleaners or harsh chemicals that may damage the cover material or affect the tool's finish. 

After cleaning, allow the cover to dry completely before storing or reusing it to prevent moisture from affecting the measuring tool's surfaces. 


### What material options are available for alignment stick covers?

The most common materials for alignment stick covers include rubber and plastic. Rubber covers are flexible and provide excellent protection against impacts and scratches, while plastic covers offer good chemical resistance and clearer visibility for measurement markings. 

Some premium covers incorporate multiple materials or layers for enhanced protection, such as rubber cores with plastic outer sleeves. These specialized covers often include additional features like precision-milled measurements or antimicrobial properties. 


### How should I store an alignment stick cover?

When not in use, store the cover in a dry environment away from direct sunlight and heat sources, which can degrade the material over time. Keep it with the associated measuring tool to maintain proper calibration and prevent loss. 

For extended storage, consider placing the cover in a protective case or zip-top bag to prevent dust accumulation. Regularly inspect the cover for signs of wear or damage before reusing it to maintain measurement accuracy.
