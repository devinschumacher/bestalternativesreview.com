## Buyer's Guide

Coretec flooring combines durability, affordability, and design options to create an attractive alternative to traditional hardwood. Understanding the key features and considerations will help you select a quality Coretec product that meets your needs. 


### Construction Quality

Coretec floors typically feature a durable overlay with a high-quality finish. Look for products with multiple layers for increased stability and wear resistance. The core material, often bamboo or recycled wood, should be protected by a dense wear layer. 

![Coretec-Flooring-2](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Coretec-Flooring-2/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Installation Options

Coretec flooring is available in both floating and nailed-down styles. Floating installations require minimal preparation and are ideal for renters or DIY projects. Nailed-down floors offer superior stability but require more precise installation and potentially more expensive subfloor preparation. 


### Variety of Styles and Colors

These floors come in a wide range of solid wood and engineered wood styles, matching various design preferences and budgets. Consider the natural wood grain patterns, available colors, and texture options to find a look that suits your space. 

![Coretec-Flooring-3](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Coretec-Flooring-3/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Maintenance Requirements

Proper maintenance extends the life of your Coretec flooring. Most products perform best with regular vacuuming and occasional damp mopping. Avoid the use of harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners, which can damage the protective finish.
