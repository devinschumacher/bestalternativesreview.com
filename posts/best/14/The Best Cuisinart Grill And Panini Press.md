## FAQ

### What's the difference between a grill and a panini press?

A grill adds a char-grilled, slightly smoky flavor to foods through direct heat and indirect heat cooking. A panini press cooks sandwiches and thin slices of meat or cheese between two heated plates, with gentle pressure for even heating and a shorter cooking time. 

Grills are ideal for outdoor cooking or when you want to replicate restaurant-quality grilled foods at home. Panini presses excel at quick, even heating of prepared foods, making them perfect for busy breakfasts, lunchtime sandwiches, or casual dinner cooking. 


### How do I clean and maintain the grill and panini press?

After each use, clean both the grill grates and the heating plates with a damp cloth or brush. For tougher food residue, use a non-abrasive cleaner like baking soda or mild dish soap. Both components should be wiped down and allowed to air dry completely before storage. 

Regular maintenance includes checking the heating elements for proper function and ensuring the plates and grates are free from deep scratches that can affect heat distribution. Replace the heating elements if you notice uneven heating or if they're no longer providing adequate warmth.

### What's the maximum temperature each appliance can reach?

The grill can reach temperatures suitable for searing steaks, grilling vegetables, and cooking meats, typically between 300°F to 450°F (150°C to 232°C). For precise temperature control during cooking, use an infrared thermometer to monitor the surface temperature. 

The panini press maintains a consistent temperature range of around 350°F to 400°F (175°C to 205°C) between its two heated plates. This temperature range ensures even cooking of sandwiches and thin cuts of meat, preventing undercooked or overcooked results. 


### Can I use the grill as a regular oven?

The grill is not designed for conventional baking or roasting. While you can cook thicker items like roasts or whole poultry, the cooking times will be significantly longer than in a conventional oven, and the results won't be as consistent. 

For baking and roasting, the grill's indirect heat and lower cooking temperatures make it less suitable compared to dedicated oven appliances. The panini press is also not intended for baking or roasting, focusing instead on quick, even heating for sandwiches and thin cuts of meat.

### What accessories are available for these appliances?

Replacement heating elements, spare grilling plates, and additional cooking accessories like drip trays and steam cookers can be purchased. The grill may also support accessories like grilling baskets, skewers, and drip pans for versatile cooking options. 

For the panini press, accessories may include interchangeable plates for different cooking needs (such as non-stick plates for eggs and cheese), and steam cookers for healthier cooking options. Both appliances can benefit from drip trays to catch excess grease and protect your countertop. 


### How do I know which appliance is right for me?

Consider your cooking habits and the types of foods you regularly prepare. A grill is ideal if you frequently cook thick cuts of meat, steaks, or vegetables. It's particularly useful for outdoor cooking or when you need to sear foods quickly. 

The panini press is more versatile for everyday cooking, especially when making sandwiches, grilling thin cuts of meat, or cooking small, prepped items. It's ideal for breakfast, lunch, or quick dinners when you need even heating and shorter cooking times.
