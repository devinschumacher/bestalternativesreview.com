## FAQ


### What types of aqua pool floats are available?

Aqua pool floats come in various shapes and sizes to suit different preferences and activities. Common types include noodle floats, which offer basic floating support, wing floats with stabilizing arms, and lounging float beds that provide extra comfort with backrests and headrests. 

Most aqua pool floats are made from high-density foam or PVC with UV protection. They range in length from 4 to 7 feet, with capacities typically between 150 to 300 pounds. Additional features may include drain valves for quick deflation, handles for easier carrying, and waterproof storage pockets. 


### How should I choose the right size float?

Selecting the appropriate size depends on your body weight and desired floating position. For casual floating, a 6-foot noodle float is suitable for most adults under 250 pounds. Those who prefer better stability can opt for a 7-foot wing float or a lounge float bed. 

Always choose a float that matches or exceeds your weight capacity. For children and individuals over 250 pounds, consider a larger or specialized float that supports extra weight while providing adequate buoyancy. 


### How do I properly care for my pool float?

To maintain your pool float's durability and hygiene, remove it from the water immediately after use and store it in a dry location. Regularly inspect the float for signs of wear, such as cracks or delamination, and inflate it completely to prevent leaks. 

Clean the float with mild soap and water when it's completely dry to remove any chlorine residue or mildew. Avoid using harsh chemicals that can damage the float material. Store the inflator hose in a protective case when not in use to prevent tangling and loss. 


### Where is the best place to store a pool float?

When not in use, store your pool float in a dry location out of direct sunlight to prevent UV damage. If possible, store it in a closed garage, shed, or covered patio where it won't be exposed to weather elements. This helps maintain the float's shape and extends its lifespan. 

A well-ventilated indoor space is ideal, but if you must store the float outdoors, choose a shaded area with good air circulation to prevent pooling water and mildew growth. Keep the storage area clean and free from debris that could damage the float. 


### How do I clean my pool float?

For regular cleaning, allow the float to air dry completely after each use. Mix mild dish soap with water in a bucket, and use a soft sponge or cloth to wipe down the exterior. Rinse with clean water and dry thoroughly before storage to prevent mildew growth. 

For stubborn stains or algae, use a solution of one part household vinegar to three parts water. Apply the solution with a brush or sponge, let it sit for five minutes, then scrub and rinse thoroughly. Avoid harsh chemicals or abrasive materials that can damage the float's surface. 


### What's the expected lifespan of a pool float?

A well-maintained pool float typically lasts 2-3 years with regular use and proper care. Factors affecting longevity include material quality, storage conditions, and exposure to sunlight and chemicals. 

Signs that indicate it's time to replace your float include visible wear such as cracks, soft spots, or delamination; reduced buoyancy that affects your floating experience; and noticeable fading or degradation of the float's surface material.
