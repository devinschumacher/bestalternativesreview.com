## Buyer's Guide

Mid-century modern mirrors blend refined simplicity with functional elegance, making them a versatile addition to any room. When selecting the right mirror, consider these key aspects to find one that enhances your space while maintaining lasting appeal. 


### Size and Proportion

Measure your intended space carefully before purchasing a mirror. The general rule is that the mirror's height should not exceed the window height above furniture. For wall-mounted mirrors, consider their visual impact when standing at eye level. 


### Frame Style and Material

Mid-century modern frames often feature clean lines and organic elements like wood grain or curved edges. Materials commonly include natural wood, metal with a vintage patina, or sleek glass with minimal decorative elements. The frame should complement rather than overpower the room's existing design. 


### Mirroring Options

Choose between framed or frameless mirrors based on your space's lighting and design elements. Framed mirrors can hide small imperfections and add visual depth, while frameless mirrors create a more open feel. Consider whether you need the mirror to double as a decorative element in addition to its functional purpose. 


### Mounting and Installation

Proper mounting is crucial for both safety and aesthetics. Wall space should be structurally sound to support the mirror's weight. Consider using backing plates with additional reinforcement for heavier mirrors or when mounting in unconventional locations. 


## FAQ


### What makes a mirror Mid Century Modern?

A mirror features Mid Century Modern design when it incorporates elements characteristic of the 1950s and 1960s. This includes clean lines, organic shapes inspired by nature, and a preference for natural materials like wood and stone. 

Mid Century Modern mirrors often have simple, geometric frames with bold lines and asymmetrical elements. The glass may have slight warping or be left slightly unhung to create a distressed effect. 


### What sizes are available for Mid Century Modern mirrors?

These mirrors typically come in standard sizes, ranging from small wall mirrors (12-24 inches) to full-length body mirrors (5-7 feet). Custom sizes can usually be ordered with most manufacturers. 

Common dimensions include 24x36 inches for smaller wall mirrors and 60x80 inches for full-length mirrors. Specialty sizes like circles or ovals are also available for unique pieces. 


### What materials are used in Mid Century Modern mirrors?

Wood is a primary material, often featuring walnut, maple, or rosewood with natural finishes. Metal accents may include aluminum, brass, or bronze. 

The glass can be single or double-paned, with both clear and textured options available. Some mirrors incorporate beveled edges or frosted sections for visual interest. 


### How should a Mid Century Modern mirror be cleaned?

For wood frames, use a soft cloth dampened with water and a mild soap like dishwashing liquid. Avoid harsh chemicals that could damage the natural finish. 

For glass surfaces, use a microfiber cloth or clean window cleaner specifically designed for glass. Avoid abrasive materials that could scratch the surface. 


### What style combinations work well with Mid Century Modern mirrors?

These mirrors pair exceptionally well with Scandinavian, minimalist, and traditional interiors. The clean lines complement both modern and vintage furniture. 

Black-and-white photographs, mid-century furniture pieces, and woven baskets create complementary styles when hung with Mid Century Modern mirrors. 


### What should you consider when buying a Mid Century Modern mirror?

Check the frame's craftsmanship, metal hardware condition, and glass clarity. Look for signs of original finishes and avoid mirrors with excessive alterations. 

Measure your space carefully before purchasing to ensure the mirror will fit properly. Consider how the mirror's style will complement your existing decor.
