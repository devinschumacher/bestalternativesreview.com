## FAQ


### What is the maximum weight capacity I need for an 85-inch TV stand?

Most 85-inch TV stands are designed to support at least 100 pounds, but to be safe, look for a stand rated for 200 pounds or more. This ensures your stand can handle the weight of your TV and any additional accessories. 

Additional factors affecting weight capacity include the TV's mounting system, stand design, and materials used. Always check the manufacturer's specifications for the exact weight limit. 


### Can I mount my 85-inch TV directly to the wall instead of using a stand?

Wall mounts are a viable alternative to TV stands for 85-inch TVs, especially for ceiling heights or space considerations. However, the weight and size of the TV make wall mounting more complex and potentially more expensive than a dedicated stand. 

Consider factors like wall thickness, stud placement, and mounting hardware requirements before choosing between stand and wall mount options. 


### What size TV bracket should I get for an 85-inch TV?

Standard 85-inch TVs require an HDM1 200 series bracket with a minimum size of 200 x 100mm. Check your TV's documentation for specific bracket requirements and compatibility. 

Ensure the bracket supports your TV's weight and provides adequate mounting points for wall anchors or concrete screws, especially for ceiling-mounted installations. 


### How do I measure for an 85-inch TV stand?

To measure for an 85-inch TV stand, determine your TV's full dimensions, including the mount and stand heights. Add 2-3 inches to the TV's height for stand clearance and 2-4 inches for mounting bracket thickness. 

Check your TV's specific measurements since frame shapes and mounts vary. Consider both the TV's vertical height and width when selecting a stand size. 


### What materials are best for 85-inch TV stands?

Metal stands offer stability and durability but can be heavier and more expensive. Wood stands provide a stylish alternative but require regular maintenance and may not support as much weight. 

Combination stands that incorporate metal supports with wood or glass panels balance aesthetics with functionality. Consider the stand's material compatibility with your room's style and maintenance requirements. 


### How much space do I need around an 85-inch TV?

Around 30 inches of space between the TV and the front of the stand provides adequate clearance for viewing and maintenance. Include additional space for wall mounts and TV stands with built-in cable management. 

Leave at least 6 inches of space between the TV's edges and surrounding furniture to prevent accidental damage and ensure proper air circulation for the TV's components.
