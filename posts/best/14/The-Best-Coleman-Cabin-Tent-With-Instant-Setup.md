Choose between single-person or two-person configurations for solo camping or couples getaways. Two-person models typically offer more storage space for gear, while advanced options may include vestibules for gear protection and more horizontal length for comfortable staking. 


### Weather Resistance

Look for tents rated for at least 3-season use, with polyester or nylon materials reinforced for wind and water resistance. The rainfly should be double-layered for maximum protection, and the tent floor needs a durable polyethylene coating to prevent water seepage. 


### Erect Design Elements

Instant setup tents use pre-attached poles and pop-top doors for quick erection, often with no more than 10 total poles needed. Consider models with pole designs that self-support in various winds, as tested by independent experts. 


### Additional Considerations

Inspect the tent's footprint for minimal ground contact areas to reduce stake damage and improve stability. The vestibule should have a boot shelf and be fully covered by the rainfly. Check the included gear bag's capacity for transporting your tent, especially if you're traveling to remote locations.
