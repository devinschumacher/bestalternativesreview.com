## Buyer's Guide

When considering a blend jet system, several key features and factors should guide your decision. This guide outlines essential considerations to help you select the right blend jet for your needs. 


### Performance and Power

Blend jet performance is influenced by several technical specifications. The motor power determines the jet's speed and force. Look for systems with pumps ranging from 1500 to 3000 watts for standard residential use. Additionally, consider the flow rate, measured in GPM (gallons per minute), which affects how quickly the jet can deliver water. For most applications, 1-2 GPM is suitable. 


### Water and Power Consumption

Efficiency is crucial for maintaining costs and environmental impact. Check the wattage to understand power consumption, and consider systems with energy-saving features. Efficient blend jets use less than 0.4 kWh per hour. Also, evaluate water consumption. While high flow rates provide more immediate results, they increase water bills. Look for systems that maintain effective pressure while minimizing water usage. 


### Installation and Compatibility

Proper installation ensures optimal performance and longevity. Consider the required water pressure (usually 40-80 psi) and check if the system can be installed under sinks or require wall-mounted brackets. Some blend jets feature universal mounting options, while others may need specific adapters for different piping sizes. Review compatibility with your existing plumbing to avoid costly modifications. 


### Materials and Durability

Long-term performance depends on material quality. The pump housing should be made from durable plastic or metal resistant to corrosion. Check if the nozzle and mixing chamber are made from corrosion-resistant materials like brass or stainless steel. Evaluate the seals and gaskets for flexibility and longevity under varying water pressures and temperatures.
