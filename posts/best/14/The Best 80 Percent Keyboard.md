## Buyer's Guide

80% keyboards represent a popular minimalist approach to keyboard design, offering a more compact layout while maintaining essential functionality. Understanding their unique features and benefits can help you make an informed choice. 


### Key Features of 80% Keyboards

These keyboards exclude the less essential keys found in full-size layouts, typically removing the right alt/grave, right command/tilde, and the right windows/launcher keys. This results in a more streamlined design that's easier to move around on your desk while still providing full alphanumeric and functional key support. 


### Size and Space Benefits

By excluding those extra keys, 80% keyboards take up approximately 25% less space than a full-size keyboard. This makes them an ideal choice for small workspaces, gaming setups where traditional gaming keyboards are too bulky, and everyday desk use where efficiency and ergonomics are prioritized over maximum functionality. 


### Compatibility and Usage Considerations

While 80% keyboards maintain standard key placement for most letters and numbers, some layouts may have shifted key positions or removed certain symbols. Users should verify compatibility with their operating system and applications before purchasing. Additionally, they're particularly well-suited for text-based work, light gaming, and tasks where frequent use of non-standard keys (like multimedia controls or launchers) isn't required. 


### Build Quality and Long-term Use

The reduced key count doesn't compromise on durability or build quality. Most 80% keyboards maintain the same robust construction as full-size models, offering both membrane and mechanical switches with proper selection. The more compact design can actually enhance durability by reducing the number of mechanical components that might wear out over time. 


## FAQ


### What is an 80 Percent Keyboard?

80 percent keyboards are a popular variant in the gaming and mechanical keyboard market. Instead of the full 104 keys found in standard keyboards, these compact models omit certain key functions, typically the Windows key, media controls, and the numeric keypad. The term "80 percent" refers to the approximate percentage of keys present in these compact layouts. 

These keyboards combine portability with customization options. Users can choose between various layouts that meet specific needs, such as increased numpad access or simplified media controls. The reduced size often results in improved ergonomics and easier cable management, making them ideal for gaming setups and small desks. 


### What Key Features Should I Look for When Choosing an 80 Percent Keyboard?

When selecting an 80 percent keyboard, consider the layout compatibility with your current setup, the number of available RGB lighting options, and the type of switches (mechanical or membrane). Quality construction materials and anti-ghosting features are crucial for gaming performance, as are durable USB ports and wrist rest designs. 

Bluetooth connectivity, customization software, and programmable key options enhance functionality, while hot-swappable switches allow for future customization. Always check for proper key travel distance, actuation force, and anti-ghosting capabilities specific to your intended use. 


### Are 80 Percent Keyboards Difficult to Use?

The difficulty in using an 80 percent keyboard primarily depends on the layout and your existing keyboard habits.
