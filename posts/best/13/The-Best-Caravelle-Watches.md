### What is the history of Caravelle Watches?

Caravelle Watches were produced between 1956 and 1986 by Swiss watchmaker SSIH. The brand is known for its affordable yet reliable watches, particularly its date-display models. 

During its production years, Caravelle developed a strong reputation for durability and functionality, though it's important to note that original vintage models can be quite valuable today. 


### What makes Caravelle's date-display watches unique?

Caravelle's date-display watches feature a small window at the 3 o'clock position that shows the current date. These watches use a mechanism called a "complication" to display both time and date accurately. 

The date feature adds practical value by helping users quickly glance at the date without fully opening the watch face, making these models particularly useful for business and travel purposes.

### How do I care for my vintage Caravelle watch?

Vintage Caravelle watches should be cleaned regularly to remove dust and oil from the crown and bezel. The best approach is to use a soft cloth and mild soap, avoiding harsh chemicals that can damage the watch's components. 

For more thorough cleaning or service, it's recommended to have the watch professionally maintained by a watchmaker experienced with vintage Swiss timepieces. 


### What movements do Caravelle watches use?

Caravelle watches typically feature either mechanical or automatic movements, though some later models incorporated quartz technology for digital displays. Mechanical movements power the watch through hand-winding, while automatics use the wearer's movement to keep the mainspring wound. 

For enthusiasts, recognizing the type of movement can affect value and maintenance requirements, as mechanical movements usually need regular servicing whereas automatics run on natural winding.

### How much should I expect to pay for a vintage Caravelle?

Vintage Caravelle watches can range widely in price based on factors like condition, movement type, and rarity. A basic handheld model might sell for $50-100, while a more complete date-display with original box and papers could fetch $200-500. 

Highly sought-after models with original factory seals or rare complications can command prices exceeding $1,000, so it's important to research specific examples before making an offer. 


### What should I look for when buying a used Caravelle watch?

When examining a used Caravelle, check for proper functioning of all features, including the date display and any complications. The bracelet or strap should be intact with no signs of excessive wear, and all components should click and move smoothly. 

Pay attention to the watch's movement: if it's a mechanical model, the hands should move smoothly without grinding. For automatic models, the crown should feel firm and operate smoothly.
