## Buyer's Guide

Cold brew coffee makers offer a convenient way to create flavorful, concentrate-style coffee for iced drinks. These specialized brewers can produce large quantities of coffee concentrate, allowing you to enjoy cold brew anytime without the hassle of manual steeping. 


### Capacity and Concentrate Output

When selecting a cold brew coffee maker, consider the intended use. Single-serving models are ideal for personal use or small offices, while larger models can produce multiple gallons of concentrate at once, perfect for household or commercial settings. The concentration yield is typically measured in ounces of coffee concentrate per gallon of water, so determine how much concentrate you need based on your consumption habits. 

![Takeya-Cold-Brew-Coffee-Maker-2](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Takeya-Cold-Brew-Coffee-Maker-2/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Brewing Methods and Controls

Cold brew coffee makers use either a batch or continuous brewing method. Batch brewers steep coffee grounds in water for several hours before decanting the concentrate. Continuous systems use a filter and pump to circulate water through coffee grounds, producing concentrate more efficiently but at a slower pace. Look for features like programmable timers, temperature controls, and automatic shut-off to ensure consistent results and ease of use. 


### Materials and Maintenance

The build quality of your cold brew coffee maker affects both its performance and longevity. Stainless steel and BPA-free plastic are common materials for the brewer body, with glass carafes providing the best visibility for concentrate storage. Simple, open designs with few moving parts require less maintenance and cleaning. Most coffee makers need regular cleaning of grounds baskets and filter components, so choose a design that makes access to these parts easy. 

![Takeya-Cold-Brew-Coffee-Maker-3](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Takeya-Cold-Brew-Coffee-Maker-3/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Storage and Concentrate Preservation

Cold brew concentrate can lose its flavor if not properly stored. Most coffee makers include a sealed carafe or container specifically designed for concentrate storage. These containers typically maintain optimal temperature and prevent oxidation. For maximum freshness, store unused concentrate in the refrigerator within two hours of brewing, and consume it within one to two weeks. Some advanced models offer built-in refrigeration to maintain optimal storage conditions.

### What is cold brew coffee?

Cold brew coffee is a coffee preparation method that involves steeping coffee grounds in cold water for an extended period, typically 12-24 hours. This process results in a smoother, less acidic coffee with a distinctively mellow flavor profile compared to traditionally brewed coffee. 

Unlike other brewing methods, cold brew does not require heat, making it ideal for cold weather or when a coffee concentrate is needed for iced coffee drinks throughout the year. 


### How does the Takeya cold brew coffee maker work?

The Takeya cold brew coffee maker features a simple yet effective design. Coffee grounds are placed in the reusable filter, which is then inserted into the glass carafe. Water is poured over the grounds, and the entire assembly is left to steep in the refrigerator for 12-24 hours. 

After steeping, the filter is removed, and the coffee concentrate is ready to be diluted with water or enjoyed black. The glass carafe is dishwasher-safe for easy cleaning.

### How do I properly maintain the coffee maker?

After each use, hand wash the glass carafe and filter with mild soap and water. Ensure both components are completely dry before storing to prevent mold and mildew growth. If using the carafe for other purposes, hand wash it and the filter separately to maintain their intended functionality. 

Store the filter in a clean, dry place when not in use. The carafe can be stored upright in a cabinet or on a shelf, but avoid stacking with heavy items directly on top to prevent potential breakage. 


### What size is the coffee concentrate yield?

When using medium grind coffee and following the 1:4 coffee-to-water ratio, one full carafe (approximately 64 ounces) will yield about 16 servings of coffee concentrate. This allows for flexibility in preparation, as you can adjust the amount of concentrate based on your preferred coffee strength. 

For dilution, start with a 1:1 ratio of coffee concentrate to water for plain coffee, and adjust based on personal taste preferences. The concentrate can be stored in the refrigerator for up to two weeks for consistency in your coffee flavor profile.
