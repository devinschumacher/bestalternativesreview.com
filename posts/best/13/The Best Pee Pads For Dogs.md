### What size range do pee pads come in?

Pee pads usually come in three standard sizes: small (for puppies or cats), medium (for small dogs), and large (for medium to large dogs). Some brands offer extra-large sizes for extra-heavy-pee situations.

### How many pee pads should I stock up on?

The number of pads needed depends on your dog's size and urinary needs. Small dogs may need 1-2 pads daily, while larger dogs might require 3-4 pads. Consider buying enough pads for 2-3 days of use if you're just starting out.

### How often should I replace the pee pads?

Refrigerated pee pads (designed for medical or travel use) should be changed daily. For standard absorbent pads, replacement frequency depends on usage – typically every 24-48 hours for daily cleaning. 

Check the pad's moisture level before replacing: if it feels damp or shows any signs of leakage, it's time for a replacement. Always keep extra pads on hand to prevent any accidents from causing damage.

Used pee pads should be treated as regular household waste. Place them in a sealed plastic bag before disposal to prevent any moisture from leaking out.
