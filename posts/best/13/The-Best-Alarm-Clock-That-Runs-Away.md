## FAQ


### What sets an alarm clock that runs away apart from regular alarm clocks?

These specialized alarm clocks feature unique mechanisms that physically move away from their base when the alarm goes off. They typically include a motorized arm or ball that rolls to a different position, requiring you to move or reset the clock manually. This design adds an engaging element to waking up, though it's essential to ensure the clock's movement is safe and won't damage nearby objects. 

Their distinctive feature requires slightly different setup compared to traditional alarm clocks. You'll need a space where the clock's movement won't cause damage, and you might want to place it where you can easily reach it after it springs away. 


### How do these alarm clocks work to wake you up?

Unlike conventional alarm clocks that rely solely on sound, these mechanical wonders use visual and sometimes auditory cues to get you out of bed. When the alarm activates, it triggers the clock's built-in mechanism to move - this could be a ball rolling to a new position or an arm springing into action. 

The sudden movement often requires you to get out of bed to view or reset the clock, effectively waking you up without the need for loud noises. The combination of visual surprise and physical engagement is designed to be more effective than traditional alarms. 


### What types of movement do these alarm clocks display?

The most common movements include rolling balls that change color or display numbers when activated. Some models feature arms that spring up from the face or display sections that rotate. There are even clock faces that transform into working clocks when the alarm goes off. 

Each type of movement serves both as a visual indicator of time and as a physical wake-up call, requiring you to interact with the clock to fully engage with the time display. 


### Can these alarm clocks be set to different times?

Most models allow basic time setting similar to traditional clocks, with an hour and minute display. However, they often lack the precision and functionality of full digital clocks, as their primary focus is the unusual time display mechanism. 

Setting the time is typically straightforward, using buttons or rotating dials on the face of the clock. The display may be less precise due to the mechanical nature of the timekeeping, so they're best for general timekeeping rather than precise scheduling. 


### Are these alarm clocks suitable for every room?

While they make exciting wake-up companions, these alarm clocks need to be placed with care. The moving parts can damage floors, furniture, or nearby objects if they're not positioned properly. 

Avoid placing them too close to delicate surfaces, and ensure the movement direction is safe. The clock should be positioned where it can't roll into hazards like electrical sockets or sharp corners. 


### How do you maintain and clean these alarm clocks?

Regular cleaning is essential to keep the clock's mechanisms working smoothly. Use a soft cloth to wipe the face and frame, and avoid getting water or liquids near the electrical components. 

If the clock uses batteries, replace them when they're fading to maintain proper timing. For mechanical parts, ensure they have free movement without obstruction, and check that the arms or balls move smoothly without binding.
