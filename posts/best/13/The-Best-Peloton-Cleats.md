## FAQ


### What are Peloton cleats?

Peloton cleats are specialized cycling shoes designed to work with the company's indoor bikes. They feature a unique clipless pedal system that allows for secure foot attachment, enhancing both performance and safety during rides. 

The cleats are interchangeable with standard road shoes, making them a versatile addition to your indoor cycling setup. Compatible with both classic and Dura-Ace-equipped Peloton bikes, they provide a precise pedal stroke and increased power transfer compared to standard shoes. 


### How do I install Peloton cleats?

Cleats can be easily installed in any existing road shoe with a 3-bolt mounting pattern using a simple hex wrench. The process involves aligning the cleat axis with the pedal spindle and securing it with the appropriate torque setting for optimal performance and safety. 

If you're new to clipless pedals, taking the time to properly align the cleat axis is crucial. The recommended setting is 2 o'clock for left feet and 10 o'clock for right feet, but you can adjust based on personal preference or specific riding needs. 


### What types of cleats are available?

Peloton offers two main types of cleats: plastic and carbon fiber. The plastic cleats are more affordable and suitable for casual riders, while the carbon fiber options are lighter and more expensive, making them ideal for competitive cyclists seeking every possible advantage. 

Both versions come with interchangeable cleat faces in either a 2-bolt or 3-bolt pattern, giving you flexibility based on your existing pedals. The main difference is that carbon fiber cleats are compatible with more expensive Dura-Ace-equipped Peloton bikes. 


### How long do Peloton cleats last?

With regular use, Peloton cleats typically last between 1,000 to 1,500 miles before needing replacement. Factors affecting longevity include riding frequency, shoe maintenance, and the quality of the cleats themselves. 

Most riders find that plastic cleats need changing slightly more frequently than carbon fiber versions, but both types offer good value for money when compared to other brands. Regular inspection for wear patterns is recommended to spot any potential issues early. 


### Can I use my own shoes with Peloton cleats?

Yes, you can use your own shoes with Peloton cleats by purchasing the cleats separately and installing them yourself. This allows you to maintain your preferred shoe style while gaining the performance benefits of clipless pedals. 

Keep in mind that the cleats are compatible with any road shoe that uses a 3-bolt mounting pattern. Popular brands like Shimano, Look, and Speedplay all produce compatible cleats if you prefer not to use official Peloton options. 


### What's the difference between official and third-party cleats?

Official Peloton cleats are made specifically for their bikes and are designed to work seamlessly with the company's proprietary technology. They come in both plastic and carbon fiber versions and are readily available through the Peloton website. 

Third-party cleats, while compatible with the 3-bolt mounting pattern, may not offer the same level of integration with Peloton's system. These alternatives can be found from various manufacturers and retailers but might not provide the same performance benefits or reliability.
