## Buyer's Guide

Aztec bedding combines bold prints with traditional craftsmanship, making it a distinctive choice for modern bedrooms. Understanding the key aspects of Aztec designs helps you select a set that complements your space while maintaining cultural sensitivity. 


### Pattern and Symbolism

Aztec designs feature ancient symbols and geometric patterns that represent various aspects of Mayan and Aztec culture. Common elements include feathered serpents, jaguars, and sun discs. Each motif carries significant meaning, from representing military power and fertility to symbolizing natural elements and cosmic order. 


### Color Palette

The traditional Aztec color scheme includes rich earthy tones like deep reds, terracotta, and ochre paired with black and white. Modern interpretations often incorporate vibrant blues, greens, and yellows while maintaining the strong contrasts typical of traditional Aztec art. 


### Care Requirements

Aztec bedding fabrics, commonly cotton or cotton blends, require regular care to maintain their quality and integrity. Machine washing in cold water with mild detergent is recommended, while air drying or using the lowest heat setting on your dryer helps preserve the patterns. Avoid bleach and harsh chemicals that can damage the fibers and fade the colors. 


### Cultural Considerations

When selecting Aztec bedding, consider the source of the designs and products. Authentic pieces often come from communities that maintain traditional craftsmanship. Opt for companies that provide clear information about their manufacturing processes and support fair trade practices to ensure cultural respect and ethical production.
