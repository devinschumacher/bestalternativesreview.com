### How deep can the Vanquish 540 detect objects?

In optimal conditions, the Vanquish 540 can detect objects up to 12 inches (30 cm) deep for most metals. This includes iron, aluminum, and stainless steel objects of various sizes. 

For smaller objects like coins, the detection depth is typically between 7-9 inches (18-23 cm). Precious metals may require shallower depths due to their density and conductivity. 


### What is V-Controlled Target Identification?

V-Controlled Target Identification analyzes the target's electrical properties to provide detailed information about what you've found. This feature gives you immediate feedback on the type of metal and its size before you unearth it. 

The system works by measuring the target's resistance and inductance, comparing these values to its internal database to determine the most likely metal type. This helps prevent false alarms and increases your confidence in each find.

### Is the Vanquish 540 suitable for beach and waterlogged environments?

The Vanquish 540 performs well in beach environments but requires specific settings for consistent detection. Using the saltwater mode and proper ground balance adjustment helps remove mineralization interference. 

For waterlogged areas like ponds or lakes, the detector can find objects down to 12 feet (3.6 meters) deep. However, significant water currents and poor visibility limit the effectiveness in these environments. 


### Can the Vanquish 540 detect objects through vegetation or ground cover?

The Vanquish 540 has limited ability to penetrate ground cover like leaves, grass, or dirt. For best results, clear a 12-inch (30 cm) path around your detecting area and use the Discrimination settings to minimize interference. 

Objects under dense vegetation may show up as distorted signals or as smaller than actual. Clearing the area of overgrowth and using the V-ID mode helps in identifying buried objects through ground cover.
