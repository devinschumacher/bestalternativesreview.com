## Buyer's Guide

Fajas reductoras combine support and compression to help manage body measurements, particularly in the waist and abdomen. Understanding key aspects will help you select a product that meets your needs while ensuring comfort and effectiveness. 


### Measurement and Fit

Begin by measuring your natural waist, hips, and the desired top edge of the faja. These measurements guide selection within your size range. Proper fit allows 1-2 inches of space between the material and your skin for optimal comfort and effectiveness. 


### Construction and Materials

Top-quality fajas feature double-layered stretch fabric with reinforced edges. The inner layer provides comfort against the skin, while the outer layer offers durability and shape retention. Velcro closures or adjustable straps enable personalized fit while maintaining ease of removal. 


### Compression Strength

Compression levels range from light (10-15 mmHg) to firm (20-30 mmHg). Light compression helps with posture and mild swelling, while firm compression aids in shaping and provides more restrictive support. Consider your comfort threshold and intended use when selecting compression level. 


### Style and Features

Basic designs offer essential waist control, while premium fajas incorporate shaping panels, moisture-wicking fabrics, or cooling elements. Consider additional features like breathable mesh panels, reflective strips for visibility, or pocketed designs for personal items.
