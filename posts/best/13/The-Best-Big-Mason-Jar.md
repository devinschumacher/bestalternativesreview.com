## FAQ


### What exactly is a big mason jar?

A big mason jar is a wide-mouthed glass container with a sealable lid. The "big" designation typically refers to jars with capacities ranging from 16 to 64 ounces, though sizes may vary among manufacturers. 

These jars are available in standard and wide-mouth varieties, with capacities ranging from 16 to 64 ounces. The wide-mouth design makes them easier to fill and prepare food. 


### What makes them different from regular mason jars?

Standard mason jars usually range from 4 to 16 ounces, making them better suited for smaller quantities. The larger jars often feature slightly thicker glass to support their increased weight. 

While both types share the same sealable lid design, big mason jars typically include specialized bands and lids for their larger size, ensuring proper sealing and preservation. 


### What are they made of?

Big mason jars are crafted from tempered glass that resists cracking and shattering. The glass walls are clear, allowing for easy visibility of contents, and the lids are typically made from BPA-free plastic or porcelain-coated metal. 

The seals use either standard ring bands made from corrosion-resistant metal or specialized bands specifically designed for larger jars. 


### What are the main features of a high-quality big mason jar?

Top-tier jars feature thick, undamaged glass walls, secure sealing mechanisms with multiple lock points, and BPA-free lids that resist warping. They often include moisture-resistant sealing rings and specialized band designs for better torque application. 

Quality jars are free from visible scratches, cracks, or manufacturing defects. The lids should maintain a consistent seal after use and show minimal lid deformation when removed. 


### What are the best uses for these jars?

These jars excel for food storage, especially for preserving fruits, vegetables, and homemade jams. They're ideal for bulk storage of dried goods, crafting, or DIY projects requiring large containers. 

Users often combine them with food-grade sealant bands and lids for preserving homemade pickles, relishes, or even homemade ice cream, thanks to their vacuum-sealing capabilities. 


### How should I care for and maintain my big mason jars?

For proper cleaning, hand wash jars with warm, soapy water and a brush if needed. Rinse thoroughly and allow to air dry completely before reuse. Store lids separated from jars to prevent warping. 

Always check the seal integrity of lids before use. If the lid doesn't sit properly on the jar's rim or the seal has deteriorated, replace with a new lid to maintain food safety standards.
