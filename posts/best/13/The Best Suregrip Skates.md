## Buyer's Guide

Suregrip skates combine performance features with the comfort required for serious skating. These inline skates are designed to handle both recreational and competitive use, with a focus on durable construction and responsive wheels. 


### Construction and Durability

The frame typically uses a dual-plastic construction that provides rigidity while reducing weight. Look for a boot made from TPU (thermoplastic polyurethane) or similar materials for enhanced durability and support. The wheels usually range from 70mm to 80mm in diameter, with urethane cores that offer good speed and responsiveness. The bearings should be high-quality, with ceramic or stainless steel inserts for longer-lasting performance. 


### Lacing System

A proper lacing system is crucial for ankle support and foot positioning. Skates with a dual-lacing system, where you can tighten the top and bottom sections independently, provide the best fit. The toe strap should be adjustable and secure to prevent forward slipping. Some models feature a T-hook mechanism that allows micro-adjustments while skating. 


### Wheel and Brake Configuration

The wheel configuration typically places the largest wheels in the back (tail) for better momentum transfer. Front wheels often sit closer together than the back pair to improve stability. Most models feature a T-shaped brake that mounts on the back wheel, with adjustable pressure points to customize stopping power. Some advanced models include heel brakes or additional braking sections for extra control. 


### Cushioning and Footbeds

High-quality footbeds with layers of cushioning provide essential shock absorption during long sessions. Look for models with between 15mm to 20mm of heel rise for proper foot alignment and support. The tongue should have flexible stitching to prevent pinching, and the liner material should be moisture-wicking to keep your feet dry during use.
