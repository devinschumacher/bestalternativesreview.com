### What ingredients are typically used in keto chips?

Keto chips are made from alternative flours or starches suitable for ketogenic diets. Common ingredients include almond flour, coconut flour, tapioca starch, and inulin. These ingredients are combined with healthy fats like olive oil or avocado oil and natural flavorings. 

Many keto chips contain no artificial sweeteners or preservatives, focusing instead on natural flavor profiles using ingredients like sea salt, herbs, and spices. 


### Are keto chips suitable for people with specific dietary needs?

Yes, keto chips are designed for individuals following ketogenic diets, which emphasize high-fat, moderate-protein, and low-carb eating. They are generally appropriate for people with diabetes, those monitoring blood sugar levels, and individuals seeking weight loss through controlled carbohydrate intake. 

However, they may not be suitable for people with certain medical conditions requiring strict carbohydrate restrictions or for those who are allergic to the primary ingredients used in these products.

### What nutritional benefits do keto chips offer?

While keto chips are typically higher in fat and lower in carbohydrates compared to traditional potato chips, they can offer some nutritional benefits. Many varieties provide healthy fats, moderate protein, and essential vitamins and minerals from their primary ingredients. 

Due to their lower carbohydrate content, keto chips can help individuals following ketogenic diets reach their macronutrient goals and maintain ketosis, though portion control remains important for overall health. 


### How should keto chips be stored?

Store opened bags of keto chips in a cool, dry place, preferably in an airtight container to maintain their quality and crispness. Unopened bags can usually be stored in the pantry for several weeks. 

Avoid storing keto chips in the refrigerator, as the moisture can affect their texture. Keep them away from direct sunlight and heat sources to prevent degradation of fats and spices.
