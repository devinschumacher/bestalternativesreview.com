## FAQ


### What is Morganite?

Morganite is a variety of the mineral beryl, known for its delicate pastel pink color. It typically ranges from soft salmon to rosé hues and gets its color from trace elements of manganese. 

First discovered in 1910, morganite was named after the American industrialist and gem enthusiast J. P. Morgan. The stone is known for its gentle beauty and has become increasingly popular in modern jewelry design. 


### What makes a pink morganite ring valuable?

The value of a pink morganite ring primarily depends on the stone's color intensity, size, and quality. The most desirable stones display strong pink tones without hints of green or gray. 

Carat weight, clarity, and cut also significantly impact value. Higher carat weights command premium prices, while well-crafted cuts enhance the stone's natural beauty. 


### How should pink morganite be cared for?

Like other beryl varieties, pink morganite should be protected from harsh chemicals and extreme temperature changes. Daily wear items like perfumes and lotions should be applied before putting on a morganite ring. 

Clean your ring with mild soap and warm water once a week. For more thorough cleaning, soak it in warm water for 10-15 minutes, then use a soft-bristle brush to remove any remaining residue. 


### What metals are commonly used with pink morganite?

While pink morganite can be set in various metals, platinum and white gold are particularly complementary, helping to enhance its natural pink hue. These metals also provide durability for the ring. 

Rose gold can work well with pink morganite, though it may slightly alter the stone's appearance. For those preferring silver metals, pink gold varieties are the best choice to maintain the stone's pink color intensity. 


### How does pink morganite compare to other pink gemstones?

Pink morganite generally falls between aquamarine and rose quartz in terms of its pink color intensity. It's softer than diamond with a hardness of 7.5-8 on the Mohs scale, but still more durable than many alternative pink gemstones. 

Jacqueline Kennedy's famous pink morganite ring (in white gold) demonstrated the stone's ability to stand on its own while remaining elegant and sophisticated.
