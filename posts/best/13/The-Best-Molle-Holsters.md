Molle holsters are designed to fit snugly over NATO-standard PALS webbing, which is found on most modern issued utility belts and backpacks. Retention is crucial for reliable weapon carry, especially in tactical environments. ### What is a Molle holster?

Molle holsters are designed to attach to MOLLE (Modular Lightweight Load-carrying Equipment) webbing systems, providing a flexible platform for carrying firearms and accessories. These holsters offer both mounting options and retention methods to suit different carry preferences and firearm types. 

Key features include quick-release mechanisms, adjustable attachment points, and secure retention solutions. They come in various designs, from conventional belts to specialized carrier systems, allowing users to customize their gear configuration. 


### How do Molle holsters attach to MOLLE webbing?

Molle holsters typically feature heavy-duty stitching or reinforced webbing at the attachment points. These areas accommodate MOLLE clips, Velcro, or other fastening systems found on tactical vests, bags, and backpacks. The design allows for quick attachment and secure retention to the webbing platform. 

Users can position the holster at different angles and locations on their MOLLE system, enabling versatile carry options. The attachment points distribute the weight evenly, preventing sagging and maintaining stability during movement.

### What types of firearms can be carried in Molle holsters?

Molle holsters are suitable for a wide range of firearms, including pistols, revolvers, and short-barreled rifles. They accommodate both duty and personal defense weapons, offering adjustable retention and mounting options for various firearm sizes. 

Specialized holsters are available for specific weapon models, providing optimal fit and function. Users can mix and match holster types on their MOLLE system to carry multiple firearms or accessories efficiently. 


### How do Molle holsters ensure secure retention?

Molle holsters employ a combination of retention methods, including thumb breaks, paddle releases, and internal clips. These mechanisms prevent accidental discharge while allowing quick draw for tactical situations. 

The holsters feature reinforced construction with double stitching and heavy-duty webbing to resist tearing and maintain integrity under stress. Users can adjust the retention tension to balance between secure carry and quick deployment.
