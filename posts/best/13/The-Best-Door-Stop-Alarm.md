### What is a Door Stop Alarm?

A door stop alarm is an electronic device designed to alert you when a door is closed. It typically consists of a magnet and a sensor that trigger an alarm when the magnetic fields are separated. 

These alarms provide peace of mind by helping prevent door closures that might trap someone inside or lock them out, particularly for individuals with mobility challenges or young children. 


### How Does a Door Stop Alarm Work?

The alarm system works through a simple magnetic sensing mechanism. The sensor unit attaches to the door frame, while the magnet mounts on the door. When the door is open, the magnetic fields remain connected, keeping the sensor inactive. 

As soon as the door closes, the magnetic fields separate, activating the sensor and triggering the alarm. The sound levels can usually be adjusted, and some models offer additional features like flashing lights.

### What Are the Benefits of a Door Stop Alarm?

Door stop alarms enhance safety by immediately alerting you when a door has been closed, especially in environments where mobility may be impaired. They prove particularly useful for elderly individuals, people with physical limitations, and families with young children. 

Besides safety, these alarms offer convenience by preventing accidental trapping and ensuring that doors remain accessible. They can also help detect unauthorized entry attempts when used in conjunction with proper security protocols. 


### Are Door Stop Alarms Easy to Install?

Installation typically requires minimal expertise. The basic setup involves mounting the sensor unit to the door frame and attaching the magnet to the door in close proximity. The alignment needs to be precise to maintain proper magnetic field communication. 

Most alarms come with clear, illustrated instructions. Some models include adhesive mounts for the sensor unit, while others might require mounting hardware. The magnet can usually be attached using nails, screws, or magnetic strips.

### What Maintenance Requirements Are There?

Basic maintenance for door stop alarms includes regular inspection of the magnetic connection between the sensor and the magnet. Ensuring the hardware remains clean and free from debris prevents false alarms and maintains sensitivity. 

The battery should be checked monthly and replaced when the alarm chirps. It's also advisable to test the alarm weekly by manually separating the magnetic fields to verify proper functionality. 


### How Long Do Door Stop Alarms Last?

With regular use and proper maintenance, door stop alarms typically last between 3-5 years. The lifespan depends on the quality of the hardware, battery longevity, and the frequency of use.
