## FAQ

![Handlebar-Tape-4](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Handlebar-Tape-4/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What is handlebar tape?

Handlebar tape is a protective covering applied around a bike's handlebars to improve grip, prevent blisters, and enhance comfort during rides. 

Modern handlebar tape typically comes in lengths of 120 cm, with widths ranging from 24 to 36 mm. It's made of layers of fabric covered in durable, non-slip backing. 


### How long does handlebar tape last?

Handlebar tape usually lasts 6-12 months with regular use, depending on riding conditions and personal maintenance habits. 

Signs it's time to replace include visible wear, peeling tape, or reduced grip due to deterioration. 

![Handlebar-Tape-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Handlebar-Tape-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What materials are handlebar tapes made from?

Standard handlebar tapes combine cotton fabric with polyurethane coating for durability and moisture resistance. 

Performance tapes often feature additional materials like silicone coating for enhanced grip, while some incorporate antimicrobial treatments to prevent bacteria growth. 


### How do you measure handlebar tape?

Measure the circumference of your handlebars, adding 40-50 cm for overlap and seam allowance. 

For drop bars, measure the width of the bars plus 5-10 cm per drop for additional grip. 

![Handlebar-Tape-6](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Handlebar-Tape-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### How do you install handlebar tape?

Apply tape in one direction, smoothing out air bubbles as you go. For complete coverage, place a second, slightly shorter piece over the joint. 

Trim excess tape, then fold the ends under twice to secure them. Use tape or rubber bands to keep the ends in place until they've adhered fully. 


### How much does handlebar tape cost?

Entry-level handlebar tape costs between $5-15 per set, while premium options with advanced features can range from $15-30. 

The total cost depends on the number of bars you're covering and whether you're replacing worn tape or upgrading to a different style.
