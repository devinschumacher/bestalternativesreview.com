### How does a cordless heat gun work?

The core component of a cordless heat gun is a high-speed airflow generator and an electric heating element. When you activate the gun, the motor draws in air, which passes through the heating element and becomes hot. This hot air is then directed out through an adjustable nozzle, allowing you to apply targeted heat to various surfaces. 

The cordless design allows for greater mobility compared to traditional heat guns, as it's powered by a rechargeable battery instead of an electrical cord. Most models feature an interchangeable nozzle system, which lets you vary the airflow and heat intensity for different applications. 


### What are the benefits of using a cordless heat gun?

Cordless heat guns offer several advantages. They eliminate the need for power outlets, making them ideal for use in tight spaces or outdoors. The rechargeable battery provides convenient portability, and most models offer runtime options that are sufficient for most jobs. The lightweight design, often less than 4 pounds, makes them comfortable to use for extended periods. 

The versatility of cordless heat guns extends to various applications, from soldering and brazing to curing adhesives and shrinking tubing. They're particularly useful in outdoor projects, automotive maintenance, and quick fixes around the home or shop.

### How long do cordless heat gun batteries last?

A typical cordless heat gun battery lasts between 30 to 45 minutes per charge, depending on the model's power requirements and energy-efficient design. Entry-level units may provide shorter runtimes, while professional-grade models often offer extended battery life. 

Most heat guns feature interchangeable battery packs, allowing you to continue working when the primary pack needs charging. This dual-battery system enhances productivity by enabling seamless switching between charged packs while the others are recharging. 


### What safety features should I look for in a cordless heat gun?

Key safety features include temperature controls that prevent overheating, automatic shut-off functions when the gun is not being used, and cool-touch housings to protect the operator from burns. Many models also incorporate low-oil shut-off to prevent damage from overheating heating elements. 

Additional safety considerations include ergonomic handles for secure grip, clear temperature displays, and durable construction to prevent accidental damage. Look for products that pass stringent safety certifications from recognized standards bodies.

### What's the difference between manual and automatic trigger control?

A manual trigger control allows continuous heat application during steady pressure. This mode is suitable for precise work where you need to maintain consistent temperature and airflow. 

Automatic trigger control typically provides a pulsating heat output when the trigger is held, which helps prevent overheating and allows the heating element to cool between pulses. This mode is ideal for applications where you need quick bursts of heat followed by cooling periods. 


### Can I use a cordless heat gun with paint?

Cordless heat guns can be used to soften painted surfaces, but they're not specifically designed for painting applications. Be cautious when applying heat to painted surfaces, as it can cause warping, blistering, or color changes. Always test on a small, hidden area first. 

The high heat from a heat gun can loosen paint more quickly than traditional methods, making it suitable for removing paint from metal or plastic. However, use extreme caution not to exceed the paint's heat tolerance and to maintain consistent temperature control.
