### Power Options

Choose between electric and manual pumps. Electric pumps offer more power for deeper milk extraction and are convenient for home use. Manual pumps are portable and ideal for travel or situations without electricity. Consider whether you need a double or single pump based on how many breasts you plan to pump simultaneously.

### Siphoning vs. Clamping Action

Siphon-style pumps create a vacuum through a sliding mechanism, while clamping pumps use a squeezing motion. Siphon pumps typically extract milk more efficiently but may not be as comfortable for some users. Clamping pumps provide a more natural breastfeeding sensation but might be less effective for some milk types. 


### Storage Compatibility

Ensure the pump accessories are compatible with your preferred storage containers. Common standards include Medela, Lansinoh, and Avent. Consider whether the pump comes with its own storage solutions or if you need to purchase additional containers compatible with your pump's specific design.

### Maintenance Requirements

Some pumps have more disposable parts, while others require frequent cleaning. Consider how much time you're willing to spend on maintenance and cleaning. Pumps with fewer parts or easier-to-clean designs may be more practical for busy schedules.
