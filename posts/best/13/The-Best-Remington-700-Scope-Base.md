## Buyer's Guide

The Remington 700 scope base is a key component for mounting optics on this popular rifle model. Understanding the available options and specifications will help you select the right base for your shooting needs. 


### Material Options

Common materials include steel, stainless steel, and aluminum. Steel bases offer excellent rigidity but are heavier. Stainless steel provides durability with better corrosion resistance. Aluminum bases are lighter but less robust. 

![Remington-700-Scope-Base-2](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Remington-700-Scope-Base-2/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Mount Configuration

Single base mounts provide basic optics support, while dual base mounts offer increased stability for heavier scopes. The mounting configuration should match your selected scope's base requirement (T22L, T22S, or 11mm ring). 


### Height Options

Bases come in different height ratings (0.350", 0.500", 0.660", etc. ). The appropriate height depends on your intended scope ring setup and desired eye relief. Proper height ensures proper scope alignment and shooting fundamentals. 

![Remington-700-Scope-Base-3](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Remington-700-Scope-Base-3/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Installation Considerations

Check your rifle's receiver for adequate base mount clearance. The base should sit securely but not interfere with bolt operation. Consider the base's alignment groove or datum point for precise scope installation.

### What are the primary benefits of using a Remington 700 scope base?

Remington 700 scope bases offer several advantages, including superior stability through their one-piece construction, reliable mounting through consistent contact with the rifle's action, and adjustable bases that allow for precise zeroing. These bases are specifically engineered to work with the Remington 700's unique design. 

Unlike two-piece bases that can loosen over time, Remington's single-piece design maintains consistent scope height and orientation, crucial for maintaining accuracy and repeatable shooting positions.

Remington 700 scope bases include mounting provisions for accessories like red dot sights and other optics. The bases feature Picatinny rails compatible with a wide range of accessories, allowing for customization while maintaining proper alignment and stability.

### What are the installation requirements for Remington 700 scope bases?

Installation of Remington 700 scope bases typically requires no additional modifications to the rifle. The bases sit directly on the rifle's action and secure through the mounting holes provided in the receiver. Proper installation involves aligning the base's mounting holes with the rifle's, ensuring all bolts are securely tightened. 

Always use the correct torque specifications when tightening base bolts to prevent damage to the rifle or base. Over-tightening can strip internal rifle components, while under-tightening will compromise the base's stability.
