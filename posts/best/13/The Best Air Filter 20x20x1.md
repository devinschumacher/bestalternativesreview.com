## Buyer's Guide

When selecting an air filter with dimensions 20x20x1 inches, several key factors influence performance and longevity. Understanding these aspects helps in choosing a filter that meets specific needs while providing cost-effective maintenance. 


### Filter Media

The filtration material determines efficiency against particles and gases. Common media include fiberglass, pleated paper, and electrostatically charged cotton. Higher-quality filters with denser media capture more allergens and pollutants but may restrict airflow. 


### MERV Ratings

Air filtration systems use Minimum Efficiency Reporting Values (MERV) to measure efficiency. Ranges typically span 1-16, where higher numbers indicate better particle capture. For residential use, MERV 8-13 balances effectiveness and energy consumption. 


### Frame Construction

Filter frames come in metal, foam, or plastic. Metal provides durability but can be noisy with forced-air systems. Foam offers good airflow and quiet operation but requires more frequent replacement. Plastic frames strike a balance between cost and performance. 


### Compatible Systems

Ensure the filter fits your system's frame size and pressure drop capacity. Excessive resistance increases energy consumption and strain on HVAC components. Consider the system's CFM (cubic feet per minute) rating to select an appropriate MERV level without compromising airflow.
