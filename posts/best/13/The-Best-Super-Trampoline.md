## Buyer's Guide

Super trampolines combine impressive bounce height with durable construction, making them a popular choice for home gyms and entertainment. Understanding the key features and specifications will help you select the right trampoline for your needs. 


### Frame Types and Durability

The frame is the trampoline's backbone and comes in three main types: steel, aluminum, and fiberglass. Steel frames are the most robust, suitable for heavy use and large users, while aluminum frames offer a good balance of weight and durability. Fiberglass frames are the lightest and most flexible, ideal for casual use or portable models. 

![Super-Trampoline-2](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Super-Trampoline-2/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Bounce Pads and Safety

Bounce pads vary in quality and design, affecting both safety and durability. Look for closed-cell foam pads with multiple layers for better impact absorption. The depth of the pad affects roll-off distance and should be deeper for more protection. Safety features like edge coating, rubber matting, and safety nets should also be standard. 


### Catchrails and Installation

Catchrails prevent the trampoline from tipping and provide additional safety by keeping users within the main bounce area. Quality catchrails are rigid and adjust to different ground types. The installation base should include ground anchors and levelers to ensure stability, with most quality trampolines requiring a minimum 8 feet by 8 feet base area. 

![Super-Trampoline-3](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Super-Trampoline-3/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Spring Quality and Number

Trial-quality springs can wear out quickly and pose a safety hazard. High-quality springs, typically made from galvanized steel or stainless steel, provide consistent bounce and last longer. The number of springs affects both performance and durability – more springs increase bounce height and reduce pressure on the surrounding frame components.
