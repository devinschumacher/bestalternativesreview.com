## FAQ

### What is a cleaver?

A cleaver is a versatile kitchen tool that combines a thick, heavy blade with a sturdy handle. It's designed for tasks that regular knives can't handle, making it essential for meat processing, cutting through tough ingredients like bone, and general kitchen chopping. 

Unlike other kitchen knives that taper from the blade's spine to the tip, a cleaver's spine remains thick and flat, giving it exceptional stability during cutting and chopping. This unique design makes it ideal for splitting wood, crushing garlic, and precisely cutting through dense foods like brisket or pork ribs. 


### How is a cleaver different from other kitchen knives?

A cleaver's primary distinction lies in its thick blade and flat spine, which provide unmatched stability during powerful cuts. Unlike chef's knives or santoku blades, which taper gradually from spine to tip, a cleaver maintains a consistent thickness throughout, allowing for controlled chopping and slicing. 

The heaviest part of a cleaver is its pakkawood handle, which features integrated metal bolstering for durability. This design differs from other kitchen knives where the heaviest part is typically near the blade, making the cleaver uniquely suited for tasks requiring both precision and brute force. 

![Cleaver-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Cleaver-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What materials are used to make cleavers?

Cleavers are available in three primary materials: stainless steel, carbon steel, and clad stainless steel. Stainless steel offers the best corrosion resistance but may not hold an edge as long as carbon steel. Carbon steel provides excellent edge retention but requires regular maintenance to prevent rust. Clad stainless steel combines the corrosion resistance of stainless steel with the sharpness of carbon steel through a layered construction. 

The blade core is usually made from high-carbon steel, while the outer layers are stainless steel. Common core thicknesses range from 1.2mm to 1.8mm, with specialized butcher's cleavers often featuring even thicker cores for maximum durability. 


### What shapes are available for cleaver blades?

Cleaver blade shapes primarily differ in length and spine width rather than curvature. Common lengths range from 150mm to 180mm, with longer blades offering more cutting surface and shorter blades providing better control for detailed work. Spine widths typically vary between 30mm and 40mm, with wider spines offering greater stability during heavy cutting tasks. 

Blade edges may feature either a straight grind or a modified chisel grind. Straight-edged cleavers excel at precise slicing and chopping, while chisel-edged cleavers provide better control for pushing and rocking motions, making them ideal for tasks like splitting wood or breaking down large cuts of meat.

### How should you maintain a cleaver?

Proper maintenance extends a cleaver's lifespan and ensures it remains safe and effective. Regularly sharpen the blade using a honing steel or sharpening stone, maintaining a uniform angle across the entire blade. After each use, clean the blade thoroughly and dry it completely to prevent rust formation. 

Store the cleaver in a dry place and use a properly fitted sheath or protective cover when not in use. This prevents accidental damage and keeps the blade's edge sharp for consistent performance in the kitchen. 


### What are the best cleavers for home use?

The best cleavers for home use balance durability, affordability, and practicality. Look for blades made from high-quality stainless steel with thick cores and robust pakkawood handles. Common weight ranges between 700g and 1000g provide sufficient heft for most home cooking tasks while remaining manageable for daily use. 

Consider brands that offer warranties and reliable customer support. Popular choices include Wusthof, Henckels, and Shun, which combine durable construction with reasonable pricing for everyday kitchen use.
