### What makes an inflatable 1-person kayak different from a traditional kayak?

Unlike rigid kayaks, inflatable kayaks use a PVC or TPU tube for their frame, which makes them more portable and resistant to damage. These kayaks sacrifice some durability and performance for their convenience. While they excel in calm waters and as a portable option for travel, they're not suitable for rough conditions or serious paddling. 


### How do you inflate an inflatable kayak?

Most modern inflatable kayaks come with a built-in inflation system that uses a dedicated pump or can be connected to an electric inflator. The process typically takes less than 30 minutes, depending on the size of the kayak and the quality of the pump. 

Ensure you inflate the kayak to the recommended pressure, usually indicated on the side of the kayak, to maintain its shape and stability. Overinflation can reduce comfort, while underinflation leads to poor performance and potential damage.

Store the kayak in a dry place when not in use, and check it monthly for any signs of wear or damage. If you notice any issues, address them promptly to prevent costly repairs down the line.
