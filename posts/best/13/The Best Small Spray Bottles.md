### What is the capacity range of small spray bottles?

Small spray bottles typically range from 1 to 20 ounces in capacity. The most common sizes fall between 4 and 16 ounces, offering a good balance between portability and product volume. 

Capacity depends on the intended use. For everyday home products, 8-ounce bottles are practical. Professional applications often require larger 16-ounce containers, while travel-sized products might range from 1 to 4 ounces. 


### What materials are small spray bottles made from?

These bottles are commonly made from BPA-free plastic or glass, with food-grade materials preferred for household use. Plastic bottles offer better portability and resistance to breakage, while glass provides superior transparency and durability. 

The sprayer mechanism usually consists of stainless steel or food-grade plastic components. These materials ensure longevity and prevent corrosion, maintaining product quality during use.

### How should small spray bottles be stored?

Store spray bottles in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and extreme temperature fluctuations. Both plastic and glass bottles can be stored upside down to prevent blockages, but ensure the bottle is upright before use. 

Avoid freezing spray bottles, as rapid temperature changes can cause cracking. Always close the bottle tightly after each use to prevent leaks and maintain product integrity. 


### What cleaning requirements do small spray bottles have?

Regularly empty and clean the bottle between uses to prevent residue buildup and maintain performance. Empty the product completely and remove the sprayer mechanism if possible for thorough cleaning. 

Hand wash with mild soap and warm water. For stubborn residue, soak the bottle in white vinegar for 20-30 minutes before cleaning. Dry completely before refilling to prevent water spots and maintain product quality.

### How can you tell if a small spray bottle needs replacement?

Signs of aging or damaged components include leakage, clogging, or corrosion of the spray mechanism. If you notice reduced spray distance or force, or unusual patterns in the stream, it may be time to replace the bottle. 

Check for cracks, discoloration, or deformation in the bottle material. Replace the bottle if you notice any changes in material integrity or performance, especially if using for food or personal care products. 


### Is it safe to put small spray bottles in the dishwasher?

Most small spray bottles can be safely cleaned in the dishwasher, but always check the manufacturer's instructions first. Clear plastic bottles are more dishwasher-safe than colored or glass containers, as colorants and glass may not withstand high temperatures. 

If your bottle is dishwasher-safe, run it through the top rack cycle with dish soap but avoid the bottom rack's harsher cleaning cycle. Hand wash any bottles containing sensitive products or those with metal parts.
