## FAQ


### What is a cartridge razor?

A cartridge razor features a replaceable cartridge containing one or more blades. The cartridge is housed in a protective frame that helps guide the blade and prevents nicks during shaving. 

These razors typically offer better blade protection than disposable razors while still being more convenient and cost-effective than straight razors. 


### How many blades are in a standard cartridge?

Standard cartridge razors usually contain 3-5 blades per cartridge. Multi-blade systems allow for closer shaves and improved cutting performance. 

The number of blades can affect razor performance, with more blades potentially providing a smoother, more efficient shave. 


### How long does a cartridge razor last?

Battery-powered cartridge razors typically last 1-2 years with regular use, depending on usage frequency and maintenance. Mechanical razors can last longer. 

Blade cartridges usually last 4-6 weeks with daily use before needing replacement, while other components may last significantly longer. 


### How should I maintain my cartridge razor?

Regular cleaning and proper storage extend the life of your razor. Remove and wash the cartridge after each use, and dry thoroughly before storage. 

Keep the razor away from extreme temperatures, moisture, and direct sunlight to prevent damage to the battery or mechanical components. 


### What are the benefits of a cartridge razor?

Cartridge razors provide a protective shaving experience with built-in lubrication, helping prevent razor burn and nicks. They're also more convenient than disposable razors and can be more cost-effective in the long run. 

Multi-blade systems can offer closer, smoother shaves, while the replaceable cartridges reduce waste compared to single-use disposables. 


### Are cartridge razors suitable for all skin types?

Most cartridge razors are suitable for all skin types, including sensitive skin. Look for models with pivoting heads or adjustable length settings for maximum comfort. 

However, individuals with severe skin conditions or razor bumps may need specialized razors. Testing on a small area first can help determine suitability.
