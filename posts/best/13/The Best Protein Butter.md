## Buyer's Guide

Protein butter combines the creamy texture of traditional butter with concentrated protein, making it a convenient addition to your nutrition routine. This buyer's guide examines the key features, nutritional benefits, and considerations when selecting protein butter products. 


### Protein Content and Sources

The primary distinction of protein butter lies in its protein content and source. Most varieties range from 10-20 grams of protein per serving, sourced from whey, casein, or plant-based options like pea and rice proteins. Understanding the protein source helps align with dietary preferences and goals. 

![Protein-Butter-2](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Protein-Butter-2/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Caloric and Nutritional Profile

While similar in basic composition to regular butter, protein butter significantly increases caloric content through added protein. A standard serving might double the protein while tripling the calories of regular butter. Carefully reviewing nutritional panels helps match intake with dietary needs and goals. 


### Texture and Spreadability

Protein butter's texture can vary based on protein type and processing methods. Whey protein tends to yield a creamier spread, while some plant-based options may have a thicker consistency. Texture preferences can influence product selection, particularly for use in baking or cooking.
