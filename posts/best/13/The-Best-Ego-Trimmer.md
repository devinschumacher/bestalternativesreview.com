## FAQ


### How long does an Ego Trimmer battery last?

Ego Trimmers use 4.0 Ah lithium-ion batteries that last approximately 40-60 minutes on a single charge, depending on usage intensity and trimmer model. The battery can be fully charged from 0% to 100% in about 3 hours. 

For extended operation, Ego offers battery packs in both 2 and 4 battery configurations. A 4-battery setup provides continuous use with quick swap capabilities, while the 2-battery setup offers longer runtime between charges. 


### What maintenance does an Ego Trimmer require?

Regular maintenance includes cleaning the motor housing after each use, checking the battery connections for corrosion, and lubricating the cutting mechanism every 10 hours of use. Replace the spark plug every year and the air filter every 20 hours or when it appears dirty. 

Storage should be done with the blade guard attached and the battery removed. Keep the trimmer in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and extreme temperature changes. 


### How do I sharpen the blade on an Ego Trimmer?

Ego Trimmers use 32-tooth aluminum alloy blades that are replaceable. To sharpen, remove the blade retaining screw, then remove the blade and hold it securely against a bench grinder at a 20-degree angle, rotating it evenly until sharp. Alternatively, use a sharpener specifically designed for aluminum blades. 

Replace the blade if sharpening does not restore proper cutting quality or if it shows signs of excessive wear, such as chipping or thinning at the edges. 


### What types of batteries are compatible with Ego Trimmers?

Ego Trimmers use 4.0 Ah lithium-ion batteries that are specific to the Ego system. These are interchangeable between trimmers within the same family but not with other brands. 

Aftermarket 4.0 Ah batteries are also available, but the Ego nameplate on genuine Ego batteries includes the model number and serial number for warranty verification. 


### How do I perform a battery inspection?

Visually inspect the battery for any signs of physical damage, such as cracks or exposed metal. Check the battery's charge indicator light to see if it displays properly. The battery should hold a charge when plugged in, and if it loses charge rapidly, it may need servicing. 

Use a volt meter to check the battery's voltage. A healthy 4.0 Ah lithium-ion battery should read between 4.1 and 4.2 volts when fully charged. If it reads below 4.0 volts, the battery may need charging or replacement. 


### Are Ego Trimmers suitable for all grass types?

Ego Trimmers are designed for cutting fescue, ryegrass, bentgrass, and other common grass types found in residential lawns. They may struggle with thick brush or heavily thatched lawns. 

For specialized grass types or difficult cutting conditions, consider using a trimmer blade specifically designed for the grass type or consult the Ego user manual for recommended settings.
