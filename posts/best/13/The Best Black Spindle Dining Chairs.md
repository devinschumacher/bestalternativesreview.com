### Maintenance and Cleaning

The chair's finish determines how much regular maintenance is needed. Polished wood requires occasional dusting and periodic reapplication of furniture wax to maintain luster. Painted spindles need simple cleaning with mild soap and water, while metal versions may rust if not properly coated. 

Upholstered chairs demand more attention to detail, with regular vacuuming and spot cleaning required to prevent stain absorption. Slatted seats are generally the easiest to maintain, as they allow air circulation that helps prevent mold and mildew growth. Always follow manufacturer recommendations for the specific care needs of your chair's materials.
