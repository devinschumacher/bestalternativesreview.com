### What are long acrylic nails?

Long acrylic nails are artificial nails created using resin and a powder mixture that's sculpted to extend beyond natural nail length. They provide length and a polished appearance while protecting the natural nail. 

Acrylic nails are bonded to the natural nail using a special cement and can last up to 2-3 weeks with proper care. They require regular maintenance appointments to shape and fill in growing-out natural nail. 


### How are long acrylic nails different fromg gel nails?

Gel nails use a UV-cured light to set the polish, while acrylic nails involve sculpting and bonding with an adhesive. Gel nails typically last 2-3 weeks with fill-ins, while acrylic nails last longer but need more frequent visits for shaping. 

Acrylic nails offer more length and thickness options, but gel nails provide a smoother polish finish and take less time to apply and remove.

### What's the process for getting long acrylic nails?

A professional applies the acrylic nail by shaping the natural nail, sculpting the extension, and bonding with a special adhesive. The final shape is refined before a UV light hardens the acrylic. 

The process usually takes 1-2 hours, including shaping and polishing time. Maintenance involves regular visits every 2-4 weeks to maintain length and refill growing-out natural nail. 


### Are long acrylic nails safe for my natural nails?

Acrylic nails are generally safe when applied by a professional. However, improper application can cause damage, including nail splitting, discoloration, and damage to the natural nail bed. 

Regular maintenance and removing acrylic nails before natural nail growth prevents over-lamination and maintains healthy nail structure.
