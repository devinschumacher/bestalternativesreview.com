## Buyer's Guide

Mini projectors combine portability with the convenience of big-screen viewing, making them ideal for home theaters, business presentations, and camping trips. Understanding key specifications and features will help you select the right model for your needs. 


### Projection Technology

Mini projectors use either LED or DLP technology. LED models offer brighter images with longer lamp life, while DLP provides sharper detail at higher resolutions. Consider your viewing environment - LED performs better in darker rooms, while DLP handles brighter spaces more effectively. 


### Resolution and Picture Quality

Resolution affects image clarity, with 1080p offering the best balance of detail and compatibility with modern content. Look for projectors with native 1080p or better. Picture quality improvements include color accuracy, contrast ratio, and refresh rate. Higher refresh rates reduce motion blur but require compatible content sources. 


### Portability and Setup

Mini projectors typically weigh between 1-2 pounds, making them easy to move and store. They usually include basic setup features like auto-adjustment and vertical lens shift. Consider models with built-in speakers and wireless connectivity for added convenience. The maximum throw distance determines screen size capabilities, so match it to your available space. 


### Power Consumption and Battery Life

Energy efficiency affects both running costs and portability. LED projectors consume less power than traditional lamps and maintain brightness over time. Battery life varies significantly, from 1-4 hours, depending on model. For extended use, look for projectors with lower power consumption and efficient cooling systems to avoid heat dissipation issues.
