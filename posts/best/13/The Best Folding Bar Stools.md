### What are the key features to consider when selecting a folding bar stool?

### What are the weight capacity options for folding bar stools?

Standard folding bar stools typically support up to 250 pounds, making them suitable for regular use by one or two people. Heavier-duty models rated for 300 to 350 pounds are available for shared spaces or commercial use. 

Always verify the weight rating before purchasing, as some lower-cost models may have reduced capacities (150-200 pounds) that aren't suitable for heavier individuals or frequent use.

### How does the folding mechanism affect the stool's usability?

The folding mechanism significantly impacts the stool's convenience and storage options. Single-action mechanisms that fold in one direction are easier to operate but take up more space when open. Double-action mechanisms fold in both directions, saving space but requiring more precise handling. 

Look for mechanisms with interlocking safety features that prevent the stool from accidentally opening during storage, while still being simple to unfold when needed. 


### What maintenance requirements should I expect for folding bar stools?

Regular maintenance includes tightening loose bolts, checking the folding mechanism for wear, and inspecting the seat for signs of cracking or sagging. Most materials require periodic cleaning with mild soap and water. 

In cold climates, consider checking for ice buildup on folding mechanisms before use. For outdoor models, additional protection against moisture and UV damage may be necessary, particularly for materials like wicker or canvas upholstery.

### How much space do folding bar stools take up when stored?

When folded, most bar stools measure between 12 to 18 inches in height, depending on the model. The footrest typically extends outward, adding about 12 inches to the overall width when folded. 

Storage options vary based on the mechanism. Single-action stools store upright and take up the most space, while double-action models can store horizontally, reducing space requirements by half.

Many folding bar stools are designed for outdoor use, featuring weather-resistant materials and protective coatings. Look for options with UV protection, water-resistant fabrics, and sealed mechanisms to resist moisture.
