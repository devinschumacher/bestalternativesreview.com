### How long does a cordless Dremel battery last?

The runtime of a cordless Dremel battery depends on the tool's power consumption and the battery's capacity. Most standard Dremel tools run on 12-volt or 18-volt batteries and can operate for 15-30 minutes on a single charge, depending on usage intensity. 

Battery life can be extended by reducing the tool's speed or using lighter-duty accessories. Modern Dremel tools also include eco-mode features that automatically reduce power consumption to preserve battery life during extended use.

Cordless Dremels incorporate several safety features to protect users during operation. ### How do I maintain a cordless Dremel?

Cordless Dremels require regular cleaning and maintenance to ensure optimal performance. After each use, the tool should be wiped down with a clean cloth to remove debris and dust. The battery should be removed and allowed to cool completely before storage. 

Regularly inspect the tool's attachments for signs of wear and replace any damaged or dull accessories. The battery should be stored in a cool, dry place when not in use and should be fully charged before first use and after every 30 charge cycles. 


### What is the difference between 12-volt and 18-volt cordless Dremels?

12-volt cordless Dremels are suitable for lighter-duty tasks and offer shorter runtime compared to 18-volt models. These tools are ideal for basic DIY projects and tasks that require less power consumption. 

18-volt cordless Dremels provide more power and runtime, making them suitable for heavier-duty applications. They offer faster cutting speeds and greater torque for tasks like metal cutting and power sanding. The trade-off is reduced portability due to increased weight and bulk.
