### Which gemstones are commonly used in mens rings?

Mens gemstone rings often feature stones like sapphires, emeralds, rubies, and tanzanite. Popular combinations include these precious stones with metals like gold, platinum, or tungsten. 

Less common but sought-after options include lab-created diamonds, tourmaline, and spessartine garnet. The choice often depends on the occasion and personal preference. 


### How durable are mens gemstone rings?

Men's rings with natural gemstones can vary in durability. Harder stones like rubies and sapphires resist scratches and wear well, while softer stones like tanzanite require more careful handling. 

Ring settings made from tungsten or tungsten carbide provide additional protection and durability compared to gold or silver settings.

### How do I clean mens gemstone rings?

For most gemstone rings, simple cleaning with warm water and mild soap is sufficient. Avoid harsh chemicals or ultrasonic cleaners that can damage settings or stones. 

Some gemstones like diamonds and emeralds have specific care requirements. Check with your jeweler for specific cleaning instructions based on your ring's design. 


### What's the best way to store mens gemstone rings?

Store each ring in its own padded box or compartment to prevent scratches and damage from contact with other jewelry. 

Keep rings away from strong chemicals and heat sources. If you're not wearing the ring, store it in a travel case when not in use.

### How can I tell if a mens gemstone is real?

Observe the stone's color, clarity, and luster. Real gemstones show consistent color throughout and natural internal characteristics called inclusions. 

Consider the setting material and jewelry certification. Look for professional certification from recognized gemological institutes. 


### What factors determine the value of mens gemstone rings?

Value depends on the gemstone quality (cut, color, clarity), the setting material, and the ring's craftsmanship. Natural stones are typically more valuable than synthetic ones. 

Rare gemstones, unique designs, and pieces from established jewelry brands generally command higher prices.
