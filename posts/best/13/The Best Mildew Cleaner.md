## FAQ


### What is mildew?

Mildew is a type of fungus that thrives in moist environments, commonly found in bathrooms, laundry rooms, and other humid areas of the home. It appears as a powdery white or grayish substance and can grow on various surfaces including shower curtains, bathroom tiles, and laundry equipment. 

Unlike mold, mildew typically doesn't produce toxic spores and is less threatening to health. However, it can cause unpleasant odors, damage materials over time, and make surfaces slippery and unsafe to walk on. 


### What surfaces can mildew cleaners effectively treat?

Mildew cleaners work on a wide variety of surfaces including ceramic tile, glass, steel, and plastic. They are particularly effective on bathroom fixtures, shower doors, sink countertops, and laundry appliances. 

These cleaners should not be used on natural stone surfaces like marble or granite, as they contain acidic ingredients that can damage these materials. Always refer to the manufacturer's instructions before application. 


### What are the key ingredients in mildew cleaners?

The most common ingredients in mildew cleaners include bleach, hydrogen peroxide, and quaternary ammonium compounds (quats). These active ingredients work together to break down mildew spores and remove stains. 

Some premium products may also contain surfactants to enhance cleaning power and additional antimicrobial agents for long-lasting protection. Always check the label for specific ingredients before purchasing. 


### How should I prepare the surface before cleaning?

Use a stiff brush or cloth to remove loose mildew and debris from the surface. For stubborn spots, you may need to soak the affected area with a water-and-detergent solution before applying the cleaner. 

Make sure the surface is completely dry before treating with mildew cleaner, as excess moisture can affect its effectiveness and can damage some materials. 


### How long does it take for mildew cleaners to work?

Most mildew cleaners begin to dissolve mildew within a few minutes of application. For tough stains, you may need to leave the cleaner on the surface for 5-10 minutes before scrubbing and rinsing. 

While the cleaning process is happening, avoid touching or disturbing the treated area to prevent spreading mildew spores to other parts of your home. 


### What should I do after using a mildew cleaner?

After cleaning, thoroughly rinse the treated surface with water and allow it to dry completely. This helps prevent residue buildup and ensures the surface returns to its original appearance. 

Regularly clean and maintain the treated areas to prevent future mildew growth. Consider using a preventive product like a mildew inhibitor for high-moisture areas.
