## FAQ


### What is edible glue?

Edible glue, also known as food-grade adhesive or confectioner's glue, is a specialized bonding agent designed for use in food applications. It's made from natural ingredients like cornstarch, tapioca starch, or modified food-grade cellulose, and has no artificial additives or preservatives. 

Unlike regular glue, edible glue is completely safe for ingestion and is commonly used in candy making, bakery decorations, and other food manufacturing processes where ingredients need to be bonded together. 


### Is edible glue safe for consumption?

Yes, edible glue is entirely safe for human consumption. It meets all food safety standards and is approved for use in food products by regulatory agencies worldwide. The ingredients are non-toxic and designed to be ingested without any adverse effects. 

However, individuals with specific food allergies should proceed with caution, as some edible glues contain corn or tapioca starch. Always verify the ingredients if you have food sensitivities or allergies. 


### What are the main types of edible glue available?

The two primary types are starch-based and cellulose-based. Starch glues are made from corn or tapioca starch and work well for quick bonding of simple ingredients. Cellulose-based glues offer stronger bonds and are better suited for holding more complex structures together. 

Some formulations include natural flavors and colors to enhance their appearance and taste when used in food products. 


### How does edible glue compare to traditional craft glues?

Unlike traditional craft glues that contain harsh chemicals and are not food-safe, edible glue is specifically designed for consumption. While it shares similar bonding properties, it's important to note that edible glue will soften when exposed to moisture and may not maintain its strength in humid conditions. 

Edible glue also has a shorter shelf life than many craft glues, so it requires proper storage to remain effective. Keep it in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. 


### What are the typical applications for edible glue?

Edible glue is primarily used in candy making to attach pieces like chocolate drops to hard candies, or to hold together fondant-covered cakes. It's also popular in homemade gummy bear recipes to keep the fruit pieces and gelatin together. 

Bakery decorators use it to create intricate designs on cakes and pastries, while candy manufacturers use it to assemble complex confections like chocolate-covered strawberries or caramel-filled cookies. 


### How should edible glue be stored?

Store edible glue in an airtight container in the refrigerator to maintain its stability and prevent separation of ingredients. The optimal temperature range is between 35-40°F (1.7-4.4°C). 

Check the container for any signs of spoilage before use, such as changes in color or texture. If the glue appears cloudy or has an off odor, discard it and use fresh product to avoid food safety risks.
