## Buyer's Guide

Glass pie dishes are popular for baking and serving, offering clear visibility into your creation while providing durability and easy cleaning. When selecting a glass pie dish, consider the following factors to ensure you choose the right size, shape, and features for your needs. 


### Size and Shape

Standard pie dish sizes range from 7 to 12 inches in diameter, with depths typically between 1.5 and 2.5 inches. Common shapes include round, square, and rectangular. Consider the size of your pie and available oven space when choosing a dish. 

![Glass-Pie-Dish-2](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Glass-Pie-Dish-2/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Material and Durability

Glass pie dishes come in various thicknesses. Thick, tempered glass offers better durability and heat resistance but is heavier. Thin glass may crack more easily but is lighter and more affordable. Check for products with reinforced rims and reinforced corners for added durability. 


### Care and Cleaning

Most glass pie dishes are dishwasher safe, making them convenient for everyday use. Consider dishes with an etched interior for easier food release and cleaner surfaces. For non-dishwasher use, select pieces with easy-to-clean interiors and exterior coatings that resist grease and stains. 

![Glass-Pie-Dish-3](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Glass-Pie-Dish-3/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Thermal Shock Resistance

Glass can crack when suddenly exposed to extreme temperature changes. Look for dishes labeled as "thermal shock resistant" or "heat resistant. " These products are designed to handle hot pies straight from the oven to the table, reducing the risk of thermal stress cracks.

### Heat Resistance and Temperature Handling

While borosilicate glass is heat resistant, avoid rapid temperature changes. Always place cold dishes in a preheated oven and remove hot dishes from the oven when fully cool. 

For freezing and baking, allow the dish to reach room temperature before use. If transferring from freezer to oven, let sit at room temperature for 30-60 minutes. 


### Care and Maintenance

Hand wash recommended for maintaining clarity and reducing water spots. If using dishwasher, use top rack and avoid harsh detergents that damage glass surfaces. 

For tough stains or baked-on food, soak the dish in warm, soapy water for 20 minutes before normal washing. Avoid abrasive cleaners and steel wool, which scratch glass surfaces.

### Storage and Lids

Cover dishes with matching lids when not in use to prevent dust accumulation and protect the glass surface. Store lids in a dry place away from direct sunlight. 

Some dishes feature snap-on lids with secure clips. For stacking, use protective paper or cloth between layers to avoid scratches and damage during storage. 


### Eco-Friendly and Sustainable Options

Browse for recycled glass options or dishes made from eco-friendly materials like biodegradable plastics for those who want a more sustainable choice. 

Consider the dish's energy efficiency - the thicker the glass, the better it retains heat. Opting for double-walled glass can further reduce energy consumption during baking.
