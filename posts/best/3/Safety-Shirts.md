### What materials are safety shirts made of?

Safety shirts are typically made from fire-resistant or heat-resistant materials such as aramid fibers (e. g. , Nomex), ceramic fibers (e. g. , Zircar), or PBI (polybenzimidazole) fibers. These materials are known for their exceptional thermal insulation properties, which helps to reduce the risk of burns and other injuries in high-heat situations. 

Other materials used in safety shirts may include cotton, polyester, or a combination of these materials. While these materials may not offer the same level of heat resistance as aramid fibers or similar materials, they can still provide some protection against cuts, abrasions, and other workplace hazards. 


### Who benefits from wearing safety shirts?

Safety shirts are essential for individuals working in high-risk occupations such as firefighting, welding, construction, or any other job that involves working with fire, heat, or hazardous materials. These shirts can help prevent injuries, burns, and other potential harm by providing a protective barrier between the wearer and their work environment. 

In addition to professionals, safety shirts can also be beneficial for hobbyists, volunteers, or anyone engaging in activities that pose a risk of injury.
