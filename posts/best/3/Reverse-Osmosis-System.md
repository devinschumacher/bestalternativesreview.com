### What is a Reverse Osmosis (RO) System?

A Reverse Osmosis System is a type of water filtration system designed to remove impurities from tap water. It uses a semi-permeable membrane to remove up to 99% of dissolved salts, particles, organics, bacteria, and pyrogens. The process is called reverse osmosis because the membrane allows water molecules to pass through, but blocks larger particles and contaminants. RO systems provide high-quality water suitable for drinking and cooking. 

The system consists of pre-filters, a reverse osmosis membrane, a storage tank, and post-filters. Pre-filters remove larger particles such as dirt, dust, and sediment. The RO membrane filters water by applying pressure to create a barrier against contaminants. Post-filters typically add minerals and improve the taste, odor, and appearance of the water. The storage tank stores the filtered water, and a faucet provides access to the purified water. 


### How does a Reverse Osmosis System work?

The Reverse Osmosis System works by forcing water through a semi-permeable membrane, which is designed to allow only water molecules to pass through. The membrane is designed to have an extremely small pore size, preventing contaminants from passing through. The process involves three steps: pre-treatment, filtration, and post-treatment. 

During pre-treatment, water may pass through sediment filters that remove dirt and debris. Next, the water is fed through a carbon filter that removes organic particles and improves taste. Then comes the reverse osmosis filtration stage, where the water is pushed through the membrane, leaving contaminants behind. The remaining water is called permeate, which passes through post-filters that improve the water's taste and add necessary minerals, such as calcium and magnesium, before it is stored in a storage tank.

### Are Reverse Osmosis Systems energy efficient?

Reverse Osmosis Systems do consume energy to function, but they generally use a minimal amount compared to other types of water filtration systems, like ultraviolet or UV systems.
