## FAQ


### What are teething rings, and what are their functions?

Teething rings are soft, flexible objects designed to help infants and toddlers cope with teething discomfort. These rings offer soothing relief by alleviating gum inflammation and irritation during the teething process. Teething rings can be found in different shapes, sizes, and materials, allowing parents and caregivers to select one that suits the needs of their child.


### What are the advantages of using teething rings?

Teething rings provide multiple benefits for both parents and babies. Primarily, they provide comfort and relief for infants experiencing intense teething pain. The act of biting and chewing on a teething ring helps alleviate gum inflammation, soreness, and irritation. Additionally, teething rings can help reduce fussiness and irritability in babies and aid in the development of fine motor skills as they grip, chew, and manipulate the ring.


### Which materials should I look for when purchasing a teething ring?

When choosing a teething ring, it's essential to consider the materials used. Opt for rings made from food-grade silicone or natural rubber, as they are non-toxic and generally safe for babies to chew on. These materials are less likely to crack or break, reducing the risk of ingestion if the ring is broken.


### How should I clean a teething ring?

Cleaning a teething ring is important to prevent the growth of bacteria and germs. Wash the teething ring after each use, and avoid using harsh cleaning agents like bleach or alcohol. Instead, use mild baby-safe soap and warm water, and clean any food particles or residue with a soft, damp cloth.
