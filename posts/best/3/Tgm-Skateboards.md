### Important Features

1. Deck Material: One of the first things to consider when buying a skateboard is the deck material. TGM Skateboards typically come with decks made from maple wood. Look for boards with a higher ply count for better durability and strength. Some decks may also feature carbon or fiberglass reinforcement for added stiffness.

### Wheel Size and Durometer

2. Wheel Size: The size of the wheels on your TGM Skateboard affects its performance. Bigger wheels provide more stability and roll over obstacles better, while smaller wheels are more maneuverable and quick. Most TGM Skateboards come with wheels in the 50-56mm range, but you may want to consider a size depending on your riding style and preferences. 


### Bearing Type

3. Bearings: High-quality bearings are crucial for smooth and efficient performance on your TGM Skateboard. Look for ABEC-rated bearings, with higher numbers indicating better precision and durability. Additionally, ceramic bearings are becoming increasingly popular due to their lower rolling resistance and durability.

### Truck Type and Size

4. Truck Type and Size: Trucks connect the deck to the wheels and determine the skateboard's turning radius. Ensure you choose the right size and type of trucks for your TGM Skateboard based on your riding style, weight, and preferred maneuverability. Popular truck brands include Independent, Venture, and Thunder.
