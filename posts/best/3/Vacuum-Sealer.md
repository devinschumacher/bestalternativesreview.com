### How does a vacuum sealer actually seal?

A vacuum sealer creates a vacuum in the bag by drawing out the air inside through suction. This is accomplished by drawing the bag into the sealing chamber using a sucking or vacuuming mechanism. Once all the air within the bag has been removed, the plastic bag is sealed closed with a heat or cold seal by applying pressure with the heat seal on the bag, melting the plastic edges which create an airtight closure. This sealed bag can then be stored in a refrigerator, freezer, or at room temperature, depending on the nature of the food item. 

It is essential to ensure you seal your food correctly to prevent spoilage, which is achieved by removing all the air and sealing the bag tightly. After each use, the sealer and bags should be cleaned to ensure optimal performance and hygiene. Most vacuum sealers come with easy-to-understand instructions and video manuals for beginners to learn the process promptly.
