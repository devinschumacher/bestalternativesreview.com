### What are washers?

Washers are circular, flat metal or plastic discs that are used to spread the load of screws and bolts evenly and prevent sinking when screwing into certain surfaces. They come in various sizes and materials to fit specific applications and provide resistance to corrosion and other wear and tear conditions. Washers are often used in assembly and construction tasks to ensure a strong and stable connection between parts. 

There are several types of washers, such as flat washers, lock washers, fender washers, and more, each with its unique features and applications. It is essential to choose the appropriate washer type and size based on the material being screwed into, the equipment being used, and the desired outcome. 


### What are the benefits of using washers?

Using washers offers several benefits when assembling or constructing parts. Firstly, washers distribute the pressure exerted by the screw or bolt, reducing the likelihood of the material deforming or cracking under stress. This is particularly important when working with materials that are prone to such issues, such as thin metal sheets or delicate plastics. 

Secondly, washers increase the effective length of the fastener, which in turn increases its holding power. This is especially useful in applications where a strong and secure connection is required, such as in heavy machinery or construction equipment. Moreover, washers prevent the fastener from sinking too far into the material, ensuring a flush surface and a more professional appearance.
