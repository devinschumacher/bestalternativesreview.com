### What's the Main Difference Between an Undermount and a Self-Rimming Sink?

The primary distinction between an undermount and a self-rimming sink lies in their installation methods and how they sit on the countertop. An undermount sink is installed beneath the countertop, providing a seamless, modern appearance with the sink's lip or rim hidden underneath. This design choice creates a continuous, streamlined look, making it especially popular for contemporary bathrooms.

In contrast, a self-rimming sink, also known as an overmount or top-mount sink, rests on top of the countertop. Its lip or rim is visible, and the sink is secured by small clips or brackets attached to the countertop. Self-rimming sinks are simpler to install but lack the sleek and modern appearance of undermount sinks.
