### What is the difference between innerspring and pocketed coil mattresses?

The main difference between innerspring and pocketed coil mattresses lies in the construction of their coil systems. In an innerspring mattress, the coils are connected to each other, forming a continuous network of support. This can result in a firmer, more traditional feel that some users may prefer. 

Pocketed coil mattresses, on the other hand, have individual coils wrapped in separate fabric pockets. This design allows each coil to function independently, providing better contouring and reduced motion transfer between coils. Pocketed coil mattresses often offer a softer, more luxurious feel compared to innerspring mattresses. 


### What is a hybrid spring mattress?

A hybrid spring mattress combines the benefits of both innerspring and memory foam or latex materials. These mattresses typically feature a layer of coils for support and durability, along with a layer of memory foam or latex for added cushioning and contouring. This design provides the best of both worlds, offering the responsiveness and bounce of a spring mattress while also providing the comfort and pressure relief of memory foam or latex. 

Hybrid mattresses are available in different configurations, with some models featuring a larger foam or latex layer, while others use more coils. These mattresses often provide excellent support and comfort, making them a popular choice for those who prefer a balance between the two materials. However, they may be more expensive than traditional spring or foam mattresses.
