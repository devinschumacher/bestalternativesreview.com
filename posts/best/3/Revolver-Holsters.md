### What types of attachment are available for revolver holsters?

There are several types of attachment systems for revolver holsters, including belt-loop, clip-on, paddle, and inside-the-waistband (IWB) designs. Belt-loop holsters are the most common and provide adjustable retention and ride height. Clip-on holsters allow for attachment to a belt or waistband without belt loops, making them convenient for casual use and easy on/off access. Paddle holsters secure the holster directly to the body, usually at the waist with an adjustable strap, while IWB holsters are hidden inside the waistband, making them more concealable. 

Each attachment style has its pros and cons. For instance, clip-on holsters offer quick and easy carry, but might not be as secure as belt-loop models. IWB holsters provide more concealment, but may require more effort to draw the gun. The roundup features a mix of these attachment styles to cater to different users' needs and preferences. 


### What is the difference between an inside-the-waistband (IWB) and an outside-the-waistband (OWB) holster?

The primary difference between inside-the-waistband (IWB) and outside-the-waistband (OWB) holsters lies in where they attach and how they carry the firearm. Both styles are designed for revolvers, but their carrying methods and visibility level vary significantly. IWB holsters are concealable and hidden inside the waistband, requiring an additional garment, such as a shirt, to completely cover the holster and firearm. IWB holsters are typically used for everyday carry or concealed carry purposes. OWB holsters, on the other hand, are worn outside of the waistband, making the firearm more visible. They offer a quicker draw and open carry options and are suitable for a variety of environments, including hunting, sport shooting, and general firearms practice.
