### What is a Sleep Sack, and Why Should You Consider it for Your Baby?

### How Do Sleep Sacks Differ from Swaddles?

While both Sleep Sacks and swaddles provide a snug and cozy sleep environment, there are a few key differences between the two. Swaddles are typically made of thin fabric and require manual wrapping, whereas Sleep Sacks are constructed from thicker, more substantial materials and incorporate sleeves that resemble a onesie. This design allows for easier application and removal by parents and provides greater freedom for baby's movement. 

Another variation is that swaddles are primarily intended for use during the first few months of a baby's life, when the snug and secure feeling of swaddling is most beneficial. In contrast, Sleep Sacks can be utilized for an extended period, even after the baby outgrows swaddling, as they are available in various sizes and offer more flexibility in terms of movement and comfort.
