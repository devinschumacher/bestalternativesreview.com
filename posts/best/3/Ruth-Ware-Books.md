### What is the plot of "The Woman in Cabin 10"?

"The Woman in Cabin 10" is a psychological thriller novel by Ruth Ware, set aboard a luxury cruise ship sailing from London to Stockholm. The book follows the story of journalist Lo Blacklock, who witnesses a woman being thrown overboard from the cabin next to hers. The plot thickens when Lo discovers that her cabinmate is dead and that the crime scene has been cleaned up, leaving her as the only witness to the horrifying event. 

As Lo becomes increasingly obsessed with uncovering the truth, she begins to suspect that those around her might not be who they seem. ### What is the author's background and literary experience?

Ruth Ware is a British author who grew up in Norfolk, England. She attended the University of Nottingham, where she earned a degree in English Literature. After graduating, she worked as a waitress, a bookseller, and a teacher before finally turning to writing full-time. Ware's first novel, "In a Dark, Dark Wood, " was published in 2014, and her most recent work, "The Turn of the Key, " was released in 2018. 

Ware has gained international acclaim for her psychological thrillers, which have been translated into over 30 languages and sold in more than 40 countries. She has been shortlisted for several literary awards, including the Stephen Leigh Literary Award for Debut Fiction and the CWA Gold Dagger Award for Best Crime Novel of the Year.
