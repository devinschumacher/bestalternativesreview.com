## Buyer's Guide

A wall-mounted squat rack is a versatile and space-saving solution for those who want to perform squats and other lower body exercises in the comfort of their own home. With various options available on the market, it's essential to consider important features, such as weight capacity, construction materials, and safety considerations, before making your purchase. In this guide, we'll outline the essential factors you should consider when buying a wall-mounted squat rack. 


### Weight Capacity

One of the most important factors to consider when purchasing a wall-mounted squat rack is its weight-bearing capacity. The rack should be able to support the weight of the user and the barbell without wobbling, tipping over, or compromising safety. Weight capacity varies across models, so it's crucial to check the manufacturer's specifications before making a purchase. As a general guideline, aim for a wall-mounted squat rack with a minimum weight capacity of 500 pounds (227 kg).

### Construction Materials

The durability and stability of a wall-mounted squat rack depend on the materials used in its construction. High-quality racks are typically made of steel or aluminum, offering excellent strength and rust resistance. Powder-coated finishes also provide an extra layer of protection against corrosion and wear.
