### Response Time

Another essential feature to consider is the monitor's response time. This refers to the amount of time it takes for the monitor to transition from one color to another. A faster response time is crucial for gaming, as it prevents ghosting or blurring of images, ensuring a smoother gameplay experience. Typical response times for gaming monitors range from 1ms to 5ms, with 1ms being the ideal sweet spot for competitive gaming. If you're more into casual gaming or general computing, a slightly slower response time may be acceptable. 


### Refresh Rate

Refresh rate, measured in Hertz (Hz), is the number of times per second the monitor can display a new image. The higher the refresh rate, the smoother and more fluid the motion on the screen appears. For gaming, you'll want a monitor with at least a 120Hz refresh rate, while dedicated gaming monitors can offer up to 360Hz. Keep in mind that even if the Viotek Monitor supports a high refresh rate, you'll need a compatible graphics card and, ideally, a powerful CPU to take full advantage of this feature.

### Panel Type

There are three main types of panels used in monitors: TN (Twisted Nematic), IPS (In-Plane Switching), and VA (Vertical Alignment). Each has its advantages and drawbacks. TN panels typically offer the fastest response times, making them popular for gaming. However, their viewing angles are limited, and colors tend to shift when viewed from an angle. IPS panels offer better viewing angles and vibrant color reproduction, making them ideal for creative work or general use. They tend to have slightly slower response times than TN panels. VA panels, on the other hand, offer excellent contrast and deep blacks, making them popular for watching movies or playing games in a dark room. They tend to have the slowest response times of the three panel types.
