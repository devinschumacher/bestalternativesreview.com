### What is the difference between suede and leather?

Although both suede and leather are derived from animal hides, they have different textures and properties. Suede is the inner layer of leather, with a softer, napped surface that provides a unique, luxurious feel. This material is typically used for clothing and accessories, as it is more pliable and breathable than leather. 

Leather, on the other hand, has a smoother, more polished surface and is typically more durable than suede. It is often used for items that require more resistance to abrasion and wear, such as shoes, bags, and furniture. While both suede and leather can be stylish and functional, the choice between them may depend on the specific needs and preferences of the wearer.

For more thorough cleaning, use a suede cleaner or a mixture of water and mild soap, but be sure to test any cleaning solution on a small, inconspicuous area first. Allow the jacket to air dry naturally, away from direct heat sources or sunlight.
