### What types of padlocks are available?

There are several types of padlocks available, including: disk-style, cylinder-style, and shackle-style padlocks. Disk-style padlocks have a flat body and a shackle that locks onto a surrounding ring, making them more compact. Cylinder-style padlocks feature a cylindrical body, while shackle-style padlocks have a U-shaped shackle. The choice of padlock depends on the user's specific needs and requirements, such as security, portability, and resistance to attacks. 

Furthermore, padlocks are available in different sizes and weights, ranging from compact and lightweight models for personal use to heavy-duty versions for industrial applications. ### What is the difference between a combination padlock and a keyed padlock?

A combination padlock typically features a numerical keypad that allows users to enter a sequence of numbers to unlock the padlock. The combination can be set and changed by the user, providing added flexibility and convenience. 

A keyed padlock, on the other hand, requires a key to unlock. These padlocks are generally more secure, as the key cannot be easily duplicated without the owner's authorization. Keyed padlocks are popular for securing valuable items and areas where security is a top priority, such as offices, storage facilities, or residential properties.
