### How do Pure Diffusers work?

Pure Diffusers work by either ultrasonic or mechanical means to break down essential oils into tiny droplets, which are then dispersed into the air. Ultrasonic diffusers use ultrasound technology to convert the essential oil into a fine mist, while mechanical diffusers use a fan to disperse the oil into the air. 

In both cases, the diffusion process helps to distribute the fragrance throughout the room, creating a pleasant aroma. The amount of time the diffuser operates can also be controlled, allowing users to customize the duration of the fragrance release. Pure Diffusers typically shut off automatically after a set time to prevent overuse or potential safety concerns.

The primary difference between ultrasonic and mechanical Pure Diffusers lies in the method used to disseminate essential oils into the air. Mechanical Pure Diffusers use a fan to disperse the essential oil into the air, creating a continuous flow of scented particles. Both ultrasonic and mechanical diffusers offer efficient methods for spreading scents around a room, but users may have a preference based on specific requirements such as noise or scent intensity.
