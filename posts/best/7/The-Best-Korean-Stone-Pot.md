Most Korean stone pots are made from granite or soapstone. ## FAQ

![Korean-Stone-Pot-4](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Korean-Stone-Pot-4/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What materials are Korean stone pots made from?

Traditional Korean stone pots are crafted from a combination of materials including clay, stone dust, and organic binders. These natural ingredients create a durable and heat-resistant base that forms the pot's structure. 

The outer layer often features a ceramic coating, which not only protects the stone but also enhances its resistance to thermal shock and corrosion from food and liquids. 


### How do Korean stone pots compare to ceramic or glass cookware?

Stone pots combine the heat retention properties of ceramic with the durability of metal. They maintain consistent temperature better than glass, which can conduct heat too quickly and cause uneven cooking. 

Unlike ceramic, stone pots are less prone to cracking from thermal shock and can handle both oven and stovetop cooking without damage. 

![Korean-Stone-Pot-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Korean-Stone-Pot-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What maintenance is required for Korean stone pots?

Most stone pots need minimal maintenance. Simple steps include cleaning with mild soap and water after each use, and applying a mineral oil coat to the outside to prevent drying and cracking. 

For particularly stubborn residue, a mixture of baking soda and water can help break down stuck-on food without harming the surface. 


### Are Korean stone pots safe for cooking acidic foods?

When properly maintained with a protective coating, stone pots are generally safe for cooking acidic foods like tomatoes and vinegar. The protective layer prevents the acidic content from reacting with the stone material. 

However, avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners that could damage the protective coating and potentially allow stone particles to leach into food. 

![Korean-Stone-Pot-6](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Korean-Stone-Pot-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Can Korean stone pots go in the dishwasher?

While some stone pots may be dishwasher-safe, it's generally recommended to hand wash them for optimal care. Dishwashers can damage the protective coating and may cause warping or uneven cleaning. 

Hand washing allows for more controlled cleaning and inspection, ensuring the protective layer remains intact and the pot maintains its aesthetic quality. 


### How do stone pots distribute heat compared to clad stainless steel cookware?

Stone pots excel at even heat distribution through their thick walls, similar to cast iron. This slow heating process prevents hot spots and ensures consistent cooking temperatures throughout the pot. 

In comparison, clad stainless steel may have faster heat response but can develop hot spots more readily, especially in thinner-walled cookware.
