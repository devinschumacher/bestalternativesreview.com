### What is a 60 degree wedge?

A 60 degree wedge is a specialized tool with a 60-degree cutting angle. This design enables precise cuts and versatile applications in woodworking and metalworking. 

Commonly used for angle work, these wedges can create consistent angles from 45 to 60 degrees. Their precision makes them valuable for both professional and DIY projects. 


### What materials are 60 degree wedges typically made from?

High-quality 60 degree wedges are usually made from alloy steel, providing durability and resistance to wear. Some premium models feature carbon steel for enhanced cutting performance. 

The handle material often includes hardwood like hickory or layered wood construction for better grip and longer-lasting performance.

### How should I maintain a 60 degree wedge?

After each use, clean the wedge with a dry cloth to remove metal shavings and debris. For storage, protect the blade with a blade cover or store it in a dry place to prevent rust. 

When the blade shows signs of wear or damage, have it professionally sharpened to maintain its cutting edge. Regular maintenance extends the tool's lifespan and ensures consistent performance. 


### What are the primary applications of a 60 degree wedge?

These wedges excel in angle cutting tasks, from creating 45-degree miters to making precise 60-degree cuts. They are essential for joinery, moldings, and angles in woodworking projects. 

Additionally, 60 degree wedges find use in metalworking for angle measurement and marking, providing a reliable reference for precise angle work.
