## Buyer's Guide

Choosing the right Ohuhu marker can significantly enhance your drawing or writing experience. These versatile tools come in various sizes and tip types, making them suitable for different applications. Here are key factors to consider when selecting Ohuhu markers. 


### Tip Styles and Sizes

Marker tip styles range from fine lines for detailed work to broad tips for larger areas. Common tip sizes include 0.3mm, 0.5mm, and 1.0mm, each offering distinct advantages based on your needs. Consider whether you'll be working on small details or covering large areas when choosing your tip size.

### Ink Characteristics

Ohuhu markers feature different ink types, including water-based and solvent-based. Water-based inks are generally more affordable and safer to use, while solvent-based inks offer better permanence and water resistance. Consider the intended use and environment for your project to select the appropriate ink type. 


### Cap and Tip Protection

A proper cap mechanism prevents ink from drying out and protects the tip shape. Look for markers with sturdy caps that seal tightly and prevent leakage. The cap should be easy to grip and open, especially for frequent use.

### How long do Ohuhu markers typically last?

With regular use, an Ohuhu marker can last between 6-12 months before the ink reservoir depletes. This lifespan can vary based on usage frequency and storage conditions. 

To extend the marker's life, store it in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight. Avoid shaking the marker vigorously, as this can mix ink with potential settling particles. 


### What is the best way to clean Ohuhu markers?

For minor ink smudges, gently wipe the tip with a damp cloth or paper towel. For stubborn stains, you can try soaking the cap in water for a few minutes before cleaning. 

Avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive materials, as these can damage the marker's plastic components. Regular maintenance helps prevent ink leaks and maintains smooth flow.

### Can Ohuhu markers be used on different surfaces?

The markers work well on regular paper, cardboard, and thin plastic. For more durable surfaces like ceramic or glass, results may vary. The ink may require more time to dry on these materials. 

Avoid using Ohuhu markers on porous surfaces like untreated wood or concrete, as the ink may bleed through or fade over time. Test the marker on a small, inconspicuous area first for best results. 


### Are Ohuhu markers resistant to fading over time?

In general, Ohuhu markers provide good color retention, with minimal fading after regular use. Proper storage in a cool, dry environment helps maintain color intensity. 

Exposure to direct sunlight or extreme temperature fluctuations can accelerate fading. For longevity, store your markers in a protective case or box to shield them from environmental factors.
