## FAQ


### What are appliance bulbs?

Appliance bulbs, also known as recessed can lights or downlights, are designed specifically for installation in ceiling fixtures with exposed metal housings. These bulbs have a unique shape and base to fit into specialized fixtures while providing consistent, downward-facing light without glare. 

They typically feature high heat resistance, long lifespan, and energy-efficient designs suitable for both residential and commercial applications. 


### What makes appliance bulbs different from standard bulbs?

Appliance bulbs are engineered for specific fixtures with metal reflectors that direct light downward. Their bases and shapes are standardized to fit these fixtures properly, unlike standard bulbs which use common base types like E26 (standard screw base). 

These bulbs also incorporate specialized shields and bases that prevent heat transfer to the fixture housing, ensuring safe and efficient operation in close-proximity installations. 


### How do I choose the right appliance bulb for my fixture?

Check the fixture's specifications for required base type (typically MR16 for halogen, R for LED), wattage limit, and any specific dimming requirements. The fixture's opening size will determine the maximum bulb diameter. 

Assess your lighting needs: task lighting requires brighter, more focused bulbs; ambient lighting works well with softer, diffused bulbs. Consider color temperature preferences and bulb lifespan expectations. 


### What are the most common types of appliance bulbs?

The primary types are halogen (MR16, PAR30), LED (Cove, A21), and HID (35mm). Halogen lamps offer warm, natural light with excellent color rendering but lower efficiency. LED bulbs provide superior energy efficiency, longer lifespan, and enhanced color options. 

HID bulbs deliver intense, white light ideal for commercial spaces but require specialized ballasts and cooling systems for proper operation. 


### How long do appliance bulbs typically last?

Halogen bulbs typically last 1,000-2,000 hours, while LED bulbs can last 25,000-30,000 hours or more. The actual lifespan depends on usage frequency, operating temperature, and proper installation. 

LED bulbs often require a longer warm-up time before reaching full brightness, though they maintain consistent output over their lifespan. Halogen bulbs may require more frequent replacement due to lower efficiency and shorter lifespan. 


### What should I consider for outdoor appliance bulb installations?

Use bulbs rated for outdoor use, which typically feature additional protective coatings and weather-resistant bases. Consider the fixture's IP rating for dust and water protection. 

For landscape or security lighting, choose bulbs with appropriate color temperature and beam angle. Mounting height and fixture orientation affect light distribution, so measure carefully before installation.
