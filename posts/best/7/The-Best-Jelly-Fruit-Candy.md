### What are the main ingredients in jelly fruit candy?

Jelly fruit candy typically consists of a sugar-based candy matrix with fruit-flavored gelatin suspended within. The primary ingredients include refined sugar, corn syrup, water, and partially purified gelatin, which gives the candy its characteristic texture and fruit-like consistency. 

Additional ingredients may include food colorings for vivid fruit colors, natural or artificial flavors for the candy's fruit taste, and minor food additives such as pectin for structural integrity and lemon juice for preservation. Some varieties may also contain small amounts of citric acid to enhance the tartness of the fruit flavor. 


### How are jelly fruit candies made?

The manufacturing process begins with dissolving gelatin in water and combining it with other ingredients like sugar, corn syrup, and flavoring agents. This mixture is heated and filtered to create a clear, fruit-flavored gel solution. The solution is then poured into candy molds and allowed to set at room temperature, where it solidifies into the characteristic jelly candy form. 

The sets of molds are usually arranged on a metal rack and placed in a curing chamber to maintain consistent temperature and humidity. After setting, the candy is removed from the molds and packaged in various configurations, from single pieces to multi-layered sticks, depending on the specific product design.
