### What are the practical considerations for using mesquite charcoal?

Mesquite charcoal needs special handling due to its intense heat and rapid consumption rate. Users typically need to light it in a chimney starter and ash it before adding it to the grill, and it burns through quickly, requiring frequent refilling. 

The dense, heavy charcoal also requires more space in your grill or smoker compared to lighter woods, making it less practical for small appliances. 


### How should mesquite charcoal be stored?

Mesquite charcoal should be stored in a dry location away from heat sources, in its original sealed bag or in a moisture-proof container. Proper storage prevents absorbing unwanted odors and maintains its quality. 

Unlike some other charcoal types, mesquite charcoal doesn't need to be re-dried before use, but regular rotation of stock ensures you're always using fresh product.
