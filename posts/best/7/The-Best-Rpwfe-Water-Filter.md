## Buyer's Guide

Rpwfe water filters are designed to improve the taste, odor, and overall quality of drinking water. Understanding their key features and considerations will help you select an appropriate filter for your needs. 


### Filter Type and Technology

Rpwfe water filters typically use activated carbon, KDF-55, and sometimes silver to remove contaminants. Activated carbon filters are effective against chlorine, odors, and some organic compounds. KDF-55 reduces heavy metals and bacteria. Silver helps inhibit bacterial growth in the filter itself. Consider whether you need a filter that targets specific contaminants like fluoride or lead. 


### Filter Capacity and Flow Rate

Filter capacity is measured in gallons and indicates how much water the filter can treat before needing replacement. Common capacities range from 250 to 3,000 gallons. Flow rate affects water pressure and is measured in gallons per minute (GPM). Higher GPM means faster water flow but may reduce filtration efficiency. Balance your filtration needs with your water usage to choose the right capacity and flow rate. 


### Installation Requirements

Most rpwfe water filters mount under the sink, requiring minimal installation effort. Check the filter's dimensions to ensure they fit your sinkspace. Some models offer space-saving designs for tight installations. Consider accessibility for future maintenance and filter changes when selecting your installation location. 


### Maintenance and Replacement

Regular maintenance ensures optimal filter performance and water quality. Common maintenance tasks include changing the filter cartridge according to the manufacturer's recommended schedule, typically every 6-12 months. Some filters require pre-filters or mineral stones to extend cartridge life. Proper maintenance extends filter lifespan and prevents contamination from cartridge degradation.
