## FAQ


### What is freeze-dried dog food?

Freeze-dried dog food is made by removing nearly all the water from raw ingredients through a process called sublimation, where frozen food is placed under vacuum until the ice turns directly into vapor, leaving behind dehydrated ingredients that maintain their shape and texture. 

Because it removes nearly all moisture, freeze-dried food has a much longer shelf life than fresh or frozen raw food, often lasting several years when properly sealed and stored in a cool, dry place. 


### How is freeze-dried food different from other types of dog food?

Unlike kibble, which is processed at high heat to create dry food, and fresh/frozen meals, which require regular refrigeration, freeze-dried food combines the nutritional benefits of raw food with the convenience of dry food. It's often closer in texture and flavor to home-cooked meals than commercial dog food options. 

Freeze-dried food typically retains more vitamins and enzymes than canned food, as it's processed at lower temperatures than canning. However, it often contains higher levels of sodium due to the rehydration process. 


### Can all dogs eat freeze-dried food?

Most dogs can eat freeze-dried food safely, but it's not suitable for dogs with certain health conditions. Always consult your vet before introducing new foods, especially if your dog has food allergies, digestive issues, or other health concerns. 

Small dogs or those with sensitive digestion may need to limit portion sizes due to the concentration of nutrients and proteins in freeze-dried food, which can be more difficult to digest in excess amounts. 


### How do I feed my dog freeze-dried food?

The simplest way to feed freeze-dried food is as a topper for your dog's regular meal. Just sprinkle it over kibble or canned food and let your dog rehydrate it by adding warm water. Some dogs enjoy eating it as-is, while others need help getting used to the texture and taste. 

For complete meals, the American Kennel Club recommends mixing one-third freeze-dried food with two-thirds water to create a meal that resembles soft, fresh raw food. Adjust the ratio based on your dog's breed size and nutritional needs. 


### How do I store freeze-dried food?

Store freeze-dried food in its original packaging in a cool, dry place. Once opened, transfer the remaining food to an airtight container to maintain the longest possible shelf life. Avoid storing it in the refrigerator, as repeated temperature changes can shorten its shelf life. 

Check the expiration date before feeding, and always discard any food that shows signs of mold, off-odor, or off-taste. Proper storage can extend its shelf life from several months to over a year, depending on the brand and original packaging. 


### How much does freeze-dried food cost?

Freeze-dried dog food generally costs more than conventional kibble per pound due to its specialized production process and premium ingredients. Budgets usually range from $1 to $4 per day per dog, depending on the brand and serving size. 

While it's more expensive than traditional pet food, the convenience and nutritional benefits can justify the cost for many pet owners. Consider it a specialty food for occasional treats or meal variation rather than a daily staple for cost-sensitive budgeting.
