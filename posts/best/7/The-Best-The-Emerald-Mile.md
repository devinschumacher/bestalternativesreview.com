When selecting a crossing, measure the distance carefully, as these events often cover 20-50 miles. Identify reliable launch points on both sides of the water body. Consider the terrain and obstacles along the route, such as rapids or currents that may affect travel time and safety.

Check weather forecasts regularly leading up to the event, as changing conditions can significantly impact navigation and safety. Essential navigation tools include a waterproof map, compass, and GPS device. Consider the reliability of these instruments in various weather conditions. Communication devices such as marine radios or waterproof phones with satellite capabilities are crucial for stay-in-touch protocols with support teams and emergency services.
