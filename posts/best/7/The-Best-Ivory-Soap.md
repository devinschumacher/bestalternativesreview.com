### What makes Ivory Soap so special?

Ivory Soap gained worldwide recognition for its incredible value - a standard-sized bar costs just a penny more than a typical soap but provides twice the cleaning power. This remarkable deal began when William Procter and James Gamble accidentally combined their household soap ingredients, creating a soap that floated due to its lower density. 

The floating property became Ivory Soap's signature feature, making it instantly recognizable and memorable. Despite this unique characteristic, the soap remains a practical and effective cleanser, available in various forms including bars, liquid, and dish soap. 


### Is Ivory Soap suitable for sensitive skin?

Ivory Soap is made with mild, natural ingredients that have stood the test of time, making it a popular choice for sensitive skin. It's hypoallergenic and dermatologist-tested, providing gentle cleansing without irritation. 

However, individuals with severe skin conditions should always consult a dermatologist before trying new products. While Ivory Soap is generally well-tolerated, everyone's skin is unique, and what works for one person might not work for another.

### How should I store Ivory Soap?

To prevent melting and maintain its shape, store Ivory Soap in a cool, dry place away from direct heat sources. This helps preserve its floating capacity and ensures it stays fresh. 

If you prefer liquid Ivory Soap, store it in a closed container at room temperature. The liquid formula remains stable for months when properly sealed and protected from sunlight. 


### Can Ivory Soap be used for laundry?

Ivory Soap has been used for laundry for generations due to its excellent cleaning power and affordability. The standard bar can be cut and dissolved in the wash cycle, while liquid Ivory Soap is perfect for adding directly to the detergent compartment. 

It's particularly effective for tough stains and removing odors. For best results, use it as you would any other laundry detergent, adjusting the amount based on the soil level and load size.
