## FAQ


### What makes Lycra shorts different from regular shorts?

Lycra shorts feature an elastic spandex blend that provides exceptional stretch and compression. The material molds to your body shape while allowing full range of motion, making them ideal for athletic activities. 

The Lycra fibers work to wick away moisture and dry quickly, keeping you comfortable during intense workouts. They also offer moderate4 UV protection, helping to safeguard your skin from harmful sun exposure. 


### How do I measure for Lycra shorts?

Measure your waist at the narrowest point while wearing your running or athletic pants. Add 1-2 inches to your measurement for comfort. Consider the rise desired (mid-rise or high-rise). 

For length, measure from your waist to your desired inseam. Lycra shorts typically offer a range of inseam options: 6 inches, 7 inches, or 8 inches. 


### What's the difference between mid-rise and high-rise Lycra shorts?

Mid-rise styles sit just above the waistband, providing maximum flexibility but less coverage. High-rise options extend higher, offering more security and protection for your lower abdomen. 

Higher rises are suitable for longer runs or when you need extra coverage. Mid-risers work well for shorter distances or gym workouts where you want less bulk. 


### How do I care for Lycra shorts?

Machine wash your Lycra shorts in cold water with a mild detergent. Avoid bleach and fabric softeners, as they can damage the elastic fibers. 

Air dry the shorts or use a low heat setting on your dryer. Always reshape them while still slightly damp to maintain their form. 


### What should I look for when choosing padded vs. non-padded Lycra shorts?

Padded shorts feature foam padding in the seat area for extra comfort during long runs or cycling. They're ideal for high-impact activities but add bulk and weight. 

Non-padded options provide minimal padding or none at all. They're great for shorter runs or casual exercise, offering less bulk and better cooling properties. 


### Can I wear Lycra shorts without a compression top?

While Lycra shorts look stylish on their own, combining them with a compression top can enhance your workout routine. The matching top helps maintain body temperature and provides extra support. 

If you choose to wear shorts alone, ensure the style matches your activity level. Padded options are best for running, while non-padded styles work well for sports like cycling or weightlifting.
