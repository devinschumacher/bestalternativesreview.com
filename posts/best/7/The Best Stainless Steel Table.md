## FAQ


### What materials are used to make stainless steel tables?

Stainless steel tables typically combine 304 or 316 grade stainless steel with aluminum or steel legs. The tabletop can be polished to a mirror finish, brushed for texture, or given a matte finish. 

The core material is 304 or 316 grade stainless steel, known for its durability and corrosion resistance. The tabletop might feature a single sheet of steel or multiple layers for added strength and resistance to scratches and impacts. 


### What types of bases are available for stainless steel tables?

Bases come in three main styles: straight legs, bun feet, and knife-edge legs. Straight legs provide a clean, modern look, bun feet offer extra stability and vintage appeal, and knife-edge legs create a sleek, contemporary silhouette. 

Leg materials include stainless steel, solid aluminum, and sometimes cast bronze. The base profile height usually ranges from 24 to 30 inches, with adjustable legs available for uneven floors. 


### How heavy are stainless steel tables?

A standard stainless steel table weighs between 150 to 250 pounds, with larger or thicker tabletops approaching 300 pounds. The weight adds stability but makes them challenging to move. 

The metal thickness usually ranges from 1/4 to 3/4 inch for residential tables and 3/8 to 1 inch for commercial models. Thickness affects both weight and durability, with heavier tabletops requiring stronger legs. 


### What maintenance requirements do stainless steel tables have?

Wipe the surface daily with a soft cloth and water. For stubborn stains, use mild soap and a non-abrasive cleaner, avoiding harsh chemicals that can damage the finish. 

Regular maintenance prevents scratches and maintains the table's shine. Check legs and hardware quarterly for wear and tighten as needed. For special finishes, follow manufacturer-specific care instructions. 


### Can stainless steel tables be customized?

Tables can be customized with various legs, finishes, and tabletop options. Some manufacturers offer custom sizes and shapes, while others restrict modifications. 

Popular customization options include different leg styles, tabletop finishes, and metal thicknesses. Check with specific manufacturers for available customization options and lead times. 


### What’s the difference between indoor and outdoor stainless steel tables?

Outdoor tables have thicker metal, sealed edges, and weather-resistant hardware to prevent rust and corrosion. They often include additional protective coatings and use heavier materials for base and legs. 

Indoor tables typically use lighter metal and simpler designs. While they can handle light outdoor use, their durability may not match specialized outdoor models after prolonged exposure to elements.
