### How do I adjust the height of the training wheels?

Training wheels usually have adjustment slots on the fork to raise or lower the wheel height. Most have between 2-4 slots spaced 1 inch apart for precise adjustments. 

Start with the training wheels positioned just behind the main wheel's axel, then lower them as your child's confidence grows. The goal is to maintain a 6-inch gap between the training wheel and the ground.

### What maintenance is required for 16 inch bikes with training wheels?

Regularly check the tire pressure and tighten any loose bolts. The training wheels also need regular maintenance - check for wobble, tighten the quick-release skewers, and inspect the rubber tips for wear. 

During the first year of use, check the training wheels every one to two months. After that, a monthly inspection should suffice, especially before extended use.

Yes, you can use regular bike helmets with 16 inch bikes and training wheels. In fact, this is recommended as your child transitions to a bike without training wheels.
