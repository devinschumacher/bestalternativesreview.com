## FAQ


### What types of materials are commonly used in ID badge lanyards?

Most ID badge lanyards are made from either polyester or nylon, as these materials offer durability and resistance to wear. They often come with metal or plastic hooks for attaching the ID badge. 

Some premium options include leather lanyards that provide a more professional appearance, while elastic lanyards offer added comfort and adjustability. 


### How do I measure the proper length for an ID badge lanyard?

The standard length for most ID badge lanyards is 36 inches, which extends to 39 inches with the clip attached. This length works well for most users. 

If you need a custom length, measure from the top of your head to your waist while wearing your uniform or business attire. Round up to the nearest inch for padding. 


### What's the difference between clip-on and breakaway lanyards?

Clip-on lanyards attach directly to the ID badge and are simple to use. They're suitable for most indoor and outdoor environments. 

Breakaway lanyards feature a clip that disconnects in case of emergency, reducing choking hazards. They're particularly recommended for healthcare, hospitality, and event industries. 


### How do I clean and maintain my ID badge lanyard?

The most common maintenance requirement is regular cleaning with mild soap and water. Avoid harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners that could damage the material. 

Store your lanyard in a dry place when not in use. If it becomes tangled, gently unravel it rather than pulling on the clip or tag end. 


### Can I reuse my ID badge lanyard?

Most ID badge lanyards can be reused with the same badge. However, if the clip shows wear or the lanyard becomes frayed, consider replacing it for safety and appearance. 

To reuse, simply clean the clip and lanyard thoroughly. Check that the tag end remains secure and that the elastic (if applicable) maintains its shape. 


### What's the best way to attach my ID badge to the lanyard?

The most secure method is to attach the badge directly to the lanyard using the provided clip. This ensures the badge stays centered and prevents tangling. 

For longer lanyards, consider using a carabiner clip to keep the badge centered and prevent it from swinging. This is especially useful for heavy badges or those with ribbons.
