Proper care extends the life of your wok and ensures consistent performance. For carbon steel woks, hand wash immediately after use and dry thoroughly to prevent rusting. Stainless steel woks require less maintenance but should be washed promptly and dried to prevent water spots.

Non-stick coating provides easier food release and simpler cleaning but may not be suitable for certain cooking methods. Stayware handles remain cooler during cooking and provide better grip. Woks with raised edges contain splatters and make cleaning easier. 


## FAQ


### What is a flat bottom wok used for?

A flat bottom wok is designed for versatile cooking on both gas and electric stovetops. Its shape makes it ideal for stir-frying, sautéing, and making quick meals like omelets and frittatas. 

Unlike traditional round-bottom woks that require a special stand, flat bottom woks sit directly on the stovetop, offering greater stability and ease of use for everyday cooking. 


### How does a flat bottom wok differ from a traditional wok?

Flat bottom woks feature a rectangular or oval shape with straight sides, in contrast to the classic curved, round-bottom design. This modification allows for better stability on standard stovetops. 

While traditional woks excel at stir-frying with their deep, curved shape, flat bottom woks offer versatility across multiple cooking methods, including searing, sautéing, and shallow frying. 


### What are the advantages of a flat bottom wok?

These woks provide excellent heat distribution, thanks to their flat base that makes full contact with the stovetop. This feature enhances cooking efficiency and helps maintain consistent temperature throughout the cooking process. 

With their stable base, flat bottom woks eliminate the need for specialized stands, making them more practical for everyday use in standard kitchen setups. 


### How should I season and maintain a flat bottom wok?

Seasoning a flat bottom wok involves applying a thin layer of oil to the cooking surface and heating it to create a protective non-stick coating. Regular maintenance includes wiping down the wok after each use and occasional seasoning to maintain its performance. 

To prevent oxidation and preserve the wok's seasoning, store it in a dry place and avoid using abrasive cleaners that can damage the surface coating. 


### What materials are suitable for flat bottom woks?

The most common materials for flat bottom woks include carbon steel, stainless steel, and cast iron. Each material has its own advantages: carbon steel is highly reactive and heats evenly, stainless steel offers durability and easy maintenance, while cast iron provides excellent heat retention and develops a natural non-stick surface with seasoning. 

When selecting materials, consider factors such as heat conductivity, durability, and maintenance requirements to choose the right wok for your cooking style and preferences. 


### Are flat bottom woks suitable for induction cooktops?

While some flat bottom woks are compatible with induction cooktops, it's important to verify the wok's compatibility with your specific model. Most induction-compatible woks feature flat, disc-shaped bases with smooth surfaces that make direct contact with the cooktop. 

If you plan to use your wok on an induction cooktop, check the manufacturer's specifications or look for induction-compatible woks specifically designed for this type of cooktop.
