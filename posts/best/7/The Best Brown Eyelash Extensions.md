## FAQ


### What are brown eyelash extensions?

Brown eyelash extensions are individually bonded fake lashes that mimic natural brown lashes. They're typically made from mink, silk, or synthetic materials and range from natural light brown to deep brown shades. 

These extensions enhance eye appearance by adding length, volume, and darkness to the lashes, giving the eyes a more defined and striking look. 


### How long do brown eyelash extensions last?

Brown eyelash extensions usually last 2-3 weeks with proper care. Natural lash shedding occurs progressively during this period, with the extensions coming off at different rates. 

For optimal longevity, avoid rubbing eyes, sleeping on your stomach, and using harsh cosmetics. Regular touch-ups every 2 weeks help maintain the full look. 


### What maintenance is needed for brown eyelash extensions?

Weekly maintenance includes removing any residue from lash glues and checking for any loose extensions. This helps prevent clumping and maintains a natural look. 

Professional maintenance involves a full set replacement every 2-3 weeks to refresh the look and remove any damaged extensions. 


### Are brown eyelash extensions safe for contact lens wearers?

Brown eyelash extensions are generally safe for contact lens wearers when following proper application and removal procedures. Always clean lashes before putting in or removing lenses. 

Avoid using waterproof mascara and sleeping with lashes on, as this can increase the risk of eye irritation and lens dislodgement. 


### What's the cost of brown eyelash extensions?

The cost of brown eyelash extensions varies by location and the type of extensions used. A full set of brown extensions typically ranges from $150 to $300. 

Touch-up prices usually range from $75 to $150 depending on the number of extensions needed to maintain the full look. 


### Are brown eyelash extensions suitable for all skin types?

Brown eyelash extensions are generally suitable for all skin types when applied by a professional. However, individuals with sensitive skin should patch test before the full application. 

Avoid application if you're experiencing skin irritation or allergies, as this can increase the risk of reaction to the lash glues.
