## FAQ


### What is hydrolyzed protein?

Hydrolyzed protein is a process where proteins from animal or plant sources are broken down into smaller peptides through controlled enzymatic action. This process results in proteins that are more easily digested and less likely to cause allergic reactions. 

The process begins by selecting high-quality protein sources, such as chicken or lamb, and subjecting them to carefully controlled conditions that break the larger protein molecules into smaller, more manageable peptides. These peptides are typically less than 2,500 daltons in molecular weight, making them highly digestible.
