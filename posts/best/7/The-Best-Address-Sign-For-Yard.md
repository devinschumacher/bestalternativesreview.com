## Buyer's Guide

Address signs for yards serve as both functional markers and decorative elements, helping ensure your home or business is easily located while adding to the curb appeal. When selecting a yard address sign, consider several key factors to find one that meets your needs while enhancing your property's appearance. 


### Size and Placement

Choose a sign that's large enough to be easily readable from a distance but not so large that it becomes unwieldy. Signs should typically be placed near the front of your property, visible when approaching from the street. If you have multiple addresses on your property, consider an additional sign or a multi-address sign that mounts on a single post. 

![Address-Sign-For-Yard-2](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Address-Sign-For-Yard-2/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Material and Durability

Common materials include plastic, metal, and engraved concrete. Plastic signs are lightweight and affordable but may fade or warp over time. Metal signs, particularly aluminum or iron, offer superior durability and a professional look but are more expensive. Engraved concrete signs provide excellent longevity but require proper placement to prevent cracking. 


### Color and Visibility

Light-colored signs work best in most landscapes as they offer good visibility regardless of the time of day. White, cream, and neutral tones blend well with various home styles. If your home has dark siding or foliage, consider a white or light-colored sign to ensure maximum visibility. 

![Address-Sign-For-Yard-3](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Address-Sign-For-Yard-3/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Neighborhood and Legal Requirements

Your local government may have specific regulations regarding sign height, placement, and material. Check with your city or county officials to ensure your chosen sign meets all legal requirements. Additionally, consider the overall style of your neighborhood - signs should complement the surrounding architecture while maintaining a cohesive appearance.

### Materials and Durability

Address signs for yards are typically made from durable materials like metal or lightweight plastic with weather-resistant coatings. The most common materials are aluminum and polypropylene, which can withstand various weather conditions, including rain, snow, and sun exposure. 

Signs made from heavy-duty aluminum with powder-coated finishes offer the longest-lasting options, while plastic signs with UV protection can still provide years of reliable service in most outdoor environments. 


### Size and Font Options

Standard residential address signs range from 6 to 12 inches in height, with font sizes typically between 24 and 48 points. The most common height is 8 inches, which provides readability while maintaining visibility from passing vehicles. 

Most address signs come with standard font options, usually in traditional block style. Custom font choices are available, but may incur additional costs. For maximum readability, stick to simple, bold fonts rather than decorative styles. 

![Address-Sign-For-Yard-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Address-Sign-For-Yard-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Installation Requirements

Address signs are typically mounted using plastic or metal T-sockets, which can be inserted into concrete, asphalt, or compact soil. The mounting process requires a drill and appropriate drill bits, as well as a level for precise placement. 

Installation depth varies by sign type: metal signs usually need 3-4 inches of depth, while plastic signs require 2-3 inches. Always check the manufacturer's specifications before installation. 


### Lighting Options

Many address signs feature integrated LED lighting, available in various color options including white, blue, and yellow. LED lights typically require 12-24 volts of power and should be connected to a transformer rated for outdoor use. 

Self-lit signs should be installed at least 5 feet off the ground to ensure safety and visibility. The lights are triggered by motion sensors or are activated by a solar panel during daylight hours.

### Customization Options

Most address signs offer customization options for numerals, letters, or symbols. This includes the ability to choose between left- and right-facing digits, or to modify common symbols like house numbers or street names. 

Customization typically requires additional ordering time and may involve a minimum quantity requirement. Special characters or symbols may be available for an additional fee. 


### Cost Considerations

The cost of address signs ranges from $10 to $50 per sign, with installation costs adding another $20-100 depending on the type of sign and mounting requirements. Premium options like custom designs or LED lighting can increase the total cost significantly. 

Price varies by material type, with aluminum signs usually costing more than plastic options. Installation costs depend on the complexity of the job, with concrete mounting requiring more labor than asphalt or soil.
