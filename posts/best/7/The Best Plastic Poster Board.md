## FAQ


### What is a plastic poster board?

A plastic poster board is a rigid, synthetic material panel used for displaying visual content. It offers durability and resistance to moisture and wear, making it ideal for indoor and outdoor presentations. 

The material composition typically includes polypropylene or similar thermoplastic resins, providing strength and flexibility in design applications. 


### What are the advantages of plastic poster boards?

Plastic poster boards resist warping, fading, and chemicals, maintaining their shape and vivid colors over time. They're also lightweight and portable, making them suitable for transport and temporary installations. 

The material's inherent properties allow for easy printing and cutting, enabling versatile design options and quick setup for presentations or displays. 


### How do you clean a plastic poster board?

Dust and wipe surfaces with a microfiber cloth using mild soap and water for print markings or stains. Avoid abrasive cleaners or materials that might damage the surface. 

Air drying is recommended to prevent water spots. For stubborn marks, try applying a non-abrasive glass cleaner followed by a gentle wipe with the cloth. 


### What kind of prints work best on plastic poster boards?

Matt and gloss finishes are both suitable for plastic poster boards. Matte prints offer better resistance to scratches and fingerprints, ideal for high-traffic display areas. 

Glossy prints provide vibrant colors and professional appearance, though they require more careful handling to avoid scuffs and damage. 


### Can you cut plastic poster boards?

Yes, plastic poster boards can be cut using common tools like scissors, Exacto knives, or rotary cutters with appropriate blades. Ensure the cutting surface is stable to prevent warping or damage. 

For straight cuts, mark the board with a pencil and score the line before cutting to reduce wear on tools and achieve cleaner results. 


### How should you store plastic poster boards?

Store boards flat to prevent warping, using rigid supports or spacers between layers if stacking multiple boards. Keep in a dry environment away from direct sunlight and extreme temperature changes. 

For long-term storage, consider rolling boards with a diameter of at least 2 inches and using acid-free paper between each board to prevent scratching.
