### What are the proper ways to use flaxseed oil on hair?

Use cold-pressed flaxseed oil as a leave-in treatment by applying 1-2 tablespoons to damp hair, focusing on the ends for maximum benefit. Rinse out after 30 minutes or leave in for longer nourishment. 

For scalp treatments, massage a small amount of warmed oil into the scalp before bed, then wash out in the morning to promote hair growth and reduce dandruff. 


### Can flaxseed oil be used with natural hair dye?

While flaxseed oil doesn't affect hair dye color, its high nutritional content can cause dye to appear slightly greener or more yellow than expected. Always test a small area before full application. 

To prevent color fading, avoid using flaxseed oil on dyed hair immediately before a color touch-up. Wait at least 24 hours after dyeing to apply flaxseed oil for best results. 


### Is flaxseed oil suitable for all hair types?

Flaxseed oil benefits all hair types, particularly dry, damaged, or chemically-treated hair. Its light consistency makes it suitable for all textures, including fine, thick, curly, and straight hair. 

Avoid using flaxseed oil if you have an allergy to plants in the mustard family or if you experience intense irritation after application. Discontinue use and consult a dermatologist if you notice persistent redness or itching. 


### How should flaxseed oil be stored?

Keep flaxseed oil in the refrigerator in a dark glass bottle to extend its shelf life and prevent oxidation. The oil should maintain its consistency and aroma when refrigerated, but may become thicker at cold temperatures. 

Before use, bring the bottle to room temperature for easier application. Discard any remaining oil if it develops an off odor or changes color, as these may indicate spoilage.
