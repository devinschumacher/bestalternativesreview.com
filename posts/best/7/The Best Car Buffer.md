## FAQ

![Car-Buffer-4](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Car-Buffer-4/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What is a car buffer?

A car buffer is a specialized tool used in automotive detail cleaning and protection processes. It consists of a disc attached to a motorized arm, which applies cleaning products evenly to vehicle surfaces while rotating. 

Buffers come in different types - dual-action and single-action - with dual-action models providing more efficient compound application and polishing capabilities. 


### What surfaces can a car buffer be used on?

Car buffers are designed to work on various automotive surfaces including paintwork, wheels, and wheels hubs. They are particularly effective for removing wax, polish residues, and surface contaminants. 

However, they should not be used on delicate surfaces like plastic trim, rubber seals, or carbon fiber trim pieces which may be damaged by the rotating buffer disc. 

![Car-Buffer-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Car-Buffer-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What types of cleaning products work with car buffers?

Car buffers are compatible with liquid and gel-based cleaning compounds specifically formulated for automotive use. These products typically come in two forms: polish and clay. 

Clay bars are particularly effective for removing brake dust and contamination from vehicle paintwork, while polishes help restore shine and protect the surface. 


### How do you maintain a car buffer?

To maintain a car buffer, regularly clean the buffer head, motor housing, and cables with a damp cloth. This prevents residue buildup and ensures optimal performance. 

Store the buffer in a dry location, away from direct sunlight and moisture. It should be unplugged and allowed to dry completely after use to prevent motor damage. 

![Car-Buffer-6](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Car-Buffer-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What safety precautions should be taken when using a car buffer?

Always use a buffer according to the manufacturer's instructions and with appropriate cleaning products. Wear protective eyewear and gloves when operating the tool. 

Ensure the vehicle is securely parked and the engine is turned off before use. Keep the buffer head in contact with the surface at all times to prevent accidental rotation of the vehicle. 


### Are car buffers suitable for all vehicle paint finishes?

Car buffers can be used on all standard vehicle paint finishes, including clearcoat, metallic, and pearlescent finishes. They are effective for both new and older vehicles. 

However, they should not be used on vehicles with damage-prone finishes like clear coat that has been scratched or damaged, as this can result in further damage to the paint surface.
