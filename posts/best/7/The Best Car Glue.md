## FAQ


### What is car glue used for?

Car glue, also known as automotive bonding agent, is designed to permanently join metal and plastic components in vehicles. It is commonly used for body repairs, panel bonding, and interior assembly. 

The primary function is to create a strong, lasting bond between parts, whether replacing a door panel or securing body panels during collision repairs. 


### What are the different types of car glue?

Common types include two-part epoxies, which require mixing for application, and one-part polyurethane glues that cure through chemical reactions with air. Each type has specific applications and curing times. 

Epoxies are favored for their superior strength and durability, while polyurethane glues offer good performance with single-step application. Selection depends on the specific repair requirements and available time for curing. 


### How strong is car glue?

High-quality car glues achieve bond strengths that rival or exceed the original material's properties. Properly applied, the bond can be as strong as 3,000 pounds per square inch (PSI) or more. 

The strength comes from its chemical bonding mechanism, which interlocks molecular structures of the adhesive with the substrate material, creating a seamless connection. 


### What surfaces does car glue adhere to?

Car glues effectively bond to most automotive materials, including steel, aluminum, and certain types of plastic. They require clean, dry surfaces free from oils, paints, or other contaminants for optimal adhesion. 

Some formulas include surface treatments like primers or cleaners specifically designed to enhance adhesion to painted or galvanized surfaces. 


### How long does car glue take to cure?

Most car glues cure within 24 to 48 hours, during which time the bond develops its full strength. Curing times can be extended for thicker applications or in cooler temperatures. 

During this period, it's important to follow the manufacturer's guidelines for recommended pressure, temperature, and environmental conditions to ensure proper curing. 


### What's the best way to mix two-part car glue?

For two-part glues, it's crucial to measure and mix equal parts A and B thoroughly using a clean, dedicated mixing tool. Mix in a single direction to avoid air entrainment, which can weaken the bond. 

Once mixed, the adhesive should be applied quickly before it begins to gel. The mixed pot life typically ranges from 10 to 20 minutes, depending on temperature and formula.
