## Buyer's Guide

Bone inlay mirrors combine ornate design with practical functionality, making them a notable addition to any room. Understanding the key aspects of these mirrors helps in selecting one that enhances both aesthetics and durability. 


### Material Quality and Craftsmanship

Bone inlay mirrors feature intricate designs created by embedding bone pieces into the mirror's surface. The quality of materials and craftsmanship significantly impacts longevity and visual appeal. Opt for mirrors with securely set bone inlays and a robust backing to prevent movement and damage. 


### Size and Proportion

Size selection depends on available wall space and room dimensions. Consider the mirror's placement in relation to furniture and window positions. Smaller mirrors work well in entryways and bathrooms, while large mirrors can transform living rooms and hallways. The frame's width should complement the room's proportions. 


### Frame Style and Finishes

The frame style should match your existing decor. Traditional wood frames pair well with classic interiors, while contemporary metal frames suit modern spaces. Available finishes range from natural wood tones to gold and bronze, allowing customization to your taste and room color scheme. 


### Mirrored Characteristics

Standard mirror glass works for casual settings, while silver-coated glass provides better light reflection and is more resistant to scratching. For enhanced visual depth, choose frame styles that slightly extend beyond the mirror's edges, creating a shadowbox effect. 


## FAQ


### What is a bone inlay mirror?

A bone inlay mirror combines traditional craftsmanship with modern design through intricate patterns and shapes created using buffalo or elk bone. The mirrors feature hand-carved patterns set into a wood frame, with the bones meticulously inlaid to create detailed designs. 

The process requires skilled artisans who carefully select, shape, and fit each bone piece into place. The final product blends reflective glass with natural bone elements to create a unique decorative statement piece. 


### What sizes are available?

Bone inlay mirrors typically come in standard frame sizes, ranging from 20" to 60" in width. Common aspect ratios include 4:5, 5:6, and 6:7, allowing them to fit various wall spaces while maintaining a balanced look. 

Custom sizes can often be ordered, with lead times that vary based on the complexity of the design and the custom dimensions requested. 


### What materials are used?

The mirrors incorporate buffalo or elk bone inlays set into a wood frame. The wood typically features a finished surface that complements the natural bone color, with options for various stain and paint finishes. 

Some manufacturers offer aluminum frame alternatives for added durability and reduced weight, though traditional wooden frames provide a more natural aesthetic. 


### How should they be maintained?

Regular dusting with a microfiber cloth is recommended to keep the mirrors clean. Avoid harsh chemicals or abrasive materials that could damage the bone inlays or wood finish. 

For deeper cleaning, use a mild soap solution and a soft brush to reach difficult areas. Always ensure the mirror is completely dry before returning it to its display location.
