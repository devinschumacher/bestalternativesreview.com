### What is a Wiffle Ball?

A Wiffle Ball is a plastic baseball that features nine perforations along its surface. This unique design allows it to travel further when hit compared to a traditional baseball, making it suitable for play in smaller spaces or with reduced effort. 

The ball was invented in 1958 by David Frick, who observed his father's difficulty hitting golf balls through tree branches. The distinctive design combines aerodynamic properties with recreational functionality, making it popular for family game nights and backyard entertainment. 


### What makes a Wiffle Ball different from a MLB baseball?

The primary difference lies in the material and construction. Wiffle Balls are made from durable plastic, while MLB baseballs are crafted from dense rubber cores with hand-sewn leather covers. The perforations in Wiffle Balls significantly alter their flight characteristics, allowing for greater distances and altered trajectories. 

This construction also affects regulation play. MLB games use precise measurements and standardized playing fields, while Wiffle Ball games often adapt to smaller spaces with informal rules. The balls have different weights and bounce characteristics that influence gameplay mechanics.
