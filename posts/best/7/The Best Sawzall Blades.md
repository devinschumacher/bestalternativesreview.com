### What materials can a Sawzall blade cut?

Sawzall blades are designed to cut through a wide range of materials, including metal, wood, plastic, and composites. The specific material compatibility depends on the blade type and tooth configuration. 

For example, metal-cutting blades have thicker teeth and a hardened edge, while wood-cutting blades feature thinner teeth spaced farther apart. Always check the blade's specifications to ensure compatibility with your intended material. 


### What's the difference between metal-cutting and wood-cutting blades?

 Metal-cutting Sawzall blades have thicker teeth and a hardened edge, making them suitable for cutting through metal, pipe, and other tough materials. These blades can handle repeated cutting and heavy-duty applications. 

Wood-cutting blades feature thinner teeth spaced farther apart, allowing for faster cutting through wood and composites. While they're less durable than metal-cutting blades, they excel at precise cuts and cleaner finishes in woodworking applications. 


### How should I maintain a Sawzall blade?

Regular maintenance extends the life of your Sawzall blade and ensures optimal performance. After each use, clean the blade and inspect it for damage or wear. Store the blade in a dry environment to prevent rust and corrosion. 

When sharpening or replacing blades, use appropriate tools and follow manufacturer guidelines. Improper handling can damage the blade or affect the saw's performance. 


### What's the proper way to install a Sawzall blade?

Before installing a Sawzall blade, ensure the blade teeth face the direction of the cut. Align the blade with the collet and tighten it securely, making sure not to over-tighten to prevent damage to the blade or saw.

### How do I choose the right Sawzall blade for my project?
