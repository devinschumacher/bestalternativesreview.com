## FAQ


### What are Cricut markers, and how do they differ from regular markers?

Cricut markers are specially designed for precise line marking and surface adhesion. Unlike standard markers, they feature a combination of specialized ink and an adhesive formula that allows them to bond permanently to various materials. 

The markers typically come in three color variants - white, black, and red - and have a unique chisel tip for angled line drawing. This specialized functionality makes them particularly suitable for automotive, technical, and artistic applications where precise marking is required. 


### What surfaces can Cricut markers adhere to?

The adhesive formula allows Cricut markers to stick to a variety of materials, including metal, glass, plastic, and most types of paper and cardstock. They work exceptionally well on smooth surfaces that don't absorb ink easily. 

However, they may not adhere effectively to porous materials like untreated wood or certain textured surfaces. For best results, test the marker on a small, inconspicuous area before full-scale application. 


### How long does the adhesive take to dry?

The ink typically needs 24-48 hours to fully cure and reach its maximum adhesion strength. During this time, avoid touching or distorting the marked surface to prevent smudging or peeling. 

Once fully cured, the marks become permanent and resistant to water, abrasion, and environmental factors. The cured ink does not fade significantly over time and maintains its adherent properties indefinitely. 


### Are Cricut markers suitable for indoor or outdoor use?

While primarily designed for indoor use, Cricut markers can be used in outdoor applications effectively. The permanent adhesive bonds the ink to surfaces, making it resistant to weather elements like sunlight, rain, and temperature changes. 

For outdoor projects, choose a location sheltered from direct sunlight and extremes of temperature. As with any permanent marking solution, test the markers on your specific surface before extensive use in outdoor environments. 


### How should Cricut markers be stored?

Store Cricut markers in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat sources to maintain their performance. The markers are best kept in their original packaging to protect their specialized ink formula. 

Avoid freezing the markers, as extreme temperature changes can affect their flow and adhesion properties. The optimal storage temperature range is between 60-77°F (15-25°C). 


### Can Cricut markers be removed from surfaces?

Due to their permanent adhesive properties, Cricut markers are not easily removable once the ink has fully cured. Attempting to peel or scrape the marks off the surface often results in damage to the underlying material. 

For surfaces that require periodic cleaning or modification, consider using a heat gun or adhesive removal products specifically designed for permanent markers. These methods may help loosen the bond but should be used cautiously to avoid damaging the surface material.
