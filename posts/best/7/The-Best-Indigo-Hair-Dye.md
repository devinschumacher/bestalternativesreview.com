## Buyer's Guide

Indigo hair dye offers a rich blue-black color that has become increasingly popular in recent years. This natural dye choice requires specific considerations due to its unique properties and application process. 


### Color Options and Depth

Indigo dye comes in varying shades, from deep black to blue-black. The intensity depends on several factors including the dye concentration, application technique, and your natural hair color. Lighter hair may result in blue undertones, while darker hair tends to produce deeper black shades. 

![Indigo-Hair-Dye-2](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Indigo-Hair-Dye-2/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Application Process

The dyeing process typically involves multiple applications over several days. After the initial dyeing, you must wait 2-3 days before applying additional layers. This multiple-step process helps achieve a more saturated color and ensures better coverage. 


### Preparation and Maintenance

Proper preparation is crucial for successful indigo dyeing. Before dyeing, bleach your hair to level 4 or 5 for even results. After dyeing, protect your color with regular treatments using hair care products specifically designed for indigo maintenance, as traditional hair products can cause fading.
