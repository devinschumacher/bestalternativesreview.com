### Frame Design and Size

At 16 inches, the frame is designed to fit smaller riders comfortably. The geometry is typically more upright than adult bikes, which helps with balance and control. Look for bikes with a proper step-through design to make mounting and dismounting easier.

### Braking System

Most 16-inch bikes feature reliable coaster brakes - you brake by simply stopping pedaling. While effective for casual riding, consider models with front brake options if you plan to ride on busier streets. Avoid bikes with poor brake positioning or worn components. 


### Tire Options

Tires range from smooth-rolling road variants to grippy off-road options. For general use, look for tires with a balance of comfort and handling. Check the tire width and pressure compatibility with your chosen bike frame to ensure optimal performance.

### Additional Considerations

Features like hand brakes, bell/canister lights, and adjustable seats enhance safety and convenience. Consider the chain type (derailleur vs. coaster brake) and sprocket compatibility with your intended use. Regular maintenance points include the pedal-to-chain connection, rear wheel quick-release, and brake cable adjustments.
