### What makes Oshkosh overalls different from other work pants?

Oshkosh overalls are engineered with reinforced stitching, heavy-duty zippers, and industrial-grade rivets at stress points for durability that exceeds standard work pants. The reinforced seat and knee pads are specifically designed to withstand the rigors of manual labor. 

Unlike basic work pants, Oshkosh overalls often feature multiple pockets with secure closures, including hidden pockets and interior pouches, providing ample storage for tools and documents. The adjustable waist and double-layered fabric in key wear areas enhance both comfort and protection during extended use. 


### What sizes are available?

Oshkosh overalls come in a wide range of sizes, from small to 5X, accommodating both standard and extended sizes. The brand offers separate sizing charts for men's and women's styles, with specific measurements for the chest, waist, and inseam to ensure proper fit. 

Extended sizes are available for both men's and women's styles, typically going up to 6X in men's and 4X in women's. Custom sizing options may be available through authorized dealers for customers requiring sizes larger than those listed on the official sizing chart. 


### How do I measure for the correct size?

To find your ideal size, measure your chest at its fullest point, waist at the natural waistline, and inseam from the crotch to the desired length. Compare these measurements to Oshkosh's sizing chart, allowing 1-2 inches of extra room for comfort in the waist and 1 inch for the chest and inseam. 

If you fall between sizes, consider ordering the larger size for added comfort, especially in the waist. Always check the specific sizing requirements for your chosen style, as measurements may vary slightly between different Oshkosh overalls. 


### What maintenance tips should I follow?

Care for your Oshkosh overalls by washing them inside out in cold water to prevent fading and preserve detailing. Avoid bleach or harsh chemicals, which can damage the waterproof coating and reduce the overall lifespan of the garment. 

Avoid machine drying, as it can shrink or damage the fabric. Instead, air dry your overalls completely before wearing. Store them in a cool, dry place when not in use, and consider using hangers with padded shoulders to prevent creasing and maintain their shape. 


### Are the overalls really waterproof?

Oshkosh overalls are constructed with a waterproof rating of 10,000mm, meaning they will resist water penetration under moderate pressure. While not completely impervious to water, they provide excellent protection in light to moderate rain or wet conditions. 

The waterproofing is applied to the outer shell of the overalls, protecting the wearer's dryness during outdoor work or light rain exposure. However, they should not be relied upon for complete water protection in deep water or extreme weather conditions. 


### Can I wear these overalls with other clothing?

While designed primarily as a standalone work uniform, Oshkosh overalls can be worn with undergarments and light outer layers. The company offers matching undershirts and work boots designed to work well with their overalls, but they can also be worn with other compatible workwear. 

However, avoid wearing loose-fitting pants or heavy outer layers with the overalls, as this can interfere with movement and comfort during work activities. The overall's design works best when paired with similarly structured clothing that allows for natural movement.
