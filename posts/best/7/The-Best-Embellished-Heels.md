### What makes a heel 'embellished'?

Embellished heels combine traditional heel design with ornamental details such as crystals, beads, sequins, or intricate metalwork. These decorative elements can wrap around the heel, cover its surface, or dangle from its sides, creating visual interest while maintaining the heel's functional design. 

The term "embellished" can also refer to patterns carved into the heel material, painted designs, or contrasting color bands that create a decorative effect while potentially providing additional grip or support. 


### How do embellishments affect heel stability?

Most embellishments are designed to be lightweight and secure, with prongs or bases that anchoring them to the heel. This secure attachment minimizes wobbling and ensures the decorative elements remain in place while walking. 

However, very heavy or wide embellishments may slightly alter the heel's center of gravity, so it's important to choose styles that feel stable when trying them on. The heel should sit firmly and correctly aligned with the ball of your foot.

### What maintenance requirements do embellished heels have?

Regular maintenance includes cleaning the heel surface with a soft cloth and mild soap to remove dirt and oils. Crystal or sequin embellishments should be protected with a clear coat spray to prevent damage from water or chemicals. 

When storing embellished heels, place them in a protective bag or shoes box to prevent scratches from movement. Avoid storing them in direct sunlight or high humidity, which can dull or damage the finishing on both the heel and embellishments. 


### Are embellished heels suitable for all occasions?

Heavily embellished heels are best reserved for special occasions or formal events where the decorative elements can be showcased. Lightly embellished options are suitable for everyday wear, providing both a touch of sparkle and decorative detail. 

Consider the nature of your activities when choosing embellished heels. Heavier decorations may be impractical for standing or active pursuits, while simple embellishments can pair well with casual outfits for a touch of elegance.

### How do I care for nail polish on embellished heels?

Nail polish applied around the embellishments should be used sparingly and carefully to avoid covering the decorative elements. When applying or touching up, keep the brush away from areas with small prongs or delicate details to prevent damage. 

For best results, apply nail polish before wearing the heels and allow it to dry completely before putting them on. Touch-ups can be applied with a small brush and protective base coat to minimize chip risk while preserving the heel's appearance. 


### What should I consider when sizing embellished heels?

Since embellishments can affect the overall shape and size of a heel, it's crucial to try on shoes with your specific heel height preference. The embellishments should sit flush against the foot without creating pressure points. 

Pay special attention to the width fitting, as embellished shoes may be slightly wider than their unadorned counterparts. Ensure comfortable movement in the toe box and proper alignment with the ball of your foot.
