### What is a bug zapper light bulb?

A bug zapper light bulb is a specialized bulb that emits ultraviolet (UV) light to attract and kill flying insects. These bulbs contain a wire mesh grid that electrifies when insects make contact, causing them to fall into a collection tray below where they are killed by electric shock. 

Standard bug zapper bulbs produce a bright, blue-white UV light that attracts insects, with some models offering adjustable illumination to conserve energy or direct light where needed. 


### How effective are bug zapper light bulbs?

Bug zapper light bulbs can be effective in reducing flying insect populations, particularly in outdoor applications where they're placed near entry points or problem areas. However, their effectiveness can vary based on several factors, including the local insect population, environmental conditions, and proper installation. 

Regular maintenance, such as emptying the collection tray and checking for bulb integrity, can significantly enhance their effectiveness. It's important to note that while they can reduce certain insect species, they may attract others that are not intended targets.

### What types of insects are typically attracted to bug zapper light bulbs?

Bug zapper light bulbs primarily attract flying insects that are sensitive to UV light, including mosquitoes, moths, fruit flies, and other nighttime flyers. These insects are attracted to the UV wavelength because they mistake it for moonlight, which they use for navigation. 

Some species of beneficial insects, such as ladybugs and butterflies, may also be attracted to the UV light, though they typically avoid direct contact with the electrified mesh.
