## Buyer's Guide

 Foam core boards combine durability with lightweight construction, making them popular for presentations, prototypes, and crafts. Understanding their key aspects will help you choose the right board for your needs. 


### Size and Dimension Specifications

Common sizes range from standard sheets measuring 24" x 36" to customized dimensions. Consider your storage space and intended use when selecting dimensions. 

Standard thicknesses include 1/8", 3/16", and 1/4". Thicker boards offer more rigidity but are heavier.

### Material Quality and Construction

Core materials vary, with polyethylene providing the best combination of strength and lightness. The facing material affects surface quality - melamine offers durability, while kraft paper provides texture for writing and printing. 

Look for boards with tight core-to-facing adhesion and consistent thickness throughout. 


### Surface Treatment Options

Common surface finishes include matte, glossy, and art paper. Matte provides the best grip for writing and printing. Glossy offers excellent print quality but can scratch easily. 

Art paper surfaces are texture-rich for mixed media projects. Consider the intended use when selecting surface finish.

### Environmental and Storage Considerations

Polyethylene cores are readily recyclable. Most manufacturers offer clear or white surface options. 

Store boards flat to prevent warping. Keep away from moisture and extreme temperatures.

### What is a foam core board?

A foam core board is a lightweight yet sturdy material used for mounting artwork, photos, and graphics. It consists of two thin sheets of rigid foam sandwiched between two pieces of paper or cardboard. 

This versatile material offers excellent protection for your artwork while allowing for easy handling and display. The foam core provides stability, preventing warping and curling that can occur with traditional paper mounting. 


### What are the primary uses of foam core boards?

These boards are widely used in art and design industries for mounting and displaying artwork, photos, and prototypes. They're ideal for presentations, portfolios, and exhibition displays. 

Additionally, foam core boards are commonly used by students, artists, and hobbyists for protecting and displaying their work during transit or display. 

![Foam-Core-Boards-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Foam-Core-Boards-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### How do foam core boards compare to other mounting materials?

Compared to traditional paper mounting, foam core offers superior stability and protection against warping and bending. It's more durable and can support heavier materials than standard mounting boards. 

However, foam core is less rigid than acrylic or plexiglass and may not be suitable for outdoor displays or UV protection. 


### What are the advantages of double-sided foam core boards?

Double-sided boards provide a convenient way to view either the mounted artwork or a design sketch on the reverse side. This feature is particularly useful for presentations or concept development. 

These boards also allow for more efficient use of space by displaying two different pieces of artwork or sketches on a single board. 

![Foam-Core-Boards-6](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Foam-Core-Boards-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### How should foam core boards be cut and shaped?

Standard foam core boards can be cut with standard craft or utility knives, while double-sided boards require specialized scoring tools to prevent delamination. Always cut on a sturdy surface to prevent bending. 

For complex shapes or curves, consider using a laser cutter or contact the manufacturer for professional cutting services to maintain the board's integrity. 


### What maintenance is required for foam core boards?

Regular maintenance for foam core boards primarily involves cleaning the surface with a soft, lint-free cloth and mild soap if needed. Avoid using harsh chemicals that can damage the foam core material. 

Store boards flat or slightly angled to prevent moisture buildup that could lead to warping. Handle with care to avoid exposing the foam core to extreme heat or cold, which can affect its structure.
