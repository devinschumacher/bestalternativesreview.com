### Materials and Construction

Trays can be made from various materials, including stainless steel, aluminum, and plastic. Stainless steel offers the best durability and corrosion resistance but can be heavy. Chafing fuel systems typically use either liquid or solid fuel sources. Liquid fuel is more convenient as it lights easily and produces consistent heat. Solid fuel tablets offer portability and long-lasting heat but require careful storage to prevent accidental ignition. 

Consider the fuel system's compatibility with your heat source. Some chafing dishes work with standard candle holders, while others use specialized heating units. Evaluate the cost of the fuel system and its environmental impact, especially if you plan to use the trays regularly.

### What is a chafing tray?

A chafing tray is a shallow, heated food vessel used to keep food warm and prevent drying out during buffet-style serving. It consists of a heat-resistant top tray that holds food and a bottom tray containing heating elements. 

The top tray usually features slotted sides to prevent spillage and a lip around the edges to catch any overflow. Modern trays often include temperature control settings and automatic shut-off features for safety. 


### How does a chafing tray work?

Chafing trays operate on a simple principle of convection heating. The bottom tray contains electric heating elements that warm the water or liquid medium inside. The heat rises through the slotted sides of the top tray, keeping the food warm and moist.
