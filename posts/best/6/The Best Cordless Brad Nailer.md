## FAQ


### What is a cordless brad nailer, and how is it different from a regular brad nailer?

A cordless brad nailer is a power tool specifically designed to drive brad nails into materials like trim, molding, and paneling. Unlike traditional pneumatic or corded electric brad nailers, it operates on battery power, offering the advantage of portability and no power cord restrictions. This makes it ideal for jobs in tight spaces or remote locations. 

The key differences include battery operation, lighter weight, and reduced cord constraints. However, they may have slightly reduced nail capacity and slower cycle times compared to their corded counterparts. 


### What types of batteries work with cordless brad nailers?

The most common battery types for cordless brad nailers are NiCd (Nickel-Cadmium), NiMH (Nickel-Metal Hydride), and Li-ion (Lithium-Ion). Li-ion batteries are the most advanced, offering longer run times, lighter weight, and no memory effect. 

Always use the battery type specifically recommended for your tool to ensure optimal performance and longevity. Additionally, consider purchasing a spare battery to maintain consistent operation during long projects. 


### What is the typical nail capacity of cordless brad nailers?

Most cordless brad nailers can handle between 1,000 to 1,500 nails per charge, depending on the model and battery type. This range typically covers standard 18-gauge brad nails up to 2 1/2 inches in length. 

For extended projects or larger nail requirements, check the specific capacity of your chosen model and consider tools with higher nail counts or additional quick-change magazines. 


### How long do cordless brad nailer batteries last?

The runtime of cordless brad nailer batteries varies based on the battery type and capacity, typically ranging from 20 to 40 minutes per charge. Factors affecting runtime include the specific model, battery type, and number of remaining nails in the magazine. 

To maximize battery life, follow recommended charging practices, avoid deep-discharge cycles, and store batteries in a cool, dry place when not in use. 


### What safety features should I look for in a cordless brad nailer?

Key safety features include magazine ejection, forward grip lockout to prevent accidental triggering, and forward grip battery retention to prevent battery removal during use. Some models also include over-insert protection and adjustable speed controls. 

Always wear appropriate personal protective equipment, including safety glasses, ear protection, and proper work attire. Follow all manufacturer instructions and never compromise on safety for convenience.

Entry-level tools can provide reliable performance for basic trim work, while professional-grade models may offer extended runtime and durability for demanding projects.
