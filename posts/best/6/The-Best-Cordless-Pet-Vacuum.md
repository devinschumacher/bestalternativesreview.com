## FAQ


### What are the key features to consider when selecting a cordless pet vacuum?

A cordless pet vacuum should combine powerful suction with efficient battery performance. Look for a minimum suction power of 125 watts and runtime of 20 minutes per charge for thorough cleaning. The vacuum should feature easy-emptying dirt cans and simple controls for quick switching between attachment tools. 

Key accessories include motorized brushes for pet hair removal, crevice tools for tight spaces, and dusting brushes for delicate surfaces. The vacuum should also have adjustable power settings and be lightweight for comfortable use. 


### How do I maintain the suction power of my cordless pet vacuum?

Regular maintenance is crucial for maintaining suction power. This includes checking and cleaning the dust canister or bag weekly, and replacing the filter every three months or as needed. The motor brush roll should be cleaned after every use to remove hair and debris. 

Air vents and intake points should be checked monthly for blockages. If you notice reduced suction power, address these maintenance points to restore performance. 


### How long does a cordless pet vacuum's battery last?

The average cordless pet vacuum battery lasts 20-30 minutes on a single charge, depending on the model and usage intensity. Factors affecting runtime include suction power settings, attachments used, and battery age. 

To maximize runtime, store the vacuum at room temperature and discharge the battery to 50% between full charges. Avoid deep-discharge cycles below 20% to extend battery life. 


### What should I consider when cleaning the motor brush roll?

The motor brush roll should be cleaned after every use to prevent hair and debris buildup. Unplug the vacuum, remove the brush roll, and gently brush out tangling hair using a stiff brush or comb. This maintenance step should take no more than 2-3 minutes per cleaning. 

If cleaning the brush roll becomes difficult, the vacuum may need professional servicing. Signs that servicing is needed include reduced suction power, unusual noise, or difficult brush roll removal. 


### What maintenance schedule should I follow for my cordless pet vacuum?

Regular cleaning and maintenance are essential for optimal performance. The maintenance schedule should include weekly checks of the dust canister and filter, monthly replacement of the motor brush roll, and quarterly cleaning of air vents and intake points. 

Inspect the vacuum daily for visible debris in the canister and filter, and replace them monthly or as needed based on usage. Store the vacuum with a partial charge (50%) and discharge it completely every two weeks to maintain battery health. 


### How can I prevent pet hair from getting stuck in the dust canister?

To reduce hair buildup in the dust canister, use the motor brush roll regularly and empty the canister after every cleaning session. A full canister can significantly reduce suction power and should be emptied when 80% full. 

Consider purchasing vacuum bags specifically designed for pet hair removal. These bags capture more debris and reduce the need for frequent emptying, extending the vacuum's runtime and maintaining consistent suction power.
