## FAQ


### What is a Cuban link anklet?

A Cuban link anklet is a type of jewelry featuring interlocking metal loops that form a distinctive, heavy chain-like design. Each link usually consists of three bars joined together, creating a robust and elegant piece. 

The anklet's thick profile makes it ideal for layering with other jewelry or wearing alone as a statement piece. The Cuban link style has become particularly popular in gold and sterling silver, though it can also be found in platinum and various colored metals. 


### What makes Cuban link anklets unique?

The hallmark of a Cuban link anklet is its distinctive three-bar design, where each metal loop interlocks with the next. This construction gives the piece exceptional durability and a distinctive visual texture. 

These anklets are typically much heavier and more substantial than traditional chains, with each link adding to the overall weight and presence of the piece. 


### How do you wear a Cuban link anklet?

Cuban link anklets are versatile and can be worn alone or layered with other anklets. Their substantial weight makes them particularly effective when worn solo, though they can also complement smaller pieces. 

These anklets sit quite low on the ankle, so they should be sized properly to avoid rubbing or discomfort. A professional fitting is recommended if you're purchasing one online. 


### What metals are Cuban link anklets made from?

The most common metals for Cuban link anklets are gold (yellow, white, or rose), sterling silver, and platinum. Some pieces also feature colored metals like rose gold or copper. 

Less common metals include bronze, brass, and various nickel alloys. The choice of metal often depends on the intended design and the piece's intended use (e. g. , everyday wear versus formal occasions). 


### How should you care for a Cuban link anklet?

Cleaning: Use a soft cloth to wipe the anklet daily, and soak it in warm water with mild dish soap once a week. Avoid harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners that could damage the metal. 

Maintenance: Inspect the chain regularly for any signs of wear, such as bent links or loose connections. Store the anklet in a jewelry box or pouch to protect it from scratches and tangles. 


### What is the history of Cuban link anklets?

The Cuban link style gained popularity in the 1920s and 1930s, inspired by Cuban jewelry designs. It became particularly associated with Art Deco jewelry and has since influenced numerous jewelry styles. 

While its origins are unclear, the style's enduring appeal stems from its blend of strength, texture, and elegant design elements. Modern Cuban link anklets continue to draw inspiration from this rich history while incorporating contemporary materials and designs.
