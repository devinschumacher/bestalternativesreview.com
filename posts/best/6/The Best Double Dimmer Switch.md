### What is a double dimmer switch and how does it work?

A double dimmer switch allows you to control two lighting fixtures with one switch, adjusting the brightness independently or simultaneously. The switch contains two dimmer mechanisms (usually 1000mA each), enabling you to select how much each fixture illuminates. 

The working mechanism typically involves selecting a mode: "Master, " where both fixtures dim together; "Slave, " where the second fixture follows the brightness of the first; or "Independent, " where each fixture's brightness is controlled separately. 


### What are the mounting requirements for a double dimmer switch?

Standard double dimmer switches measure approximately 4.5 inches in length and 2.25 inches in depth, similar to single switches. They require a 2-gang electrical box and a 3-wire cable for power input. Most switches come with mounting screws and blank covers for unused gang spaces. 

Ensure that your electrical box can support the weight of the switch and provide proper insulation. Check local electrical codes for specific requirements regarding wire sizing and box depth. 

![Double-Dimmer-Switch-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Double-Dimmer-Switch-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Can a double dimmer switch be installed in a bathroom?

Yes, double dimmer switches can be installed in bathrooms, but you must use moisture-resistant devices specifically designed for humid environments. Look for switches marked IP44 or higher, which protect against water splashes from all directions. 

Follow additional safety guidelines for bathroom installations, including using ground-fault circuit interrupters (GFCIs) and keeping wires away from water sources to prevent electrical hazards. 


### What types of bulbs are compatible with double dimmer switches?

Most double dimmer switches are compatible with LED, CFL, halogen, and incandescent bulbs rated for dimming. Always check the switch's specifications for maximum voltage and wattage limits, typically between 120V and 600W per dimmer. 

Some switches also support dimmable ballasts for fluorescent fixtures. For residential use, common compatibility ranges are 120-240V and 15-600W per dimmer, with some models handling up to 1000W per channel.

### How do you program the dimmer settings?

Double dimmer switches often have simple programming methods. To set initial brightness levels, turn on the power and use the control buttons to adjust each fixture's brightness independently. Some models store these settings automatically. 

If the switch requires programming, consult the manual for specific steps. Common methods include holding specific button combinations or using a short pulse after power-on to enter setup mode. 


### What maintenance is needed for double dimmer switches?

Regular maintenance for double dimmer switches includes checking for loose mounting screws and ensuring all electrical connections remain secure. The slide mechanisms should move smoothly without excessive resistance. 

Every few years, test the switch's functionality and replace bulbs that show dimming issues. For switches with exposed dimming controls, consider applying a small amount of silicone spray to protect the mechanisms from dust and moisture.
