## FAQ


### What is a fence privacy screen?

A fence privacy screen is a structure, often made from plants or manufactured materials, installed alongside a fence to enhance privacy and create visual interest. It's designed to reduce visibility through the fence while maintaining its support and functionality. 

These screens can vary in height, material, and design to suit different preferences and needs. Common options include trellises with climbing plants, lattice panels, and freestanding planters. 


### How much space does a fence privacy screen require?

The space requirement depends on the screen's design and the fence's existing configuration. Most freestanding screens range from 4 to 6 feet tall and 2 to 3 feet wide, while lattice panels typically add about 3 to 6 inches to the fence's height, depending on the spacing. 

Ensure there's adequate clearance for plant growth, especially with vertical gardens or climbing vines, and consider the screen's impact on your overall landscape design. 


### What are the main materials used in fence privacy screens?

The most common materials include wood, metal, plastic, and natural elements like bamboo and live plants. Each material offers different benefits in terms of durability, cost, and maintenance requirements. 

Wood and metal structures often offer the longest lifespan with proper care, while plastic options provide a more affordable and low-maintenance alternative. Live plants, particularly deciduous vine species, offer natural screening with seasonal changes. 


### How do I maintain a fence privacy screen?

Regular maintenance requirements vary by material and design. For wood and metal screens, this usually involves checking for damage, cleaning, and applying protective coatings as needed. Plant-based screens require watering, pruning, and occasional pest control. 

Check all connections and support structures annually to ensure the screen remains secure and functional. For metal screens, consider applying protective treatments to prevent rust and corrosion. 


### What is the typical range of prices for fence privacy screens?

Prices vary significantly based on materials, size, and installation complexity. Basic metal or plastic panels can cost $100 to $300 per piece, while larger wooden structures or custom designs may range from $200 to $1000 or more. 

Consider purchasing screens in sets or groups to potentially qualify for bulk discounts. Installation costs can add an additional $200 to $600 per section, depending on the length and complexity of the installation. 


### How long do fence privacy screens typically last?

With proper care and installation, metal and plastic panels can last 10 to 20 years. Wooden structures, when protected with sealants and regular maintenance, typically last 15 to 25 years. Plant-based screens maintain visual appeal for 5 to 10 years before needing replacement or significant maintenance. 

Expect some degradation over time, particularly in outdoor environments with exposure to weather elements. Regular inspection and timely maintenance can significantly extend the screen's lifespan.
