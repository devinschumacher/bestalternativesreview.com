### What safety certifications should I look for in a mermaid doll?

Mermaid dolls intended for children should carry safety certifications from recognized organizations such as ASTM F963 in the USA or EN 71 in Europe. These certifications ensure the doll has been tested for toxic substances, choking hazards, and other safety concerns. 

Check if the doll is labeled as "phthalate-free" and "non-toxic. " Additionally, verify that small parts or accessories are securely attached to prevent choking risks for young children. 


### What age range is suitable for mermaid dolls?

Most mermaid dolls are designed for children aged 3 to 9 years. This age range is appropriate because the dolls typically contain small parts and require some level of motor skill for play. 

Check the manufacturer's recommended age range on the product packaging to ensure it matches your child's developmental stage. 


### How should I care for my mermaid doll?

Mermaid dolls usually need basic care similar to other soft toys. Machine wash the doll in a mesh bag using mild detergent on a gentle cycle. Avoid using bleach or fabric softeners, which can damage the fabric or elastics. 

During storage, fold or hang the doll to maintain its shape. Keep it away from direct sunlight and extreme temperatures to prevent fading or damage to the materials. 


### What are the key features to consider when selecting a mermaid doll?

Key features include the doll's poseability (ability to move limbs), material quality (softness and durability), and included accessories (swimsuit, corset, jewelry). Consider what aspects are most important for your child's play style. 

Note the doll's size and weight, as these factors can affect ease of play and storage requirements.
