## FAQ


### What are promise rings, and why do people get matching ones?

Promise rings are a type of jewelry that often represents a commitment between friends or romantic partners before traditional engagement or wedding rings. They typically feature a small band with a simple design, allowing friends to show their support for each other's significant others. 

Matching promise rings demonstrate unity and solidarity between friends, as both individuals wear similar bands as a visible symbol of their support. They're particularly popular among couples who want to display their relationship without the commitment of engagement rings. 


### What metals are commonly used in promise rings?

Promise rings are often made from affordable metals like gold-filled, rose gold, or sterling silver, making them suitable for everyday wear without the expense of pure gold or platinum. The choice of metal usually depends on personal preference and the intended use of the ring. 

Many promise rings incorporate rhodium plating, which gives them a bright white finish and helps resist tarnishing. This plating, combined with the metal base, provides durability while keeping costs manageable. 


### What gemstone options are available for promise rings?

Promise rings commonly feature basic, symbolic gemstones like cubic zirconia, simulated diamonds, or colorful birthstones. These options offer a personal touch without the investment of high-quality diamonds or colored gemstones. 

Some rings include simple enamel designs or small, decorative birthstone settings. The gemstone selection often reflects personal preferences or meaningful connections between the friends or partners. 


### How should you care for your matching promise rings?

To maintain their appearance, clean your matching promise rings regularly with mild soap and warm water. Professional cleaning every 6-12 months can also help maintain their brilliance and extend their lifespan. 

Store your rings individually in soft pouches or boxes to prevent scratches and tangling. Avoid exposure to harsh chemicals and extreme temperatures, which can damage the metal or stones. 


### What should you consider when buying matching promise rings?

When purchasing matching promise rings, consider the occasion and the daily wear requirements. The metal's durability and your budget will influence your choice between simulated diamonds and colored gemstones versus simple metal bands. 

Ensure the sizing allows for comfortable wear, and consider a warranty or insurance option for your investment. The message of friendship conveyed through matching rings should be as meaningful as possible. 


### How can you properly size a matching promise ring?

To ensure your matching promise ring fits correctly, measure your ring size during the middle of the day when your fingers are at room temperature. Use a flexible ring sizer or a piece of string to get an accurate measurement. 

Allow about 1/2 size for daily wear, as hands can swell slightly throughout the day. If you're buying for someone else, ask about their ring size directly or consider a universal sizing option if available.
