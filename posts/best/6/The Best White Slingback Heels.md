### How should white slingback heels be cared for?

Regular cleaning and proper storage are essential for maintaining the appearance of white slingback heels. Dust the shoes after each wearing and use a soft cloth to remove any residue. For deeper cleaning, you can use a specialized shoe cleaner or mild soap with water. 

Store white heels in a dust bag or on a shoe rack away from direct sunlight and moisture. Use heel pads to prevent damage during storage and wear. 


### What materials are commonly used in white slingback heels?

The upper portion of white slingback heels is usually made from leather, suede, or satin. Leather offers durability and a classic look, while suede provides a softer feel. Satin adds an elegant touch and is ideal for formal occasions. 

The heels themselves are typically made from various materials, including rubber, wood, or a combination of materials. Ensure the material matches the intended use and your personal style preference.

### How do I care for white leather slingback heels?

White leather requires special attention to maintain its color and texture. After cleaning, apply a leather protector to prevent stains and fading. Use a soft, non-abrasive cloth to polish the leather regularly and maintain its sheen. 

Avoid letting water sit on the leather as it can cause discoloration. If the leather gets wet, gently blot with a dry cloth and allow it to air dry completely before wearing again. 


### What should I consider when sizing white slingback heels?

White slingback heels often run narrow due to their design. If you have wide feet, consider ordering a half size larger than your usual shoe size. The arch of your foot should sit naturally in the heel rather than sliding forward. 

Test the heel height while wearing the shoes to ensure they are comfortable for your preferred level of walking. Heavier heel heights may require more time to adjust to, while lower heights provide better stability.
