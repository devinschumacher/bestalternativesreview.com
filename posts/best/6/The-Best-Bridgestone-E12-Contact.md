## FAQ


### What is the Bridgestone E12 Contact?

The Bridgestone E12 Contact is a high-performance summer tire designed for modern sports cars and premium sedans. It features an optimized tread pattern and premium materials to deliver superior handling, reduced noise, and enhanced longevity compared to its predecessor. 

Developed with advanced silica technology in the tread compound, the E12 Contact provides exceptional wet and dry traction while maintaining a balance of comfort and performance. 


### What makes the E12 Contact different from Bridgestone's previous summer tire, the Potenza S007A?

The E12 Contact introduces several technological advancements over the S007A, including a revised shoulder pattern for improved wear resistance and a quieter ride. The new tire also incorporates Bridgestone's latest manufacturing techniques to enhance consistency and performance across the footprint. 

The primary difference is in the treadwear compound, which offers more balanced wear characteristics while maintaining the high-performance capabilities of the S007A. 


### How does the E12 Contact's silica tread compound improve performance?

The silica-filled tread compound significantly enhances the tire's wet grip properties by providing better water evacuation and more stable contact with the roads. This results in shorter braking distances on wet surfaces compared to non-silica compounds. 

During dry conditions, the compound maintains its shape at higher temperatures, preventing the typical softening that can occur with traditional rubber compounds under prolonged stress. 


### What are the key benefits of the E12 Contact's revised shoulder pattern?

The updated shoulder design reduces irregular wear by promoting more uniform contact pressure distribution across the tire's footprint. This results in extended tread life and improved ride quality. 

At high speeds, the redesigned shoulders also contribute to better stability and reduced noise levels compared to the previous shoulder pattern. 


### How much longer is the E12 Contact expected to last compared to its predecessor?

Independent testing has shown the E12 Contact can offer 5-10% longer tread life than the Potenza S007A, depending on driving conditions and vehicle weight. This is due to the improved wear resistance in key areas of the tread pattern. 

Drivers should still expect approximately 75,000 miles of normal use from a set of four E12 Contact tires, with performance remaining consistent throughout much of the tire's lifespan. 


### What is the recommended PSI for the E12 Contact?

The recommended inflation pressure for the Bridgestone E12 Contact varies based on the vehicle and load configuration. It's important to check your vehicle's owner manual or tire information placard for the correct maximum pressure. 

Most cars will perform best with the manufacturer's recommended pressure, which typically falls between 30-35 PSI. Underinflated tires can lead to increased wear and reduced performance, while overinflation can affect ride quality and stability.
