## FAQ


### What types of clips are available for string lights?

Common clip styles include S-hooks, G-hooks, and straight clips. S-hooks wrap around the mounting point, G-hooks create a right-angle shape for stable support, and straight clips are simple and space-saving. 

Material options range from plastic to metal. Plastic clips are lightweight and ideal for indoor use, while metal clips offer durability for outdoor installations. The choice depends on the specific lighting and environmental conditions. 


### How often should clips be checked for wear?

Inspect clips annually for signs of wear before each lighting season. This ensures safety and prevents failures during use. 

Storage considerations: Properly store clips in a dry place to prevent corrosion, especially if you live in a humid climate. 


### What's the maximum weight capacity for each clip type?

S-hooks typically support up to 10 pounds, making them suitable for small strings or decorative installations. 

G-hooks and straight clips support approximately 15 pounds each, ideal for medium to heavy-duty applications like kitchen pendant lights or outdoor fixtures. 


### How long do clips last under normal use?

With proper maintenance, plastic clips typically last 5-7 years. Metal clips, when protected from corrosion, can last 10-15 years. 

Signs of wear to watch for include rusting (in metal clips), warping, or cracks in the plastic. Replace any clips showing these signs immediately. 


### Can I reuse clips from old string lights?

Most clips can be reused, provided they show no signs of wear or damage. 

Use the opportunity to upgrade to clips with better material quality or improved designs for enhanced safety and longevity. 


### What's the best way to install clips for string lights?

Space clips every 12-18 inches along the string, adjusting based on the weight of the bulbs and mounting conditions. 

For outdoor installations, use weather-resistant clips and fasteners to prevent corrosion and maintain proper light display.
