### What is a snake plant?

Snake plants, scientifically known as Sansevieria, are tough, low-maintenance indoor plants with upright sword-shaped leaves. They come in various colors and patterns, including green, yellow, and variegated varieties. 

Their common name comes from the plant's tall, upright leaves that can reach up to 4 feet tall, resembling a serpent's curved body. 


### How do snake plants benefit indoor spaces?

Snake plants improve indoor air quality by removing toxins like benzene, formaldehyde, and trichloroethylene from the air, making them valuable for home and office environments. 

They're also natural humidifiers, adding moisture to the air through their process of transpiration, which helps maintain comfortable indoor humidity levels. 

![Snake-Plants-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Snake-Plants-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### How much light do snake plants need?

Most snake plants thrive in indirect light, such as bright, filtered sunlight through a sheer curtain. They can tolerate low light conditions but will grow taller and leggier if they don't receive enough light. 

Some varieties, like the "Moonshine" or "Hahnii" cultivars, can tolerate very low light, making them great choices for dark corners of your home. 


### How often should I water a snake plant?

Water your snake plant sparingly, allowing the soil to dry completely between waterings. Overwatering is the most common way to kill a snake plant, so it's important to ensure proper drainage in your pot. 

During the growing season (spring and summer), water once every 2-3 weeks. In fall and winter, reduce watering to once every 4-6 weeks, as growth slows during these months. 

![Snake-Plants-6](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Snake-Plants-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Can I propagate snake plants?

Snake plants can be easily propagated through division or leaf cuttings. To propagate by division, gently separate the plant's root ball and replant the daughter plants in fresh potting soil. 

For leaf cuttings, use a healthy, mature leaf and cut it into 3-4 inch sections. Allow the cut end to callous over for a day or two, then bury it in fresh potting soil. Keep the soil moist until new growth appears. 


### What are the common pests that affect snake plants?

The most common pests to affect snake plants include spider mites, mealybugs, and scale insects. These pests can be controlled by regularly inspecting the plant and treating with insecticidal soap or neem oil. 

Spider mites can be particularly problematic in dry indoor environments. Increase humidity around the plant and wipe the leaves with a damp cloth to remove these pests.
