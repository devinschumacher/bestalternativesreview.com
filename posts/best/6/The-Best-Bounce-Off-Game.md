The playing surface affects the game's difficulty and durability. Wall-mounted games provide consistent bounce properties, while free-standing units may vary based on the material and construction. Indoor use typically requires smoother surfaces, while outdoor versions need more robust construction to withstand weather conditions.

Game controls vary from simple button presses to detailed aiming mechanisms. Consider whether you prefer a quick-fire button for basic shots or an analog control system for precise aiming. Ergonomic factors like grip design and button placement can affect gameplay comfort, especially during extended sessions.
