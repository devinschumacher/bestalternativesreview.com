### What is the difference between wood and metal drill bits?

Wood drill bits, typically made from HSS, have a pilots point and flutes designed for cutting wood fibers. They are designed for smooth, clean cuts in wood and particle board. 

Metal drill bits, also made from HSS or carbide, have a center point and straight flutes. They are designed for precision drilling in metal, with variations for stainless steel, aluminum, and bronze. 


### What is the correct drill speed for Milwaukee drill bits?

The recommended drill speed (RPM) for Milwaukee drill bits varies by material: - Wood: 1,000-1,500 RPM

 - General metal: 600-1,000 RPM

 - Thin metal: 1,000-1,200 RPM

Always start with a lower speed and gradually increase as the bit begins to cut. Adjust speed based on bit diameter and material hardness.
