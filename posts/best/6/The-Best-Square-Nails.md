### What are square nails, and how are they different from standard nails?

Square nails feature a squared-off tip instead of the traditional point. This design is particularly popular in Scandinavian and minimalist nail styles, offering a clean, modern look while maintaining the strength and protection of a properly filed nail. 

The squared-off tip is slightly shorter than a standard nail and gives the appearance of a slightly more natural look without the potential for accidental injury associated with pointed nails. The reduced tip length also makes them a practical choice for activities that require bare hands, such as cooking or writing. 


### How are square nails measured, and what size should I choose?

Square nails are measured in length, similar to other nail types. Common sizes include 1/4 inch, 3/8 inch, and 1/2 inch, with the most popular option being 3/8 inch. The size you choose should balance your comfort with the look you desire. 

For daily wear, 3/8 inch nails are recommended as they provide a good balance of length and strength. Smaller sizes may break more easily, while larger sizes might be uncomfortable, especially in tight-fitting footwear.

### What is the installation process for square nails?

Installation requires a basic understanding of proper nail care. First, file your natural nails to a smooth surface, removing any sharp edges. Apply nail adhesive to both the natural nail and the square nail, then carefully position the square nail on the natural nail. Press firmly to ensure proper adhesion. 

For best results, use a strong quality nail adhesive and follow proper drying times to ensure a secure bond. Regularly check and maintain the nails to prevent them from coming loose over time. 


### How do I maintain the longevity of square nails?

Proper maintenance is key to keeping square nails in excellent condition. Regularly file and shape the natural nail to prevent uneven growth and maintain the squared-off appearance. Keep the nails dry, especially in humid environments, to prevent damage and peeling. 

Use a quality nail polish remover without harsh chemicals, as many solvents can damage the adhesive. For extra protection, consider using a base coat formulated for extended wear to help prevent chipping and damage.

### Are square nails suitable for all skin types?

The majority of skin types can accommodate square nails, but individuals with sensitive skin may experience irritation more readily. If you have sensitive skin or recent nail damage, it's recommended to consult a professional before attempting home installation. 

Those with thick or brittle natural nails may need to monitor the square nails closely for any signs of discomfort or irritation. If issues arise, consider consulting a nail technician for personalized advice. 


### Can I use nail polish with square nails?

Most nail polish formulas are compatible with square nails, but results may vary. Clear nail polish adheres best, while nail polishes with textured finishes might not adhere as predictably due to the squared-off surface. 

For long-lasting wear, apply a base coat specifically designed for extended wear. This helps prevent chipping and ensures better adhesion to the squared nails. Always follow product instructions for optimal results.
