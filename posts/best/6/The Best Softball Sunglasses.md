## Buyer's Guide

Softball sunglasses combine protective performance with stylish design, essential for athletes who spend hours in the sun. Understanding key aspects of these eyewear essentials can help you select a pair that enhances your game while keeping your eyes safe and comfortable. 


### UV Protection

Sunglasses must block 99-100% of both UVA and UVB rays to prevent eye damage. Look for lenses with a UV400 label or lenses that are explicitly marked as providing complete UV protection. 

![Softball-Sunglasses-2](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Softball-Sunglasses-2/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Lens Options

Standard yellow lenses increase visibility of the ball and surroundings, while amber lenses reduce glare. Photochromic lenses automatically adjust to changing light conditions, and polarized lenses minimize reflective glare from surfaces like dirt or uniform fabrics. 


### Frame Durability

Sports sunglasses need robust frames that resist bending and snapping under impact. Choose frames made from durable materials like stainless steel or high-quality plastic blends, with secure nose grips and earpieces to prevent sliding. 

![Softball-Sunglasses-3](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Softball-Sunglasses-3/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Fit and Comfort

Proper fit prevents movement and discomfort during play. Adjust temples to maintain a secure, non-sliding fit, and ensure the frame comfortably stays above the eyebrows without pressing. Temporal lenses should sit flat on the cheeks to block side glare.

### What materials are used to make softball sunglasses?

Softball sunglasses typically feature a durable polycarbonate lens material that provides excellent impact resistance and UV protection. The frames often combine lightweight metals and flexible plastic components for durability during play.

Select sunglasses that sit just above your eyebrows with the temple arms measuring between 140-160mm. Proper frame size ensures the lenses remain centered and prevent light peripheral vision problems during gameplay.

### What features should I look for in softball sunglasses?

Key features include UV400 protection, scratch-resistant coatings, and anti-fog treatments. Look for temples with grip detailing and nose pads made from hypoallergenic materials to prevent slippage during intense play.

### How do I care for my softball sunglasses?

Clean the lenses with a soft microfiber cloth or dedicated lens cleaner, avoiding harsh chemicals that can damage the protective coatings. Store the sunglasses in a padded case when not in use to prevent scratches and maintain their shape. 

Regularly check the temple arms and nose pads for wear, replacing them if they show signs of damage or loss of elasticity to ensure a secure fit.

### Can I wear softball sunglasses with a helmet?

Many softball sunglasses are designed specifically for helmet compatibility, featuring extended brow bars and slightly larger frame sizes. Always check the product specifications to confirm proper fit and ventilation when using a helmet. 

Avoid using sunglasses with adjustable straps, as they can interfere with helmet attachments and create safety hazards during gameplay.
