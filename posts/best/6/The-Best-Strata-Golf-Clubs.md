## Buyer's Guide

When selecting strata golf clubs, several key factors influence performance and compatibility with your swing. Understanding these aspects helps in choosing clubs that optimize your game. 


### Shaft Material and Construction

Shafts come in two primary materials: steel and graphite. Steel shafts offer more feedback and are preferred by players with faster swing speeds. Graphite is lighter and more flexible, ideal for slower swing speeds and older players. Consider your swing characteristics to select the appropriate shaft material. 

![Strata-Golf-Clubs-2](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Strata-Golf-Clubs-2/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Club Flex and Stiffness

Club flex refers to the shaft's stiffness. Most players use regular flex, but options include senior (faster swing speeds) and ladies (slower swing speeds). The club's stiffness should match your swing speed to maintain consistent ball speeds and trajectories. 


### Closure and Open Clubface Settings

Strata clubs often feature adjustable settings for clubface closure/openness. These adjustments allow fine-tuning of ball flight, with closed settings promoting fade or draw shots and open settings aiding in draw or hook prevention. Determine your preferred ball flight and adjust accordingly. 

![Strata-Golf-Clubs-3](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Strata-Golf-Clubs-3/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Weight and Balancing

Club weight affects both feel and launch conditions. Heavier clubs promote higher launch angles, beneficial for players with slower swing speeds. Lighter clubs may be preferred for players aiming to generate more speed through the ball. Consider your swing characteristics and comfort level when choosing weight.
