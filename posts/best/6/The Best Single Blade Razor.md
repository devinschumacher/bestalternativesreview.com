## FAQ


### What is a single blade razor?

A single blade razor consists of one cutting blade housed in a protective frame. This basic design offers simplicity and straightforward performance, ideal for daily shaving routines. 

Modern single blade razors incorporate sophisticated technologies like adjustable blade angles and micro-fine blade edges to enhance close, comfortable shaving. 


### How does a single blade razor differ from a multi-blade razor?

Single blade razors use a single cutting edge while multi-blade razors employ multiple blades moving independently. The single blade design is generally simpler and requires fewer maintenance steps. 

Multi-blade razors create more surface area where bacteria can grow, necessitating regular cleaning and descaling to prevent irritation and infection. 


### What maintenance is required for a single blade razor?

The blade should be replaced every 4-6 weeks or when it shows dullness. The razor head and base need weekly cleaning to remove trapped hair and residue. 

Storage should include air drying to prevent rust, and occasional lubrication of the blade can extend its lifespan and improve performance. 


### Can single blade razors handle all types of facial hair?

While effective for most types of facial hair, single blade razors may struggle with thicker, coarser hair or significant stubble growth. These razors work best for maintaining a smooth shave on regular grooming days. 

For tougher cases of facial hair, consider additional razors with different blade angles or lengths to target specific areas effectively. 


### Are single blade razors suitable for sensitive skin?

Due to their straightforward design, single blade razors reduce the risk of irritation and razor burn compared to multi-blade alternatives. The fewer cutting blades mean less risk of cutting the skin. 

However, individuals with especially sensitive skin may still experience irritation, particularly if the blade becomes dull or the razor isn't adequately maintained. 


### How does water resistance affect single blade razor performance?

Water-resistant single blade razors maintain optimal performance during wet-shaving, preventing blade corrosion and maintaining shaving comfort. Non-water-resistant models may dull more quickly and become less effective when used in damp conditions. 

Before wet shaving, ensure the razor is completely dry to prevent water damage.
