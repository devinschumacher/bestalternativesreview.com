## FAQ

### What exactly is a 360 security camera?

A 360 security camera offers panoramic coverage with a single device, typically featuring a 360-degree horizontally rotating lens and a wide-angle vertical view. These cameras capture a full-sphere of activity around their mounting location, making them ideal for monitoring expansive areas like warehouses, parking lots, or large rooms. 

These cameras use advanced image processing to deliver clear, artifact-free footage across their wide field of view. Most models also support features like motion detection, night vision, and cloud storage, providing comprehensive security coverage with minimal equipment. 


### Do 360 security cameras have drawbacks?

While 360 security cameras offer extensive coverage, their large field of view can sometimes result in lower resolution per individual scene. This means objects far from the camera's center may appear smaller and less detailed in the recorded footage. 

Additionally, their wide-angle perspective can make it challenging to focus on specific events or faces, particularly in crowded or busy environments where multiple activities are occurring simultaneously. 

![360-Security-Cameras-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/360-Security-Cameras-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### How well do 360 cameras perform in low light conditions?

Most 360 security cameras feature built-in infrared (IR) LEDs that activate in low light to maintain visible and clear footage. These cameras typically provide good performance down to 0-5 feet from the camera, though image quality may degrade in complete darkness. 

Some advanced models include additional features like True Night Vision or 2x Digital Zoom, which can help maintain image clarity at greater distances in low light conditions. 


### What is the typical installation process for a 360 security camera?

Installation usually requires choosing a suitable mounting location that provides unobstructed 360-degree viewing around the camera. The camera mounts to a wall or ceiling, and the included mounting hardware should secure it firmly in place. 

Power usually connects via standard wall outlets or PoE (Power over Ethernet) cables, while data transmission occurs via Wi-Fi or Ethernet connection to your home network. 

![360-Security-Cameras-6](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/360-Security-Cameras-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Can 360 cameras detect motion across their entire field of view?

Most 360 security cameras have motion detection sensors that cover the complete 360-degree horizontal view but may have less sensitive or non-functioning perimeters vertically. This means motion detection is typically most effective at the camera's horizontal mid-point. 

Some advanced models feature adjustable detection zones or multiple detection zones, allowing you to set specific areas of interest within the camera's field of view. 


### How much storage do 360 cameras typically require?

Standard 360 security cameras consume between 20-60GB per month of constant recording, depending on resolution and motion-triggered recording settings. This storage requirement assumes typical home usage with occasional video capture. 

For continuous recording or high-resolution footage, you may need 100-200GB per month or more. Cloud storage services or local microSD card storage are common options for managing this data volume.
