### How do I choose the right frame for my 36x24 artwork?

Selecting the right frame involves considering the artwork's style, color scheme, and intended display location. Consider whether you prefer a traditional, contemporary, or rustic frame design that complements your space. The frame's color should harmonize with the artwork and room decor, while the frame's style (e. g. , matte black, gold leaf) should match the artwork's tone.

### What considerations are important when mounting a 36x24 frame?

Proper mounting requires attention to both the frame's stability and the artwork's preservation. Use acid-free mounting materials to prevent damage to the artwork over time. Ensure that the frame's mounting points are evenly spaced and securely attached to prevent warping or sagging. 

Consider the artwork's weight and the frame's ability to support it. For heavier pieces, use additional support brackets or backing. Properly position the frame's weight center to prevent tipping, especially if mounting in an area with high foot traffic or vibration.

When mounting a 36x24 frame, allow at least 1-2 inches of space between the frame and surrounding objects to prevent damage and maintain visual balance. Consider the frame's profile size, as deeper frames may require more space to prevent obstruction.

### What maintenance is required for a 36x24 frame?

If the frame includes protective features like UV coating or glass, monitor these elements for signs of degradation. Replace protective layers as needed to maintain the artwork's condition.
