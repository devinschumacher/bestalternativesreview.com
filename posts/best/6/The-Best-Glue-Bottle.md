### Dispensing Mechanism

The most common types are squeeze bottles and trigger sprayers. Squeeze bottles provide precise control and are ideal for most applications. Trigger sprayers offer more consistent flow and are particularly useful for fine-detail work or spot repairs. 


### Material Compatibility

Ensure the bottle material is compatible with the adhesive you'll be using, as certain chemicals can damage plastic or break down glass. PVC, polyethylene, and certain types of glass are generally safe options for most adhesives.

### What types of glue bottles are available?

Glue bottles come in various types: waterproof, acid-free, and clear. Waterproof options prevent dilution when exposed to moisture, while acid-free glues protect archival materials. Clear bottles allow easy visualization of the remaining adhesive. 

The most common sizes range from 20ml to 400ml, with capacity decisions based on intended use and frequency of application. Smaller bottles are ideal for craft projects, while larger ones suit professional gluing needs. 


### What's the best glue bottle material?

Plastic bottles offer excellent durability and chemical resistance at an affordable cost. They're lightweight, shatterproof, and available in various styles, including squeeze and trigger-top mechanisms. 

Glass bottles provide superior clarity for viewing adhesive levels but are heavier and more fragile. They're best for storing acid-free and waterproof glues where breakage risk is minimal.

### How should glue bottles be stored?

Store glue bottles in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and extreme temperatures. This prevents adhesive degradation and ensures optimal performance. Always keep bottles upright to maintain proper flow. 

Avoid freezing glue bottles as this can damage the bottle and degrade the adhesive. If using outside temperature extremes, allow bottles to reach room temperature before use. 


### What's the shelf life of glue bottles?

Unopened glue bottles typically maintain optimal performance for 12-18 months when stored properly. Once opened, adhesive stability varies by type: white craft glue lasts 6-12 months, while archival-quality adhesives may last up to three years. 

Check for changes in color, consistency, or smell before using opened bottles. Discontinue use if the adhesive appears altered or doesn't flow freely.

### How can I prevent glue bottle leaks?

Always squeeze the bottle during application to maintain an even flow and prevent air from entering. This helps maintain consistent pressure and reduces the risk of leakage. 

Avoid over-tightening bottle caps, which can create excess pressure and cause leaks. Clean bottle openings thoroughly after use and store bottles upright to maintain proper seal integrity. 


### What's the difference between squeeze and trigger-top bottles?

Squeeze bottles offer precise control over adhesive application through a simple, manual mechanism. They're ideal for detail work and small-scale projects requiring accurate dispensing. 

Trigger-top bottles provide consistent flow through a spring-loaded release mechanism, making them easier to control for larger application areas. These are particularly useful for spreading adhesives or applying thin lines.
