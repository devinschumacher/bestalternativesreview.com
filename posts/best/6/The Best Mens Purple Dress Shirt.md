## FAQ


### What makes a dress shirt dressy enough for formal occasions?

A dress shirt typically distinguishes itself through several specific features: a crisp, clean finish, constructed from premium materials like cotton or silk, and cut to fit precisely. The fabric quality often determines a shirt's longevity and its ability to maintain a formal appearance. Common formal-cut elements include a pointed collar, button-down collar, or spread collar, tailored cuffs, and a straight hemline. 

Additional dress elements might include double or single button cuffs, mother-of-pearl buttons, and reinforced stitching at key points of stress. The shirt's color and pattern choice also significantly impact its formality level - solid colors and simple patterns like stripes or dots are generally more suited to formal wear than busy prints or loud patterns. 


### How does purple compare to other colors for formal wear?

Purple dress shirts offer a striking alternative to traditional colors, combining the power of blue with the warmth of red. While black, navy, and white remain the most versatile colors for formal occasions, purple can add a dynamic accent when worn correctly. It pairs particularly well with gold, silver, and grey accessories. 

Consider the tone of the purple - deeper shades tend to play better in darker lighting and formal settings, while lighter pastel tones may feel more appropriate for daytime events or more casual formal occasions. 


### What care requirements should I expect for a dress shirt?

Care requirements vary by material and construction, but most dress shirts require regular dry cleaning for best results, especially if made from silk or wool. Even when machine washable, most dress shirts benefit from gentle cycles and cool water to maintain their shape and color. Reshape the shirt while it's damp after washing to maintain its crease-free appearance. 

Iron the shirt on a cotton setting, using a pressing cloth if the shirt has fine or embroidered details. The collar and cuffs typically require extra attention, as these areas show wear more quickly. A professional cleaning twice annually is recommended for regular wear, while less frequent use might allow between four and six months between professional cleanings. 


### How do I choose the right fit for a dress shirt?

A well-fitted dress shirt should feel neither tight nor loose. Start by checking the measurements of your current shirts to establish your size. Modern men's dress shirts typically range from 34 to 44 inches in chest size, with common widths between 14 and 17 inches. 

The shirt should button easily to the collar point (usually the second to last button), with enough room for two fingers between the buttoned cuff and your wrist. The shirttail should hang about 1-2 inches below the waistband of your trousers when buttoned. 


### What's the difference between silk and cotton dress shirts?

Silk dress shirts are prized for their exceptional softness, natural luster, and ability to maintain a crease-free appearance. However, they require more maintenance, typically needing professional cleaning and frequent pressing to maintain their appearance. Most silk shirts should be dry cleaned to prevent color fading and damage from water. 

Cotton dress shirts offer superior durability and a wider range of pricing options. High-quality cotton shirts can maintain their shape and color through repeated wear and wash. Look for dress-quality cotton options that combine breathable cotton with features like mother-of-pearl buttons and reinforced stitching. 


### Can I wear a purple dress shirt with any suit?

Purple dress shirts pair particularly well with tan, brown, and dark grey suits. The warm undertones of these colors complement the royal undertones of purple, making them ideal for both fall and winter weddings or formal events. Keep in mind that very dark or very light purple may not work with all suit colors - experiment with different combinations to find what looks best. 

If you're matching a purple shirt to other garments like a tie or pocket square, consider the intensity of the color. A lighter purple shirt might work with a darker purple accessory, while a deeper shade would look striking with a lighter or patterned accessory.
