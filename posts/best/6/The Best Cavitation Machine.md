## FAQ


### What is a cavitation machine?

A cavitation machine is a specialized device that generates intense pressure waves to break down fat cells and improve skin quality. It works by creating cavitation bubbles in the treatment area, which then collapse, producing micro-jolts that target fat cells while stimulating collagen production. 

The process occurs in multiple stages: first, the machine creates sub-atmospheric conditions around the targeted fat cells. Then, powerful pulses of sound waves cause these bubbles to expand and contract rapidly. When these bubbles collapse, they release energy that ruptures fat cells while leaving surrounding tissues unharmed. 


### How effective are cavitation machines?

Cavitation machines have shown significant effectiveness in reducing fat, particularly when used in conjunction with lifestyle changes and other weight loss methods. While results may vary, many users report visible improvements in body contours after several sessions. 

Research indicates that cavitation can reduce fat thickness by an average of 1.5-3 inches, depending on the machine's specifications and the number of treatment sessions. The process also stimulates collagen production, leading to firmer, more youthful-looking skin over time. 


### What areas of the body can be treated?

Cavitation machines can target multiple areas of the body, including the abdomen, hips, thighs, arms, and buttocks. The treatment is particularly effective in areas with stubborn fat deposits that have not responded to diet and exercise. 

While the machines can safely treat most body areas, they may not be suitable for certain regions, such as the face, neck, or areas with poor blood circulation. Always consult with a certified professional to determine the best treatment plan for your specific body type and goals. 


### Are there any risks or side effects?

When used properly by a trained professional, cavitation machines are generally safe with minimal side effects. Most users experience temporary redness, mild swelling, or minor discomfort during or after treatment, but these effects typically resolve within an hour. 

However, some individuals may experience more significant side effects, including prolonged soreness, bruising, or skin irritation. These occurrences are rare, but they emphasize the importance of using reputable machines and trained professionals to minimize risks. 


### How many treatments are needed?

To achieve optimal results, most individuals require 6-12 sessions, spaced approximately two weeks apart. The frequency of treatments depends on the initial fat thickness, body composition, and individual response to the procedure. 

While some see improvements after 4-6 sessions, achieving a noticeable change often requires the full course of treatments. Maintenance sessions may be recommended every 3-6 months to sustain results and address any new fat deposits that may develop. 


### What should I expect during a treatment session?

A typical cavitation session lasts 20-30 minutes and involves applying the machine's applicator to the treatment area. The sensation is described as a combination of pressure and vibration, with some users experiencing a tingling or buzzing sensation. 

During the session, you will remain fully clothed and lie on a treatment table. The machine's applicator will move over the treatment area in small sections, requiring the technician to reposition the device between passes to ensure complete coverage.
