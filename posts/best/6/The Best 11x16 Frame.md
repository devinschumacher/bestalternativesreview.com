## Buyer's Guide

When selecting an 11x16 frame, several factors come into play to determine its suitability for your artwork or photograph. Understanding these aspects will help you make an informed decision that enhances both the protection and display of your piece. 


### Size Compatibility

The frame should perfectly match the dimensions of your print, allowing minimal gap between the mat and frame edges. This ensures proper mounting and visual appeal without loose edges or gaps. 


### MATERIAL AND FINISH

Common frame materials include wood, aluminum, and plexiglass. Wood offers warmth and classic appeal, aluminum provides durability at a lighter weight, and plexiglass enhances visibility while protecting the artwork from dust and UV rays. Consider the frame's finish options, ranging from natural wood grains to painted or stained surfaces, depending on your desired look. 


### STURDY BACKING AND GLASS OPTIONS

A sturdy backing prevents warping, while UV-filtering glass or acrylic protects your piece from fading. Check if the frame includes hang tags for additional security and organization. 


### BORDER WIDTH AND STYLING

Borders should be wide enough to frame the artwork effectively but not so large they overpower the piece. Consider whether you prefer plain frames, molding details, or decorative elements that complement your artwork's style. 


## FAQ


### What sizes are available for 11x16 frames?

Standard 11x16 frames are typically available in this exact size, though some manufacturers may offer slight variations. 

The frame's border width usually ranges from 1/2 inch to 1 inch, providing different visual effects while maintaining the same overall dimensions. 


### What materials are 11x16 frames made from?

Common materials include aluminum, which is lightweight and durable, and cherry hardwood, which adds a natural aesthetic. 

Metal frames often feature powder coating for corrosion resistance, while wooden frames may be finished with varnish or lacquer. 


### What's the difference between single and double matting?

Single matting displays artwork with one layer of matting, while double matting uses two layers for added depth. 

Double matting requires 11x16 frames with deeper rabbets (frame pockets) to accommodate both matting layers. 


### How do I clean an 11x16 frame?

Use a soft, lint-free cloth with mild soap and water to clean the frame's surface, avoiding harsh chemicals. 

For frames with intricate details or decorative elements, consider using a microfiber cloth to prevent damage. 


### What's the minimum margin between artwork and frame edges?

Margins typically range from 1/8 inch to 1/4 inch between the artwork and frame edges. 

Check your artwork's border requirements before selecting frame dimensions to prevent cropping or misalignment. 


### Can I return or exchange an 11x16 frame?

Most retailers accept returns within 30 days for unused frames in original packaging.
