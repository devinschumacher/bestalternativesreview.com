## FAQ


### What makes full grain leather superior to other leather grades?

Full grain leather is the highest quality grade, featuring the complete top layer of the hide without any artificial coatings or surface modifications. This natural material is more durable, breathable, and develops a unique patina over time, enhancing its appearance with regular use. 

The thickness and natural grain texture provide superior strength and resistance to scratches and wear compared to leather that has been sanded or split. This makes full grain wallets more long-lasting and valuable as a collectible item. 


### How can I tell if a wallet is made from genuine full grain leather?

Full grain leather typically features a distinct natural grain pattern with minor imperfections that vary from hide to hide. The surface should feel slightly rough to the touch, indicating the natural texture hasn't been altered. 

When viewed against a light source, you should see slight variations in color and slight surface inconsistencies that are characteristic of genuine full grain leather. The edges of the leather may also be left unfinished, adding to its authentic appearance and durability. 


### Are full grain leather wallets worth the higher cost?

Full grain leather wallets generally command a higher price due to their superior quality and craftsmanship. While they represent a significant initial investment, these wallets are built to last, often developing a rich patina and becoming more valuable over time. 

The durability reduces the need for frequent replacements, making them a cost-effective choice in the long run. Factoring in their potential to appreciate in value, premium leather wallets often represent a worthwhile investment for both their practical and aesthetic qualities. 


### Can full grain leather wallets get wet?

While full grain leather has natural water resistance due to its dense structure, prolonged exposure to moisture can damage the wallet. It's important to protect it from rain, condensation, and spills to maintain its integrity and appearance. 

In case of accidental exposure to water, it's crucial to remove any cards or items, gently blot the leather with a dry cloth, and allow it to air dry completely in a cool, well-ventilated area away from direct heat sources. 


### How do I maintain a full grain leather wallet?

Regular maintenance keeps full grain leather looking its best. Use a leather conditioner every 6-12 months to keep the leather supple and protect it from drying out. Apply the conditioner in a well-ventilated area, following the product instructions. 

Use a soft-bristled brush or clean cloth to remove dust and debris, and wipe away any accumulated grime. Avoid harsh chemicals, abrasive cleaners, and leaving the wallet in extreme temperatures to maintain its quality and longevity. 


### How long do full grain leather wallets typically last?

With proper care, full grain leather wallets can last 10 years or more, developing a distinctive patina that individualizes each piece. Factors affecting longevity include usage frequency, environmental conditions, and maintenance practices. 

The natural qualities of full grain leather mean that while the wallet may develop minor scuffs or scratches over time, these imperfections can add character rather than compromise the wallet's structural integrity.
