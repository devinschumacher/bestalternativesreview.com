## FAQ


### What features should I look for in a hardwood floor vacuum?

A hardwood floor vacuum should have a powerful motor capable of lifting pet hair and debris without clogging. Look for machines with thin, flexible attachments designed specifically for hardwood floors to reach tight corners and under furniture. 

HEPA filtration is essential for removing fine dust and allergens. The vacuum should feature a sealed system to prevent dust re-circulation and a large dirt container or bag for efficient debris collection. 


### How do I maintain my hardwood floor vacuum?

Regularly empty the dirt container or replace the bag, and clean the filter to maintain optimal suction power. Most vacuums have a washable filter that needs to be cleaned according to the manufacturer's instructions. 

Store the vacuum in a dry place, keeping the hose and attachments organized. Run the brush roll and motor for a few seconds monthly to lubricate moving parts and prevent wear. 


### How often should I vacuum my hardwood floors?

Vacuum your hardwood floors at least once a week to remove dirt, dust, and debris. For busy areas or homes with pets, daily vacuuming may be necessary. 

Before using your vacuum, check your hardwood floor's cleaning requirements. Some floors may need protection with a floor guard or special vacuum attachments. 


### What should I consider when choosing between upright and canister vacuums?

Upright vacuums offer better suction and are more powerful, making them suitable for heavy-duty cleaning. They are also more versatile, with longer hoses and attachments for reaching high corners. 

Canister vacuums are generally lighter and more maneuverable, with the motor and power supply in a separate base unit. They are easier to store and often have better dust collection capabilities due to their vertical hose design. 


### How can I prevent scratches and damage to my hardwood floors while vacuuming?

Use vacuum attachments with soft rubber hoses and dust straps to prevent scratches. Always vacuum in the direction of the wood grain to minimize surface abrasion. 

Check your vacuum's footprint - some models have wider bodies that can damage hardwood floors. Opt for vacuums with a smaller footprint or use furniture protectors to shield the floor during cleaning. 


### Are hardwood floor vacuums suitable for pets?

Yes, hardwood floor vacuums excel at pet hair removal, making them ideal for homes with pets. Choose a model with powerful suction and a HEPA filter to trap pet dander and allergens. 

Consider a vacuum with a built-in pet hair attachment for easier grooming sessions. Regular vacuuming can significantly reduce pet hair and improve your home's air quality.
