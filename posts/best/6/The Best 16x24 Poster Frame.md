### What's the difference between a 16x24 poster frame and other sizes?

### Can I display art larger than 16x24 inches in a 16x24 frame?

While 16x24 frames are specifically sized for this dimension, you can still use them as the outer frame in a custom matting solution. By selecting a larger piece of artwork and matting it to fit within the frame's dimensions, you create an elegant display that keeps your artwork properly protected and centered. 

However, displaying art larger than the frame's dimensions without proper matting risk damage to both the frame and the artwork. Always measure your piece carefully and consider professional assistance for custom framing projects. 


### What materials are 16x24 frames typically made from?

16x24 frames are commonly made from durable materials that can protect your artwork while complementing any room's decor. Popular options include PVC, engineered wood, and metal, each providing different levels of affordability, weight, and environmental impact. 

Higher-quality frames often feature aluminum construction for lasting durability, while wood frames can add a touch of warmth and character to your display. Many frames also offer reversible mounting options, allowing you to display vertical or horizontal artwork interchangeably. 


### How do I choose between matte and frame styles?

The choice between matte and framed styles depends on your artwork and personal preference. Matte frames provide clean lines and allow the artwork to "breathe, " making them ideal for photographic prints and minimalist designs. 

Framed styles, with their borders and decorative elements, work well for artistic paintings and vintage prints. Consider the room's existing decor and the artwork's visual weight to select a frame that enhances rather than competes with your piece. 


### Can I display unframed artwork in a 16x24 frame?

Standard 16x24 frames are designed with specific mounting hardware and corners that may not accommodate unframed artwork. Attempting to display an unframed piece can damage the frame, the artwork, or both. 

If you have unframed artwork measuring 16x24 inches, consult the frame's documentation or manufacturer for proper installation instructions. Most modern 16x24 frames include pre-drilled holes or built-in brackets specifically for this purpose. 


### What maintenance does a 16x24 frame require?

Regularly inspect your frame for any damage, especially around the corners and mounting points. While a basic dusting with a microfiber cloth maintains most frames, wood and metal frames may require occasional cleaning with mild soap and water or dedicated framing cleaner. 

Check that the glass remains secure and the artwork is properly mounted. If you notice any wear or damage, address it promptly to prevent further deterioration. Professional cleaning is recommended every 3-5 years for optimal preservation of both frame and artwork.
