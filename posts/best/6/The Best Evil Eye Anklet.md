## FAQ


### What is an evil eye anklet?

The evil eye anklet is a traditional piece of jewelry that originated in the Mediterranean and Middle East. It features a small blue glass or stone charm shaped like an eye, believed to ward off negative energies and protect the wearer. 

These anklets combine practicality with cultural significance, often worn as a daily accessory for their protective properties. 


### How many different styles of evil eye anklets are there?

The evil eye comes in various shapes and sizes, but the most common style features a blue stone or glass cabochon shaped like an eye. Some variations include additional charms like coins or silver filigree work, while others feature multiple eyes in a cluster. 

These differing styles allow for personalization and can represent different protective qualities, with some specific designs offering additional blessings or attributes. 


### What materials are evil eye anklets made from?

Traditional evil eye anklets are crafted from glass or precious stones, particularly turquoise and lapis lazuli, known for their healing properties. Additionally, silver and gold filigree work are common, often inlaid with colored stones. 

Modern versions can include a wider range of materials, from acrylic and plastic to recycled metals and beads, while maintaining the traditional eye shape and protective symbolism. 


### How do you wear an evil eye anklet?

The evil eye can be worn singly as a statement piece or in multiples to increase its protective strength. The most common placement is around the ankle, just above the heel, but they can also be worn as bracelets, keychains, or pendants. 

It is believed that wearing the evil eye close to the heart or on the body, rather than hanging separately, enhances its protective effect. 


### What is the cultural significance behind the evil eye?

Throughout history, many cultures developed the belief that a malevolent glance could cause misfortune, illness, or even death. The evil eye protects against this unseen harm through its protective charm. 

The symbol has been used in jewelry, architecture, and everyday objects in cultures spanning from ancient Greece to modern-day Middle Eastern countries, representing protection and good luck. 


### How should you care for your evil eye anklet?

Clean your evil eye anklet regularly with a soft cloth and mild soap to maintain its luster and protect the materials. If your anklet includes precious stones or detailed metal work, consider professional cleaning twice yearly. 

Avoid wearing your evil eye anklet in situations where it could be damaged, such as during intense physical activities, and store it properly when not in use to prevent scratches and tangling.
