### Bow Design and Placement

The bow's position can significantly impact the design's balance and appeal. Mid-shank bows create visual interest without altering the overall height, while ankle or side-tuck placements draw attention to different parts of the foot. The bow's size should be proportionate to the heel's circumference, with finer bows suitable for narrower heels and larger ones for wider styles. 


### Material Quality and Maintenance

Invest in heels with genuine leather or high-quality synthetic materials for durability and professional appearance. Leather requires regular conditioning to maintain its finish, while synthetic materials may need less maintenance but might not develop the same patina. Consider the heel's base material; metal heels resist wear but can scratch easily, while plastic bases are more prone to damage.
