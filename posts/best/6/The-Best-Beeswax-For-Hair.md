### What is beeswax?

Beeswax is a natural substance produced by honey bees as a building material for their hives. It's made up of long-chain fatty acids and alcohol, which gives it properties like waterproofing and heat resistance. 

When used in hair products, beeswax provides structure, helps retain moisture, and adds definition to styles without weighing hair down. 


### How does beeswax affect hair?

Beeswax creates a protective barrier on the hair shaft, sealing in moisture and reducing protein loss. This helps prevent breakage and keeps hair healthy and strong. 

The natural properties of beeswax also help repair damaged hair by coating the shaft and sealing in nutrients and hydration.

### Can anyone use beeswax for hair?

Most people can use beeswax hair products safely. However, those with severe allergies to bees or bee products should avoid using them. 

If you have sensitive skin or experience irritation from other hair products, it's a good idea to patch test a beeswax product before incorporating it into your regular routine. 


### What types of hair can benefit from beeswax?

Beeswax is particularly beneficial for dry, damaged, or color-treated hair. It helps retain moisture and adds shine, making it ideal for maintaining salon-quality results at home.

### How do I use beeswax hair products?

Start by applying a small amount of beeswax to the palms of your hands and warming it between your hands to soften. Then, work it through the ends of your hair before styling. 

For more controlled styling, work the beeswax through sections of your hair while styling. Avoid applying it to the roots, as this can make hair look greasy. 


### Do I need to wash out beeswax hair products?

Unlike other styling products, beeswax typically doesn't need to be washed out between washes. Its natural properties help maintain hair health and provide ongoing benefits.
