## FAQ


### What makes an army fleece jacket different from other fleece jackets?

Army fleece jackets are specifically designed to meet military standards for durability, warmth, and functionality. They typically feature reinforced stitching, taped seams, and heavier gauge fabric compared to civilian versions. 

These jackets often include practical military features like multiple pockets, adjustable cuffs, and reinforced elbows. The insulation is usually more robust, providing better cold weather protection. 


### Are army fleece jackets suitable for civilian use?

Civilian users can benefit from army fleece jackets due to their superior warmth and durability. They provide excellent cold weather protection and are suitable for outdoor activities, commuting, or casual wear. 

The reinforced construction and military-grade insulation make them ideal for harsh winter conditions or extended outdoor excursions where regular fleece jackets might fail. 


### What sizes and styles are available?

Most army fleece jackets come in standard military sizes: small, medium, large, and extra-large. Some manufacturers offer additional sizes up to 3XL or 4XL, though less common. 

Styles typically include jackets with storm flaps, utility pockets, and adjustable hem toggles. Some models may also feature military-style webbing or velcro closures. 


### How should I care for my army fleece jacket?

Machine wash cold with mild detergent using a gentle cycle. Avoid bleach or fabric softeners as they can damage the insulation. Air dry or use a low heat setting on your dryer. 

Store in a cool, dry place when not in use. Consider using a garment bag to protect it during transit or storage. Avoid hanging by the shoulders to prevent sagging. 


### What's the difference between 200 and 300 gram weight fleece?

200g fleece provides basic insulation suitable for cool weather, while 300g offers enhanced warmth for colder conditions. The higher weight also makes the jacket slightly bulkier and more insulating. 

Most army fleece jackets fall in the 300g to 400g weight range, offering adequate warmth for temperatures around freezing. The weight also affects the jacket's thickness and drape. 


### Can army fleece jackets be resized?

Standard sizing ensures most jackets fit well within their size range, but minor adjustments can be made through tailoring. This includes letting out sleeves, taking in the waist, or adjusting the hem. 

For significant size changes, consider purchasing a new jacket as the elasticity limits of the material may not allow for substantial alterations while maintaining the original fit and function.
