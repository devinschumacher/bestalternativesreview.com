## FAQ


### What makes a good quality brass bathroom mirror?

A high-quality brass bathroom mirror combines durable construction with visual appeal. Look for mirrors with solid brass frames rather than plated zinc, which can chip and peel over time. A well-made brass mirror will have clean lines and minimal decorative elements, allowing the metal's natural luster to shine through. 

The mirror surface itself should be thick and distortion-free, with minimal bubbles or inclusions when viewed from the side. Consider mirrors with anti-reflective coatings to reduce glare and improve clarity, especially if the mirror will be used during morning routines. 


### How should I maintain a brass bathroom mirror?

Regular maintenance with a soft, dry cloth is essential for keeping your brass mirror looking its best. For cleaning, use a mild brass cleaner followed by a microfiber cloth to remove any residue and restore shine. It's important to avoid abrasive materials that can scratch the mirror surface. 

Store the mirror in its original packaging or in a protective covering when not in use to prevent scratches and maintain its appearance. 


### What size mirror works best in a bathroom?

Standard bathroom mirror sizes range from 30 to 42 inches in height and 24 to 36 inches in width. The most practical size is usually 36 inches high, which provides ample viewing space for grooming. Consider the room's dimensions and ceiling height when selecting a size to ensure it fits properly. 

For a master bathroom, larger mirrors (42 inches or more) can be beneficial, especially when multiple people need to get ready simultaneously. Smaller bathrooms may require multiple smaller mirrors or a smaller single mirror to save space. 


### Can I install a brass bathroom mirror myself?

Many brass bathroom mirrors come with mounting hardware, making them suitable for basic wall installation. However, factors like mirror weight, wall type, and existing plumbing may affect your ability to install it yourself. If you're unsure about your capabilities, it's best to consult the manufacturer's instructions or hire a professional. 

For heavy mirrors or specific installation requirements, such as mounting over a vanity with existing plumbing, professional installation is recommended to ensure safety and proper alignment. 


### How do I choose the right finish for my brass mirror?

Brass bathroom mirrors typically come in two primary finishes: raw brass and antique brass (also called aged brass). Raw brass maintains its natural golden color and requires periodic polishing to maintain shine, while antique brass develops a rich, patinated surface that develops character over time. 

Your choice should align with your bathroom's existing fixtures and design style. Raw brass pairs well with modern and industrial decor, while antique brass complements traditional and vintage designs. 


### What should I consider when matching hardware with a brass mirror?

Bathroom fixtures and hardware should share either the same finish or complementary tones. When selecting accessories for your brass mirror, consider pieces with either the same raw brass or antique brass finish. This visual consistency helps create a cohesive look in your bathroom. 

For a more contemporary feel, you might opt for raw brass fixtures with your antique brass mirror. Conversely, antique brass fixtures would work best with a mirror that has experienced similar aging.
