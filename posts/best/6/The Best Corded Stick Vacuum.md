## FAQ


### Why choose a corded stick vacuum?

Corded stick vacuums offer a compact, maneuverable alternative to traditional vacuums while maintaining adequate power for everyday cleaning tasks. Unlike cordless models, they avoid the convenience drawbacks of limited runtime and need for frequent charging. 

The corded design provides consistent suction power without the risk of sudden power loss, making them particularly suitable for quick cleanups and spot-treating pet hair and dust. 


### How long do corded stick vacuums last on a single charge?

Since they don't use batteries, corded stick vacuums operate continuously as long as the power source remains connected. This feature eliminates the need for charging cycles and extends the machine's operational life. 

The longest they'll run is determined by the length of the power cord, typically ranging from 15 to 30 feet for standard models. This allows users to clean larger areas without losing suction power. 


### Are corded stick vacuums suitable for pet hair?

Most corded stick vacuums excel at pet hair removal due to their powerful suction and specialized attachments. The long, flexible design allows easy access to pet beds, furniture, and car interiors where pet hair tends to accumulate. 

For optimal results, models with motorized brush rolls and dedicated pet hair tools are particularly effective. These features help break loose trapped hair and remove it efficiently from fabrics and surfaces. 


### Can corded stick vacuums handle deep cleaning tasks?

While not as powerful as standalone deep cleaning vacuums, corded stick vacuums can handle regular cleaning needs effectively. They're best for quick daily sweeps and maintaining clean surfaces rather than comprehensive deep cleaning. 

For thorough cleaning jobs, these vacuums work well in combination with regular vacuuming. Their convenience makes them ideal for maintaining clean homes between scheduled deep cleaning sessions. 


### Are corded stick vacuums easy to maintain?

Corded stick vacuums generally have straightforward maintenance requirements, including emptying the dust cup, cleaning filters, and checking the power cord. Regular maintenance ensures optimal performance and longevity. 

Most models require minimal upkeep, and their design allows for quick access to essential maintenance points. Professional-grade models may offer additional features like washable filters, which slightly increase maintenance requirements. 


### What are the main drawbacks of corded stick vacuums?

The primary limitation of corded stick vacuums is their reliance on a power outlet, which restricts cleaning to areas near electrical sources. This limitation affects their versatility compared to cordless models. 

While they perform well for quick cleanups, their suction power isn't typically as strong as that of dedicated deep cleaning vacuums. This makes them less suitable for particularly stubborn dirt and debris.
