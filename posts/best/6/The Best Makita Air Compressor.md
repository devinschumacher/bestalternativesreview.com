### How do I choose the right size of air compressor?

Selecting the right size requires considering your specific air tool needs and usage frequency. Start by checking the CFM (cubic feet per minute) and HP (horsepower) requirements of your tools. A general rule is to choose an air compressor with at least 1.5 times the total tool requirements in CFM. 

For occasional use, a 10-gallon portable compressor might suffice, while regular workshops may need 20-gallon models capable of handling multiple tools simultaneously. Always account for future expansion needs when making your selection. 


### Can I connect multiple tools to one air compressor?

Most air compressors allow connecting multiple tools, but the total demand must not exceed the compressor's output capacity. Consider using pressure switches to manage tool priority and prevent overloading. For heavy-duty applications, portable compressors are often more practical due to their mobility and reduced power draw. 

Keep in mind that continuous operation with multiple tools may require larger storage tanks and higher CFM ratings. Always refer to your compressor's manual for specific limitations and recommended configurations. 


### How often should I perform maintenance on my air compressor?

Regular maintenance extends the lifespan of your air compressor and ensures optimal performance. Basic checks should be performed weekly, including verifying oil levels, examining air filters, and checking for leaks in hoses and fittings. 

More comprehensive maintenance, such as changing oil and filters, should be done every 6 months to a year based on usage frequency. Always follow the manufacturer's recommended service intervals and replace any worn parts promptly. 


### What is the average lifespan of a Makita air compressor?

Makita air compressors typically last between 5 to 10 years with regular maintenance and proper usage. Factors influencing longevity include service intervals, operating conditions, and adherence to manufacturer guidelines. 

To maximize your compressor's lifespan, follow these best practices: keep it clean and dry, protect from extreme temperatures, and avoid operating with contaminated air filters. Regularly servicing the electrical components and lubricating moving parts will also significantly extend its operational life. 


### Can I run an air compressor without a dedicated circuit?

Running an air compressor on a shared circuit can cause voltage drops affecting both the compressor and connected tools. The National Electric Code recommends using a dedicated 20-amp circuit for portable compressors and 30-amp circuit for larger stationary models. 

While some compressors may start on a shared circuit, they may run inefficiently or shut down unexpectedly. For reliable performance, it's essential to provide a dedicated circuit with the appropriate amperage rating to handle the compressor's peak load.
