## FAQ

![Dip-Nails-4](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Dip-Nails-4/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What are dip nails made of?

Dip nails are typically made from a mixture of rubber, acrylic, and plastic. This blend creates a durable nail that can be repeatedly dip-coated in color without degrading. 

The rubber component provides flexibility, while the acrylic and plastic enhance strength and resistance to chipping. This composition makes dip nails ideal for frequent nail enhancement applications. 


### How long do dip nails last?

Dip nails usually last between 2 to 4 weeks with proper care. Factors affecting longevity include nail application technique, frequency of use, and individual nail health. 

Proper maintenance, including regular filing and keeping nails dry between applications, can help extend the life of dip nails. 

![Dip-Nails-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Dip-Nails-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Can anyone use dip nails?

Most people can use dip nails, but certain medical conditions may affect suitability. Those with severe allergies, diabetes, or poor blood circulation should consult a healthcare provider before using nail enhancements. 

Always check the product packaging for specific guidelines and perform a patch test before full application to ensure compatibility. 


### How are dip nails applied?

Dip nails are applied by soaking them in a specialized dip solution, coating them with color, and then sealing them with a top coat. The process requires precise application to achieve the desired result. 

Application typically involves filing the natural nail into shape, preparing the nail plate, and carefully placing the dip nail in position before curing under a UV lamp. 

![Dip-Nails-6](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Dip-Nails-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What maintenance is needed for dip nails?

Regular maintenance is essential for dip nails. This includes weekly filing to maintain shape, keeping nails dry between applications to prevent delamination, and occasional re-application of top coat to maintain shine. 

Avoid harsh chemicals, excessive water exposure, and repeated use of metal polishers, which can damage the nail surface and shorten its life. 


### Are dip nails safe to use?

When used correctly, dip nails are safe for most people. They are free from harsh chemicals like formaldehyde, toluene, and DBP, making them a safer alternative to traditional acrylic nails. 

However, always follow proper application techniques and use products from reputable manufacturers to minimize health risks.
