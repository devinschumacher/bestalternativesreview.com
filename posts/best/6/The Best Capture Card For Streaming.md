## FAQ


### What is a capture card, and why do I need one for streaming?

A capture card is a device that allows your computer to capture video and audio inputs and convert them into digital format for streaming. It's essential for streaming because it handles the complex task of capturing high-quality video from external sources like cameras or game consoles, processing the data, and sending it to your chosen streaming service. 

Without a capture card, you would need to use the computer's built-in capabilities, which can't handle the raw data from these sources efficiently. This results in poor video quality, lag, or even system crashes during streaming sessions. 


### What are the main types of capture cards available in the market?

There are two primary categories of capture cards: standalone units for general-purpose streaming and graphics card-based solutions for gaming and professional applications. Standalone capture cards offer basic features like HDMI input and straightforward setup, while graphics card-based solutions provide advanced functionality like frame capture and video processing capabilities. 

Specialized capture cards also exist for specific use cases, such as those designed for professional gaming, live event streaming, or multi-device setups. The choice depends on your specific streaming requirements and budget. 


### How does a capture card handle multiple video inputs?

Modern capture cards include multiple input ports to handle multiple video sources. The most common configurations support two or four simultaneous inputs, which can be from different devices like cameras, gaming consoles, or streaming devices. 

Some capture cards feature switching capabilities that allow you to choose which input is captured and streamed. This is particularly useful for live events where you might need to switch between different sources, like a presenter and a display screen. 


### What is the difference between an HDMI capture card and a USB capture card?

HDMI capture cards connect directly to the HDMI output of your device (like a gaming console or digital camera), while USB capture cards require a video source that can transmit through a USB connection (like some webcams). 

HDMI capture cards typically offer better performance and fewer latency issues due to their direct connection method. They also provide higher video resolutions and refresh rates, making them ideal for gaming and professional streaming applications. 


### How does a capture card affect streaming quality?

The quality of a capture card directly impacts your streaming experience. Higher-end cards process video with less compression and maintain consistent frame rates, resulting in smoother streaming and fewer dropped frames. 

Key factors include color accuracy, video processing capabilities (like motion estimation and compensation), and latency. A good card should maintain consistent quality under all conditions, from basic gameplay to complex live streaming scenarios. 


### What should I consider when choosing the right capture card?

The primary considerations are your video input sources, desired output resolution, and whether you need specialized features like frame capture or advanced processing capabilities. Compatibility with your computer's architecture (Windows, Mac, or Linux) is also crucial. 

Consider your budget, as capture cards range from under $100 for basic home streaming to several hundred dollars for professional applications. Reading reviews and comparing specifications can help you find the right balance of features and performance for your needs.
