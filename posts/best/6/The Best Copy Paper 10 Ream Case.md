### Paper Weight and Caliper

Check the paper weight, typically measured in pounds (lb) in the United States, as it affects printing quality and cost. Generally, 20 lb to 24 lb paper suits general office printing, while heavier weights like 24 lb to 28 lb offer better durability for high-volume printing. 


### Brightness and Optical Whiteness

Higher brightness levels (measured in Percent Total Hemispherical Reflectance, or PTW) produce crisper black text and clearer images. Standard office paper ranges from 40 PTW to 65 PTW. For professional documents, consider paper with 65 PTW or higher. 


### Coating and Surface Finish

Uncoated paper provides good readability and is suitable for most office printing tasks. Coated paper, with its smooth surface, excels in reproducing fine details and vibrant colors but may show more ink marks. Consider coated paper for high-end printing needs. 


### Size and Quantity Considerations

The standard paper size is 8.5 x 11 inches, ensuring compatibility with most office equipment. When purchasing in ream cases, calculate your monthly usage to determine the right quantity. Consider paper storage and handling capacity when selecting container size.
