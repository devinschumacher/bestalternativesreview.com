## FAQ


### What is "My Side of the Mountain" about?

Set in the Catskill Mountains of New York, the story follows a boy who is forced to leave his busy city life behind when his parents move to a remote mountain. There, he learns survival skills, forms a bond with a wild hawk, and discovers the beauty of nature. 

Published in 1959, the book has become a classic of young adult literature, encouraging readers to appreciate the natural world and develop independence. 


### Who wrote "My Side of the Mountain"?

The book was written by Jean Craighead George, an American author known for her work in both children's literature and science writing. She drew inspiration from her own experiences as a naturalist. 

George used her background in biology to create a realistic portrayal of wilderness survival, making the book both entertaining and educational. 


### How old is the book?

Published in 1959, "My Side of the Mountain" is a classic young adult novel that has remained relevant for decades. Its themes of independence and nature exploration continue to resonate with readers. 

While the book's setting is in 1959, its universal appeal has ensured its continued popularity in modern times. 


### What challenges does the main character face?

His biggest challenges come from his own fears and the physical demands of survival, as well as the need to communicate with and eventually tame a wild hawk named Fern. 


### How does the story end?

The book concludes with Ben successfully establishing himself in the mountain wilderness. He has built a small cabin, learned to survive, and formed a deep connection with nature. 

The ending shows his growing independence and self-reliance, as well as his newfound ability to appreciate the beauty and solitude of his mountain home. 


### What lessons can readers learn from the story?

"My Side of the Mountain" teaches readers about the importance of self-reliance, resilience, and respecting nature. It also highlights the value of taking calculated risks to pursue personal growth.
