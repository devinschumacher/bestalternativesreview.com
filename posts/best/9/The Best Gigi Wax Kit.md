## FAQ


### What is included in a Gigi Wax Kit?

A typical Gigi Wax Kit includes essential tools for natural waxing, such as a precision tweezer, waxing cloth, clean cotton pads, and a small plastic container for the wax. Some kits also come with a warming dish and instructions. 

Most kits contain enough supplies for several at-home waxing sessions, making them a practical choice for regular grooming. 


### How long does a Gigi Wax Kit last?

With proper storage, a Gigi Wax Kit can last several months to a year, depending on how frequently it's used. The wax typically has an expiration date of 2-3 years from the manufacturing date. 

For best results, use the kit within its recommended timeframe and replace any single-use items as needed. 


### Is Gigi Wax safe for sensitive skin?

Gigi Wax is designed for sensitive skin and contains no harsh chemicals. However, as with any new product, it's recommended to perform a patch test before regular use. 

If you experience redness, irritation, or itching after applying the wax, discontinue use and consult a dermatologist if symptoms persist. 


### How do I properly store a Gigi Wax Kit?

Store your Gigi Wax Kit in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep the wax container sealed tightly to maintain its quality. 

Avoid storing it in extreme temperatures, as wax expands and contracts with heat and cold, potentially affecting its consistency. 


### How often can I use Gigi Wax?

For best results, wait 2-3 days between waxing sessions to allow your skin to heal properly. This frequency helps prevent ingrown hairs and skin irritation. 

If you have particularly fast-growing hair, you may opt for weekly sessions, but always listen to your skin's cues. 


### What type of hair is suitable for Gigi Wax?

Gigi Wax works best on medium-length hair, approximately 1/4 to 1/2 inch in length. Longer hair may require trimming before waxing. 

Avoid waxing hair that is shorter than 1/4 inch, as it may not adhere properly to the wax, and longer than 1 inch, which can increase the risk of breakage.
