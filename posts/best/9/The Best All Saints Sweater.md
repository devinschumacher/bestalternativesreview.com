### What is the All Saints brand?

All Saints is a British fashion brand founded in 1996, known for its streetwear-inspired fashion. The brand offers clothing for both men and women, combining urban style with a modern twist. 

Initially established as a streetwear label, All Saints has evolved to incorporate elements of design and craftsmanship, creating a distinct visual identity in the fashion market. 


### What materials are used in All Saints sweaters?

Most All Saints sweaters are made from premium quality cotton and polyester blends. These materials offer a balance of comfort and durability, suitable for everyday wear. 

Some winter collections feature wool blends for added warmth and texture. The brand also incorporates modern materials like moisture-wicking fabrics in their performance-based sweaters.

### How are All Saints sweaters sized?

All Saints uses standard sizing charts, with measurements provided in inches for both chest and length. The brand offers sizes ranging from S to 3XL. 

It's recommended to check the specific size guide for each collection, as sizing can vary slightly between seasons and designs. 


### Are All Saints sweaters machine washable?

Yes, All Saints sweaters are machine washable using standard settings. The most common recommended wash cycle is a gentle or delicate cycle. 

When washing, it's advisable to use a mild detergent and avoid using bleach or fabric softeners, as these can affect the quality and appearance of the fabric.

### What care instructions should I follow?

After washing, air drying is recommended to maintain the shape and quality of the sweater. Avoid using a dryer, as high heat can shrink the fibers. 

If ironing is needed, use a low to medium heat setting and avoid direct ironing on the print or any embellishments. 


### How should I store my All Saints sweater?

Store your sweater in a cool, dry place when not in use. Consider using padded hangers to prevent stretching and maintain shape. 

If you choose to fold your sweater, place it in a drawer with other similar garments to prevent snagging. Avoid storing sweaters in plastic bags, as this can trap moisture and lead to mildew.
