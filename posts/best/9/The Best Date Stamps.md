## FAQ


### What are date stamps, and why are they useful?

Date stamps are specialized tools that automatically print dates onto materials like paper, cardboard, or plastic. They save time and reduce errors compared to writing dates by hand. These stamps are particularly useful for businesses, households, and individuals who need to track when items were received, produced, or used. 

They work by storing a date format and a corresponding ink pad. When you press down, the stamp applies the current date in your chosen format. Most date stamps can print dates in common formats like MM/DD/YYYY or DD/MM/YYYY, and some models allow customization of the format to suit specific needs. 


### What types of date stamps are available?

There are three main types: manual, semi-automatic, and automatic. Manual date stamps require you to press and turn a handle to apply ink, while semi-automatic models have a built-in reservoir that reduces ink changes. Automatic models use a spring mechanism to stamp the date with minimal effort. 

The most common formats for date stamps include MM/DD/YYYY, DD/MM/YYYY, and custom formats. Some advanced models offer features like multiple date formats, peel-off ink pads, and digital displays for setting the date. 


### How accurate are date stamps?

Date stamps are generally very accurate, as they print the current date from your device's clock and calendar. However, their accuracy depends on two factors: your device's clock being correctly set and the date stamp being properly maintained and used. 

Most date stamps use a spring-loaded mechanism that aligns the date stamp plate with the current date. This mechanical alignment ensures that the stamp prints the correct date, making manual errors less likely. 


### Can date stamps be customized?

Customization options vary by model, but many date stamps allow you to select between multiple date formats. Some models offer peel-off ink pads that can be replaced with different colors or materials suitable for specific applications. 

More advanced units may include features like digital displays for setting the date, multiple date format options, and programmable settings for consecutive numbering or time-stamping. 


### How long do date stamps last?

The lifespan of a date stamp depends on several factors, including usage frequency, maintenance, and ink quality. With regular use, a basic manual date stamp can last 2-3 years between ink changes, while automatic models typically need new ink pads every 6-12 months. 

Proper maintenance, including cleaning the stamp plate and ensuring the ink pad remains properly sealed, can extend the stamp's lifespan. Consider factors like usage intensity and environmental conditions when estimating the stamp's longevity. 


### What maintenance is required for date stamps?

Regular maintenance helps ensure your date stamp remains accurate and operates efficiently. Basic care includes cleaning the stamp plate after each use with mild soap and water, then drying thoroughly. Store the ink pad in its protective sleeve when not in use to prevent drying out. 

For automatic models, regular inspection of the spring mechanism and plate alignment ensures consistent date printing. Replace ink pads before they become dry or faded for the best printing quality. Basic cleaning and inspection monthly will help maintain optimal performance.
