## FAQ


### What size dehumidifier do I need for my space?

The size of your dehumidifier should match the humidity level and square footage of the space. Use the following guide: 70-100 gallons per day for 1,000-1,200 square feet, 100-130 gallons for 1,201-1,500 square feet, and 130+ gallons for spaces over 1,500 square feet. 

Consider the room's airflow, insulation quality, and whether it contains sources of moisture like shower areas or kitchens. Higher humidity levels or restricted airflow may require a larger capacity dehumidifier. 


### How much water does a dehumidifier collect?

Standard dehumidifiers collect 40-170 pints of water daily, with commercial models exceeding 200 pints. Higher capacity units extract more water, but also require emptying more frequently. 

The average household dehumidifier removes 1-2 gallons per hour, while industrial models can process up to 7 gallons per hour. Calculate your specific needs based on the space size and local humidity levels. 


### What's the difference between dehumidifiers?

Price difference mainly reflects water collection capacity, energy efficiency, and specialty features like quiet operation or remote monitoring. Higher costs often equate to better performance and longer lifespan. 

Entry-level models cost $100-$300, mid-range $300-$600, and premium units exceed $600. Consider your budget alongside essential features like adjustable settings and quiet operation for optimal value. 


### How often should I empty the water tank?

Check daily for full tanks in humid conditions, emptying when the indicator light comes on or the tank reaches capacity. This prevents overflow and maintains continuous operation. 

Consider purchasing a dehumidifier with automatic drainage or larger tanks if daily emptying is inconvenient. Professional-grade models can process water continuously without human intervention. 


### What maintenance is required for dehumidifiers?

Regularly clean the water tank, filter, and condenser coil monthly using mild soap and water. Remove any built-up dust or debris from these components to maintain optimal performance. 

Check the drain hose for kinks or blockages weekly and replace it if discolored or damaged. This simple maintenance prevents water leaks and extends the unit's lifespan. 


### How much electricity do dehumidifiers consume?

Standard dehumidifiers use 150-400 watts continuously, resulting in $25-$65 per year in electricity based on usage. Higher capacity units consume more power but maintain consistent humidity levels. 

Energy Star-certified models use 125-250 watts, offering improved efficiency without compromising performance. Calculate your energy costs based on local electricity rates and usage frequency.
