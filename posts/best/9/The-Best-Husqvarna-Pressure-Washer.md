### What is the maximum pressure and flow rate of Husqvarna pressure washers?

Husqvarna pressure washers are available in various models with different specifications. Most consumer-grade units offer between 2000 to 3000 PSI at flow rates typically ranging from 1.8 to 2.5 GPM. Professional-grade models can achieve up to 4000 PSI with flow rates around 2.8 GPM. 

The maximum pressure and flow rate determine the power of the water jet. Higher pressure (up to 4000 PSI) and flow rate are suitable for tough jobs like removing heavy grease, paint, or baked-on dirt. For light cleaning tasks, you might prefer a lower pressure setting around 2000 to 2500 PSI. 


### What is the difference between electric and gas-powered Husqvarna pressure washers?

Electric pressure washers are more affordable, quieter, and easier to use since they don't require fuel. They're typically lighter and less complicated to maintain. However, they're limited by power outlet availability and can't be used in remote locations. 

Gas-powered Husqvarna pressure washers offer greater mobility and can be used anywhere without power. They provide consistent performance regardless of electricity availability but require regular maintenance, fuel storage, and proper handling of emissions.

### What features should I look for when choosing a Husqvarna pressure washer?

Consider the water supply connection (hose or tank-fed), trigger gun type, and nozzle options. Professional models often include rotary gun systems and multiple nozzle sizes for different cleaning needs. Also, check for additional accessories like soap injectors and extension wands. 

Look for reliable features like a pressure relief valve, corrosion protection, and professional-grade construction. Evaluate the weight, size, and overall build quality to ensure it matches your intended use and storage space. 


### How do I maintain a Husqvarna pressure washer?

Regular maintenance includes checking and changing the filter, draining water from the pump after use, and inspecting and replacing the wand and hose connections. For gas models, this also includes checking fuel levels and air filters. 

Deep cleaning involves breaking down the pump and cleaning it, as well as servicing the motor and electrical components (for electric models). Refer to the owner's manual for specific recommendations based on your model.

### How long does a Husqvarna pressure washer last?

The lifespan of a Husqvarna pressure washer typically ranges from 3 to 5 years with regular use and proper maintenance. Professional-grade models can last longer with commercial use and regular servicing. 

To maximize longevity, store the pressure washer in a dry, protected area when not in use. Avoid running the motor dry or letting it sit with standing water in the pump. Regular maintenance and follow-up with the manufacturer's recommendations can significantly extend the tool's lifespan. 


### What is the difference between a hot water and cold water Husqvarna pressure washer?

Hot water pressure washers heat the water before it reaches the pump, usually through an electric heating element. This is beneficial for removing tough grime, bacteria, and organic material, as the heat helps break down the bonds holding contaminants in place. 

Cold water pressure washers maintain the water at ambient temperature. They're more cost-effective and require less maintenance, but they're not as effective at removing heavy grime, especially organic material. Some newer models include a cold water booster pump that provides improved cleaning power without the added complexity of heating.
