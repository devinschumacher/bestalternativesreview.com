## FAQ


### What is a bike chain cleaner, and why do I need one?

A bike chain cleaner removes dirt, grime, and moisture from your bicycle chain, keeping it clean and functioning properly. Regular cleaning prevents wear and extends the life of your chain while maintaining optimal pedaling efficiency. 

Most bike chain cleaners consist of a container with a mesh filter and a built-in brush. They work by allowing you to rotate the chain through the cleaner, agitating the parts and removing contaminants. 


### How often should I clean my bike chain?

Clean your bike chain after every 100-150 miles of riding or after each use if you ride in dirty conditions like mud or saltwater. Regular cleaning prevents built-up crud from causing premature wear and corrosion. 

If you notice the chain skipping, making noise, or feeling stiff, it's time for a thorough cleaning and lube application. 


### Can I use any household cleaner to clean my bike chain?

Using household cleaners is not recommended as they can damage the chain's coating and leave residue. Always use a chain-specific cleaner designed to safely remove grime without compromising the chain's integrity. 

If you're in a pinch and have no bike-specific cleaner, you can use distilled water and a degreaser safe for metal surfaces, but rinse thoroughly with clean water afterward to prevent corrosion. 


### How do I properly clean my bike chain?

First, remove the chain from the bike and place it in the cleaner. Rotate the chain through the cleaning solution, allowing the brush to agitate the links. For tough grime, you may need to clean each link individually. 

After cleaning, thoroughly rinse the chain with clean water, being careful not to introduce debris. If using chain cleaner fluid, follow up by thoroughly washing and drying the chain before lubrication. 


### What's the best way to dry a wet bike chain?

The most effective method is to wipe the chain with a clean, soft cloth to remove excess water and debris. Ensure each link is dry before lubrication to prevent attracting dirt and moisture. 

If you need to speed up the drying process, you can air-dry the chain in a well-ventilated area but avoid using heat sources that can warp or damage the chain. 


### How do I know when my chain needs replacement?

Inspect the chain for visible wear, such as stretched links or noticeable dents. Measure the chain for wear by placing two new, aligned links next to a half-inch of new chain - if there's more than. 5mm of space, it's time to replace the chain. 

Regular maintenance, including proper cleaning and lubrication, can extend the life of your chain. Replace chains before they affect your bike's shifting performance or chain wear indicator shows it's time.
