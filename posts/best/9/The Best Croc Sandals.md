### What makes Croc sandals unique?

### What's the difference between indoor and outdoor Crocs?

Indoor Crocs feature thinner soles and lighter construction, with some models including non-slip rubber strips on the bottom for added grip. These are ideal for home use, light indoor activities, and short-term outdoor use in protected areas. 

Outdoor Crocs are built for more demanding conditions, with robust soles that resist wear and tear from sand, rocks, and other outdoor elements. They typically include deeper grooves for superior traction and are suitable for extended periods of use in various weather conditions. 


### How do you properly care for Croc sandals?

Clean your Crocs regularly using mild soap and warm water, paying special attention to the grooved soles to remove dirt and bacteria. Allow them to air dry completely before wearing again to prevent mold growth. 

For deeper cleaning, you can use a croc brush specifically designed for these sandals. Periodically, you can also use a mold-resistant treatment to help prevent fungal growth. 


### Can you wear Croc sandals in the water?

While Crocs are water-resistant, they are not fully waterproof. Avoid wearing them in pools, spas, or other wet environments unless specifically labeled as water shoes. 

If you plan to wear them near water, consider pairing them with water socks to protect the interior from water damage and maintain their shape. 


### How long do Croc sandals typically last?

With regular wear, Croc sandals typically last 1-2 years when cared for properly. Factors that affect durability include frequency of use, care routine, and environmental conditions. 

To extend their lifespan, rotate between multiple pairs, avoid wearing them in harsh conditions or extended periods, and follow the care instructions provided by the manufacturer.
