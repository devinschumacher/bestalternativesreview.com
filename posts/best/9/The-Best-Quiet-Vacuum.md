## Buyer's Guide

A quiet vacuum cleaner removes dirt and dust from your home while minimizing noise disturbance. Key features and considerations include sound level measurement, motor type, filtration efficiency, and operating modes. 


### Sound Level Measurement

The sound level, measured in decibels (dB), indicates how noisy the vacuum cleaner operates. Higher-quality vacuums typically have sound levels below 75 dB, though some specialized models can achieve 65 dB or less. Professional-grade units may exceed 75 dB due to their powerful suction capabilities. 


### Motor Type

There are two primary motor types: brushless DC motors and traditional AC motors. Brushless DC motors are more energy-efficient and quieter, with sound levels frequently below 70 dB. AC motors, while less expensive, generate more heat and noise, often exceeding 75 dB. 


### Filtration Efficiency

Filter systems trap fine particles and reduce emissions. High-efficiency particulate air (HEPA) filters capture 99.97% of particles measuring 0.3 microns or larger, ensuring minimal dust recirculation. Pre-filters catch larger debris, extending filter life and maintaining airflow. 


### Operating Modes

Modern vacuums offer various operating modes to balance suction power and noise. Eco-mode reduces power consumption and noise for everyday cleaning, while turbo mode provides maximum suction for tough cleaning tasks. Some models include special modes for quiet operation during late-night cleaning sessions.
