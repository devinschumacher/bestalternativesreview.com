## Buyer's Guide

When considering an induction cooktop, several key factors come into play. Understanding how induction cooking works sets the foundation for your choice. Unlike traditional gas or electric cooktops, induction cooking heats only the cookware placed on it, making it more energy-efficient and responsive to temperature changes. 


### Key Features to Consider

### What is an induction cooktop?

Induction cooktops use electromagnetic energy to heat cookware directly, making them one of the most efficient cooking methods available. The cooktop surface remains cool while providing precise temperature control and rapid heating capabilities. 

This technology requires cookware made of magnetic materials like iron or steel, which create an electrical current when placed on the induction coil. This current generates heat within the cookware itself, cooking your food more evenly and efficiently than traditional electric or gas cooktops.
