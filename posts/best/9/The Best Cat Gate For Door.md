## FAQ


### What is a cat door?

A cat door is a specialized pet door designed to allow indoor cats safe, supervised access to outdoor spaces. It features a controlled entry mechanism that prevents unattended exit while allowing cats to leave when desired. 

These doors are typically installed in screens or solid doors connecting indoor and outdoor areas, providing cats with exercise and natural stimulation while maintaining home safety. 


### What materials are cat doors made from?

Most cat doors are made from durable plastic or nylon composite materials that resist scratching and weather damage. High-quality models incorporate reinforced edges and weather-sealing to prevent drafts and keep outdoor elements out. 

Gate frames often use metal or heavy-duty plastic, while door panels may include reinforcing bars or mesh to withstand claw penetration and maintain structural integrity. 


### What size cat door do I need?

Measure your cat's body length, including their tail, to determine the appropriate door size. Standard pet doors range from 3 to 5 inches wide and 7 to 9 inches tall, which works for most domestic cats. 

Consider the age and breed of your cat. Older or larger breeds may need wider doors, while younger or smaller cats might benefit from slightly smaller openings to prevent accidental escape. 


### How do I install a cat door?

Measure your door frame accurately and choose a suitable location for the installation. Most doors come with detailed instructions and included mounting hardware. The process typically involves mounting the frame, installing the door panel, and securing it with provided screws or fasteners. 

Prepare the opening by removing any existing door hardware or weather stripping. Make any necessary adjustments to the frame to ensure a proper fit before installing the door panel. 


### How much does a cat door cost?

Entry-level cat doors typically range from $20 to $50, while premium models with advanced features can cost between $50 and $150. Installation kits and mounting accessories may add additional costs. 

Consider the material quality, brand reputation, and included features when determining value. Higher-priced options often offer better durability, easier maintenance, and additional security features. 


### How do I teach my cat to use a door?

Start by placing food near the door and gradually move it through the door opening. Encourage your cat to follow, offering treats and praise to reinforce the behavior. Keep the door partially open during this process to make it easier for your cat to navigate. 

Once your cat is comfortable with the partially open door, close it completely and use treats to guide your cat through. Gradually increase the difficulty by timing how long your cat remains outside before re-entering or using the door in various environments.
