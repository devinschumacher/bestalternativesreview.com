## Buyer's Guide

Square diamond rings blend the brilliance of round diamonds with unique geometric appeal. ### Setting Options

The setting plays a crucial role in enhancing a square diamond's brilliance and protecting its corners. Common settings include princess cut, with four-pronged corners for maximum visibility and security; modified oval, which combines elements of oval and square cuts for added length; and cushion cut, offering softer lines and larger table facets. Consider how the setting complements the diamond's cut and your personal style.

### Size and Proportion

Square diamonds come in various proportions, from petite micro-pave settings to larger statement pieces. The ideal ratio between length and width should be 1:1 for traditional princess cuts, while modified oval and cushion cuts allow for slightly different measurements. Ensure the ring fits comfortably on your finger and scales appropriately with your body type. 


### Clarity and Culet

Square diamonds often expose corners and edges, making clarity grading particularly important. Look for inclusions in less noticeable areas and opt for higher clarity grades unless you're seeking an earthy, natural look. The culet, or point on the bottom of the diamond, should be clean to prevent extra facetting and potential stress points. A carefully chosen culet shape maintains the diamond's integrity while minimizing visual distractions.

### What makes a square diamond different from a round diamond?

Square diamonds, often called emerald or asscher cuts, feature four equal sides and square corners, which gives them a distinct rectangular shape compared to the standard round brilliant cut. This unique geometry results in different light reflection patterns and visual effects compared to round diamonds. 

While both square and round diamonds require precise cutting for optimal brilliance, square diamonds typically have fewer facets (58-60 vs. 57-58 in round cuts), which can affect their light performance and fire. 


### What carat weight is suitable for a square diamond ring?

Carat weight selection depends on your budget and intended finger size. Smaller fingers usually look best with 1-1.5 carat stones, while larger fingers can accommodate 1.5-2 carats. Micro settings may work for smaller hands with 0.5-1 carat stones. 

Keep in mind that the 4Cs (cut, color, clarity, carat weight) affect the ring's appearance. A well-cut 1-carat stone will appear larger and more brilliant than a poorly cut 1.5-carat stone of the same size.

### How should square diamond rings be cleaned?

Use mild soap and warm water for daily cleaning. Avoid harsh chemicals and ultrasonic cleaners, which can damage the stone's setting or facets. Regular cleaning helps maintain the stone's brilliance and clarity. 

Store the ring in a soft cloth pouch when not worn to protect it from scratches and damage. 


### Are square diamond rings suitable for everyday wear?

While square diamonds can be worn daily, consider the setting and ring size. Micro prong settings and larger stones may need to be removed for intense activities. Proper sizing ensures the ring won't snag on clothing or during physical activities. 

These rings are ideal for those who prefer cleaner lines and want a distinctive piece that stands out in any setting.

### How much more do square diamond rings typically cost?

Square diamonds often cost 10-20% less than round diamonds of the same quality due to their unique shape and less complex cutting process. However, prices vary based on the stone's quality and specific design elements. 

Asscher and square emerald cuts may cost more due to their higher demand and specific cutting requirements compared to modern square cuts. 


### What maintenance requirements do square diamond rings have?

Regular cleaning and inspection are essential. Check prongs monthly for tightness, especially in micro prong settings. The stone's protective elements, such as bevels or facets, should be checked annually for wear. 

Consider insurance coverage and professional examination every 6-12 months to maintain the ring's value and ensure proper setting.
