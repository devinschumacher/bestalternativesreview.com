## Buyer's Guide

Airspun powder, known for its fine texture and superior flow properties, is widely used in industrial applications such as powder coating, automotive parts finishing, and electrostatic painting. This specialized powder coating material requires careful consideration of several key factors to achieve optimal performance. 


### Particle Size and Distribution

The particle size and distribution of airspun powder significantly impact coating quality and efficiency. Manufacturers typically specify this through D50 values, indicating the median particle size. A narrower size distribution generally leads to better flow and fewer defects in the finished coating. 


### Flow Properties

Flow properties determine how easily the powder coats surfaces and builds thickness during the curing process. Airspun powders require specific flow characteristics to ensure uniform application without clogging application equipment. Understanding these properties helps select powders suitable for automated or manual application methods. 


### Compatibility with Additives

Many applications require modifying the base powder properties through additives. These can affect conductivity, viscosity, and cure characteristics. When selecting airspun powder, consider whether you need self-priming, color-enhancing, or other additives that might impact performance. 


### Application and Curing Conditions

The intended application method (electrostatic, gravity feed, etc. ) and curing conditions (temperature, humidity, cure time) influence powder selection. Airspun powders designed for specific processes may offer advantages in flow, adhesion, or appearance compared to general-purpose alternatives.
