## FAQ


### What are keto tortillas?

Keto tortillas are specifically designed for ketogenic (keto) diets, providing an alternative to traditional flour tortillas while maintaining low carbohydrate content. These tortillas are typically made from keto-friendly ingredients such as almond flour, coconut flour, or egg whites, making them suitable for those following a ketogenic lifestyle. 

Compared to regular tortillas, keto tortillas offer a longer-lasting keto-friendly option, helping maintain ketosis and supporting weight loss goals. They're ideal for wraps, tacos, and other keto-friendly recipes requiring a tortilla base. 


### What makes a tortilla "keto-friendly"?

A keto tortilla must contain fewer than 5 grams of net carbs per serving to maintain its ketogenic properties. Net carbs are calculated by subtracting fiber from total carbohydrates, as fiber doesn't impact blood sugar levels. Look for tortillas with minimal added sugars and artificial ingredients. 

Ingredients like almond flour, coconut flour, or eggs form the base of most keto tortillas, with some versions incorporating other low-carb flours or fats like avocado oil or butter. These ingredients provide structure while keeping carbohydrate content extremely low. 


### How do keto tortillas compare to regular tortillas?

Regular flour tortillas typically contain between 60-70 grams of carbohydrates per serving, making them unsuitable for ketogenic diets. In contrast, keto tortillas range from 2-4 grams of net carbs per serving, allowing them to fit seamlessly into a ketogenic meal plan. 

The texture and flavor of keto tortillas can vary significantly between brands. Some have a slightly dense consistency similar to whole wheat tortillas, while others mimic the light, flexible texture of traditional flour tortillas. Flavors range from unflavored to herbal and cheese-inspired options. 


### Are keto tortillas safe for everyone?

Keto tortillas are generally safe for most people following a ketogenic diet, provided you don't have specific allergies or sensitivities to their ingredients. However, they may not be suitable for individuals with nut allergies, as many varieties contain almond flour. 

People with diabetes or other metabolic conditions should monitor their blood sugar levels when introducing any new food into their diet. For these individuals, keto tortillas can be an excellent choice but should still be consumed in moderation as part of a balanced meal plan. 


### Where can I find keto tortillas?

Keto tortillas are widely available in health food stores, natural food markets, and online retailers specializing in ketogenic products. You can also find them in some mainstream grocery stores, particularly those with dedicated health food sections.

### How should I store keto tortillas?

Most keto tortillas come in vacuum-sealed packages and should be stored in a cool, dry place after opening the package. They typically remain fresh for 4-6 weeks when stored properly. 

For optimal freshness, transfer opened tortillas to an airtight container with a paper towel to absorb excess moisture. Store them in the refrigerator for up to 1 week or freeze for longer storage. Thaw frozen tortillas at room temperature before use.
