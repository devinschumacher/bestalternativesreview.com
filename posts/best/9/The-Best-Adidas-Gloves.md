### What materials are used in Adidas gloves?

Adidas gloves incorporate a blend of materials tailored for specific uses. The primary material is usually a synthetic blend that offers durability and moisture resistance. For gloves designed for warmth, a polyester and acrylic blend provides excellent insulation while maintaining flexibility. 

Technical gloves use a mix of elastic spandex and moisture-wicking fabric like polypropylene to keep hands dry in active conditions. All materials undergo rigorous testing to meet Adidas' standards for performance and longevity. 


### How do I choose the right size Adidas glove?

Adidas recommends measuring your hand length and width to determine the correct glove size. The length should extend from your wrist to the tip of your middle finger, while the width should be measured across the palm of your hand at its fullest point. 

Most gloves come in half-size increments, so if your measurements fall between sizes, err on the side that offers more room for warmth and flexibility. Always try on gloves if possible to ensure proper fit.

### What maintenance tips do you recommend for Adidas gloves?

Hand wash your Adidas gloves with mild detergent in cold water to prevent color fading and fabric breakdown. Avoid using bleach or harsh chemicals that can damage the fabric's integrity. 

Avoid placing your gloves in direct sunlight or heat sources, as this can cause the materials to degrade. Store your gloves in a cool, dry place and consider using a glove bag to protect them during transport or storage. 


### How do I care for special materials in Adidas gloves?

Gloves with special treatments like water repellency or thermal insulation require a bit more care. After use, treat water-resistant gloves with a waterproofing spray to maintain their protective properties. 

For gloves with insulated linings, avoid machine drying as heat can damage the insulation. Allow them to air dry naturally instead. If the gloves use a moisture management system, regularly replace or wash the moisture-wicking layers to maintain performance.
