### Usage and Mixing

Milk powder can be prepared by mixing it with water or using it directly in recipes. The standard preparation ratio is typically 1 part milk powder to 4 parts water, though this may vary by product. Always mix thoroughly to avoid lumps, and consider using a warm water source to dissolve the powder more easily.

The product typically includes added nutrients such as vitamins and minerals to enhance its nutritional profile. It's widely used for breakfast cereals, baking, and as an alternative to fresh milk in various recipes. 


### What are the main ingredients in Nido Milk Powder?

The primary ingredient is skimmed milk powder, which forms the base of the product. Additional components include vitamin A palmitate, vitamin D3, vitamin C, vitamin B12, iron, calcium carbonate, and lutein. 

These ingredients are carefully selected to provide a balanced nutritional profile, including essential vitamins and minerals that may be lacking in certain diets. 


### How is Nido Milk Powder made?

The milk undergoes pasteurization to eliminate bacteria, followed by concentration through evaporation to remove water. The remaining milk solids are then spray-dried into fine particles to create the powder. 

The manufacturing process also includes standardized nutrient fortification to ensure consistent nutritional content across all batches.
