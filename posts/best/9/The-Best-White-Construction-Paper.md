### What is construction paper?

Construction paper is a thick, textured paper commonly used for crafting, arts and crafts projects, and school assignments. It's versatile, coming in various colors, thicknesses, and sizes. 

Unlike standard printer paper, construction paper holds its shape better when folded or cut, making it ideal for creating 3D models, collages, and other creative projects. 


### What is white construction paper?

White construction paper is similar to standard construction paper but specifically refers to the uncoated, white version, which is often more durable and less prone to tearing when cut or folded. 

It's commonly used for making paper airplanes, constructing simple models, and creating crafts that require a bit more heft than regular paper.

### What are the dimensions of standard white construction paper?

Standard white construction paper is typically available in 9x12 inches, although other sizes can be found. This dimension makes it suitable for most school projects and simple crafts. 

The weight usually ranges from 20 to 24 lb, providing a balance between thickness and ease of manipulation. 


### How thick is white construction paper?

White construction paper typically ranges from 20 to 24 lb in weight, which means it's slightly thicker than normal printer paper but less heavy than cardstock. 

This thickness makes it ideal for basic projects like coloring pages, simple foldables, and homework assignments that require a bit more durability than standard paper.

### What are the best uses for white construction paper?

White construction paper excels in projects that require stability when cut or folded, such as paper airplanes, simple 3D models, and basic origami projects. 

It's also excellent for coloring, drawing, and basic printing, offering a good surface quality for standard pens and markers. 


### Is white construction paper suitable for printing?

White construction paper can be used for printing, especially for basic documents and simple graphics. However, it's not ideal for high-quality printing due to its uneven surface and lower weight. 

For printing purposes, consider the weight and texture of the paper, as thicker, coated papers often produce better print quality.
