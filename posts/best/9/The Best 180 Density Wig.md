## FAQ


### What is 180 Density?

180 Density refers to the thickness of a wig, indicating that it contains 180 individual strands per square inch. This level of density produces a realistically natural look that closely resembles human hair. 

Wigs with 180 density typically weigh between 15-20 grams and require minimal to moderate maintenance. The increased number of strands makes them more versatile for styling and gives them better volume and texture compared to lower-density options. 


### Who should consider an 180 Density Wig?

180 density wigs are ideal for individuals who prefer natural-looking hair that feels very close to real human hair. This includes people with thinning hair, those undergoing chemotherapy, and those who want a longer-lasting alternative to daily styling. 

Regular hairdressers and salons often recommend 180 density wigs as they offer a balance of quality and realism. However, they may not be suitable for heavy styling or those who need wigs for extended periods due to their slightly higher maintenance requirements. 


### How do you style an 180 Density Wig?

These wigs can be styled using a wide range of techniques, including hot tools, curling irons, and straighteners. The increased hair volume means styling might take a bit longer, but the results mimic natural hair's texture and movement. 

Common styling techniques include layering, texturizing, and root lifting to create volume. Because of their density, 180 wigs often hold styling better than lower density options, making them suitable for both casual and formal occasions. 


### What's the difference between 180 and 190 Density?

While 180 density wigs have 180 strands per square inch, 190 density wigs contain 190 strands per square inch. The higher density in 190 wigs results in slightly more volume and texture but may be more challenging to style due to increased weight. 

The choice between 180 and 190 density often comes down to personal preference. Those who value natural-looking texture might prefer 180, while those seeking maximum volume might opt for 190. The main factor is ensuring the density matches your styling goals and comfort level. 


### How long does an 180 Density Wig typically last?

180 density wigs, when cared for properly, can last 2-3 years with regular use. Proper care includes occasional washing, proper storage, and avoiding heat styling tools without protection. The lifespan can vary based on usage frequency and care practices. 

Quality hair materials used in 180 density wigs typically last longer than cheaper alternatives. Premium materials resist tangling and breakage better, extending the wig's usable life and maintaining its appearance more consistently. 


### Are 180 Density Wigs suitable for daily wear?

These wigs are generally suitable for daily wear, especially with proper care. They maintain their shape well and can be styled for various occasions. However, they require regular maintenance and attention to detail to keep them looking their best. 

While realistic enough for daily wear, some users might prefer lighter options for extreme heat or humidity. The increased hair volume can make them feel slightly heavier than lower density wigs during extended wear.
