### How do Mushroom Lamps Work?

Mushroom Lamps contain specially cultivated mushroom colonies that produce light through a process called bioluminescence. When the mushroom mycelium (root-like structures) absorb nutrients, they emit a soft, natural light, similar to how fireflies produce light. 

The light produced by Mushroom Lamps varies throughout their lifecycle. Initially, the glow may be faint, but it increases as the mushrooms grow and develop their light-producing capabilities. This natural light cycle creates a dynamic and ever-changing illumination effect.
