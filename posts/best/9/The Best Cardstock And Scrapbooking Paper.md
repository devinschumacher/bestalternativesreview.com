## Buyer's Guide

Cardstock and scrapbooking paper offer distinct advantages for crafting projects, each with specific characteristics that affect their suitability for various applications. Understanding these differences helps in selecting materials that meet your creative needs while considering durability and print quality. 


### Material Quality and Durability

Cardstock typically offers better durability due to its thicker construction, making it more suitable for cardmaking and dimensional elements. Scrapbooking papers tend to be lighter weight, ideal for layouts but less resistant to folding and creasing. 


### Print Quality and Surface Texture

For photographic prints or detailed digital designs, the surface texture of cardstock can affect how the image appears. A smooth surface provides better print quality, while textured cardstock adds visual interest but may conceal fine details. Scrapbooking papers often have decorative patterns that enhance visual appeal but may not support high-quality photographic prints.
