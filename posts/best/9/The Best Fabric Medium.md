## FAQ

![Fabric-Medium-4](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Fabric-Medium-4/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What is a Fabric Medium?

A fabric medium is a substance that helps seal dyes to fabric, making the colors more vibrant and durable. It's typically applied between the fabric and dye, improving the quality of the finished product. 

There are several types of fabric mediums, including liquid, spray, and paste forms. Each has its own properties for specific dyeing techniques and fabric types. 


### How Do Fabric Mediums Work?

When a fabric medium is applied to fabric before dyeing, it helps the dye adhere more effectively. The medium can create a barrier that prevents the dye from bleeding or fading, while still allowing the color to penetrate the fabric. 

Many fabric mediums also act as a heat stabilizer, helping the dye maintain its color intensity during washing and wear. They can improve the overall quality of the dye job by making colors more vivid and the fabric more smooth. 

![Fabric-Medium-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Fabric-Medium-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What Fabric Medium Should I Use?

The best fabric medium depends on your specific project and the type of fabric you're working with. For most cotton fabrics, a basic liquid medium works well. Polyester and other synthetic fabrics often require specialized mediums to achieve good results. 

For natural fiber fabrics like wool and silk, look for mediums specifically designed for protein fibers. Always check product recommendations and label instructions for the best results. 


### How Do I Use Fabric Medium?

Most fabric mediums can be applied using a brush, roller, or sprayer, depending on the product and your comfort level. Apply a thin, even layer to the fabric before dyeing, or mix the medium with dye according to the product instructions. 

Always allow the medium to dry completely before applying additional layers of dye or heat-setting the fabric. This ensures proper adhesion and prevents color bleeding during subsequent steps. 

![Fabric-Medium-6](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Fabric-Medium-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Are Fabric Mediums Safe to Use?

Most commercial fabric mediums are considered safe for home use when handled properly. They typically contain water-soluble or heat-volatile ingredients that remove any residual chemicals during the dyeing process. 

It's important to follow safety guidelines, including wearing gloves and working in a well-ventilated area. Some mediums may contain strong chemicals, so always read and follow the manufacturer's safety instructions. 


### How Do I Clean Up After Using Fabric Medium?

Clean up with water immediately after application, as most fabric mediums are water-soluble. Dispose of brushes, rollers, or sprayers in a appropriate manner, according to local regulations. 

For disposal, follow local guidelines for household hazardous waste. Some municipalities have specific requirements for disposing of dye-related materials, so check your local regulations before disposing of any materials.
