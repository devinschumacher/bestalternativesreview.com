## FAQ


### What are the main features of the Taylormade Speedblade irons?

The Taylormade Speedblade irons combine a thin, fast-face design with a high moment of inertia (MOI) to deliver exceptional distance and forgiveness. The ultra-thin face angles up to 11 degrees across the sole, while the deep cup-faced cavity provides stability and energy transfer at impact. 

These irons incorporate a T9S+ stainless-steel face layer, tungsten weighting, and a lightweight frame to reduce hand weight while maintaining precision. The speed-instead-of-size design helps prevent fat shots and promotes consistent ball striking. 


### How do the Speedblade irons compare to Taylormade's previous iron models?

The Speedblade represents a significant technological advancement for Taylormade. Compared to previous models, the Speedblade features a more aggressive speed face design that's thinner by 20% (0.8mm), while maintaining better shape retention and consistency. 

The new frame structure is 40% lighter, which helps reduce hand and wrist stress during the swing. The face angle and sole design have also been optimized for improved turf interaction and better ball flight. 


### What are the key playing characteristics of the Speedblade irons?

The Speedblade offers excellent distance through its thin face design and efficient energy transfer. players report higher ball speeds and longer carry distances compared to their previous irons. The increased MOI provides better forgiveness on mis-hits, particularly from different lie angles. 

These irons excel in the fairway, with players noting improved ball speeds and consistent draw potential. The thin face and cup-face design maintain good ball shape, though some might find the feel less responsive compared to players' irons. 


### Which Speedblade iron model is best for my game?

The 1 model is ideal for players seeking maximum distance and speed, with a slightly more aggressive face shape. The 2 offers a balanced mix of distance and forgiveness, while the 3 provides the most versatility through its increased sole width and slightly lower MOI.
