## FAQ


### What are the key features of airplane headphones?

Designed specifically for in-flight entertainment, airplane headphones combine high-quality sound with essential flight amenities. Key features include noise cancellation, comfortable ear cushions, and secure mounting options to resist air turbulence. 

Modern airplane headphones often incorporate advanced audio technology, such as Bluetooth connectivity for wireless listening and built-in microphones for clear in-flight communication. 


### How do airplane headphones prevent sound leakage?

Most airplane headphones use closed-back designs that block ambient noise while keeping the sound inside the headphones. The ear cups are sealed to prevent sound from escaping, ensuring a private listening experience without disturbing other passengers. 

Some models feature acoustic dampening materials in the ear cushions that absorb sound, further reducing leakage and maintaining the audio quality for the wearer. 


### What's the difference between active and passive noise cancellation?

Passive noise cancellation occurs naturally through the physical design of the headphones, such as the closed-back construction and acoustic materials that block external sounds from reaching the ears. 

Active noise cancellation uses electronic components to detect ambient noise and generate counteractive sound waves. This advanced technology provides more effective noise reduction but requires power and specialized hardware. 


### Are airplane headphones suitable for long flights?

Many airplane headphones are specifically designed for extended use, with features like durable cables and padded ear cups that can provide comfort during long flights. Quality headphones can maintain their sound clarity even after hundreds of flights. 

For long trips, consider models with adjustable headbands and multiple cups to accommodate different head sizes, as this can significantly impact comfort during extended wear. 


### What should I look for in the cable design?

Look for headphones with reinforced cables that can handle frequent folding and twisting without breaking. Braided cables are common in travel-grade headphones as they resist tangling and damage during storage. 

Some models offer detachable cables or accessories that allow you to switch between different cables based on your needs, such as using a shorter cable for office use and a longer cable for travel. 


### How do I maintain my airplane headphones?

Regular maintenance includes wiping the ear cups and headband with a soft, damp cloth and allowing them to air dry completely before storing. Store the headphones in their case when not in use to protect them from dust and damage. 

Inspect the cables and connections regularly for wear and replace them if they show signs of fraying or damage. Always unplug the headphones before cleaning the audio drivers to prevent electrical shorts.
