The primary function of a Fuzz Buster is to maintain the appearance of furniture and clothing by removing loose, floating hair that can make surfaces appear unkempt. 


### How does a Fuzz Buster work?

Fuzz Busters operate by gently rubbing the fabric surface with their flexible grooming elements. This physical action dislodges and collects loose hair, pet fur, and other small particles. 

Most models feature replaceable brushes or combs that can be switched based on the surface being cleaned and the type of hair encountered. 


### What surfaces can a Fuzz Buster clean?

Fuzz Busters are effective on a wide range of surfaces including fabric upholstery, car seats, sofas, and even some types of carpeting. They work best on loosely woven fabrics and carpets with shorter piles. 

While they can be used on thicker fabrics, you may need to apply more pressure or use specialized attachments for deeper cleaning. 


### How often should I use a Fuzz Buster?

For daily use, Fuzz Busters should be employed weekly to remove newly deposited hair and prevent it from embedding into the fabric. High-traffic areas and homes with pets may require more frequent cleaning. 

During heavy use or seasonal changes when shedding increases, you may need to use the Fuzz Buster every few days. 


### What maintenance is required for a Fuzz Buster?

The grooming elements of a Fuzz Buster need regular cleaning to maintain their effectiveness. This typically involves shaking out loose hair after each use and removing any tangles.
