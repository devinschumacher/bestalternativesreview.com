### What is the difference between sterling silver and silver plate?

Sterling silver is an alloy consisting of 92.5% pure silver and 7.5% other metals, making it durable and long-lasting. Silver plate, on the other hand, consists of a thin layer of pure silver bonded to a base metal like nickel or copper. While sterling silver maintains its value and requires minimal maintenance, silver plate can scratch and tarnish more easily. 

Sterling silver items are usually marked with an "S" or ". 925" stamp, while silver plate items may be marked with "Sterling Plate" or "Silver Plate. " The quality and thickness of the silver plating can vary significantly between manufacturers. 


### How should silver plate chargers be cleaned?

The best way to clean silver plate chargers is using a soft cloth dampened with warm water and mild dish soap. Avoid harsh abrasives or bleach, as these can damage the plating. A solution of one part white vinegar to two parts water can also be used to remove tough stains or fingerprints. 

After cleaning, buff the chargers with a soft, clean cloth to remove any residue and restore their shine. Avoid stacking silver plate pieces together when not in use, as this can cause scratching. Store them separately or use anti-tarnish cloths to protect the surface.
