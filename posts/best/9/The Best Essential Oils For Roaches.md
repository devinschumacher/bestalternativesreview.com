### How should essential oils be safely used around pets and children?

Essential oils can be toxic to pets and harmful to children when ingested. Always use these products in well-ventilated areas and store them out of reach. Avoid applying directly to skin or using near sources of heat. 

When using essential oils in households with pets or children, it's best to create natural barriers rather than direct treatments. Place oil-soaked cloths at entry points or create misty sprays for air freshening. 


### What precautions should be taken when using essential oils?

Essential oils should never be used undiluted on skin or ingested. Always dilute them with a carrier oil like coconut or almond before topical application. The general rule is to keep concentrations below 3% in any application. 

Some oils can cause skin irritation, so perform patch tests on small areas before broader use. Additionally, avoid using oils near electronic equipment or in areas with open flames.
