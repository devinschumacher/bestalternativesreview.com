## FAQ


### What is Aquaphor?

Aquaphor is a topical skin care product known for its healing and protective properties. It's commonly used to treat various skin conditions, including sunburns, due to its soothing and moisturizing effects. 

The product features a unique combination of ingredients, including petrolatum, mineral oil, and various emollients, which work together to create a protective barrier on the skin while providing necessary moisture. 


### How effective is Aquaphor for sunburn relief?

Many people find Aquaphor effective for sunburn relief due to its cooling properties and ability to soothe irritated skin. It helps reduce pain and provides a protective layer to prevent further damage. 

However, it's important to note that while Aquaphor can provide relief, it should not be considered a substitute for proper sunburn treatment. Severe cases or burns requiring medical attention should be evaluated by a healthcare professional. 


### When should I use Aquaphor for sunburn?

Aquaphor can be used as soon as you notice sunburn symptoms, such as redness, pain, or warmth. It's particularly helpful when applied to mild to moderate sunburns after cooling the affected area. 

Avoid using Aquaphor on severe sunburns with blistering, as it might not be appropriate for your specific condition. Always consult with a healthcare provider if you're unsure about the severity of your sunburn. 


### How do I use Aquaphor for sunburn relief?

Clean and gently pat dry the sunburned area. Apply a thin layer of Aquaphor to the affected skin, avoiding breaks in the skin or blisters. Cover the treated area with a loose bandage if necessary. 

Reapply as needed, typically every 2-3 hours or more frequently if the area becomes wet or oily. Continue using Aquaphor until the sunburn has fully healed, usually 3-5 days for mild to moderate cases. 


### Can Aquaphor be used on sensitive skin?

Yes, Aquaphor is generally safe for use on sensitive skin and is commonly recommended for treating sensitive skin conditions. Its natural ingredients make it less likely to cause irritation compared to some over-the-counter treatments. 

However, if you have specific skin sensitivities or conditions, it's advisable to perform a patch test before widespread application to ensure compatibility. 


### Are there any potential side effects?

The most common side effect of using Aquaphor is that it can make the skin feel too greasy or oily, especially if applied in thick layers or left on for extended periods. This can be particularly noticeable on sunburned skin, which may already be producing more oil due to the inflammatory response. 

Some individuals may experience mild irritation, particularly if the product is applied to raw or blistered skin. If you notice persistent redness, itching, or worsening symptoms, discontinue use and consult a healthcare provider.
