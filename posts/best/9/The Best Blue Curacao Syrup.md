## Buyer's Guide

Blue Curacao syrup adds a vibrant, distinctive blue color and orange-scented flavor to mixed drinks. When selecting syrup, consider these key factors to find the right product for your needs. 


### Color Intensity

Blue Curacao color can range from light to dark blue. If you're using the syrup for an Instagram-worthy display, opt for a richer shade. Keep in mind that darker colors may also affect the drink's appearance. 


### Flavor Profile

Original Blue Curacao has a subtle orange flavor with undertones of citrus and sweetness. Consider whether you want a more pronounced citrus note or a smoother, sweeter syrup. 


### Sweetness Level

Blue Curacao syrups vary in sweetness. Some are designed for traditional recipes where they're mixed with spirits, while others are naturally sweeter for use in mocktails or cocktail dressings. 


### Storage Requirements

Since Blue Curacao contains alcohol, it has a longer shelf life and can be stored at room temperature. However, once opened, keep the bottle in a cool, dry place and use it within 6-8 months for best flavor. 


## FAQ


### What is Blue Curacao Syrup?

Blue Curacao syrup is a flavored syrup that combines the familiar orange flavor of traditional Curacao liqueur with a distinctive blue color. It's made from orange oil, sugar, and food coloring, creating a vibrant blue-hued syrup that's popular for both cooking and drinking applications. 

This versatile syrup is commonly used in baking, cocktails, and as a dessert topping. Its balanced sweetness and unique flavor make it a popular ingredient in both home cooking and professional bartending. 


### What makes Blue Curacao Syrup different from other blue-colored syrups?

While many blue-colored syrups use artificial food dyes, Blue Curacao syrup gets its distinctive color from natural orange oil. This gives it a slightly different shade and a slight citrus undertone that you won't find in artificially colored alternatives. 

The syrup's flavor profile is also distinct, featuring a robust orange flavor characteristic of traditional Curacao liqueur, rather than the sweeter, more artificial taste of some blue food colorings. 


### How should I store Blue Curacao Syrup?

For best results, store unopened Blue Curacao syrup in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Once opened, keep the bottle tightly sealed and refrigerate it to extend its shelf life. Proper storage helps maintain its color and ensures the syrup remains free from contamination. 

The syrup typically lasts for several months when properly stored. Check the bottle's expiration date and use it within the recommended timeframe for the best flavor and quality. 


### What are some recommended uses for Blue Curacao Syrup?

This versatile syrup works well in both sweet and savory applications. For desserts, try using it as a glaze for baked goods, a topping for ice cream, or an ingredient in homemade caramel.
