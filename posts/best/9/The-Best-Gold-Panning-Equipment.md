## FAQ


### What is gold panning equipment used for?

Gold panning equipment is designed to separate gold particles from sand and sediment in placer deposits. The primary tool is the gold pan, a shallow metal bowl with raised edges that allows water to circulate while retaining heavier gold particles. 

Additional tools include screens for sorting crushed material, sluices for processing larger amounts of dirt, and magnetized equipment to recover magnetic iron and magnetic gold nuggets found in certain deposits. 


### What materials are used to make quality gold pans?

Higher-quality gold pans are typically made from stainless steel, aluminum, or heavy-duty plastic. Stainless steel pans resist corrosion and retain shape, making them suitable for both freshwater and saltwater prospecting. 

Aluminum pans are lightweight and durable but may dent more easily. Plastic pans offer good value but might not withstand harsh environments as well as metal alternatives. 


### How can I tell if I've found gold in my pan?

Gold appears yellow, has a metallic luster, and does not react with acid when tested with nitric acid. True gold will remain unblemished, while other metals will show etching or color changes. 

Look for flakes, nuggets, or grains heavier than sand. Gold often collects in streambeds as small particles or within larger rocks, requiring careful examination of your pan's contents. 


### What should I consider when choosing a gold pan size?

Basket pans, measuring 10-15 inches in diameter, are versatile and suitable for most prospecting situations. They allow for thorough washing of streambed material while being manageable for travel and storage. 

Smaller pans (6-8 inches) are ideal for backpacking trips or when working in tight spaces. Larger pans catch more particles but require more water and space to wash effectively. 


### Can I use household tools instead of specialized equipment?

While basic gold panning can be done with household tools, specialized equipment enhances recovery rates and efficiency. Basic tools like sieves and magnetized bars can be homemade, but dedicated prospecting tools offer better performance and durability. 

Specialized equipment includes sluice boxes, dredges, and gravity separators that may require purchase due to their complexity and size. These tools significantly increase processing capacity and refine recovery techniques. 


### What safety precautions should I take when prospecting?

Always check local regulations before prospecting, as mining rights and environmental laws can vary greatly by region. Wear appropriate clothing and footwear, and carry basic first-aid supplies, water, and snacks. 

Notify someone of your plans and expected return time, especially when prospecting alone or in remote areas. Be aware of wildlife and weather conditions, and avoid working near steep banks or slippery terrain.
