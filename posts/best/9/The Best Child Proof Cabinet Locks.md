## Buyer's Guide

Childproof cabinet locks are an essential safety measure for households with young children. These locks prevent access to potentially hazardous items while ensuring convenient use for adults. When choosing cabinet locks, consider several key factors to find the right solution for your needs. 


### Lock Types

There are three primary types of cabinet locks: external, internal, and bi-directional. External locks mount on the outside of the cabinet door and are primarily decorative, while internal locks install on the inside of the door for a clean look. Bi-directional locks function as both types, securing both open and closed cabinet doors.

### Catch Compatibility

Ensure the lock's catch mechanism matches your cabinet's hardware. Common catch types include cam locks, ball catch locks, and L-shaped catches. Some locks feature universal catches that work with multiple hardware styles, making them more versatile for various cabinet configurations. 


### Tamper-Resistant Features

Look for locks with Tamper-Resistant Certification, which tests the lock's ability to prevent removal without damage. Tamper-evident locks also provide visual confirmation when the lock has been compromised. Choose a lock with a sturdy construction, such as stainless steel or reinforced plastic, to resist forced removal attempts.

### Installation and Compatibility

Consider the lock's compatibility with your cabinet's interior dimensions. Some locks come with adjustable mounting options, while others have fixed sizes. The lock's mounting depth should not exceed the cabinet's available space to ensure proper closure. Always check the manufacturer's specifications for correct installation guidelines.

### What types of locks are available for cabinet doors?

Common types include knob covers that flip down to create a child-proof button lock, slide-bar locks that require a specific movement to unlock, and barrel locks that replace the original cabinet handle. Some options have simple push-button mechanisms, while others use electronic keys or combinations. 

Consider factors like the thickness of your cabinet doors, the type of hardware you're replacing, and whether you need locks for both interior and exterior doors. Specialized locks for medicine cabinets and tall kitchen cabinets may also differ in design and functionality. 


### How do I install a child proof cabinet lock?

Installation typically requires basic tools like a screwdriver and can take about 15-30 minutes per door. Begin by removing the cabinet's existing hardware and cleaning the mounting surface. Align the lock mechanism with the holes in the cabinet and attach it with provided screws. Test the lock mechanism to ensure proper function before reattaching the cabinet door. 

Some locks have adjustable mechanisms that allow slight movement along the door surface. This can be helpful for ensuring consistent locking pressure and proper operation. Always follow the manufacturer's instructions for specific installation details.
