### What is Korean Rice Wine?

Korean Rice Wine, also known as makgeolli, is a traditional Korean alcoholic beverage made from rice, wheat, or barley. It has a unique and slightly sour taste due to the lactobacilli bacteria used in the fermentation process. It is commonly consumed as a social beverage during gatherings and celebrations. 

Makgeolli is traditionally unfiltered, giving it a milky appearance and a slight haze. It typically has a lower alcohol content compared to other alcoholic beverages, making it an excellent choice for those who are new to drinking or prefer something milder. Makgeolli is also rich in nutrients, making it a healthier option compared to other alcoholic beverages. 


### How is Korean Rice Wine made?

The production process of Korean Rice Wine involves several steps. First, the rice is washed and steamed to soften it. After that, it is mixed with nuruk, a type of powder made from fermented rice or barley. Nuruk contains natural enzymes that help break down the starch in the rice, making it easier for the lactobacilli bacteria to ferment the rice into alcohol. 

Next, the mixture is left to ferment for several days, during which the lactobacilli bacteria convert the sugars in the rice into alcohol.
