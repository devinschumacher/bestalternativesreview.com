### What sets a USB-A charger apart from a USB-C charger?

Both USB-A and USB-C chargers use USB ports for charging devices, but they differ in their connectors and data transfer capabilities. Rectangular-shaped USB-A connectors have been the standard for charging and data transfer until the advent of USB-C technology. Smaller, square-shaped USB-C connectors can transfer data at higher speeds than USB-A connectors.

While USB-A chargers can still be used to charge modern iPhones, using USB-C chargers is advantageous due to faster charging and data transfer speeds. Apple supports USB-C in newer iPhone models, such as the 11, 12, and 13. Thus, if you need to charge or transfer data between an iPhone and a computer or other device, opt for a USB-C charger for optimal results.


### Is it safe to use a wireless charger for an iPhone?

Wireless chargers are generally safe to use with iPhones when they are MFi (Made for iPhone) certified. These chargers adhere to Apple's safety standards and guidelines, ensuring compatibility with your iPhone and preventing damage or safety risks to its battery life. Always check for the MFi certification before buying a wireless charger for your iPhone.

To ensure safety and proper functionality, place your iPhone on the charger's surface correctly and follow the manufacturer's guidelines. Keep the charging surface away from moisture, water, or humid environments, and place it on a stable, flat surface to avoid electrical malfunctions or tipping over while charging your iPhone.
