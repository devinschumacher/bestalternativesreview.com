### Material

The primary material for a metal shovel is typically aluminum, stainless steel, or high-temp carbon steel. Each material has its advantages and disadvantages. Aluminum shovels are lightweight and corrosion-resistant, making them perfect for general-purpose use and sandy or rocky terrains. Stainless steel shovels are also lightweight, durable, and resistant to corrosion. However, they may be more expensive than aluminum models. High-temp carbon steel shovels are incredibly strong and durable but may be heavier and more expensive compared to aluminum and stainless steel models. 


### Size and Weight

Consider the size and weight of the metal shovel you'd like to purchase. If you need a shovel for smaller tasks or for traveling, a smaller, lighter aluminum or stainless steel model may be more suitable for you. For larger, more challenging tasks, a high-temp carbon steel shovel might be a better choice, even if it is heavier. Ensure the size and weight of the shovel are appropriate for your physical ability and the type of work you'll be doing. 


### Type

There are different types of metal shovels, including flat-edged, pointed-edged, or scooped. Each type has its specific use. Flat-edged shovels are great for digging, moving debris, and scooping wet materials. Pointed-edged shovels are excellent for cutting into hard surfaces and prying up roots or rocks. Scooped shovels excel at gathering and moving loose, dry materials like sand or gravel. Consider the type of work you'll be doing and choose a metal shovel that best suits your needs.

### How do I care for and maintain a metal shovel?

Proper maintenance of a metal shovel ensures it remains in good working condition for an extended period. Firstly, after each use, ensure to rinse and clean the shovel, removing any residue or debris. For thorough cleaning, use a mild detergent and warm water to remove any stubborn dirt or grime. Ensure you pat the shovel thoroughly dry to prevent rust from forming. 

Wiping down the shovel with a rust-inhibiting liquid or spray is also recommended. Store the shovel in a dry, clean space to keep rust and corrosion at bay. Always handle the shovel with care, avoiding harsh impact or banging against surfaces to prevent dents or damage. Additionally, periodically re-oil any metal parts to maintain a protective layer and minimize wear. 


### How do I store a metal shovel?

Storing a metal shovel correctly ensures its longevity and extends its useful life. Allow the shovel to air dry completely before storing, as moisture can lead to rust. If possible, store the shovel in a well-ventilated area to further reduce the risk of rust. Hang the shovel from a hook or support that can handle its weight, taking care not to stress the handle. Store the shovel horizontally if the handle cannot be easily hung for storage. Avoid stacking other tools or equipment on top of the shovel, as this can dent or damage the blade. 

If storage space is limited, consider collapsible or foldable shovel designs that can be stored in tight spaces or under bed. For larger shovels, you may need to consider a dedicated storage area or rack to ensure the proper storage and space requirements are met. Regularly check the shovel's condition to ensure it remains in good working order and address any concerns promptly.
