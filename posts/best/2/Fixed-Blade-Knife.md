### What's the distinction between a full tang and a partial tang fixed blade knife?

When discussing the tang, it refers to the portion of the blade that extends into the handle. The length of the tang can vary, either being full or partial, depending on the design of the knife. In a full tang blade, the entire length of the tang is utilized as the handle's material. As a result, it becomes a robust and sturdy knife, ideal for heavy-duty tasks. Due to its robustness and balance, full tang knives usually command a higher price tag. 

On the contrary, a partial tang knife has a blade that extends into the handle for a shorter distance. The handle, typically made of materials such as plastic, wood, or rubber, is designed to accommodate the partial tang. Partial tang knives are generally lighter and more affordable compared to full tang knives, yet they might not match the durability or balance of their full tang counterparts.
