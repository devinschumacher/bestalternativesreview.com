## FAQ


### Why are polarized lenses important for fishing sunglasses?

Polarized lenses are crucial for fishing sunglasses because they help reduce glare and enhance visual clarity. The polarization process blocks horizontal sunlight rays, thereby eliminating glare and reflective distractions that could hinder your ability to spot fish beneath the water surface. This allows you to see through the water more easily, spotting fish at different depths and making your fishing experience more enjoyable and productive. 

Additionally, polarized lenses provide 100% UV protection, protecting your eyes' health and comfort from harmful UV rays. This is especially important if you're outdoors for extended periods of time while fishing, as unprotected eyes can lead to various eye-related issues such as cataracts, macular degeneration, and skin cancer. 


### What makes fishing sunglasses differently from regular sunglasses?

While fishing sunglasses and regular sunglasses share the same purpose of providing UV protection and reducing glare, fishing sunglasses are specifically designed to address the challenges fishermen face on the water. These challenges include dealing with different lighting conditions (i. e. , bright sunlight reflecting off the water), the glare on the water, and the need for increased visual clarity to see underwater structures and spot fish. 

Fishing sunglasses also feature tinted lens colors that are tailored for specific lighting conditions, such as amber or copper lenses for low light situations, or gray or blue lenses for bright, sunny days. They often come with polarized or mirrored lenses, providing enhanced visual clarity and contrast. Additionally, fishing sunglasses are typically designed with wider lenses, which offer more coverage and protection against sunlight and wind, making fishing a more comfortable experience. 


### What is amber-tinted lens color good for in fishing?

Amber-tinted lenses are popular choices for fishing sunglasses, especially during low-light conditions or in the morning or evening hours. Amber lenses can enhance your view underwater by minimizing color distortion and increasing contrast, helping you spot fish more easily. This color enhances depth perception, making it easier to see the bottom of the water and navigate submerged features such as rocks and reefs. 

Overall, amber lenses are versatile and suitable for a wide range of fishing scenarios. However, they may not be ideal for bright, sunny days, as they can make it more challenging to see the water surface. In that case, you might want to consider using grey or blue lenses for better performance on brighter days where glaring sunlight is more prevalent. 


### What is the significance of mirrored lenses in fishing sunglasses?

Mirrored lenses are a type of tinted lens commonly used in fishing sunglasses that provide a unique visual appeal while offering practical benefits. Mirrored lenses have a thin, metallic coating applied to the lens surface, causing the sunglasses to appear less reflective from a distance, making it difficult for others to see the wearer's eyes. This feature offers a degree of privacy and allows the fisherman to avoid being detected by nearby fish. 

Beyond aesthetics, mirrored lenses also offer several functional advantages. They provide an extra layer of UV protection and can help reduce glare by diffusing sunlight. As a result, it's easier for the wearer to see beneath the water's surface and spot fish, increasing their chances of success on the water. However, it's essential to choose the right mirrored lens color for the lighting conditions you'll be fishing in. 


### What is the difference between polycarbonate and glass lenses when it comes to fishing sunglasses?

When choosing fishing sunglasses, there are two primary types of lenses available: polycarbonate and glass (or optical). The key difference between the two types lies in their materials, durability, weight, and impact resistance. Polycarbonate lenses are made from a thermoplastic material, making them lightweight, impact-resistant, and significantly more durable than glass lenses. This makes them ideal for the rugged conditions experienced by anglers and other outdoor enthusiasts. 

On the other hand, glass lenses may be heavier but offer sharper visual clarity and are typically priced lower compared to their polycarbonate counterparts. Additionally, glass lenses provide better scratch resistance, which can be beneficial if you're frequently handling your sunglasses while fishing. However, glass lenses may be more prone to cracking or breakage, so they may not be the best choice for those who require their sunglasses to be highly resistant to impact and durability.

When choosing the right fishing sunglasses, consider factors such as the primary fishing environment, the lighting conditions (including brightness and color temperature), the frame shape and size, and any additional features such as polarization, lens color, and lens material. Your personal preferences, budget, and eye comfort can also influence your decision. 

Some fishing sunglasses are specifically designed for freshwater or saltwater environments, high-glare situations, or to protect against airborne debris. Researching different models and reading reviews can help you determine the best sunglasses for your needs and budget. It's recommended to try them on in person, if possible, to ensure their comfort and fit.
