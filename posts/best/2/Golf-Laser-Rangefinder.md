### How do golf laser rangefinders work?

Golf laser rangefinders use laser technology to measure the distance from the golfer to the target. When the user presses the button, the device emits a laser beam that travels to the target. The beam is then reflected back to the rangefinder, where the device calculates the time it took for the signal to travel round trip. By knowing the speed of light, the rangefinder can accurately calculate the distance between the golfer and the target. 

There are two primary types of golf laser rangefinders - line of sight and slope-adjusted. Line of sight rangefinders provide a direct measurement to the target, while slope-adjusted rangefinders factor in elevation changes to provide a more accurate measurement. Both types offer benefits depending on the golfer's needs, with slope-adjusted rangefinders often being more helpful for players dealing with hills and undulating terrain.
