### What is the difference between single and double-sided sharpening stones?

A single-sided sharpening stone has a single, unidirectional grit, while a double-sided stone has two separate grits on opposite sides. Each side of a double-sided stone functions as a separate single-sided stone. When used individually, they sharpen the knife at a progressive angle, gradually refining the edge. 

If you only plan to sharpen a specific type of knife, a single-sided stone with the appropriate grit may suffice. Double-sided stones, however, offer more versatility for sharpening various types of knives and maintaining their sharpness over time. Additionally, when using a single-sided stone, it's necessary to ensure the other side is protected from contamination and damage. 


### Can a serrated blade be sharpened by a wheel sharpener?

For better results, consider using a bench grinder or honing stones specifically designed for sharpening serrated blades, such as diamond stones or fine-grit leather whetstones. 

Dedicated sharpeners, specifically designed for serrated blades, are more reliable and often have special abrasives that can successfully sharpen the serrations without damaging them. ### What is a ceramic sharpener?
