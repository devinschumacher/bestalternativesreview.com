### Ozeki Sake: History and Tradition

Established in 1837, Ozeki Brewery has a long and rich history of Sake production. The brewery is located in Nada, a region known for its ideal climate and water quality, providing the perfect environment for Sake production. Ozeki Sake is made using the traditional Japanese method, Yamahai, which involves a slow and labor-intensive process that results in a unique, full-bodied flavor profile.

### Careful Selection of Rice and Water

The quality of the rice used in Ozeki Sake production is paramount. High-quality Yamada Nishiki rice, also known as "superior grade" rice, is used, giving the Sake its sought-after smooth texture and delicate sweetness. Ozeki Sake also utilizes mineral-rich water from the Hakusan Mountains, enhancing the Sake's overall taste and aroma. 


### Variety of Flavors and Aromas

Ozeki Sake offers a wide range of flavors and aromas to suit different taste preferences. Their product line includes Junmai, Honjozo, and Ginjo Sake, each with its unique characteristics. Junmai Sake, made without any added alcohol, has a rich and full-bodied flavor. Honjozo Sake, with a small amount of alcohol added, has a clean and dry taste. Ginjo Sake, made with meticulously selected rice, results in a pure, elegant flavor. Choose the one that best fits your palate!

The Ozeki Sake is made from the finest Yamada Nishiki rice and pristine water from the Mount Fuji region, ensuring a unique and exquisite flavor profile.

### What makes Ozeki Sake special?

Ozeki Sake stands out in the competitive market due to its rigorous production process, which includes a unique "kuchikami" or mouth tasting method, where saké masters taste and adjust the fermentation process to achieve the perfect balance of flavors. This extra attention to detail results in a high-quality and sophisticated saké that is both smooth and complex in taste, with a subtle sweetness and a hint of umami. 

Additionally, Ozeki Sake is committed to sustainable practices, using local water resources from Mount Fuji and carefully sourcing its ingredients to protect the environment and preserve the region's rich culinary heritage. This dedication to quality, innovation, and respect for tradition sets Ozeki Sake apart in the world of saké.

### What types of Ozeki Sake are available?

Ozeki offers a diverse range of saké styles and grades to cater to various tastes and preferences. Some of the most popular options include Junmai, Junmai Ginjo, Ginjo, and Nigori. While the Junmai sakés are made with polished rice and no additional alcohol, Junmai Ginjo sakés retain more of the nutrients from the rice and are polished to a greater extent. Ginjo sakés have been aged for a shorter period, resulting in a lighter, more aromatic flavor profile. Finally, Nigori sakés feature a milky and cloudy appearance due to their unfiltered nature and offer a unique balance of sweet and savory flavors. 

Ozeki also produces seasonal and limited edition sakés, such as their "Daiginjo" and "Kin no Yaki" varieties, which are crafted from premium ingredients and showcase the skill and innovation of the Ozeki brewery. Be sure to explore their full lineup to discover the perfect Ozeki Sake for your next culinary adventure. 


### How should Ozeki Sake be served and paired?

Ozeki Sake should be served in a traditional Japanese saké cup, or "choko, " which allows for the proper appreciation of its aroma, temperature, and flavors. It is best enjoyed at a temperature between 50°F (10°C) and 59°F (15°C), depending on your preference. Some prefer their saké chilled, while others enjoy it slightly warmed. 

When it comes to pairing, Ozeki Sake is incredibly versatile. It can complement a wide range of dishes, from sushi and sashimi to grilled meats, seafood, and vegetarian dishes. For the best pairing, it is advisable to match the intensity of the saké with the richness of the food.
