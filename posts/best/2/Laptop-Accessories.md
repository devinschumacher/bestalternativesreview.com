### What is the difference between a USB-A and USB-C port in laptop accessories?

A USB-A port and a USB-C port are both universal serial bus (USB) connectors but have different shapes, sizes, and capabilities. The USB-A port is a standard rectangular plug connector, whereas a USB-C port is a newer, smaller, and more versatile connector in a rounded or oval shape. The main difference between the two lies in their data transfer rates and power delivery capabilities. 

For data transfer, a USB-C port enables faster transfer speeds when used with compatible USB-C devices or cables. It can transfer data at a rate of up to 10 Gbps, which is around 10 times faster than a standard USB-A port. The USB-C port can also be used for charging devices and can deliver up to 100 watts of power, making it capable of charging laptops and mobile devices quickly and efficiently.
