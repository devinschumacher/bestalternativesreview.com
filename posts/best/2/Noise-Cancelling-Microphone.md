### What is a noise-canceling microphone?

### How does a noise-canceling microphone work?

There are two primary methods of noise-cancellation in microphones: active noise cancellation (ANC) and passive noise cancellation. Though the technology behind these approaches varies, the outcome is the same - improved audio quality via the suppression of background noise and amplification of the main sound source.

In ANC-equipped microphones, the device has a built-in speaker that generates a sound wave with a unique pattern, known as an anti-noise wave. This anti-noise wave is designed to counteract the unwanted noise frequencies. The two sound waves - the noise wave and the anti-noise wave - are usually 180° out of phase with each other, resulting in a significant reduction of unwanted noise while preserving the main sound source.
