### Goose down vs. duck down - what's the difference?

<div><a href="https://serp.ly/@outrun/amazon/Goose+Down+Comforter"><img src="https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Goose-Down-Comforter-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black" alt="Goose-Down-Comforter-6" height="540"></a></div>

Both goose down and duck down are sourced from birds, known for their warmth and insulation properties. The primary difference between them lies in their origin and quality. Goose down is typically regarded as superior due to its larger down clusters, which can trap more air, resulting in better insulation and fillpower.

Additionally, goose down is often sourced from older, non-laying geese, offering a more plentiful and uniform distribution of down clusters compared to duck down, which comes from younger, more active birds. This results in smaller down clusters and a lower fillpower for duck down. As a result, goose down comforters tend to be more expensive than their duck down counterparts.

### How can you identify a high-quality goose down comforter?

To determine the quality of a goose down comforter, consider the fillpower, fill weight, and source of the down. Fillpower measures the amount of space a single ounce of down can occupy, reflecting its warmth and insulation properties. Higher fillpower numbers indicate superior quality.

Fill weight refers to the total amount of down used in the comforter, with higher fill weights resulting in warmer, more insulating comforters. Finally, the source of the down is crucial, with down sourced from older, non-laying geese or geese raised for meat often offering higher fillpower and better quality than down from young, active ducks.
