### How does a 10,000 BTU air conditioner work?

A 10,000 BTU air conditioner operates on a similar principle as most other air conditioners: removing heat from the air. The air conditioner draws air from the room through the intake fan, passing it over a series of cold coils filled with a refrigerant. As the air flows over these cold coils, heat is absorbed, and the refrigerant evaporates, becoming a gas. 

Once the refrigerant gas has absorbed sufficient heat, it moves to the compressor. In the compressor, the gas is compressed, increasing its pressure and temperature. The high-pressure gas then moves through the condenser coils, where it releases the collected heat to the outside air through a process called condensation. The now-cooled refrigerant gas returns to the evaporator coils, where the cycle begins anew.
