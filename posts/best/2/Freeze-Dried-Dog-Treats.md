## FAQ


### What are freeze-dried canine snacks?

Freeze-dried dog snacks are created by extracting moisture from the food using a process called sublimation. This process involves converting a solid directly into a gas, leaving behind a highly concentrated, lightweight, and nutrient-dense snack that dogs of all sizes can enjoy. Their popularity is on the rise due to their simplicity, safety, and convenience. 

Freeze-dried dog snacks are rich in nutrients and proteins, making them a healthier choice for dogs compared to traditional snacks. They can be used for training, rewarding good behavior, or offering a delicious treat that your dog will love. Additionally, they are easy to store and transport, making them perfect for active dog owners who are always on the go. 


### How are freeze-dried dog snacks produced?

The production process for freeze-dried dog snacks involves a technique known as sublimation. First, the food is frozen and then exposed to high-pressure gas, usually carbon dioxide, at low temperatures. The gas displaces the liquid water in the food, turning it into a solid ice-like substance. This process effectively eliminates the water content without affecting the food's texture or nutritional value. 

Once the moisture is removed from the food, it is placed in a vacuum chamber that absorbs the remaining moisture. The final product is a lightweight, crunchy, highly concentrated snack that retains most of the nutrients and flavors of the original ingredient. These snacks can be made from a variety of ingredients, such as meat, vegetables, and fruits, allowing pet owners to choose the perfect option based on their dog's dietary needs and preferences. 


### Can freeze-dried treats really prolong shelf life?

Yes, freeze-dried treats can significantly extend the shelf life of your dog's favorite snacks. The removal of moisture and air during the sublimation process creates a barrier against spoilage and microbial growth. The treats will typically maintain their quality for up to two years or more after production, depending on optimal storage conditions such as airtight packaging and a cool, dry environment. 

The extended shelf life is one of the main advantages of freeze-dried dog snacks. Since the treats do not require refrigeration, it's easier for you to keep a supply on hand for those last-minute training sessions or when you're on the go with your furry friend. Just remember to check the packaging dates, and you'll have a reliable source of snacks for your dog's needs. 


### Are freeze-dried treats suitable for all dogs?
