### What is the difference between 3528 and 5050 LED Strip Lights?

The main difference between 3528 and 5050 LED Strip Lights lies in the size and shape of the LED chips used. A 3528 LED Strip Light has a smaller LED chip (3.5mm x 2.8mm) and a lower power rating, typically between 1.2w and 6w per meter. These strips are commonly used for low-power applications and have a lower lumen output per meter. 

On the other hand, a 5050 LED Strip Light features a larger LED chip (5mm x 3mm) and a higher power rating, usually between 3w and 12w per meter. These strips produce more light per meter and are often used for higher-powered applications, such as spotlights and accent lighting. The increased size of the LED chip also results in a more consistent light output and a longer lifespan.
