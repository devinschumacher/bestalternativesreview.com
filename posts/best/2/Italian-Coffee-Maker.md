### What is the difference between an espresso machine and a moka pot?

Espresso machines and moka pots are both Italian coffee makers that use the same brewing principle, but they differ in their design and functionality. Both devices force hot water through finely ground coffee beans under pressure to produce a rich, full-bodied espresso or moka coffee. However, they differ in how they generate and control the pressure. 

Espresso machines use a pump or a steam pressure to force water through the coffee grounds, while moka pots rely solely on the air pressure created by boiling water. This means that espresso machines produce a more consistent and controlled espresso, with a creamier texture, while moka pots offer a stronger and more robust coffee taste. Additionally, espresso machines come in various types, including stovetop and electric, while moka pots are generally stovetop-only, making them easier to use and clean.
