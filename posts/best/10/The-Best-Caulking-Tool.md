## FAQ


### What is the difference between a caulk gun and a caulk applicator?

A caulk gun is a mechanical tool that feeds caulk from a tube through a nozzle, while a caulk applicator is a manual tool with interchangeable tips that apply caulk directly from a tube or pot. 

Caulk guns are better for professional or large-scale applications requiring consistent pressure, while caulk applicators offer more control for detailed work in tight spaces. 


### How do I choose the right nozzle for my caulk gun?

First, determine the application type (general, window, or decorative). The most common nozzle sizes range from 1/4" to 3/8", with smaller sizes providing more precise application but reduced caulk flow. 

Consider the texture of your surface and the joint width - narrower joints typically require smaller nozzles, while wider joints need larger ones for proper bead formation. 


### What's the best way to maintain a caulk gun?

After use, remove any remaining caulk with a suitable caulk cleaning solution. Do not use mineral spirits, as they can damage the gun's interior. 

Store your caulk gun in a clean, dry environment with the nozzle end elevated slightly to prevent caulk from sitting in the trigger mechanism. 


### Can I use an old caulk gun for new caulk?

For best results, yes - you can reuse your caulk gun with different types of caulk, including latex, butyl rubber, and polyurethane. However, thoroughly clean the gun first to prevent mixing of components. 

If you're switching between one-component and two-component caulk systems, clean the gun with manufacturer-approved cleaners before reuse. 


### What are the benefits of a caulk applicator?

Caulk applicators offer superior control over caulk application, making them ideal for detailed work around windows, doors, and decorative elements. They are particularly effective for achieving smooth, consistent bead formations in tight spaces. 

These tools are also more versatile, allowing the use of different tip sizes and types (straight, round, or angled) for various application needs without the need for multiple gun systems. 


### How do I properly seal electrical outlets with caulk?

Use a weather-resistant, silicone caulk specifically designed for electrical applications. Apply the caulk in a thin, continuous bead around the outlet cover, ensuring complete coverage of the gaps. 

After application, allow the caulk to cure according to the manufacturer's instructions before reinstalling the outlet cover. This prevents moisture infiltration and ensures proper electrical function.
