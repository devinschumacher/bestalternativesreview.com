## FAQ


### What is creatine?

Create a high-energy compound found in all cells, particularly in muscle tissue. It helps supply energy to muscles during short bursts of intense activity, such as weightlifting or sprinting. Creatine also plays a role in muscle growth and repair. 

The human body naturally produces small amounts of creatine, primarily in the liver, kidneys, and pancreas. It's also obtained through dietary sources, mainly red meat and fish. 


### What are the benefits of Six Star Creatine?

Research shows that Six Star Creatine can improve performance in high-intensity activities, including strength training and sprinting. Users often report increased muscle mass, reduced fatigue, and improved recovery times. 

Many people also experience enhanced workout intensity and longer-lasting energy during training sessions. The specific benefits may vary based on individual factors and usage


### How does Six Star Creatine work?

When taken orally, Six Star Creatine is absorbed into muscle cells, where it's converted to creatine phosphate. This form of creatine rapidly releases energy to power muscle contractions during intense exercise. 

As creatine levels increase in muscle cells, they help maintain proper pH balance and reduce the buildup of waste products like lactic acid, allowing for more intense and longer workouts. 


### What are the potential side effects of Six Star Creatine?

The most common side effect is gastrointestinal distress, including stomach cramps and diarrhea, especially when starting to use creatine. These symptoms usually resolve within a few weeks as the body adjusts. 

Rare cases may experience dehydration, especially during intense training sessions. It's important to stay well-hydrated and consider reducing training intensity during the initial weeks of use. 


### Who should avoid Six Star Creatine?

People with severe kidney disease or kidney failure should avoid creatine supplementation without medical supervision. Some medications may interact with creatine, so it's important to consult a healthcare provider first. 

Individuals with diabetes, liver disease, or hormonal imbalances should also exercise caution and seek medical advice before using creatine. 


### What are the recommended dosages for Six Star Creatine?

The most common loading phase dose is 20 grams per day for 5-7 days, split into four 5-gram servings. After the loading phase, maintenance doses typically range from 3-5 grams per day. 

For optimal results, take creatine with water and a carbohydrate-rich meal to enhance absorption. Consult the specific product instructions for the recommended dosage.
