### What is a cordless jump rope?

A cordless jump rope consists of a handle at each end connected by a flexible shaft, with a handle grip and a weight at the end that creates the round shape of the rope. Unlike traditional jump ropes, it features electronic components that power LED lights and track performance metrics without the need for cables. 

The rope's core material is usually PVC or TPR (Thermoplastic Rubber), while the handles are made from plastic or rubber for grip. LED lights are located around the rope's circumference or in the handles, and sensors measure the user's rotations per minute (RPM), jump count, and other metrics. 


### How do cordless jump ropes measure performance?

These jump ropes use built-in sensors to track rotational speed and count jumps. The LED light displays current RPM, accumulates jump count, and may show workout duration and calories burned through integrated algorithms. 

Sensors typically measure in 1-2 RPM increments and update data every 0.5-1 second. Advanced models can sync with mobile apps for detailed analytics, including jump height analysis and workout history.

### How long do cordless jump rope batteries last?

### Can cordless jump ropes be used outside?

Many cordless jump ropes are designed for indoor use due to their LED displays and smaller size. However, some premium models feature outdoor-friendly construction with waterproof handles and durable materials, suitable for light rain or dew. 

For outdoor use in cold weather, look for ropes with cold-resistant materials and built-in heaters for the handles. Ensure the rope's battery can withstand temperature extremes, and consider models with extra-long handles for outdoor jumps. 


### What maintenance is required for cordless jump ropes?

Regular maintenance includes cleaning the rope and handles with a soft cloth and mild soap, checking the battery level, and updating the display settings. Battery should be charged before first use and maintained within 20% charge to prevent damage.
