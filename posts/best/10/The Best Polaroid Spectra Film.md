### What is Polaroid Spectra Film?

Spectra Film from Polaroid offers vibrant, high-resolution color prints with exceptional sharpness. It's designed for both instant and traditional cameras, delivering professional-quality photos with a glossy finish. 

The film uses a proprietary process that combines fine granular emulsion with enhanced color pigments, resulting in rich, saturated colors and improved contrast compared to standard chromo films. 


### What camera formats does Polaroid Spectra Film support?

Spectra Film is available for Polaroid i-Type instant cameras, as well as traditional 35mm SLR and point-and-shoot cameras. The film comes in widths of 60mm (3x4 inch prints) and 90mm (3x6 inch prints). 

For i-Type cameras, you'll need to purchase the dedicated Spectra Film packs, which contain processing chemistry and printing materials. Traditional cameras require separate film and processing supplies.

### What are the key technical specifications of Spectra Film?

Spectra Film operates at ISO 800 and produces prints with a resolution of 3480 x 2296 pixels. The film supports print sizes up to 3x4 inches for 60mm, and 3x6 inches for 90mm. Each roll contains 10 individual photos. 

The film requires specific processing conditions: a processing temperature of 77°F (25°C) and a processing time of 42 seconds. Using the proper chemistry and equipment ensures the best results. 


### What is the color gamut of Spectra Film?

Spectra Film has an extended color gamut with 95% coverage of the sRGB color space, delivering vivid, lifelike colors. The film particularly excels in capturing greens, reds, and skin tones. 

Each color layer in the film formulation is optimized for maximum color saturation while maintaining excellent shadow detail. This combination results in prints that compare favorably to digital photography.

### What is the difference between Spectra Film and other Polaroid films?

Spectra Film uses a more advanced 4-layer color process and fine grain emulsion compared to older Polaroid chromo films. This results in richer colors, higher resolution, and better contrast. 

The film's processing requirements are also more stringent, demanding exact temperature and time control. This can impact home processing convenience but delivers superior professional results. 


### How long does Spectra Film remain viable after exposure?

Spectra Film prints should be developed within 24 hours of exposure for best results. Prints developed immediately after exposure typically show minimal color shift. 

After 24 hours, slight color changes may occur, particularly in the blue tones. Most prints retain their original color appearance for several weeks when stored properly.
