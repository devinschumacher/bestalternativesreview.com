## Buyer's Guide

The All-Clad roasting pan represents a significant investment in your culinary toolkit, combining premium materials with sophisticated designs. Understanding its features will help you select the right model for your needs and cooking style. 


### Construction and Materials

All-Clad roasting pans typically feature their signature three-layer stainless steel construction, with aluminum at the core and stainless steel outer and bottom layers. This configuration provides excellent heat distribution while maintaining durability. Some models incorporate stainless steel throughout for added longevity. 


### Size and Capacity

Standard roasting pan sizes range from 5 to 13 quarts, with wider pans offering less height for taller roasts and deeper pans providing more oven stability. Consider your typical meal sizes and roast dimensions when selecting a capacity. 


### Features and Accessories

Modern All-Clad roasting pans often include features like wire racks, glazing on cooking surfaces, and specialized handles. The wire rack allows for even cooking with direct heat exposure, while glazing prevents metal-to-metal contact during placement. Handle designs range from straight to curved, with some models incorporating cooling features for safer transport. 


### Maintenance and Cleaning

Proper care extends the life of your All-Clad roasting pan. Use stainless steel-specific cleaners and avoid abrasive materials that can scratch the surface. Hand washing with mild soap and warm water is recommended for optimal results. Regular cleaning and appropriate storage will maintain the pan's performance and appearance. 


## FAQ


### Are All Clad roasting pans oven-safe?

Yes, All Clad roasting pans are oven-safe. They can be used in the oven at temperatures up to 500°F (260°C), making them suitable for roasting meats, vegetables, and other dishes. 

The pans' construction, which combines aluminum with stainless steel, ensures even heat distribution and prevents hotspots that could damage delicate foods or cause uneven cooking.
