## Buyer's Guide

When selecting a cobra putter, several key aspects determine performance and comfort. The **putter design** matters significantly, as it shapes how you address the ball and strike it. Traditional versus non-traditional designs differ in their visual appeal and how they align your eyes with the ball. 


### Weight and Balance

Putter weight influences feel and control. Most golfers prefer between 330-350 grams, though heavier options can promote more consistent strokes. Pay attention to the **balance point**, which should align with your body's center of gravity for natural stroke mechanics. 


### Face Cup Technology

Modern cobras incorporate face cup technology to increase ball speed and forgiveness. The **depth and shape of the face cup** affect how it impacts the ball. Deeper cups generally provide more distance, while shallower designs promote consistency and straighter rolls. 


### Materials and Construction

The putter's materials determine its resonance and feel. **Maraging steel** and stainless steel typically offer excellent durability and consistent feedback. Carbon fiber composite materials can provide unique sensations but may vary in feel between manufacturers. 


### Shaft Selection

The shaft affects ball speed and feedback. Standard **putter shafts** range in flex from R (regular) to S (stiff), with most golfers preferring R or slightly firmer. The shaft diameter should match your grip size and strength for optimal control.
