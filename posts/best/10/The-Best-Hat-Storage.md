### What types of hats are suitable for storage?

Hats made from materials like wool, felt, and straw can develop flat spots or lose their shape when stored improperly. Opt for hats with sturdy, well-defined crowns and straight brims to ensure proper storage. 

Leather and fur hats require special care. Always have them professionally lined and stored in fur-specific containers to prevent damage from moisture and pests. 


### What are the benefits of using hat blocks?

Hat blocks maintain the original shape of your hats by providing a mold-like structure. They're particularly beneficial for felt and wool hats that might crush or develop creases when stored folded. 

Blocks made from materials like plastic or foam retain the hat's shape while allowing ventilation, which helps prevent moisture buildup and mold growth.

### How should I store hats to prevent dust accumulation?

Use dust covers specifically designed for hats. These protect the surface of the hat while allowing air circulation, which helps prevent moisture-related damage. 

Consider storing hats in sealed containers with desiccant packets to absorb excess moisture and protect the interior of the hat from dust and debris. 


### What size storage container is appropriate for a collection of hats?

Measure the diameter of your largest hat plus at least 4 inches for ventilation space. Add 6 inches to the height of the tallest hat for the container height. 

If you're storing multiple hats, opt for a container with adjustable dividers to prevent hats from shifting during storage and potential damage from abrasion.

### How often should hats be aired out?

Air out hats every 3-4 months, especially if stored in humid environments. Remove the hat from its storage container and place it in a well-ventilated area for 24-48 hours. 

After airing out, check the hat for any signs of mold, pests, or changes in shape. If any issues are detected, address them immediately to prevent further damage.
