## FAQ


### What types of materials can gold utensils scratch or damage?

Gold utensils, while highly resistant to scratches, can be damaged or scratched by harder metals like stainless steel, silver, or other base metals. These utensils may also be vulnerable to scratches from acidic foods and abrasive cleaning products. 

The surface of gold utensils can be compromised by harsh abrasives, so it's important to use appropriate cleaning tools and avoid rough handling to maintain their integrity. 


### How should gold utensils be cleaned to maintain their quality?

Regular cleaning with a soft cloth and mild soap is recommended. For deeper cleaning, solutions specifically designed for fine metal utensils can be used. Avoid harsh abrasives and acidic cleaners that may damage the gold surface. 

After cleaning, dry the utensils thoroughly to prevent water spots and ensure they maintain their shine. 


### What's the best way to store gold utensils?

Store gold utensils in a soft-lined jewelry box or pouch to prevent scratches from other metals or objects. Avoid stacking utensils together, as this can damage both the gold pieces and any accompanying silver or stainless steel utensils. 

Regular inspection for any signs of damage or scratches is recommended, especially if they're stored with other metal utensils or in high-traffic areas. 


### How can you tell if a utensil is made of solid gold?

Look for clear markings or stamps indicating the gold content, such as 14K, 18K, or 24K. Solid gold utensils should bear these markings, and you can verify authenticity through a gold-testing kit or professional appraisal. 

Professional jewelers often use specialized tools to assess the gold content of items, providing accurate certification of the utensil's quality. 


### Can gold utensils be resized or repaired?

Most gold utensils can be resized or repaired by a professional jeweler. However, very thin or damaged utensils may not be restorable without compromising their integrity. 

Regular inspection by a jeweler can help identify potential issues before they become major problems, ensuring your gold utensils remain in top condition. 


### What's the difference between gold-plated and solid gold utensils?

Gold-plated utensils have a thin layer of gold applied to a base metal, typically brass or silver. While they can be affordable, this plating can wear off over time with regular use. 

Solid gold utensils are made entirely of gold, with the gold content indicated by stamps or markings. These utensils maintain their value better but are also more expensive.
