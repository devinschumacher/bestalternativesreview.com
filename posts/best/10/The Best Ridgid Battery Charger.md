## FAQ


### What battery types does the Ridgid charger support?

The Ridgid charger is designed to work with their own line of batteries, specifically 12V and 18V Lithium-Ion batteries used in their power tools. 

It's important to note that while the charger can handle both voltage ratings, you should only connect the appropriate battery type to avoid damage to the charger or battery. 


### How long does it take to fully charge a battery?

Full charge times vary by battery capacity, but most Ridgid batteries will charge to 80% in about 30 minutes. The final 20% will take longer, typically between 1 to 3 hours depending on the model. 

For optimal performance and battery health, it's recommended to perform a full charge cycle - including the final 20% - every 3-4 months even if the battery appears fully charged. 


### Can the charger be used in different environments?

The charger is rated for use in standard indoor and outdoor environments, including workshops and construction sites. It's not recommended for use in extreme temperature conditions beyond the operating range specified in the manual. 

When using the charger outdoors, ensure it's placed on a stable, dry surface to prevent tipping or damage. Avoid using it in direct rain or where water may splash onto the unit. 


### What is the warranty coverage for the Ridgid charger?

The charger typically carries a two-year warranty against manufacturing defects. This means if the charger fails due to a manufacturing defect within the first two years, Ridgid will repair or replace it at no cost. 

Extended warranties are available, and Ridgid also offers protective services that cover accidental damage and loss. Be sure to review these options if you're concerned about additional coverage beyond the basic warranty period. 


### How do I properly store a fully charged battery?

For best performance, store fully charged batteries in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat sources. Ideally, maintain a storage temperature between 68°F and 77°F (20°C to 25°C). 

Before storing, run the battery until it reaches 40% charge. This helps extend battery life and ensures the charger is working correctly. Store the battery in the charger or in a protective case to prevent short circuits. 


### Can I use the charger while the battery is hot from recent use?

It's generally safe to charge a battery immediately after use, but allow it to cool to room temperature first if it was recently in heavy use. This ensures more consistent charging and helps prevent overheating issues. 

During the initial charge cycle after use, the battery and charger may become warm. This is normal, but avoid touching the battery terminals or charging cables while the charger is operating.
