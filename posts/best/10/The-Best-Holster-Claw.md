Check the manufacturer's specifications for maximum weight capacity to ensure the claw can support your intended items without risk of failure. The weight rating determines how much load the claw can safely bear, protecting both the item being held and the mounting surface.

### Mounting Options

Holster claws require a sturdy mounting point, so consider the material and thickness of your wall or mounting surface. Some claws feature adjustable mounting feet or expansion anchors for versatility in mounting options. Understanding these capabilities helps in properly securing the claw to prevent accidental dislodgement. 


### Length and Reach

The effective reach of a holster claw depends on its length from the mounting point to the tip. Longer claws provide greater reach but may be more difficult to mount securely. Consider whether you need a claw that extends farther from the wall or if a shorter option with a wider base would be more appropriate for your space.

## FAQ

![Holster-Claw-4](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Holster-Claw-4/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What is a holster claw?

A holster claw, also known as a holster hook, is a specialized tool designed to securely hold firearm holsters in place. It features a curved, claw-like shape that screws into drywall or similar surfaces. 

These hooks come in different specifications to accommodate various holster sizes and weights, ensuring proper weight distribution and preventing sagging or separation. 


### What materials can I use a holster claw with?

Holster claws are compatible with belt holsters, crossdraw holsters, appendix holsters, and shoulder holsters that attach via spring clips or velcro. 

They work effectively with both leather and polymer holsters, providing a reliable attachment point for both concealed carry and open carry setups. 

![Holster-Claw-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Holster-Claw-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### How do I install a holster claw?

Measure the distance between your mounting points and select the appropriate size claw. Use a wall anchor that matches the claw's specifications and a suitable drill bit. 

Screw the claw into the wall, ensuring it's securely fastened. Align your holster and test its weight distribution before securing the clip or attachment point. 


### What maintenance do holster claws require?

Regularly check screws and anchors to ensure they remain tight and secure. Dust and debris should be cleaned from the mounting points annually. 

Inspect the claws for any signs of wear or corrosion, especially if exposed to moisture or harsh environmental conditions. 

![Holster-Claw-6](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Holster-Claw-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Can I use holster claws for anything other than holsters?

Holster claws are primarily designed for firearm holsters but can be used for similar items like camera holsters, tool pouches, or any gear that requires wall-mounted storage. 

Ensure the weight and size of the item match the claw's specifications to prevent damage to the mounting system or item itself. 


### What are the main considerations when choosing a holster claw?

Key factors include wall material, holster weight, and mounting requirements. Choose a claw that matches your specific needs for size, material compatibility, and weight capacity. 

Consider the mounting point's accessibility, especially if you plan to attach multiple items or need quick access to your holster.
