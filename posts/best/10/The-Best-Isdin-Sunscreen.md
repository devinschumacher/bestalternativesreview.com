## Buyer's Guide

Protecting your skin from the sun's harmful rays is crucial, and the right sunscreen can make all the difference. When choosing an Isdin sunscreen, consider the following key factors to find the product that best suits your needs. 


### Sun Protection Factor (SPF)

SPF indicates a product's ability to screen out UVB rays, which cause sunburn. Higher SPF numbers offer better protection, but no sunscreen can block 100% of UVB rays. For most daily activities, an SPF 30 provides adequate protection. For extended outdoor exposure, consider products with SPF 50 or higher. 


### UVA Protection

UVA rays penetrate deeper into the skin and contribute to aging and skin cancer. Look for sunscreens that offer broad-spectrum protection, which shields against both UVA and UVB rays. Check if the product includes ingredients like avobenzone, titanium dioxide, or zinc oxide, which are effective UVA blockers. 


### Water Resistance

Sweat and water can wash away sunscreen, so water resistance is important, especially for outdoor activities. If you'll be swimming or sweating heavily, choose a product labeled "very water resistant" or "water resistant for 80 minutes. " Remember to reapply sunscreen after getting wet or towel drying. 


### Skin Type and Sensitivity

Your skin type and any existing conditions should influence your choice. People with sensitive skin should opt for hypoallergenic, fragrance-free products to reduce the risk of irritation. Oil-free formulas are suitable for combination or oily skin, while thicker creams work well for dry or eczema-prone areas.
