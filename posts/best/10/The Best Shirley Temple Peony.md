### What is a Shirley Temple peony?

A Shirley Temple peony is a popular hybrid peony variety that features large, double flowers with soft pastel colors, typically in shades of pink and white. The blooms resemble Shirley Temple's famous bobbed haircut, hence the name. 

The exact color range can vary from soft blush to palest pink, with occasional white blooms, making it a versatile addition to any garden or bouquet. 


### When do Shirley Temple peonies bloom?

Shirley Temple peonies typically begin blooming in mid to late spring, usually between late April and early June, depending on your climate zone. 

The blooming period lasts about one week, followed by a few weeks of vegetative growth before they become dormant in summer. 

![Shirley-Temple-Peony-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Shirley-Temple-Peony-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### How do Shirley Temple peonies grow?

These peonies prefer full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil that's rich in organic matter. They thrive in USDA hardiness zones 3-8. 

Plant them 2-3 feet apart with the eyes (bud areas) facing up, at a depth of two to three times their root ball size. 


### What are the maintenance requirements?

Water regularly during dry periods, especially when flowering, but avoid overwatering to prevent root diseases. 

Deadhead spent blooms to encourage more flowers, and prune back foliage in late fall after it has turned brown.

### Are Shirley Temple peonies deer resistant?

While deer may nibble on peony foliage if other food sources are unavailable, Shirley Temple peonies are generally considered deer-resistant. 

However, in areas with high deer populations, they might still be vulnerable. Consider using deer repellents or fencing for added protection. 


### How long do Shirley Temple peonies live?

With proper care, Shirley Temple peonies can live for 20 years or more, blooming reliably in the garden and cut flowers. 

They may take 3-4 years to establish after planting before producing their first blooms.
