### What is a crease protector?

A crease protector is a specialized sheet material designed to prevent folding and creasing in items like photographs, prints, and documents. These protective sheets can be inserted between paper layers to prevent direct contact that causes creasing. 

They work by creating a buffer between paper layers, reducing friction and pressure that causes folding. Crease protectors come in various materials and thicknesses, with options for both single and multiple sheet protection. 


### How do crease protectors prevent damage?

Creatively-designed crease protectors absorb pressure and distribute force evenly between paper layers. This prevents deep creasing that can occur when paper layers fold directly against each other. 

Many protectors include protective films or coatings that reduce friction between paper surfaces, further minimizing the risk of creasing and damage.

### What options are available for different materials?

Creative manufacturers offer crease protectors for various materials, including photo paper, acid-free documents, and fine art prints. Some protectors are suitable for both single sheets and multiple-page documents. 

Special coatings and materials are available for moisture-sensitive items like watercolor paintings and digital prints, ensuring safe protection without affecting material integrity. 


### Can crease protectors be reused multiple times?

Quality crease protectors made from durable, non-stick materials can be reused multiple times when proper handling techniques are used. However, repeated use may eventually lead to wear and loss of protective properties. 

For best results, consider purchasing protectors in packs to allow rotation between documents, extending their useful lifespan and maintaining optimal protection.

Creative protectors come in various sizes to match common print and document dimensions. Standard options include sizes for standard paper (8.5" x 11"), photo prints (4" x 6" and 5" x 7"), and larger art prints.
