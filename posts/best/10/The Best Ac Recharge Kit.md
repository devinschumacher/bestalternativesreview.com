## FAQ


### What is an AC recharge kit?

AC recharge kits are specialized tools designed to recharge automotive air conditioning systems. These kits typically include a refrigerant recovery/compression unit, a manifold gauge set, and adapters for various AC systems. 

Using these kits, users can add or recover refrigerant, check system pressures, and diagnose leaks in AC systems. 


### What refrigerants do AC recharge kits support?

Most modern AC recharge kits support R134a refrigerant, which is compatible with most passenger vehicles manufactured since 1994. Some kits also include adapters for R12, an older refrigerant, though its use is declining. 

Check your vehicle's owner manual to confirm the correct refrigerant type before purchasing a kit. 


### How do I know if my AC needs recharging?

Signs your AC needs recharging include reduced cooling performance, warm air from the vents, or ice formation on the evaporator core. A hissing sound or low pressure readings when checking system pressure are also indicators. 

It's important to note that these symptoms can also indicate other issues, so professional diagnosis is recommended. 


### Can I recharge my AC myself?

Recharging an AC system safely requires proper equipment and knowledge. Some kits include built-in safety features, but working with refrigerants and electrical systems can be dangerous if not done correctly. 

If you're unfamiliar with AC systems, consider having a professional service your vehicle.

### How long do AC recharge kits last?
