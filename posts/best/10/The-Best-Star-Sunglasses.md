Star sunglasses combine style with practicality, protecting your eyes while enhancing your appearance. When choosing the right pair, consider several key factors to find sunglasses that suit your needs and preferences. 


### UV Protection

Sunglasses should block 99-100% of both UVA and UVB rays to protect your eyes from harmful solar radiation. Look for lenses with UV protection ratings clearly stated on the packaging.

### Lens Material and Construction

Modern sunglass lenses are typically made from polycarbonate, Trivex, or glass. Polycarbonate and Trivex offer excellent impact resistance at a lighter weight, while glass provides better optical clarity. 

Consider the frame material as well, with lightweight options like titanium or flexible nylon providing better comfort during various activities. 


### Style and Fit

Select a frame style that complements your face shape while providing adequate coverage. Consider the wrap-around design, which can reduce glare and protect more of your peripheral vision. 

The temples should sit comfortably behind your ears, and the nose bridge should distribute weight evenly to prevent slipping.

### Prescription Compatibility

If you wear glasses, look for sunglass frames that can accommodate prescription lenses or consider purchasing separate prescription sunglasses. 

For contact lens wearers, consider frames with adequate ventilation to prevent dryness during extended wear.
