### Blade Steel Quality

Look for sets with blades made from high-quality stainless steel or carbon steel. Carbon steel blades maintain their sharpness exceptionally well but require regular oiling to prevent rusting. Stainless steel offers superior corrosion resistance but may not retain its edge as long as carbon steel. 


### Construction and Durability

Check the construction quality by examining the bolster (the thick part where the blade meets the handle) and how the blade is fixed to the tang (the extended part of the blade). A proper tang should be fully integrated with the handle, allowing for better balance and longevity.

### What maintenance is needed for Japanese knife sets?

Regular maintenance includes wiping the blades dry after each use to prevent rust. Use a soft cloth and mild soap for cleaning, avoiding abrasive materials that can dull the edges. 

Sharpen the blades using a honing rod weekly to maintain their edge, and have them professionally sharpened every 6-12 months depending on usage. 


### How should Japanese knife sets be stored?

Store Japanese knife sets in a secure location to prevent accidents and damage. Use blade protectors or arrange the knives in a block with the blades facing the same direction. 

A knife block with individual slots is ideal, or consider a magnetic strip for wall mounting. Avoid sheaths that don't allow ventilation, as moisture can damage the blades and handles.
