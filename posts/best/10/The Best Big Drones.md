## FAQ

![Big-Drones-4](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Big-Drones-4/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What is a big drone?

A big drone, also known as a large unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV), refers to aircraft that are significantly larger than typical consumer drones. These drones often weigh between 20 and 50 kilograms and have wingspans ranging from 2 to 4 meters. They are designed for both commercial and military applications, offering enhanced payload capacity and longer flight durations. 

The boundaries between "big" and "small" drones can be blurry, as regulations and design specifications vary by region. In general, any drone that requires additional certification or operates beyond visual line-of-sight (BVLOS) regulations would be considered a big drone. 


### How far can a big drone fly?

Most big drones have a maximum range of 25-30 kilometers, allowing them to maintain communication with their operator through integrated telemetry systems. Some advanced models can maintain contact over 50 kilometers using extended-range radio systems or satellite communications. 

Operational ranges can be reduced by external factors such as terrain obstacles, atmospheric conditions, and payload size. For extended missions beyond visual line-of-sight (BVLOS), drones may need to employ sophisticated navigation systems or autonomous flight protocols. 

![Big-Drones-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Big-Drones-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Are big drones suitable for photography?

While some big drones are equipped with camera mounts and stabilization systems, their primary design focus is on payload capacity rather than specialized photography features. The added weight and size make them less ideal for high-definition videography or aerial cinematography compared to smaller consumer drones. 

Specialized drone photographers may opt for smaller, more agile models with dedicated camera systems. However, big drones can still capture impressive aerial footage, particularly when carrying heavy camera equipment or performing industrial inspections. 


### What are the legal restrictions on big drone use?

Most countries have specific regulations for operating drones over 25 kilograms, requiring additional certifications, insurance, and registration. These restrictions vary significantly by jurisdiction, with some regions allowing BVLOS operations while others prohibit flight over populated areas. 

Commercial operators often face stricter requirements, including regular maintenance checks, operator certification, and compliance with airspace regulations. Military drones are subject to even more rigorous protocols, including national security restrictions and international law considerations.

### How do big drones differ from model aircraft?

While both categories fall under unmanned aircraft systems (UAS), big drones typically incorporate advanced avionics, communication systems, and payload capabilities that differentiate them from hobbyist model aircraft. These features enable more complex operations and broader application areas. 

Hobbyist model aircraft usually weigh less than 20 kilograms and are designed for recreational use, with simpler flight control systems. They often lack the sophisticated navigation, communication, and payload capabilities of big drones, which are specifically engineered for professional or military applications. 


### What industries use big drones?

Big drones find applications in a variety of industries, including agriculture for crop monitoring, construction for site surveys, and infrastructure inspection for bridges and power lines. They are also used in law enforcement for surveillance and search-and-rescue operations. 

Other specialized applications include aerial mapping for geospatial data collection, environmental monitoring for wildlife tracking, and military operations for intelligence gathering and target assessment. The versatility of these systems allows them to perform tasks that would be impractical or dangerous for humans.
