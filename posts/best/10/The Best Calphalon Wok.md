## Buyer's Guide

A Calphalon wok combines traditional Asian cooking methods with advanced cookware technology, making it an essential tool for serious home cooks. Understanding the key features and considerations will help you select the right wok for your cooking style and kitchen needs. 


### Size and Shape

The wok's dimensions significantly impact cooking efficiency and versatility. A larger diameter allows more food to be cooked at once, while taller sides provide better heat distribution and prevent food from spilling over. Consider your cooking style: round-bottomed woks are ideal for stir-frying and pan-searing, while flat-bottomed models work better on induction cooktops and gas stoves. 


### Materials and Coatings

Most Calphalon woks feature durable aluminum construction with hard-anodized coatings that resist scratches and damage. These coatings come in various types: classic clad combines aluminum core with stainless steel exterior, while professional-grade versions use fully clad aluminum with reinforced base. For easier cleaning and longer-lasting performance, look for woks with ceramic or PFOA-free non-stick interiors. 


### Base and Stand Compatibility

The wok's base design affects cooktop compatibility and stability. Traditional round-bottomed woks work well on electric stoves but may wobble on gas or induction cooktops. Flat- or oval-bottomed woks address these stability concerns but reduce cooking surface area. Consider purchasing a multi-tiered stand that provides optimal heat distribution and prevents food from splattering. 


### Maintenance and Cleaning

Proper maintenance ensures your wok maintains its performance and aesthetic quality. After each use, hand wash the wok with mild soap and warm water, avoiding harsh chemicals that can damage the coating. Dry thoroughly to prevent water spots and use a paper towel to apply a thin layer of cooking oil to the interior before storing. Regular maintenance, including periodic re-seasoning, helps maintain non-stick properties and prevents rust formation.

### What makes the Calphalon Non-Stick coating different?

The Calphalon non-stick coating uses a durable PFOA-free formula that prevents food from sticking and makes cleaning easy. Unlike other brands, Calphalon additives like PTFE and PFA ensure long-lasting release properties without compromising safety. 

The coating can withstand temperatures up to 450°F (232°C), making it suitable for most cooking methods. The specialized formulation also prevents scratching from utensils while maintaining smooth, cookware-safe surfaces. 


### How should I care for my Calphalon Wok?

Hand wash Calphalon woks with mild dish soap immediately after use to prevent food residue buildup. Avoid using harsh detergents, steel wool, or abrasive pads that can damage the non-stick coating. 

For tough residues, soak the wok briefly in warm water before cleaning. Dry thoroughly to prevent water spots and ensure the non-stick surface remains in optimal condition. 


### How heavy is a Calphalon Wok compared to competitors?

Calphalon woks typically weigh between 3 to 5 pounds, depending on the size and whether it includes a lid. The stainless steel construction provides good weight while maintaining maneuverability during cooking.

### What sizes are available in Calphalon Woks?
