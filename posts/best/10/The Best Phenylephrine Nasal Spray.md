## FAQ

### What is a phenylephrine nasal spray?

A phenylephrine nasal spray is a medication used to relieve nasal congestion. The active ingredient, phenylephrine, is an alpha-adrenergic receptor agonist that constricts blood vessels in the nasal passages, reducing swelling and opening up the airways. 

These sprays typically come in short-term use formulas for immediate relief of symptoms associated with the common cold, allergies, or other upper respiratory conditions. 


### How do I use a phenylephrine nasal spray?

The recommended dosage is usually 1-2 sprays in each nostril every 4-6 hours, not to exceed 6-8 sprays per day, depending on the specific product label instructions. 

Use the spray early in the day to avoid disrupting sleep patterns. It's important to shake the bottle well before each use and hold your head upright while spraying to ensure proper administration.

### What are the common side effects?

Common side effects may include nosebleeds, nasal irritation, and a bitter taste. These typically resolve on their own or with the adjustment of usage frequency. 

Some users may experience headaches, dizziness, or increased blood pressure. If these persist or worsen, consult a healthcare provider. 


### Who should avoid using phenylephrine nasal sprays?

People under 12 years old should not use these sprays due to potential developmental risks. Pregnant or breastfeeding women should consult their healthcare provider before use. 

Avoid use if you have high blood pressure, prostate problems, or thyroid conditions, as these sprays may worsen these conditions. Also, do not use if you are allergic to the active ingredient.

### Are there any drug interactions?

Do not use phenylephrine nasal sprays if you are taking monoamine oxidase inhibitors (MAOIs) like isocarboxazid or tranylcypromine. This combination can cause dangerously high blood pressure. 

Inform your healthcare provider about all other medications you are taking, as interactions can occur with decongestants, antihistamines, and other drugs that affect blood pressure. 


### What is the expiration date and storage requirements?

Check the expiration date on the bottle. Phenylephrine nasal sprays typically have a shelf life of 1-3 years from the manufacturing date. Use them before the expiration date for optimal effectiveness. 

Store the spray at room temperature, between 68-77°F (20-25°C), and protect it from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep out of reach of children and pets.
