Orthofeet boots are particularly beneficial for individuals with foot conditions such as plantar fasciitis, arch pain, or heel spurs. They are also recommended for those who spend long hours on their feet, have poor circulation, or experience fatigue during walking or standing.

### How do I properly clean and maintain Orthofeet boots?

Regular maintenance is crucial to extend the life of your Orthofeet boots. Clean the boots using a mild soap and water solution, avoiding harsh chemicals that could damage the materials. For particularly dirty areas, use a soft-bristled brush to remove any stubborn dirt or debris. 

Allow the boots to air dry completely before wearing them again. Store them in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent color fading and material degradation. Regular care will help maintain their supportive properties and overall condition. 


### Can Orthofeet boots be worn with socks?

Yes, Orthofeet boots are designed to work well with standard socks. We recommend wearing moisture-wicking socks to enhance comfort and reduce toe pressure. Avoid extremely thick socks that might compress the footwear's internal components, which could affect fit and support. 

If you have special medical needs or conditions, consider wearing compression socks that are compatible with your orthotic insoles. Always ensure that there is adequate space between your foot and the boot's interior to maintain proper arch support and circulation. 


### How long do Orthofeet boots typically last?

With regular wear and proper care, Orthofeet boots should last between 2 to 3 years, depending on usage frequency and foot type. The boots retain their supportive properties and comfort levels throughout this period, making them a worthwhile long-term investment. 

Signs that indicate it's time to replace your boots include noticeable wear patterns on the outsole, decreased arch support, or if you begin to experience foot pain while wearing them. Regular check-ups for fit and support are recommended, especially for individuals with changing foot conditions.
