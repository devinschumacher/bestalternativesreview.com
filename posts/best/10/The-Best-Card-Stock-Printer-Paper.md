## FAQ


### What is card stock printer paper?

Card stock printer paper is a thicker, heavier paper that's ideal for printing professional-quality documents, business cards, invitations, andphoto prints. Its sturdier construction prevents curling and provides a premium finish. 

The thickness of card stock is measured in points (pt), where 1 point equals 1/72 of an inch. Common thicknesses range from 10pt to 20pt, with higher numbers indicating thicker paper. 


### What are the benefits of using card stock?

Card stock creates a more professional appearance for your printed materials, with improved durability and a premium feel. Its thicker texture helps prevent paper jams in printers and adds a tactile quality to your documents. 

Unlike regular paper, card stock can handle moisture and folding better, making it ideal for business cards, invitations, and photo prints that may see regular handling or exposure to elements. 


### How does card stock compare to photo paper?

While both card stock and photo paper produce high-quality prints, they serve different purposes. Photo paper is optimized for visual reproduction, offering excellent color accuracy and fine detail for images. Card stock, on the other hand, focuses on texture and durability, providing a premium base for photos and graphics. 

For printing photos, consider using card stock with a photo coating or a heavier weight to protect your prints and enhance their longevity. 


### What printer settings should I use with card stock?

Always check your printer's manual for specific recommendations, as different models may require different settings. Generally, set your paper thickness to "card stock" or "heavier weight, " and adjust the print quality to match the importance of the document. 

Most printers will default to a slower print speed when using card stock, which can help prevent paper jams and ensure better image quality. Print test pages to determine the optimal settings for your specific printer and paper combination. 


### Can I print photos on card stock?

While card stock can be used for photo printing, the results may vary depending on the paper quality and printer settings. Look for card stock with a photo coating or finish specifically designed for printing photos. 

Higher-quality card stock can produce excellent results, offering better moisture resistance and print longevity compared to regular paper. Consider the intended use – for professional prints, invest in dedicated photo card stock for the best results. 


### How should I store printed card stock documents?

Store printed card stock documents away from direct sunlight and moisture to maintain their quality. Consider using acid-free storage solutions to protect the paper from potential damage. 

Avoid folding or bending card stock documents when possible, as the material's thickness can make it more prone to cracking. Keep important documents in a cool, dry place to preserve their quality and durability.
