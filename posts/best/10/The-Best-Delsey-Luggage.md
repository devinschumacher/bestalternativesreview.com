### Material Durability

Delsey offers luggage in various materials, including hardshell, softshell, and leather options. Hardshell cases typically provide the most protection with their rigid construction, while softshell bags and leather pieces offer more flexibility and weight savings. Consider the environmental factors you'll encounter during your travels when selecting a material, as some may perform better in extreme temperatures or humidity. 


### Wheel Quality

The quality of wheels significantly impacts luggage mobility and longevity. Delsey offers both two-wheeled and four-wheeled options, with four-wheeled cases generally providing smoother rolling and better stability. Look for luggage with wide, gliding wheels rather than small, fixed wheels, which can be more difficult to maneuver through crowded airports. The wheel mechanism should also be durable enough to handle repeated use over the years. 


### Interior Organization

A well-organized interior can make packing and unpacking much more efficient during your travels. Delsey cases often feature multiple compartments, mesh pockets, and dedicated spaces for shoes and electronics. Consider cases with thoughtful organizational features such as expandable sides, separate garment compartments, or built-in toiletry sets. These features can help keep your belongings neatly packed and easily accessible throughout your trip. 


### Size and Weight Limitations

Airline restrictions on luggage size and weight are crucial considerations when selecting a Delsey piece. Most carry-on options fall within standard size limits, but larger checked bags may require careful measurement to ensure compliance. Additionally, some airlines charge extra fees for overweight luggage, so it's important to weigh your options to avoid unexpected charges. Opting for a lighter case can also make it easier to carry through busy airports and up and down stairs at hotels.
