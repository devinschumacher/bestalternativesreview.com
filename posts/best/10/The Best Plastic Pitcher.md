### Materials and Durability

Plastic pitchers are available in various materials, including BPA-free plastic, Tritan, and polypropylene. While BPA-free plastic is the most common, Tritan offers superior durability and impact resistance, making it a popular choice for active users. Polypropylene is lightweight and flexible, ideal for hot climates where water expands. 

Most plastic pitchers last between 2-3 years with regular use, depending on the material quality and cleaning frequency. Impact resistance can vary, with Tritan and polypropylene resisting cracks and scratches better than cheaper BPA-free plastics.

### Maintenance Requirements

Regular maintenance includes weekly cleaning with warm water and mild detergent. For deeper cleaning, remove filters and disassemble the pitcher if possible. Rinse all parts thoroughly before reassembly to prevent mold and bacteria buildup. 

Store the pitcher upright in a dry place when not in use. Avoid storing in direct sunlight or humid environments, as this can cause plastic warping and degradation over time. 


### Environmental Impact

Plastic pitchers have a lighter environmental footprint than glass alternatives due to their lower weight and reduced transportation emissions. The primary environmental concern is proper disposal; choose BPA-free or recyclable materials to minimize impact.
