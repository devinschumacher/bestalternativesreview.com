### What are alcohol markers?

Alcohol markers, also known as solvent-based markers, use a blend of alcohol and dye in their ink formulation. This combination allows the ink to dry quickly on most surfaces without leaving a residue. 

These markers are popular for crafting, scrapbooking, and technical applications where rapid drying and solvent-based ink properties are beneficial. 


### What surfaces can alcohol markers be used on?

Alcohol markers work well on paper, cardboard, plastic, glass, and some metal surfaces. However, avoid using them on highly porous materials like untreated wood or dense fabric, as the ink may not adhere properly. 

Many alcohol markers resist common solvents like water and alcohol, so additional protective coatings may be necessary for permanent or extended use on certain surfaces.

### What makes alcohol markers different from regular permanent markers?

Regular permanent markers use an acrylate-based ink that dries slowly and creates a water-resistant film on most surfaces. Alcohol markers, on the other hand, employ a solvent-based ink that dries extremely quickly and can be removed with alcohol-based products. 

Alcohol markers generally offer better flow and more vibrant color options compared to traditional permanent markers but lack the water-resistant properties of their counterparts. 


### How should alcohol markers be stored?

Store alcohol markers in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat sources, as exposure to extreme temperatures can degrade the ink quality over time. 

Keep the cap securely fastened on the marker when not in use to prevent evaporation of the alcohol content, which could affect ink flow and color intensity.
