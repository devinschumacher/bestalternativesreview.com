### Power Source and Operation

Most Whoosh cleaners use compressed air cartridges for propulsion, though some models incorporate electric pumps. The cleaning process involves positioning the device near the target surface and actuating it to release the stream of particles.

### Cartridge Compatibility and Lifespan

Carefully check if your model uses industry-standard 123 or 303 cartridges. The number of cleanings per cartridge varies by model - typically between 100-200 discharges for 123 and 303 types. Consider this when selecting a product based on your cleaning frequency. 


### Control and Directionality

Some Whoosh devices offer adjustable triggers or rotary valves for controlling the air pressure and particle flow. This feature allows for fine-tuning the cleaning effect based on the surface material and debris type. Better models provide consistent particle size distribution for more effective cleaning.

### Usage Tips and Maintenance

To maintain optimal performance, store your Whoosh cleaner in a dry environment and check O-ring seals regularly for proper cartridge sealing. When not in use, leave the nozzle cap on to prevent debris from entering the chamber. Proper maintenance extends the tool's lifespan and ensures consistent cleaning performance.

### How does Whoosh Screen Cleaner work?

Whoosh Screen Cleaner works by utilizing a combination of solvents and surfactants that efficiently break down and remove oils, dirt, and fingerprints from both glass and oleophobic coated surfaces. The cleaner's formula quickly evaporates, leaving no residue behind to cause streaks or water spots on the screen surface.

Proper ventilation is recommended during use to prevent inhalation of fumes, especially when cleaning for extended periods.

Whoosh Screen Cleaner should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat sources, as excessive temperatures can affect the product's effectiveness and safety. The optimal storage temperature range is typically between 15-25°C (59-77°F).
