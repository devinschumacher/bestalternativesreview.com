### What sizes do Aquafina water bottles come in?

Aquafina offers several size options for their water bottles, including 16.9-ounce (500 ml), 24-ounce (710 ml), 33.8-ounce (1 L), and 67.6-ounce (2 L) bottles. The most common sizes are the 16.9-ounce and 24-ounce bottles, perfect for everyday carry and office use. 

The larger 33.8-ounce and 67.6-ounce bottles are ideal for at-home storage or picnics. The company's hydration stations, available in 1-gallon (3.78 L) capacity, are a practical choice for keeping water refreshed and cool in the fridge. 


### What materials are Aquafina water bottles made from?

Aquafina bottles are typically made from polyethylene terephthalate (PET), a widely-used plastic material known for its durability and ability to maintain water quality. The PET material can be recycled multiple times, making it an environmentally friendly option. 

Some Aquafina bottles feature a BPA-free Tritan copolyester construction, particularly in their Contour bottle line. This material offers greater impact resistance and a more durable structure compared to traditional PET bottles.

### How should Aquafina water bottles be cleaned?

Regular cleaning with warm water and mild soap is recommended to maintain hygiene and water quality. The bottles can be hand-washed or placed in the dishwasher, though the bottle caps should be cleaned separately to prevent spillage. 

For stubborn stains or mineral buildup, soak the bottle in a solution of equal parts vinegar and water for 30 minutes before washing. This natural cleaning method effectively removes most residues without harsh chemicals. 


### What is the recommended storage temperature for Aquafina water bottles?

Aquafina bottles perform best when stored between 40°F and 75°F (4°C to 24°C). Storing water bottles in extremely cold or hot temperatures can affect the water quality and the bottle's material integrity. Avoid leaving bottles in direct sunlight or near heating devices. 

If storing bottles in the refrigerator, allow them to reach room temperature before drinking to prevent condensation from dripping into the bottle. This practice also helps maintain optimal water temperature for consumption.

### Can Aquafina water bottles be frozen?

Yes, Aquafina water bottles are designed to withstand freezing temperatures. However, some users report that the bottles may expand slightly when frozen, particularly in older models or lower-quality versions. If you plan to freeze your bottles, consider using them for this purpose exclusively to prevent any potential leaks or damage. 

Freezing water bottles can be detrimental to bottles made from softer materials or those with scratches, as these can compromise the seal and cause leakage. Always check the bottle's material and condition before attempting to freeze it. 


### How often should Aquafina water bottles be replaced?

Water bottles should be replaced every 6 to 12 months, depending on usage frequency and environmental factors. Daily users may need to replace bottles more frequently due to increased wear and tear. 

Symptoms indicating it's time to replace a water bottle include visible scratches, dents, or cloudiness in the plastic, unusual odors, and changes in the bottle's shape. Additionally, look for any signs of leakage or reduced pressure when drinking, which may indicate a compromised seal.
