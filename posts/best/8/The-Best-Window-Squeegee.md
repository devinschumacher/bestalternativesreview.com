## Buyer's Guide

A window squeegee is an essential tool for cleaning windows, offering precision and efficiency in your cleaning routine. Understanding the key features and considerations will help you select the right squeegee for your needs, ensuring both effective cleaning and comfortable use. 


### Handle Design

The handle should provide a secure and comfortable grip, with options for different hand sizes and preferences. Triangular handles offer ergonomics for various grip styles, while extended handles allow reach for high windows without ladder use. 


### Head Material and Size

brisquees made from sapphire glass or toughened plastic resist scratches and maintain clarity. The head size ranges from small, precise heads for corners to large, flat heads for wide windowpanes. Consider whether you need multiple sizes for different window cleaning tasks. 


### Squeegee Flexibility

Flexible heads accommodate curved window shapes and remove water more effectively by channeling it away from the window surface. Less flexible heads are better for straight edges but may crack in cold temperatures. 


### Additional Features

Look for drip guards to prevent water from pooling at the bottom of the squeegee, which can warp window frames. Some squeegees include built-in holders for convenient storage and quick access. Consider how these features match your cleaning habits and preferences. 


## FAQ


### What is a window squeegee?

A window squeegee is a tool specifically designed for cleaning windows. It features a long handle with a rubber blade that efficiently removes water and cleaning solutions while leaving the glass surface streak-free. 

The rubber blade on a window squeegee is typically made from durable, specialized rubber compounds that prevent scratching while providing good water absorption and release properties. 


### How does a window squeegee work?

To use a window squeegee, first apply appropriate cleaning solution to the window surface. Position the rubber blade against the wet area and draw it across the glass in a scraping motion, lifting the blade slightly at the end to maintain contact with the surface. 

The squeegee's design creates a thin layer of water between the blade and glass, allowing it to effectively remove dirt, grime, and excess solution while leaving the glass dry and streak-free. 


### What are the key features of a high-quality window squeegee?

A high-quality window squeegee typically includes a rigid, long handle for stability and reach, a flexible rubber blade that conforms to curved surfaces, and a sturdy construction that resists warping and bending over time. 

Additional features might include adjustable blade tension, replaceable blade systems, or specialized ergonomic designs for improved comfort during extended use. 


### How should I store a window squeegee?

To maintain a window squeegee's effectiveness, store it in a clean, dry place when not in use. The handle can be leaned against a wall, or the squeegee can be hung from a specialized holder if available. 

Avoid storing the squeegee near excessive heat or moisture, as this can affect the rubber blade's integrity and the handle's stability over time. 


### What maintenance is required for a window squeegee?

Regular maintenance for a window squeegee involves cleaning the rubber blade after each use to remove debris and prevent scratching the glass. The blade should be wiped with a slightly damp cloth and allowed to air dry completely before storage. 

Check the blade for signs of wear, such as cracking or loss of flexibility. Replace the blade when it shows significant deterioration or loses its effectiveness. The handle should be inspected for cracks or warping, and any damage should be addressed promptly.
