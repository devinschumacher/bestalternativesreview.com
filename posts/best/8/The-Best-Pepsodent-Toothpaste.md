### What ingredients are in Pepsodent toothpaste?

Pepsodent offers several varieties with different ingredient lists, but common components include hydrated silica for cleaning, fluoride for cavity protection, and coconut oil in some variants. The toothpaste also contains sweeteners like sucralose and flavors such as spearmint. 

Additional ingredients may include water, sodium lauryl sulfate for foam production, and colorants for visual appeal. The specific formulation can vary between products, so always check the label for detailed ingredient information. 


### Are Pepsodent products suitable for sensitive teeth?

Some Pepsodent varieties contain potassium nitrate, which helps reduce tooth sensitivity by creating a protective layer over exposed dentin. These products are specifically formulated to address sensitive teeth while providing regular cleaning and protection. 

However, if you experience excessive sensitivity or persistent discomfort, it's recommended to consult a dental professional before using any toothpaste labeled for sensitivity.

### How long does Pepsodent toothpaste last once opened?

Unopened Pepsodent toothpaste typically has a shelf life of 2 to 3 years from the manufacturing date. Once the tube is opened, the toothpaste remains effective for about 6 months when stored properly in a cool, dry place. 

Avoid leaving the toothpaste exposed to air or storing it near the sink, as this can accelerate degradation. For best results, replace your toothpaste every 3-6 months, or sooner if you notice changes in texture or smell. 


### Can Pepsodent toothpaste be used for children?

Most Pepsodent products contain fluoride, which is generally safe for children over the age of 6 when used properly. The American Dental Association recommends using a pea-sized amount of fluoride toothpaste for children aged 3-6, and a small, pea-sized amount for those under 3. 

For younger children, supervise toothbrushing and ensure they spit out excess toothpaste rather than swallowing it. Always check the product label for specific age recommendations and usage instructions.
