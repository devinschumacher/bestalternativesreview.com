## FAQ


### What is a 75% keyboard?

A 75% keyboard is a compact keyboard layout that omits certain keys found on standard 104-key keyboards. This design reduces the physical footprint of the keyboard while maintaining essential functionality for most users. 

Key differences include the absence of dedicated media keys, additional function keys, and some specialized navigation keys. Despite these omissions, 75% keyboards typically retain all basic alphanumeric and modifier keys. 


### Why choose a 75% keyboard?

75% keyboards offer several advantages, including more desk space, reduced weight, and improved ergonomics due to their compact design. They are particularly suitable for users who work in tight spaces or prefer a minimalistic keyboard layout. 

The compact form factor doesn't compromise on essential features, making these keyboards ideal for both home and office use. They excel in situations where traditional full-size keyboards are impractical. 


### What are the main key differences from a full-size keyboard?

75% keyboards notably lack the additional row of function keys (F1-F12) and dedicated media controls (play/pause, volume). The layout also removes certain less frequently used keys, such as the ISO quote/quotedblleft key and the additional navigation keys. 

Despite these differences, 75% keyboards maintain a full QWERTY layout with all standard alphanumeric keys and modifier keys (Ctrl, Shift, Alt). This layout provides excellent support for text input and basic typing tasks. 


### How do I choose the right 75% keyboard?

Consider your specific needs and space limitations when selecting a 75% keyboard. Think about whether you need the additional keys found on full-size keyboards or if the compact layout suits your workflow better. 

Physical attributes like keyswitch type (linear, tactile, clicky), typing feel, and wrist rest design are crucial for long-term comfort and productivity. Read reviews and consider the brand's reputation for quality and durability. 


### Can I use a 75% keyboard for gaming?

While 75% keyboards are perfectly suitable for gaming, you might miss out on dedicated function keys and media controls that some games use. For competitive gaming, a full-size keyboard might offer more customization options and key mapping capabilities. 

For casual gaming, the reduced size and improved ergonomics of a 75% keyboard can actually enhance your gaming experience by providing better hand placement and reducing accidental key presses. 


### What should I consider for long-term use?

Invest in a keyboard with durable keyswitches that offer good typing feedback, whether you prefer linear, tactile, or clicky switches. Consider the build quality of the frame and any included wrist rests for enhanced comfort during extended use. 

Pay attention to the keyboard's connectivity options (USB, wireless), longevity of the batteries if wireless, and any additional features like n-key rollover or anti-ghosting for reliable performance under load.
