## FAQ


### What materials are ball pit balls made from?

Most ball pit balls are made from durable PVC, which is lightweight, waterproof, and resistant to punctures. Some premium options use TPU (thermoplastic polyurethane) for added resilience and better bounce. 

Lower-quality balls may be made from thin PVC or even rubber, which can wear out quickly and pose safety risks. Always choose balls specifically designed for ball pits and rated for childcare environments. 


### Which ball sizes are best for ball pits?

Standard ball sizes range from 2-3 inches in diameter, with 2.5-inch balls being most common. These provide good balance between containment and free movement in the pit. 

Smaller balls (1.5-2 inches) are ideal for younger children or indoor spaces with lower ceilings. Larger balls (3+ inches) offer more bounce but may be harder to scoop out of the pit. 


### How many balls should I fill my pit with?

A general rule is to use 6-8 balls per square foot of pit surface area. This provides plenty of balls while maintaining good visibility and ease of movement. 

For smaller pits or younger children, consider using 10-12 balls per square foot to increase play value. Always follow the manufacturer's recommendations for your specific pit size. 


### What maintenance do ball pit balls require?

Regularly check ball pits for worn or damaged balls. Remove any balls showing signs of cracking, softening, or other defects to ensure safety. 

Wash the balls monthly with mild soap and water to remove dirt and grime. Allow them to air dry completely before refilling the pit to prevent mold growth. 


### How long do ball pit balls last?

With proper care, standard PVC balls typically last 5-7 years. TPU balls may last 10 years or more due to their superior durability. 

Ball life is affected by usage frequency, environmental factors (sun exposure, temperature changes), and maintenance practices. Replace balls showing signs of wear or damage. 


### What safety considerations should I keep in mind?

Ensure all balls are clean and free of defects before use. Regularly inspect the pit for loose balls that could cause choking hazards. 

Supervise children at all times when using ball pits. Consider the ball size and number relative to the pit's dimensions to prevent overcrowding and potential accidents.
