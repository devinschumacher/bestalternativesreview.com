## FAQ


### What is a cricket sweater?

A cricket sweater is a specialized pullover garment worn by cricket players, particularly batsmen and wicket-keepers. The sweater provides protection against sun exposure and airborne debris while maintaining flexibility for movement. 

These sweaters typically feature a high collar, long sleeves, and a snug fit around the torso. They may also include additional protective elements like reinforced elbows or panels across the chest. 


### What materials are used in cricket sweaters?

Cricket sweaters are usually made from a blend of polyester and spandex, providing a balance of moisture-wicking, breathability, and stretch. High-quality options incorporate mesh panels for better ventilation during play. 

The outer material often includes UV protection to shield players from harmful sun rays, while the inner layer may be softer for comfort against skin contact. 


### How do I choose the right size?

Measure your torso from armpit to armpit, then add 1-2 inches for expansion. This measurement should determine the appropriate chest size in the sweater. 

Ensure the sweater allows about 2 inches of movement between your fingers and the sleeve when your arm is bent at the elbow. 


### Can I wear a cricket sweater under my uniform?

While some players prefer wearing a regular sweater under their kit for added warmth, dedicated cricket sweaters are designed to fit directly over the uniform. This ensures proper protection and ventilation without additional layers. 

Always check the manufacturer's recommendations to see if their specific model can be worn under the uniform. 


### How should I wash and care for a cricket sweater?

Machine wash the sweater in cold water using a mild detergent to prevent color bleeding and damage to the elastic. Avoid bleach or fabric softeners as they can compromise the material's properties. 

Air dry the sweater to maintain its shape and prevent shrinkage. If you must use a dryer, use low heat and remove it when still slightly damp to finish drying naturally. 


### What's the difference between a batting and wicket-keeper's sweater?

Batting sweaters prioritize protection with features like reinforced elbows and chest panels, while wicket-keeper's models include additional padding around the hands and knees. 

Both types share common elements like UV protection, moisture-wicking, and flexible fits, but wicket-keeper's sweaters typically incorporate more specialized padding for impact protection.
