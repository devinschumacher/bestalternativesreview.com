While there's no formal standard, a true Viking chair combines durable, sturdy craftsmanship with traditional Nordic design elements. Some even incorporate functional aspects like storage or folding mechanisms inspired by historical Viking furniture. 


### What materials are Viking chairs made from?

The most common materials for Viking chairs are wood, metal, and leather. Wood (oak, pine, or Baltic birch) forms the basis of many traditional designs. Metal elements like iron or aluminum are often used for legs, frames, or decorative accents. Leather upholstery provides durability and a classic look. 

Alternatively, some chairs use synthetic materials that mimic leather while offering greater resistance to scratches and fading. Plastic or resin components might also be present in modern interpretations. 


### How do I maintain a Viking chair?

Regular maintenance keeps your Viking chair looking its best. Dust it weekly with a microfiber cloth to remove surface dirt and debris. For wooden chairs, apply a wood cleaner specifically designed for furniture. If the chair has metal parts, clean them with mild soap and water to prevent rust. 

Leather chairs require special care. Use a leather conditioner monthly to keep the surface supple and prevent cracking. Avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners that can damage the finish. If the chair has decorative elements like wood or metal, consult the manufacturer's guidelines for specific care instructions. 


### What size options are available for Viking chairs?
