## FAQ


### What is Liquid Plastic?

Liquid Plastic is a unique material that combines the convenience of liquids with the strength and durability of traditional plastics. It flows like a liquid when applied but solidifies to create a protective barrier similar to regular plastic. 

The product is widely used for creating custom molds, casting solutions, and protective coatings due to its remarkable versatility and ease of application. 


### How Long Does Liquid Plastic Take to Harden?

Most Liquid Plastic products harden within 24-48 hours at room temperature, though this can vary slightly between different brands and formulations. 

If thicker applications or specific curing conditions are required, some products may need additional time, while others can cure in as little as 30 minutes under optimal conditions. 


### What Surfaces Can Liquid Plastic Be Applied To?

Many Liquid Plastic formulations adhere well to a variety of surfaces including metal, wood, glass, and plastic. However, the specific compatibility can vary between products, so it's important to check the manufacturer's instructions. 

To ensure proper adhesion, surfaces should be clean, dry, and free from oils, dust, or other contaminants before application. 


### What Tools Are Needed for Working with Liquid Plastic?

The primary tools required are mixing cups, mixing sticks, and application tools like brushes or sprayers. Some specialized tools may be needed for detailed work or specific applications. 

Personal protective equipment such as gloves and safety glasses should also be worn to protect both the user and the quality of the final product. 


### What Are the Common Uses for Liquid Plastic?

Common applications include mold making, casting, coating protection, and temporary barriers. It's regularly used in both professional and DIY projects where quick-setting plastic materials are needed. 

Additional uses include product encapsulation, electrical insulation, and surface protection in harsh environments. 


### What are the Safety Considerations When Using Liquid Plastic?

Most Liquid Plastic products are safe for use, but proper ventilation is essential during application and curing. Always follow the manufacturer's safety guidelines and wear appropriate protective equipment. 

If ingested or mishandled, most formulas are not harmful, but direct contact with eyes or prolonged skin exposure should be avoided.
