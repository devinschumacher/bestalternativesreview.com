## FAQ


### What is craft glue?

Craft glue, also known as craft adhesive, is specifically formulated for bonding paper, fabric, and other lightweight materials commonly used in crafting projects. Unlike traditional wood glues, craft glues dry clear and are resistant to moisture and tearing, making them ideal for scrapbooking, card making, and other paper crafts. 

Common types of craft glue include acid-free adhesives for archival storage, double-sided tape for quick applications, and specialized glues for specific materials like foam or leather. Always check the product's specifications to ensure it's suitable for your project's requirements. 


### What surfaces can craft glue bond?

Craft glue excels at bonding paper, cardboard, and lightweight cardboard materials. It works well on smooth surfaces like cardstock, tissue paper, and photographic prints. For best results, ensure both surfaces are clean, dry, and free from dust or oils before application. 

While some craft glues can bond light fabrics and foam, heavier materials or those with uneven surfaces may require a different adhesive. Always test the glue on a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure proper adhesion and desired results. 


### How should I apply craft glue?

For most craft glues, apply the adhesive to one surface and press the other surface firmly in place. This method helps prevent air bubbles and ensures even distribution of the adhesive. For double-sided tape, peel back the protective release paper and press the sticky side onto the surface you want to adhere. 

When working with cardstock or paper, use a foam brush or small brush to apply the glue rather than directly dipping the paper into the adhesive container. This prevents soaking through the material and can improve control over the amount of glue applied. 


### How long does craft glue take to dry?

Craft glue typically dries within 2-24 hours, depending on humidity and temperature. The adhesive remains tacky during this time, which helps maintain bond strength. For best results, allow the glued materials to dry completely before handling or displaying the finished project. 

During the drying process, avoid moving or manipulating the bonded materials. This helps prevent stress on the bond and ensures optimal adhesion. The full strength of the bond usually develops within 24 hours, though some glues may take longer to reach their peak performance. 


### What makes craft glue different from wood glue?

Wood glue is specifically designed for bonding wood products and requires clamping pressure to maintain proper adhesion. Craft glue, on the other hand, dries clear and is formulated for lightweight materials like paper, fabric, and foam. It's more moisture-resistant than wood glue and bonds quickly without the need for clamps. 

Wood glue typically requires more precise application and clamping to prevent gaps and ensure a strong bond. Craft glue works well for quick, casual bonding projects but may not provide the same level of strength or durability as wood glue for structural applications. 


### How do I clean up craft glue?

Spilled craft glue can usually be cleaned up with a damp paper towel or cloth. Wipe up excess glue immediately to prevent it from drying and becoming difficult to remove. For glue that has started to bond to surfaces, you may need to use a small amount of rubbing alcohol to dissolve the adhesive before cleaning with a damp cloth. 

For brushes or applicators that have come into contact with craft glue, run them under warm water and soap immediately after use. Avoid leaving the applicator in the glue container, as this can cause the glue to harden and become difficult to remove.
