## FAQ


### What is the Mortal Kombat Arcade Machine?

The Mortal Kombat Arcade Machine is an interactive gaming system that allows users to experience the classic Mortal Kombat fighting game. Using punch and kick buttons, players can compete against each other in one-on-one combat matches featuring the game's signature fatalities and finishing moves. 

Each arcade machine requires a physical connection to a computer running the Mortal Kombat game software. Multiple machines can be networked together to create local multiplayer tournaments. 


### How does the fighting system work?

The arcade machine uses traditional punch and kick buttons to control character movements and attacks. Players need to quickly react to their opponent's actions and time their button presses to perform combos and special moves. 

Pressing multiple buttons simultaneously can execute complex attacks, while the machine's software determines which moves land based on button timing and sequence. 


### What characters can I play as?

Standard Mortal Kombat Arcade Machines typically include the main roster of characters from the game, including Scorpion, Sub-Zero, and several of Raiden's allies. Some machines may offer additional characters through software updates or premium content packs. 

Each character has unique attacks and special movesets, allowing players to develop different strategies depending on their chosen fighter. 


### How is the game scored?

In multiplayer mode, the game tracks who lands more successful hits during a round. The first player to reach a predetermined score limit (usually 10-20 points) wins the match. 

During online tournaments, all player performances are transmitted to a central server, which determines the final rankings and winner based on total points scored across all matches. 


### What are fatalities?

Fatalities are graphic finishing moves that players can execute on their opponent during a match. These over-the-top death animations typically involve extreme violence and are triggered by specific button combinations during an opponent's critical moments. 

While fatalities add visual flair to matches, they do not affect the match outcome. The game continues until one player reaches the set score limit, regardless of the number of fatalities performed. 


### How does online play work?

Players can participate in online tournaments by connecting their local machines to the internet. The game software matches players based on skill level and randomly pairs them for battle. 

Online matches follow the same basic rules as local matches, with each player's performance transmitted to a central server for ranking purposes. Winners accumulate points that determine their final standing in the tournament.
