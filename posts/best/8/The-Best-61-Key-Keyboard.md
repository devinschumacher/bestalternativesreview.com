## Buyer's Guide

A 61 key keyboard combines essential functionality with portability, suitable for casual typing, basic gaming, and music production. Understanding the key features will help you find a keyboard that meets your specific needs. 


### Key Layout and Functionality

A standard 61 key layout typically includes the F and J keys with the raised bumps for home row positioning. Essential functionality covers typing, basic media controls, and navigation. For gaming, consider keyboards with dedicated media keys and improved travel distance for comfortable typing. 


### Build Quality and Durability

Entry-level 61 key keyboards often use membrane switches with rubber dome actuation. Mid-range options offer mechanical switches with various actuation forces for different typing preferences. Look for keyboards with sturdy construction, such as PBT keycaps and metal or plastic frameworks, for lasting performance. 


### Connectivity Options

USB is the most common connection method, offering reliable performance and immediate compatibility with Windows and Mac systems. Some keyboards feature a USB-C port for power and data transfer. Consider the importance of connectivity options based on your intended use and workspace limitations. 


### Additional Features and Considerations

Programmable macro keys allow customization of frequently used commands, enhancing productivity for both typing and gaming. Backlit keyboards improve visibility in low-light environments but consume more power. Factor in features like wrist rests, anti-ghosting support, and RGB lighting based on your specific use case and preferences.
