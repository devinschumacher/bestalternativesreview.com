## FAQ

### What is a line wrench?

A line wrench, also known as a tie rod wrench or pressure vessel wrench, is specialized tool designed for turning steel line fittings found in automotive, marine, and industrial applications. It has adjustable jaws that conform to the shape of the fitting while applying precise torque. 

These wrenches typically feature a ratcheting mechanism and are available in various sizes to accommodate different fitting diameters. They are essential for safely and effectively tightening or loosening steel line connections without damaging the surrounding components. 


### What are the main uses of a line wrench?

Line wrenches are primarily used in environments where precise torque application is critical for maintaining proper system pressure and preventing leaks. Common applications include automotive brake systems, hydraulic equipment, marine plumbing, and industrial fluid handling systems. 

Their ability to provide consistent torque while maintaining a secure grip makes them ideal for tight, hard-to-reach spaces where conventional wrenches cannot be effectively used. 

![Line-Wrench-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Line-Wrench-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### How do you maintain a line wrench?

Proper maintenance includes regular cleaning to remove debris and grit that can damage the working surfaces. The adjustable mechanism should be lubricated periodically to ensure smooth operation, and the jaws should be checked for wear or deformation. 

Store the wrench in a dry environment when not in use and avoid dropping or excessive force application, which can compromise its precision and longevity. 


### What types of line wrenches are available?

There are two main types: quick-release and non-quick-release. Quick-release line wrenches allow rapid repositioning between different fitting sizes without manual adjustment, while non-quick-release models require manual jaw positioning for each size change. 

Some specialized versions include angle adapters for working in tight spaces and double-ended designs for applications requiring both tightening and removal functions. 

![Line-Wrench-6](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Line-Wrench-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### When should you use a line wrench instead of a regular wrench?

Use a line wrench whenever you need to work with steel line fittings in pressurized systems or when precision torque application is required. These wrenches provide better control and prevent damage to both the fitting and the surrounding components when compared to standard wrenches. 

Regular wrenches should be used for general-purpose applications where steel line fittings are not involved, as they lack the specialized features needed for secure engagement with these particular fixtures. 


### How much should I expect to pay for a quality line wrench?

Entry-level line wrenches start around $30-50 for basic models with quick-release features. Mid-range options with durable construction and multiple-size capabilities typically cost between $50-100. 

Premium line wrenches featuring precision-machined jaws, heavy-duty ratcheting mechanisms, and additional specialized tools can range from $100-200. Investing in a high-quality wrench is recommended for frequent or professional use.
