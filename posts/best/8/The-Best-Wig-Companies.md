## FAQ


### How do I choose the right wig company?

Selecting the right wig company involves considering several key factors. Look for companies with positive customer reviews and a history of delivering high-quality products. Consider whether they offer a wide range of styles, colors, and hair textures that match your preferences. 

Check if the company provides detailed measurements and styling instructions to ensure a proper fit and natural look. Reputable companies often offer returns or exchanges, so this policy can also be a deciding factor. 


### What should I consider when measuring for a wig?

To get an accurate measurement, measure your head from the ear to ear across the widest part of the head, including the crown. For wig length, measure from your hairline to where you want the wig's bottom edge to sit. 

These measurements help ensure a properly fitting wig that looks natural and comfortable. Keep in mind that different wig types and styles may require slightly different measurements. 


### How do I maintain my wig properly?

Cleaning your wig regularly, whether it's synthetic or human hair, prevents matting and tangling. Most wigs need to be washed monthly, using a specialized wig shampoo and conditioning treatment. Always dry your wig completely before storing to prevent mold and mildew growth. 

Store your wig in a breathable mesh bag or a wig stands with headrests. Avoid direct sunlight and extreme temperatures, which can damage the fiber and reduce the wig's lifespan. Avoid using regular hair products like styling gel or mousse, as they can degrade synthetic fibers over time. 


### Can I customize a wig to match my natural hair color?

Most wig companies offer bleaching services to adjust the base color to match your natural hair shade. For more precise matching, consider buying a wig with slightly lighter or darker natural roots and touching up the roots with hair color. 

Professional wig stylists can make additional adjustments, such as highlights or subtle color variations, to create a more natural transition between your scalp and the wig. Always test color samples first to ensure the final result matches your expectations. 


### What is the difference between synthetic and human hair wigs?

Synthetic wigs are made from plastic fibers and are generally less expensive, more durable, and require less maintenance. They're available in many colors and styles but may feel less natural and might not move or react to heat like real hair. 

Human hair wigs use natural hair fibers, making them more expensive but providing a more realistic look and feel. They're available in various grades, from low to high quality, and some can be cut, colored, and styled like real hair. Human hair wigs typically require more care and maintenance. 


### How long does a wig typically last?

Synthetic wigs generally last 1 to 3 years with regular use and proper care. High-quality human hair wigs can last 5 years or more with careful maintenance, but daily use may reduce this lifespan. 

Factors affecting durability include usage frequency, care practices, and handling. Professional-quality wigs may last longer than budget options but still require periodic replacement or repair as natural wear occurs.
