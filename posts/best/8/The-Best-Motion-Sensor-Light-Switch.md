Most motion sensor light switches operate on standard household voltage (120V in the US) and come with both wireless and wired options. Wireless switches require pairing with a compatible hub or using battery power, while wired switches are connected directly to the electrical system. 

Opt for switches with adjustable sensitivity levels to prevent unnecessary light activation from pets or minor disturbances. Look for models with a response time under 1 second for immediate lights-on functionality.

Consider the switch's form factor and mounting requirements. Common styles include surface-mounted, surface-lift, and flush-mount options. The installer should pay attention to switch depth and whether it needs box or back-wiring.

Some motion sensor switches include built-in nighttime timers that extinguish lights after a few minutes of inactivity. This helps preserve battery life in wireless models or reduces energy consumption in wired setups.

### What types of motion sensor light switches are available?

Traditional motion sensor light switches detect movement in the space and turn lights on or off accordingly. Smart motion sensor light switches add connectivity, allowing you to control lighting via your smartphone or voice commands. 

Common sensor types include PIR (Passive Infrared) for heat and motion detection, and microwave sensors for rapid movement detection. Some modern units combine both technologies for improved accuracy. 


### How do motion sensor light switches save energy?

Motion sensors automatically turn off lights when no movement is detected, preventing unnecessary energy consumption. This is particularly effective in rooms with infrequent activity, such as basements or guest bathrooms. 

By reducing idle light usage, these switches can significantly lower energy bills and environmental impact, making them a practical choice for energy-conscious homeowners.

### What is the typical installation process for a motion sensor light switch?

Installation involves replacing your existing switch with a compatible unit. The process requires basic electrical knowledge and tools, including a voltage tester and screwdriver set. 

Connecting the sensor requires identifying the correct wires for power and load. The unit typically mounts in the same box as your previous switch, with adjustments for sensor placement and coverage. 


### What factors affect the sensor's detection range?

The sensor's range depends on its type and sensitivity settings. PIR sensors generally detect motion up to 30 feet, while microwave sensors can detect rapid movement at longer distances. 

Maintenance, environmental factors, and installation height also impact performance. Regular cleaning, proper mounting, and avoiding direct sunlight or air vents can improve sensor reliability.
