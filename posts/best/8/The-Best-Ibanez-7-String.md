## Buyer's Guide

Choosing the right 7-string guitar requires careful consideration of several key factors. This guide outlines essential features and considerations to help you select an Ibanez 7-string that matches your playing style and budget. 


### Body Style

Ibanez offers various body styles, including thin-tiled, double-cutaway, and full-cutaway designs. Consider your playing position and technique - thin-tiled bodies are more comfortable for seated players, while full-cutaway bodies provide easier access to higher frets. 


### Neck Construction

Neck-through or set-neck construction significantly affects playability and sustain. Neck-through designs are more solid and resistant to warping but may be heavier. Set-neck guitars offer excellent sustain and a vintage feel, though they can be more susceptible to neck separation. 


### Fretboard Radius

Most 7-strings feature either a 12" or 16" radius fretboard. A 12" radius provides better intonation for alternate tunings but may be less comfortable for players used to modern necks. The 16" radius strikes a balance between playability and versatility, making it suitable for various playing styles. 


### Bridge Configuration

Through-body bridges offer the best sustain and clarity, though they require careful setup. Offset-tailpiece designs provide decent sustain with easier intonation adjustment, making them a practical choice for intermediate players. Floyd Rose tremolo systems deliver exceptional tuning stability but demand regular maintenance.
