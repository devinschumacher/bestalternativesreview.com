## FAQ


### What is a coolant reservoir cap?

A coolant reservoir cap, also known as a radiator filler cap or expansion tank cap, is a safety device that attaches to the top of a vehicle's coolant reservoir. Its primary function is to maintain pressure within the cooling system, preventing leaks and airlocks that can affect engine performance. 

The cap features a breather valve that allows for proper ventilation while preventing coolant loss. It contains a rubber seal that maintains pressure and a fuse element that pops out if the pressure becomes too high, protecting the system from over-pressurization. 


### How do I know if my coolant reservoir cap needs replacement?

Look for signs of leakage around the cap, such as wet spots or coolant puddles. The rubber seal can deteriorate over time and allow coolant to escape. 

Check for visual damage to the cap itself, including cracks, warping, or missing parts. A compromised cap can lead to coolant loss and decreased system performance. 


### What happens if my coolant reservoir cap fails?

Without proper pressure maintenance, the cooling system may experience leaks, airlocks, or reduced efficiency. This can lead to overheating, reduced engine performance, and potential damage to engine components. 

Additionally, a failed cap can cause coolant to evaporate more quickly, requiring more frequent refilling and potentially leading to low coolant levels and related issues. 


### Can I clean a coolant reservoir cap?

Mild soap and water can be used to clean the exterior of the cap. Avoid using abrasive cleaners or harsh chemicals that could damage the rubber seal or vent mechanism. 

Do not attempt to clean or lubricate the internal components of the cap. This can affect its proper function and pressure regulation. 


### What's the difference between coolant reservoir caps and radiator caps?

While both caps regulate pressure in the cooling system, a radiator cap attaches directly to the radiator and is designed for older vehicles. A coolant reservoir cap connects to the expansion tank and is more commonly used in modern vehicles. 

Radiator caps typically have a higher pressure rating and are less likely to vent coolant, while reservoir caps maintain pressure while allowing for coolant expansion and easy refilling. 


### Do coolant reservoir caps need maintenance?

The rubber seal should be inspected during regular vehicle maintenance to ensure it remains intact and seals properly. Check for signs of cracking, swelling, or softening that could indicate deterioration. 

The cap's pressure rating should also be verified compared to your vehicle's specifications. If the cap is several years old or has been damaged, it's recommended to replace it to ensure proper system performance.
