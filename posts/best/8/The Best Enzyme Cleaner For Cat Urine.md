## FAQ


### What is an enzyme cleaner?

Enzyme cleaners are specialized products designed to break down and neutralize urine's organic compounds through enzymatic action. Unlike traditional cleaners that merely mask odors, enzyme cleaners permanently eliminate urine stains and eliminate the associated ammonia odor. 

These products contain specific proteins that target and consume the proteins in urine, effectively "digesting" the waste material. This process prevents it from re-crystallizing and provides lasting odor elimination. 


### How effective are enzyme cleaners?

Many enzyme cleaners have proven highly effective through independent testing and user reviews. They are particularly successful in eliminating tough, long-standing stains and overcoming the persistent ammonia odor associated with cat urine. 

While natural enzyme cleaners may vary slightly in effectiveness, most reputable brands perform well. It's important to follow the manufacturer's instructions for best results. 


### What makes enzyme cleaners different from other cleaners?

Unlike conventional cleaners, which often use harsh chemicals to mask odors, enzyme cleaners work at the molecular level to break down urine's constituent proteins. This fundamental difference results in more thorough cleaning and lasting odor removal. 

Traditional cleaners may temporarily eliminate visible stains and smells but can leave residue that attracts bacteria and may harbor future odor issues. Enzyme cleaners clean both the surface and underlying biological contamination. 


### How should I use an enzyme cleaner?

Most enzyme cleaners require application directly to the stain before allowing it to sit undisturbed for 5-10 minutes. Lightly scrubbing the area can enhance effectiveness, but avoid excessive scrubbing which may damage the surface. 

After allowing time for the enzyme action, thoroughly rinse the area with water. Follow up with regular cleaning and maintenance according to the product's recommendations. 


### Can enzyme cleaners be used on all surfaces?

Enzyme cleaners are generally safe for various surfaces including carpets, tile, hardwood, and upholstery. However, it's important to test the product on a small, hidden area first to ensure it doesn't damage the material. 

Avoid using enzyme cleaners on unfinished wood, certain fabrics, or surfaces with intricate designs where damage might be more noticeable. 


### How long do enzyme cleaners take to work?

Enzyme cleaners begin working immediately upon application, but complete breakdown of the urine proteins requires time. Most products show noticeable results within 10-15 minutes, with optimal results typically achieved after 30 minutes to 2 hours of application time. 

For heavy or deeply embedded stains, multiple applications over several hours may be necessary to achieve complete removal of the stain and odor.
