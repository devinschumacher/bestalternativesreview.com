## FAQ


### What is an AC leak detector?

AC leak detectors are specialized tools designed to identify refrigerant leaks in air conditioning systems. These devices work by detecting the presence of refrigerant gas, which has distinct electrical properties, and signaling when a leak is detected. 

The primary purpose of an AC leak detector is to help maintenance professionals and HVAC technicians locate and repair leaks efficiently, ensuring optimal system performance and energy efficiency. 


### How does an AC leak detector work?

AC leak detectors typically use electrical conductivity to detect refrigerant leaks. The device measures the electrical properties of the air and identifies when refrigerant gas, which conducts electricity, is present in the detection field. 

When a leak is detected, the AC leak detector will sound an alarm, light up, or display a message on its screen to alert the user. Some advanced models can also measure the size of the leak based on the strength of the signal. 


### What types of AC leak detectors are available?

There are two main types of AC leak detectors: contact and non-contact. Contact detectors require placement in direct contact with the suspected area to detect a leak. Non-contact detectors use long-range sensors to detect leaks from a distance, typically up to 3 feet away. 

Advanced models combine both contact and non-contact features, offering greater versatility in leak detection. 


### How accurate are AC leak detectors?

Modern AC leak detectors are highly accurate when used correctly. They can detect leaks as small as 0.1 oz per year, which is the equivalent of a pinhole-size leak. 

However, environmental factors such as temperature, humidity, and the presence of other conductive materials can affect detection accuracy. It's important to use the device in appropriate conditions for best results. 


### How long do AC leak detectors last?

The lifespan of an AC leak detector varies depending on the quality of the device and how frequently it's used. Most reliable models last between 3 to 5 years with regular use. 

Key components like batteries and sensor heads should be checked regularly. Some manufacturers recommend replacing them every 2 years to maintain optimal performance. 


### What should I consider when choosing an AC leak detector?

When selecting an AC leak detector, consider the detection range, battery life, and whether you need contact or non-contact capabilities. Advanced features like built-in diagnostic tools and leak measurement capabilities can be helpful for professional use. 

Check the device's compatibility with different refrigerants and ensure it's appropriate for the size of AC systems you'll be using it with. Read reviews and compare reliability ratings before making a purchase.
