## FAQ


### What is hair powder used for?

Hair powder serves multiple purposes in hair styling and grooming. It helps control oil, absorb excess moisture, and provides texture for styling. Popular uses include setting hair in place, creating volume at the roots, and preventing flat, greasy-looking hair throughout the day. 

Common applications range from touch-ups between washes to creating natural-looking texture in styling. It's particularly useful for fine hair types that require extra lift and control. 


### How do you apply hair powder?

Lightly dust the powder onto your hands and run your fingers through your hair, focusing on the roots and areas prone to oiliness. Gently massage the powder into your scalp and hair to distribute it evenly. 

If clumping occurs, use a wide-tooth comb to separate and spread the powder throughout your hair. For complete coverage, you can also use a fine-mesh sieve to apply the powder directly to your scalp. 


### What types of hair powder are available?

Hair powders come in various forms, including baking soda, rice powder, cornstarch, and special hair-forming agents. Each type offers different properties – baking soda absorbs oil, rice powder provides texture, and cornstarch helps set styles. 

Multipurpose hair powders combine these ingredients to offer comprehensive care, while specialty powders address specific needs like anti-dandruff formulas or scent-infused options. 


### How long does hair powder last?

Hair powder typically remains effective for several months when stored in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Properly sealed containers prevent caking and maintain powder quality. 

For optimal performance, replace your hair powder every 3-6 months, especially if you notice changes in texture or scent. This ensures you're using fresh product for the best results. 


### Can hair powder damage hair or scalp?

When used correctly, hair powder is generally safe for both hair and scalp. It helps maintain scalp health by absorbing excess oil and moisture, promoting a balanced environment for hair growth. 

However, individuals with sensitive skin or existing scalp conditions should patch test new products and monitor their scalp's reaction. If irritation occurs, discontinue use and consult a dermatologist. 


### What are the benefits of using hair powder?

Regular use of hair powder can lead to reduced hair washing frequency, as it helps maintain scalp health and controls oil production. This extends between-wash intervals while keeping hair fresh and manageable. 

It's particularly beneficial for oily scalp types who struggle with daily greasiness, as it provides long-lasting control without the harsh effects of frequent washing. Additionally, it helps maintain styling efforts throughout the day, reducing the need for constant retouching.
