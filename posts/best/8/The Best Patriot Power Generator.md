## Buyer's Guide

A power generator from Patriot Power can provide reliable backup electricity during outages, but choosing the right unit requires considering several key factors to ensure it meets your specific needs and budget. 


### Generator Size and Output

The primary specification is the generator's output capacity, measured in watts or kilowatts. Calculate your total electrical load by adding up the wattage requirements of all connected appliances and devices. The generator should have at least 125% of your total load capacity to account for starting currents. 

![Patriot-Power-Generator-2](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Patriot-Power-Generator-2/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Transfer Switch Compatibility

For whole-house backup, the generator must be compatible with your existing transfer switch. This safety device prevents backfeeding electrical current into the utility grid while ensuring circuit breakers trip automatically during outages. 


### Fuel Efficiency and Storage

Consider the generator's fuel type (gasoline, propane, or diesel) and its fuel efficiency. Calculate the required fuel tank size based on expected outage duration and generator runtime. Propane and diesel models typically offer better fuel efficiency and longer operating times. 

![Patriot-Power-Generator-3](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Patriot-Power-Generator-3/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Maintenance Requirements and Warranty

Compare maintenance schedules and component warranties from different Patriot Power models. Reliable generators should have serviceable air filters, spark plugs, and cooling systems. The manufacturer's warranty terms can also affect long-term reliability and total cost of ownership.
