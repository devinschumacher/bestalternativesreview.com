### Material Considerations

Most king size metal bed frames are made from either steel or aluminum. Steel provides superior strength and durability but can be heavier and more expensive. Aluminum frames are lighter and more resistant to corrosion, making them a practical choice for coastal or humid environments. The surface finish can also affect appearance and maintenance requirements – choose between powder coating for lasting color or untreated metal for a raw industrial look. 


### Size and Spacing

Standard king size measurements are 76 inches wide by 80 inches long. Ensure adequate clearance around the frame for walking and furniture placement. Consider the distance between slats, which should be narrow enough to prevent limb entrapment but wide enough for comfort. Proper bed frame rail spacing prevents tipping and allows safe access to the sleeping surface. 


### Assembly and Installation

Metal bed frames typically require assembly, which varies by model complexity. Some frames attach directly to the mattress, while others use box spring supports. Consider the frame's weight and your physical ability to handle pieces. Many beds come with pre-drilled holes and clear instructions, but heavier frames may need professional installation or specialized tools.
