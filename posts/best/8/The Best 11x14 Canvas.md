## FAQ


### What is an 11x14 canvas?

Also known as a half-inch deep canvas, this standard art size measures 11 inches by 14 inches. The term "half-inch deep" refers to the thickness of the canvas, which provides more surface area than a standard print while keeping the overall profile modest. 

The 11x14 format is popular for both printing photos and creating original artwork. Its dimensions make it versatile for various projects while remaining manageable for framing and display. 


### What type of canvas is used for 11x14 pieces?

Professional-grade cotton canvas is the most common choice for 11x14 pieces, offering good texture and longevity. Cheaper alternatives like polyester or vinyl may be found, but they lack the natural grain and durability of cotton canvas. 

Some manufacturers offer textured, linen-like, or fine-art-specific canvas options, which can affect the final look and feel of the artwork. 


### What are some common uses for 11x14 canvas?

Printed photos: The size works well for 8x10 prints, trimming off 2 inches on each side. This can create a visually striking display when paired with a matching frame. 

Artwork display: Original paintings, drawings, or mixed media pieces can be mounted on 11x14 canvas for both personal use and gallery display. The size makes it suitable for both small rooms and offices. 


### What's the difference between gallery wrap and traditional framing?

Gallery wrap: The canvas edges are wrapped around the stretcher bars and secured, creating a clean gallery look without visible frames. This mounting method works with both printed photos and original artwork. 

Traditional framing: The canvas is mounted within a framed opening, with the edges tucked under the frame's molding. This method provides more visual framing options and can be used with either gallery-wrap or loose-mounted pieces. 


### How do I choose the right canvas type?

For printed photos: Opt for quality cotton canvas with a slight texture for the best print results. Avoid overly glossy finishes that can affect color accuracy. 

For original artwork: Consider the specific requirements of your medium. Acrylic paints may require a primer-coated canvas, while oils might benefit from a slightly textured surface. 


### What should I consider when purchasing an 11x14 canvas?

Print quality: If purchasing for printing, verify the canvas supports the chosen print method (inkjet, UV, etc. ). Higher-end canvases often offer better ink adhesion and color reproduction. 

Environmental factors: If the canvas will be hung in a humid or direct sunlight exposure area, choose UV-resistant and moisture-rated materials to prevent fading and warping.
