## Buyer's Guide

Operation board games offer a blend of strategic thinking and precise timing. The goal is to remove the surgical tool from a patient's body without touching the surgical margins, earning points based on successful removals. Here's what you need to consider when choosing the right Operation game for your group. 


### Gameplay Mechanics

Standard Operation gameplay requires quick reflexes and hand-eye coordination. The game typically includes multiple levels of difficulty and increasing numbers of surgical tools. Some variants add twists like power-ups or time bonuses to keep the gameplay fresh. 


### Components Quality

The physical quality of the game pieces significantly impacts the playing experience. Look for sturdy tools that fit snugly into the body cavity and durable play mat. Well-made pieces reduce frustration and ensure consistent gameplay. 


### Player Accommodation

Original Operation supports 2-6 players, but some versions accommodate up to 8 players. Consider the number of players when choosing a game, as larger groups may require multiple sets or alternative games that support more participants. 


### Game Duration and Setup

Each game typically takes 10-15 minutes to play, making it a good choice for short gaming sessions. The setup process should be quick, with clear instructions for a smooth transition from unboxing to playing.

Operation is a classic board game where players remove tiny plastic pieces from a surgical tray without touching the red "operation" line. The first player to complete their "patient" wins. 

The original Operation game was released in 1963 and has since undergone several redesigns while maintaining its core mechanics and difficulty.
