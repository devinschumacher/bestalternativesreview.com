## FAQ


### What is moissanite?

Moissanite is a synthetic form of silicon carbide, created in labs to mimic the appearance of diamond. It's known for its brilliance and fire, making it an affordable alternative to traditional diamond jewelry. 

The gemstone was discovered in 1893 by physicist Dr. Henri Moissan, who found it in a meteorite crater. Modern moissanite production began in the 1990s, offering consumers a high-quality, ethical alternative to natural diamonds. 


### How does a moissanite tennis bracelet compare to diamond alternatives?

While moissanite can appear similar to diamonds in terms of sparkling properties, it lacks their inherent value and rarity. A moissanite tennis bracelet typically costs less than a diamond version, making it a budget-friendly option that maintains jewelry quality and style. 

The durability of moissanite (9.25 on the Mohs scale) makes it suitable for daily wear, though it may scratch more easily than diamonds. Both stones require similar care - regular cleaning and protection from harsh chemicals. 


### What are the carat weight options for moissanite tennis bracelets?

Moissanite is available in carat weights ranging from 1.0 to 4.0 carats for tennis bracelets. Weights below 1.0 carat are common, suitable for everyday wear or as replacement stones for smaller jewelry pieces. 

Higher carat weights become more challenging to set in tennis bracelets due to their length and need for secure prongs. We recommend consulting with a jeweler to determine the ideal weight based on your preference and intended use. 


### How should I care for my moissanite tennis bracelet?

Clean your moissanite tennis bracelet weekly using mild dish soap and warm water. Use a soft-bristle brush to clean around the stones if needed. The stone's brilliance can be maintained by avoiding contact with household chemicals and removing jewelry before activities like household chores or exercise. 

Storage is important to prevent tangling or scratching. You can use a jewelry organizer with separate compartments for each piece or wrap the bracelet in a soft cloth when not in use. 


### What are the differences between melee and solitaire moissanite tennis bracelets?

Melee moissanite tennis bracelets feature multiple smaller stones set closely together, creating a continuous, glittering band. These typically range from 1mm to 2mm in size, offering a dazzling visual effect with a more substantial look than single-stone designs. 

Solitaire moissanite tennis bracelets showcase a single, larger center stone with smaller stones surrounding it in a halo or prong setting. This design style emphasizes the primary stone while adding additional sparkle to the piece.
