### What are backflow incense cones?

Backflow incense cones are specialized cones designed to prevent air from flowing back into incense burners. These cones feature a small hole at the base that maintains airflow while preventing residual smoke from exiting the burner. 

They typically measure between 5-7 inches in height and come in various fragrances, making them suitable for both daily use and special occasions. 


### How do backflow incense cones work?

Backflow cones function through a built-in restrictor in the base that allows proper airflow during burning while capturing smoke particles within the cone structure. This design helps minimize smoke dispersion and keeps the environment cleaner. 

The restrictor works by creating a slight pressure difference inside the cone, which prevents air from reversing flow and drawing smoke back into the burner. This mechanism also reduces the risk of uncontrolled flame spread.

### Can any incense cone be used with a backflow adapter?

Backflow adapters are specifically designed for use with standard incense sticks, converting them into backflow cones. While most regular incense sticks will work, shorter or thicker sticks may not fit properly. 

Adapters typically include a replaceable restrictor base that screws into the end of an incense stick. This base must maintain a tight seal to function effectively, so ensure the adapter matches the stick diameter. 


### Are backflow incense cones safe for all users?

Backflow incense cones are generally safe for most users when used according to manufacturer instructions. However, they should not be used by individuals with respiratory conditions unless recommended by a healthcare provider. 

The restricted airflow can create higher temperatures during burning, which may be uncomfortable for some users. Always place cones on heat-resistant surfaces and keep away from flammable materials.

### How long do backflow incense cones typically last?

Backflow cones usually burn for 15-20 minutes per stick, though this can vary slightly based on fragrance type and environmental factors. Each cone typically lasts between 4-6 inches when fully burned. 

Replacement restrictor bases should be considered every 10-15 uses to maintain optimal airflow and prevent buildup of residue in the adapter. 


### What maintenance is required for backflow cones?

Before each use, check the restrictor base for proper seating and remove any accumulated ash. Avoid using force when screwing the restrictor base in to prevent damage. 

Rinse the base with water and dry completely before storing. The restrictor base can be cleaned with mild soap and water if needed, but avoid using harsh chemicals that may damage the plastic.
