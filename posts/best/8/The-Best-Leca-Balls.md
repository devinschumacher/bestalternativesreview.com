### What are Leca Balls?

Leca balls, also known as expanded clay pebbles, are lightweight ceramic balls made from baked clay. They're designed for water absorption and drainage in hydroponics, gardening, and pet care applications. 

These porous balls have a rigid yet crush-resistant structure that makes them ideal for supporting plant roots while maintaining proper air circulation around the root zone. 


### What sizes are available?

The most common sizes range from 6mm to 20mm in diameter. Larger sizes are typically used for structural support in plant containers, while smaller sizes improve drainage in soil mixes. 

Size selection depends on the specific application: smaller balls for aquariums and potting mixes, larger balls for building hydroponic systems and improving soil aeration.

### How are Leca Balls used?

In gardening, Leca balls are used as a substrate in container gardens, especially in hot climates where soil drainage is crucial. They're also effective in raised beds and vertical gardening systems. 

For hydroponics, Leca balls serve as a lightweight growing medium that prevents plant roots from becoming waterlogged while providing necessary oxygenation. 


### How do they compare to other substrates?

Unlike traditional growing media, Leca balls don't decompose over time or harbor pests, making them a long-term solution for both indoor and outdoor growing applications. 

Compared to perlite or vermiculite, Leca balls maintain their shape and size, providing superior support for plant roots while facilitating better water retention and nutrient delivery.

### Are they safe for aquatic use?

Yes, Leca balls are non-toxic and stable, making them safe for both freshwater and saltwater aquariums. They're commonly used in marine aquariums to improve water quality and support live rock growth. 

The porous structure of Leca balls helps filter water by removing debris and provides a surface for beneficial bacteria to colonize, improving overall aquarium health. 


### How do you clean Leca Balls?
