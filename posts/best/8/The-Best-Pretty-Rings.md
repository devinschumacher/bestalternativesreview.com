## FAQ


### What materials are commonly used in pretty rings?

Pretty rings often feature a variety of materials, with gold and silver being the most traditional choices. White metals like platinum and rhodium-coated options have gained popularity due to their durability and resistance to tarnish. 

For colored rings, manufacturers use precious metals paired with gemstones. Common gemstone materials include diamond, ruby, sapphire, and emerald, each requiring specific care considerations to maintain their brilliance. 


### How can I determine the metal content of a pretty ring?

Metal purity is typically marked on the ring itself. Look for a stamp indicating karat value (14k, 18k, etc. ) on gold pieces or milligram weight on platinum items. This marking indicates the percentage of pure metal in the ring. 

If you cannot see or read the marking, consider professional testing through a jeweler or metal testing service that uses spectrometry to analyze the metal composition. 


### What care requirements should I expect for different metal finishes?

White gold requires regular polishing and occasional rhodium plating to maintain its shine, which lasts about 6-12 months. Gold maintains its luster longer, but may need professional cleaning twice yearly. 

Platinum and white metals need less frequent maintenance, with quarterly cleanings recommended. Natural gemstones like pearls need daily care, while high-quality diamonds require professional cleaning twice yearly. 


### How can I size a pretty ring properly?

Ring sizing kits are available at jewelry stores and online, allowing you to measure your finger's specific size. Consider buying a half-size larger for everyday wear or 1/4 size larger if the ring will be worn during physical activities. 

Remember to measure your finger at the widest point and account for the ring's style - thicker bands typically fit smaller than thinner ones of the same width. 


### What’s the best way to display pretty rings?

Rings look best when displayed individually in separate cases, protecting them from scratches and damage. For storing multiple pieces, consider a jewelry box with individual compartments or lay them flat in a jewelry cloth to prevent tangling and scuffing. 

Avoid stacking rings on a single display surface as they risk damaging each other. Keep jewelry in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent yellowing or fading of materials. 


### How do price points vary for different pretty ring styles?

Entry-level rings often range from $50-200, featuring base metals and smaller gemstone alternatives. Mid-range options (around $200-500) offer better quality metals and genuine gemstone options at affordable prices. 

High-end rings exceeding $500 typically feature higher karat gold, white metals, or platinum, with real gemstones certified for quality and durability. Luxury brands often command premium prices for their craftsmanship and materials.
