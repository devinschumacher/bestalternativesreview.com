## FAQ


### What types of medical information can be stored on a bracelet?

Medical bracelets commonly store essential health information such as allergies, blood type, medical conditions, and emergency contact details. They can also include emergency medical IDs that provide critical instructions for medical professionals. 

Some bracelets feature QR codes that, when scanned, reveal detailed medical records including medications, doctors' names, and additional health conditions. The information can range from basic to comprehensive, depending on the bracelet's design and intended use. 


### What materials are medical bracelets made from?

The most common materials are stainless steel, niobium, and surgical-grade aluminum. These materials resist corrosion and are suitable for wearing daily. Plastic and rubber options are available for those who prefer lightweight bracelets. 

Specialty materials like titanium and tungsten carbide are used for their durability and hypoallergenic properties. Each material has its own advantages in terms of appearance, comfort, and resistance to wear. 


### How do I choose the right size bracelet?

Measure around your wrist at its thickest point using a flexible measuring tape. Add 1/2 inch (1.27 cm) to determine the appropriate bracelet size. This allows for comfortable movement and proper display of the medical information. 

Most bracelets come in standard sizes that range from 6 to 8 inches (15 to 20 cm). If you have wide or narrow wrists, consider custom sizing options to ensure a secure fit. 


### What maintenance is needed for a medical bracelet?

Clean your bracelet regularly with mild soap and warm water. Avoid harsh chemicals and abrasive materials that could damage the surface. For jewelry with engravings, use a soft cloth to prevent scratches. 

Avoid storing your bracelet near magnets or electronic devices, as these can affect certain materials. If your bracelet has adjustable clasps, check them regularly to ensure they remain functional. 


### How should I replace a lost medical bracelet?

Contact the manufacturer or retailer who sold you the bracelet to request a replacement. Provide them with your original order information and any specific details about your bracelet, such as material and size. 

Some companies offer replacement programs that require proof of purchase, while others may have specific policies for lost items. It's important to replace your bracelet promptly to maintain your medical information's visibility. 


### Are medical bracelets covered by insurance?

Insurance coverage for medical bracelets varies by provider and plan. Some insurance policies may cover bracelets that serve a medical purpose, such as insulin pumps or other medical devices. 

Major medical conditions that significantly impact daily life may be eligible for specific accommodations, including specialized medical identification equipment. Check with your insurance provider for specific coverage details.
