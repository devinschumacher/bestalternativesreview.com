### What is pepper spray?

Pepper spray, also known as OC (oleoresin capsicum) spray, is a non-lethal self-defense weapon that uses hot pepper extract to temporarily incapacitate attackers. It works by causing intense burning and irritation in the eyes, nose, and throat, making it difficult to breathe and severely reducing mobility. 

The active ingredient is capsaicin, derived from chili peppers. When sprayed into the eyes, it triggers an intense burning sensation that peaks within 30-45 seconds and lasts 3-10 minutes. A spray's effectiveness is measured in SPV (standard pepper spray velocity), with higher values indicating greater range and impact force. 


### How effective is pepper spray?

Pepper spray has a proven track record of stopping attacks when used correctly. According to multiple studies, pepper spray incapacitates between 75-95% of attacks within 15 seconds on average. However, its effectiveness can vary based on factors like attacker size, wind conditions, and how quickly you can activate the spray. 

It's important to note that pepper spray only provides a deterrent effect and should not be relied upon as a primary means of protection. Always combine its use with proper situational awareness and escape techniques.

### What types of pepper spray are available?

The main types of pepper spray include canisters, pens, and keychain versions. Canisters typically hold 3-5 ounces of spray with a range of 10-20 feet. Pens and keychains range from 0.4-1.5 ounces and have shorter ranges (3-10 feet), making them suitable for carrying in a purse or pocket. 

Some modern models include features like lockable triggers, safety locks, and tactical designs. The active ingredient concentration should range from 1% (mild) to 2% (extra strength), though most legal standards limit domestic sales to 1-1.2% for personal defense purposes. 


### How do I properly use pepper spray?

To use pepper spray effectively, maintain a distance of 3-6 feet from your attacker and activate the spray as they approach. Point the nozzle just above their face at a 45-degree angle and press the trigger while keeping your eyes up and out of harm's way. Spray in short bursts rather than holding the trigger; reposition after each burst to avoid the spray. 

Practice proper technique regularly to ensure readiness in high-stress situations. Consider training with the specific model you plan to use, as different designs may require slight adjustments in grip and aiming position.

### How should I maintain my pepper spray?

Store your pepper spray in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight, which can degrade the active ingredients over time. Check the expiration date and replace the spray every 3-5 years, as older formulas may lose effectiveness. After each use, wipe down the device with a mild soap solution and allow it to dry completely before storage. 

Regularly inspect the sprayer's components for signs of wear or damage. If you notice any leaks, restricted spray patterns, or unusual behavior, replace the device immediately. Remember that high temperatures can affect the spray pattern and effectiveness, so avoid exposing your pepper spray to extreme heat or cold. 


### Are pepper sprays legal?

Pepper spray regulations vary by country and region, so it's crucial to check local laws before purchasing. In many places, pepper spray is legal for self-defense purposes without a permit, but some areas restrict purchase to individuals over 18 years old or prohibit carrying certain concentrations or types. 

When traveling, be aware that some countries have strict restrictions on carrying pepper spray across borders. Always check with your destination's customs regulations and consider alternative defensive tools if travel requirements don't allow pepper spray possession.
