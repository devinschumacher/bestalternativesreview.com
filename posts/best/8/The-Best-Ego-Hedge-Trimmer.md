## Buyer's Guide

Hedge trimmers, particularly those with the EGO brand, represent a significant investment for garden maintenance. Understanding the key features and specifications ensures you select a tool that matches your trimming needs while delivering reliable performance. 


### Power Source Options

EGO hedge trimmers offer both corded and cordless options. Consider your clipping distance requirements and whether you need continuous power. Lithium-ion batteries provide flexibility, while garden mains power enables longer trimming sessions without recharging. 


### Performance Specifications

Key specifications include blade type (bypass or oscillating), cut length, and cutting height. Bypass blades offer cleaner cuts, while oscillating blades handle thicker branches. The cut length determines how much material you can remove in one pass, and the cutting height dictates the tool's reach for tall hedges. 


### Corded vs. Cordless Considerations

Corded trimmers eliminate power interruptions but require accessible electrical outlets. They excel in closely managed gardens with predictable trim patterns. Cordless trimmers offer greater mobility and flexibility, suitable for larger gardens or those with scattered hedge locations. Factor in battery runtime and charging speed to match your trimming habits. 


### Maintenance and Durability

Look for tools with adjustable height mechanisms for long-term height maintenance and ergonomic handles for comfortable use. The trimmer's weight distribution impacts handling, while the blade's material and construction affect longevity. Regular maintenance, including periodic sharpening and blade inspection, extends the tool's useful life.

### How long do Ego hedge trimmer batteries last?

With regular use, each charge can last from 30 to 60 minutes, depending on the model and trimmer load. This typically translates to trimming 8-20 bushes per charge, with smaller shrubs requiring less power. 

To extend battery life, it's recommended to store them at room temperature when not in use and follow proper charging procedures, such as maintaining between 20-80% charge level. 


### What maintenance do Ego hedge trimmers require?

Regular maintenance includes cleaning the cutting head after each use, checking and tightening the guide bar bolts, and applying lubricant to the moving parts. The motor should be protected from water and debris to prevent damage. 

The battery needs protective caps when not in use and should be charged and discharged regularly to maintain its health. The blade should be sharpened annually or as needed to maintain optimal performance. 


### Can Ego hedge trimmers handle challenging jobs like thick foliage?

Most Ego hedge trimmers are designed to handle thick foliage, with some models featuring adjustable guide bars and powerful motors to cut through branches up to 1.25 inches in diameter. 

However, extremely heavy loads or tightly packed foliage may reduce cutting efficiency, so it's best to test the specific model's capabilities with similar materials before tackling tough jobs.
