## FAQ


### What is Carnauba Wax?

Carnauba wax is a natural, plant-based wax derived from the leaves of the Brazilian carnauba palm tree (Copernicia cerifera). It's renowned for its exceptionally high melting point and water-resistant properties, making it ideal for automotive care, furniture polish, and crafting projects. 

The wax is harvested through a sustainable process where leaves are collected when they naturally fall from the tree. Each leaf yields about 20 grams of wax, contributing to its eco-friendly reputation. 


### What makes Carnauba Wax unique?

Unlike other natural waxes, Carnauba wax has a melting point of 160-180°F (71-82°C), which makes it suitable for outdoor applications and extreme temperature environments. This thermal stability also means it remains solid at room temperature and only melts when heated. 

Its natural water resistance and hydrophobic properties help protect surfaces from water damage and contamination. The wax's molecular structure gives it superior gloss retention and durability compared to many synthetic alternatives. 


### How should Carnauba Wax be applied?

Clean the surface thoroughly before application. Mix the powder with a compatible carrier oil or solvent and apply evenly using a clean cloth. Wipe off excess product while it's still tacky to ensure uniform coverage. For best results, apply in a well-ventilated area and avoid extremely hot temperatures during application. 

Let the wax cure according to the manufacturer's instructions. This typically takes 15-30 minutes, after which the surface may be sealed with a second coat for added protection. The final product should have a natural, slightly yellow-to-clear appearance. 


### Is Carnauba Wax safe for all surfaces?

Carnauba wax is generally safe for most non-porous surfaces, including automotive finishes, furniture, and electronic devices. However, it may not adhere well to absorbent materials like paper or untreated wood. Test on a small, hidden area before application to ensure compatibility. 

Some surfaces may require a different type of protection. For example, electronic devices should avoid abrasive waxes, while leather and certain plastics may need specialized care products. 


### How long does Carnauba Wax last?

With proper application and maintenance, a single application of carnauba wax provides protection for 3-6 months, depending on environmental factors like sun exposure, humidity, and frequency of use. Regular washing can accelerate the wear process. 

Regular recoating every 2-3 months helps maintain the original appearance and protection. Harsh chemicals, consistent temperature fluctuations, and heavy traffic areas may require more frequent applications.
