### What features should I look for in a baby bath?

A baby bath should have non-slip bases to prevent tipping and accidents. The shape should be easy to grip and maneuver, with a secure lid to keep the water warm and clean. 

Consider a bath that includes a built-in scale for weighing your baby or a suction-cup design that attaches to the bathtub for stability. 


### Are portable baby baths safe?

Yes, portable baby baths are safe when used properly. Always supervise your baby during bath time, and never leave the bath unattended. 

Ensure the portable bath is stable and won't tip over. Check that the water temperature is comfortable before placing your baby in the bath. 

![Baby-Bath-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Baby-Bath-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### How do I clean and maintain a baby bath?

After each use, clean the baby bath with soap and water. Dry it completely before storing. 

Regularly check for any cracks, leaks, or signs of wear. Replace the bath if you notice any damage that could compromise its safety. 


### Can I put essential oils in the baby bath?

No, essential oils should never be added to a baby bath. They can be too strong and potentially harmful if ingested or absorbed through the skin. 

Use only specially formulated baby bath products that have been designed for safe use with infants. 

![Baby-Bath-6](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Baby-Bath-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### How much water should I use for the baby bath?

Fill the baby bath with about 2-3 inches of water, which is roughly equivalent to two average-sized cups. 

Always test the water temperature with your hand before placing your baby in the bath to ensure it's between 98-100°F (36-38°C). 


### How often should I replace a baby bath?

The lifespan of a baby bath typically ranges from 3-5 years, depending on usage and care. Signs that it's time to replace include cracks, warping, or difficulty keeping water in. 

Regularly inspect the bath for any damage. If you notice significant wear or changes in the material, it's best to replace it to maintain safety and hygiene.
