### Material Quality and Construction

The durability of frilly socks largely depends on their material quality and construction. Look for socks made from sturdy cotton blends that can hold up to regular wear. The elastic bands should be reinforced to prevent stretching, and the frills themselves need to be sewn securely to avoid unraveling. 

![Frilly-Socks-2](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Frilly-Socks-2/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Fit and Sizing

Proper fit is crucial for both comfort and durability. Measure your foot to determine the correct size, ensuring there's about an inch of room between the sock and your heel. The elastic band should provide a snug fit without feeling tight, while the frills should sit flat without bunching up. 


### Frill Style and Placement

The frills can dramatically affect the sock's appearance and comfort. Opt for evenly spaced frills that match the width of the foot for a balanced look. The placement matters too - frills around the ankle can create a playful cottage-core aesthetic, while those near the toe add a touch of whimsy. 

![Frilly-Socks-3](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Frilly-Socks-3/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Care and Maintenance

Regular care will keep your frilly socks looking their best. Machine wash them with similar colors using a gentle cycle and avoid bleach, as it can damage the elastic. Store folded rather than balled up to prevent frill distortion. Consider separating dark and light colors to prevent color bleeding during washing.
