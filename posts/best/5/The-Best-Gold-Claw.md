### Material and Design

Most gold claws are made from stainless steel or surgical-grade stainless steel due to their durability and resistance to corrosion. The curved design helps reach beneath the nail bed, while the claw shape provides better control than flat instruments. The handle should be securely attached and comfortably shaped for extended use. 


### Sizing and Shape Variations

Gold claws come in different sizes and shapes to accommodate various nail depths and thicknesses. Common variations include straight and angled claws, as well as versions with or without insulated handles. The appropriate size can significantly impact both effectiveness and user comfort during the procedure. 


### Handle Design

Handle designs vary between traditional straight handles and curved, ergonomically shaped options. Insulated handles provide better grip and prevent accidental needle sticks during use. The handle length should allow for a secure grip while maintaining control over the claw's movement. 


### Additional Features

Some gold claws include built-in nail clippers or files for removing debris. Others have replaceable claws to extend tool lifespan. Consider whether you need additional features based on your specific use case, as these can affect both performance and longevity. 


## FAQ


### What is a Gold Claw?

A Gold Claw is a specialized jewelry tool designed for creating claw settings in jewelry making. It features a small, curved claw head that can grip and secure gemstones into prongs. 

The claw head is typically crafted from gold or a gold alloy, matching the common materials used in jewelry settings. It comes in various sizes to accommodate different stone shapes and prong specifications. 


### How does a Gold Claw work?

When setting a gemstone, the Gold Claw's curved head is placed under the stone's girdle and pressed down into the prong setting. This action securely locks the stone in place while maintaining its natural shape. 

The claw's precision helps prevent scratches during setting and ensures a flush finish when the stone is seated in the prong cup. 


### What types of stones can be set with a Gold Claw?

Gold Claws are versatile and suitable for most gemstone shapes, including round, oval, emerald, and pear cuts. They work best with stones that have a defined table and girdle. 

While they can handle standard cabochons, Gold Claws may not be the best choice for very deep, concave stones or those with unusual shapes requiring specialized settings. 


### How should I maintain a Gold Claw?

Regular cleaning with mild soap and warm water helps maintain the tool's shape and functionality. It's important to avoid harsh chemicals that could damage the gold alloy. 

Store the Gold Claw in its original case or a soft pouch to prevent scratches and maintain its precision during storage. 


### Can I use a Gold Claw for both platinum and white gold settings?

Yes, Gold Claws can be used in both platinum and white gold settings. However, when working with platinum, consider the increased hardness and the need for extra precision to avoid damaging the stone. 

For white gold settings, standard gold-plated claws are often sufficient, but check the specific prong specifications to ensure compatibility. 


### Are Gold Claws accurate for setting precious stones?

Gold Claws provide excellent accuracy for setting precious stones when used properly. The precision of the claw head allows for secure stone placement while preserving the stone's natural shape. 

Proper technique and regular tool maintenance are crucial for achieving accurate results when setting valuable gemstones.
