## FAQ


### What is eye glass cleaner?

Eye glass cleaner is a specialized solution designed to remove streaks, smudges, and other debris from glasses and lenses without damaging the delicate surface. It's particularly important for contact lens wearers and anyone with prescription lenses. 

These cleaners typically come in convenient spray bottles or wipe forms, making them easy to use on both glasses and contact lenses. They're specifically formulated to be safe for the materials used in lenses, including plastic, glass, and various types of coating. 


### What makes eye glass cleaner different from regular window cleaner?

Eye glass cleaner contains mild, lens-safe ingredients that prevent scratching and damage to the lens surface. Regular window cleaner can contain harsh chemicals that may degrade lens coatings or leave residue that affects vision. 

Eye glass cleaners also usually include features like anti-fog properties, which help prevent misting in cold environments, and UV protection, which helps shield eyes from harmful rays. 


### Can I use regular household cleaners on my glasses?

No, regular household cleaners can damage the lens coating and potentially scratch the lens surface. This can reduce the clarity of your vision and shorten the life of your lenses. 

Always use a product specifically designed for glasses and lenses to ensure they remain clear and safe to wear. 


### What ingredients should I look for in an eye glass cleaner?

Effective eye glass cleaners typically contain mild surfactants to remove grime, along with protective elements like UV filters and anti-fog agents. Look for products that specifically mention lens safety and compatibility with eyeglass materials. 

Avoid cleaners with harsh chemicals like ammonia, bleach, or alcohol, as these can degrade lens coatings and damage your glasses. 


### How often should I use eye glass cleaner?

Clean your glasses daily, or as needed, depending on how often you wear them and how much exposure they have to environmental factors like dust, pollen, and pollution. Always wash your hands before handling your glasses to reduce the risk of smudges. 

If you don't have access to a dedicated cleaner, you can use warm water and a mild soap specifically designed for glasses. Make sure to thoroughly dry your glasses afterward to prevent water spots. 


### Can I reuse eye glass cleaning wipes?

Most glass cleaning wipes are intended for single use to avoid cross-contamination and maintain their effectiveness. Reusing wipes can spread bacteria and residue, potentially harming your vision and lenses. 

If you need to clean multiple lenses, it's best to use fresh wipes for each lens or wash and reuse them carefully, storing them in a clean, dry container when not in use.
