## FAQ


### What is sublimation printer paper?

Sublimation printer paper is a specialized type of thermal paper designed for use with sublimation printers. It consists of three layers: a sublimation coating on top, a high-quality photographic paper middle layer, and a protective backing. 

This paper reacts to heat by undergoing a molecular transformation where the solid dye turns directly into a gas, which then bonds permanently with the surface of the substrate being printed on, such as polyester or plastic. 


### What makes sublimation printer paper different from regular photo paper?

Sublimation paper features a unique trilayer construction that allows it to bond colors permanently to polyester and plastic substrates through heat transfer, unlike regular photo paper which produces surface coatings that can peel or flake off. 

The sublimation coating on these papers contains up to 100% solid dye, delivering rich, vibrant colors that regular photo papers can't match. This coating also ensures the ink's longevity and resistance to fading, especially when printed on heat-tolerant materials. 


### How does sublimation printing work?

During the printing process, the sublimation paper passes through the printer's thermal print head, which selectively applies heat to specific areas of the paper. This heat causes the solid dye to turn into a gas and bond directly with the substrate below the paper. 

The paper is then removed from the substrate, and the heat is applied again to fuse the now-gaseous ink permanently into the material's surface. This process produces a high-quality, full-color image with excellent durability and resistance to fading. 


### What types of materials can be printed on with sublimation paper?

The primary substrate for sublimation printing is polyester, commonly found in items like photo transfers, t-shirts, and plastic products. The process works by transferring the image from the paper to the polyester surface using heat and pressure. 

Sublimation paper can also be used on other heat-sensitive materials like PVC, acrylic, and certain types of plastic films, though proper pre-treatment and settings may be required for these substrates. 


### What are the advantages of using sublimation printer paper?

Sublimation printing produces extremely vibrant, full-color images with excellent detail resolution. The process bonds the ink permanently to the substrate, resulting in images that are resistant to fading, peeling, or water damage. 

This method allows for the creation of transferable images that can be applied to a wide range of substrates, from polyester clothing to hard plastic items, making it a versatile choice for both commercial and home printing applications. 


### How do I properly care for and store sublimation printer paper?

Store unopened sublimation paper rolls in their original packaging to protect them from environmental factors that could affect print quality. Always handle the paper carefully to avoid creasing or damage to the protective backing. 

Avoid exposure to extreme temperatures, direct sunlight, and humidity, as these can degrade the paper's quality. When printing, use the recommended paper type settings on your printer to prevent paper jams and ensure optimal print results.
