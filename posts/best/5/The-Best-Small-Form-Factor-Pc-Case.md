## Buyer's Guide

Choosing the right small form factor PC case requires considering several key factors to ensure your build performs optimally while remaining compact. This guide will help you evaluate the most important aspects of small form factor cases, from internal dimensions to cooling solutions. 


### 1. Internal Dimensions and Space Allocation

The most crucial specification of any small form factor case is its internal dimensions. The standard form factors are Micro ATX and Mini-ITX, each with distinct size limitations. Common dimensions include 170mm x 170mm for the motherboard, with height typically ranging from 350mm to 420mm. Ensure ample space for components, particularly graphics cards and power supplies, as these occupy significant volumes. 


### 2. Cooling Solutions

Adequate cooling is essential, especially in tight spaces where airflow can be restricted. Look for cases with multiple PCIe expansion slots positioned away from critical cooling areas. The case should provide a balanced approach between heatsink clearance and airflow management. Additionally, consider cases with integrated cable management systems and dust filters to maintain airflow quality. 


### 3. Connectivity and Features

Small form factor cases often present compromises in connectivity compared to full-size towers. Essential ports include USB 3.0, USB Type-C, and video outputs. Check for features like front panel USB ports, audio jacks, and network connectivity options. Some cases offer customizable RGB lighting through LED strips, which can be a practical addition for visual appeal or system identification. 


### 4. Build Quality and Case Design

Build quality affects both the longevity and aesthetic appeal of your system. Look for cases with sturdy construction, solid metal panels, and reliable latching mechanisms. The case design should facilitate easy component installation while maintaining structural integrity. Mini-ITX motherboards are the smallest standard form factor, measuring just 170mm by 170mm. While they have fewer expansion options, they excel in space-constrained environments, supporting most modern processors in a highly compact package.
