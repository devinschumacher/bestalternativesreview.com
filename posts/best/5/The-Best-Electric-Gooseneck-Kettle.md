### What makes an electric gooseneck kettle different from a standard electric kettle?

Electric gooseneck kettles feature a spout that extends upward and outward from the body of the kettle, allowing for precise pouring control. This design is particularly beneficial for making tea, as it enables users to direct the flow of water with accuracy, preventing spills and ensuring consistent water levels in teacups. 

Standard electric kettles lack this specialized spout design and typically have a simple, straight pouring spout that extends from the body of the kettle. While functional for basic water heating, gooseneck kettles offer enhanced precision and control for specific brewing requirements. 


### How do electric gooseneck kettles heat water?

Electric gooseneck kettles heat water using an electrical heating element that's either immersed in the water or housed in a separate base. The element heats the water rapidly and efficiently, with some models featuring rapid boil technology that can heat water to the desired temperature in under 3 minutes. 

Most kettles include temperature settings or indicator lights to show when the water has reached the correct temperature, typically between 195°F and 205°F for optimal tea brewing. Some advanced models feature precise temperature control, allowing users to set specific temperature points for different brewing needs.
