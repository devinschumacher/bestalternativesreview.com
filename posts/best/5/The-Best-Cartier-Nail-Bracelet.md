### Product Features

Key elements include the bracelet's width, which typically ranges from 2 to 4 millimeters, and the type of movement, such as automatic or wrist-winding. The case diameter usually falls between 32 and 38 millimeters, providing adequate space for proper timekeeping while remaining discreet on the wrist. 

Materials common in Cartier nail bracelets are stainless steel, 18-karat gold, and platinum, with diamond accents enhancing the luxury appeal. The bracelet's design allows for easy positioning on the wrist while minimizing bulk. 


### Battery and Power

Automatic movement requires regular winding, with daily turns sufficient for proper functioning. Battery-powered models typically need replacement every four to five years, depending on usage. Both types maintain accurate timekeeping, though automatic movements also display the date and sometimes small seconds. 

Power reserve indicators on automatic models show when winding is necessary, while battery levels on electronic displays need periodic checking to prevent unexpected time loss. 


### Care and Maintenance

Regular cleaning with a soft cloth removes surface grime, while the bracelet should be professionally serviced every 2-3 years to check movement operation and replace worn parts. The case back securing mechanism needs periodic adjustment to maintain water resistance. 

When storing the bracelet, keep it away from strong magnets and extreme temperature changes. The watchband should be sized to fit comfortably over the wearer's hand, allowing for slight movement without stretching.
