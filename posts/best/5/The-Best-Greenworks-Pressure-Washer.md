### Engine Power

The pressure washer's engine produces both pressure and flow rate, measured in pounds per square inch (psi) and gallons per minute (gpm), respectively. Higher psi enables penetrating dirt removal, while increased gpm allows faster surface coverage. Common residential models range from 1000 to 2000 psi and 1.5 to 2.5 gpm.

A detachable hose with a quick-connect fitting ensures uninterrupted operation, while an interchangeable spray nozzle allows adjusting between different patterns - standard fan, turbo for heavy-duty cleaning, or fan with soap for gentle washing. Most units connect to a standard garden hose, though some may offer additional water source options like direct fill or tank system compatibility. Ensure the unit can be easily connected to your existing water supply while maintaining proper pressure.

### What is a Greenworks pressure washer?

A Greenworks pressure washer is an outdoor cleaning tool that uses high-pressure water to remove dirt, grime, and debris from surfaces. It combines powerful water flow with adjustable pressure settings for versatile cleaning capabilities. 

The term "Greenworks" typically refers to the brand's commitment to developing products with energy-efficient motors and durable construction. This pressure washer model likely features similar eco-friendly design principles while delivering reliable performance. 


### What surfaces can I clean with a Greenworks pressure washer?

You can use a Greenworks pressure washer to clean a variety of surfaces, including driveways, patios, decks, and siding. It's effective for removing stubborn grime, paint residue, and moss from outdoor structures and concrete. 

Avoid using it on painted surfaces or delicate materials that might damage when subjected to high-pressure water. Always test the pressure washer on a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure proper cleaning without damage.

### How much pressure does a Greenworks pressure washer provide?

Greenworks pressure washers typically offer adjustable pressure settings, allowing you to choose between 1,200 to 1,800 PSI (pounds per square inch). The higher pressure setting is ideal for tough jobs like removing heavy grease or baked-on grime, while the lower setting is suitable for more delicate surfaces. 

The exact pressure range may vary depending on the specific model, so be sure to check the product specifications to determine which setting range your unit provides. 


### What is the ideal working distance for a Greenworks pressure washer?

For optimal results, maintain a working distance of 2 to 3 feet between the pressure washer and the surface you're cleaning. This distance helps prevent damage to both the surface and your equipment while ensuring the water stream reaches the correct pressure setting. 

Adjust your stance based on the pressure setting: move closer for higher pressure to maintain effective cleaning without damaging the surface, and move farther back for lower pressure settings when cleaning more delicate materials.

### How much water pressure does a Greenworks pressure washer use?

A typical Greenworks pressure washer uses between 1.5 to 2 gallons of water per minute (GPM) at full pressure. The water flow rate can vary depending on the model, but most pressure washers in this category operate within this general range. 

Water pressure is measured in PSI, while flow rate is measured in GPM. The combination of these two factors determines the overall cleaning power of the pressure washer. Higher PSI with moderate GPM provides intense cleaning, while slightly lower pressure with higher GPM delivers consistent cleaning power for larger areas. 


### How long does a Greenworks pressure washer last?

With regular maintenance and proper use, a Greenworks pressure washer can last between 3 to 5 years with daily use. The lifespan depends on several factors, including the frequency of use, maintenance practices, and environmental conditions. 

To extend the life of your Greenworks pressure washer, store it in a dry location when not in use, protect it from extreme temperatures, and follow the manufacturer's maintenance recommendations for regular service intervals.
