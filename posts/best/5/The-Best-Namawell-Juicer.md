## Buyer's Guide

A Namawell juicer combines efficiency and durability to extract fresh juices from your fruits and vegetables. Understanding the key features and considerations will help you choose the right model for your juice-making needs. 


### Mechanism and Efficiency

The juicer's mechanism determines its effectiveness in extracting juice. Key types include masticating (slow press) juicers that process food through a single gear, and centrifugal juicers that use high-speed blades to extract juice. Masticating juicers are generally more efficient at extracting juice while minimizing oxidation, making them suitable for leafy greens and wheatgrass. 


### Capacity and Portability

Consider the juicer's capacity and whether you need a portable option. Desktop models are ideal for regular use, processing between 16-20 ounces of produce at once. Handheld or portable masticating juicers are great for occasional use or travel, processing smaller quantities but maintaining high efficiency. 


### Material and Design

Safety and durability are paramount. Look for juicers made from food-grade materials with BPA-free parts. The design should include easy-to-clean parts and intuitive controls. Some models feature automatic pulp ejection systems and residue-free designs, which can significantly reduce cleanup time. 


### Power and Noise Level

Power affects the juicer's performance and operational costs. Higher wattage (150-200 watts) supports tougher materials like wheatgrass and multiple fruits simultaneously. Noise level is another consideration; masticating juicers tend to be quieter than centrifugal models, making them suitable for use in shared spaces.
