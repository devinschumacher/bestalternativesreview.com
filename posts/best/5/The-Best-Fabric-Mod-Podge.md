## FAQ


### What is Fabric Mod Podge?

 Fabric Mod Podge is a craft supply that combines the functions of decoupage medium, adhesive, and sealer in one product. It's specifically formulated for use with fabric, paper, and other lightweight materials on craft projects. 

The clear, water-based formula dries to a flexible, transparent film that protects your project while maintaining its original texture. It's commonly used for creating fabric jewelry, home decor items, and crafting projects that incorporate mixed media techniques. 


### What types of fabrics work best with Fabric Mod Podge?

Lightweight fabrics like cotton, linen, and Rayon work best with Fabric Mod Podge. Avoid using it on heavy fabrics such as denim or thick upholstery fabrics, as they may not adhere properly or risk distorting the material. 

The fabric should be clean, dry, and free of sizing or finishes that might interfere with adhesion. For best results, test the product on a small, inconspicuous area before applying it to your project. 


### How many coats of Fabric Mod Podge are needed?

Typically, you'll need two thin coats to ensure complete coverage and proper adhesion. The first coat helps the material adhere to the fabric, while the second sealing coat provides protection and gives the completed project a smooth finish. 

Allow at least 24 hours between coats, and between 24-48 hours for the project to fully cure and reach its final strength. 


### Can Fabric Mod Podge be used on leather?

While Fabric Mod Podge performs well on fabric, it's not recommended for use on leather. Leather requires different adhesives that can better bond to its surface without causing damage or distortion. 

For leather projects, consider using a dedicated leather adhesive or a product specifically formulated for bonding materials to leather surfaces. 


### What surfaces can Fabric Mod Podge be used on?

 Fabric Mod Podge can be applied to a wide range of surfaces including fabric, paper, cardboard, burlap, and lightweight wood. It works well on porous materials that need protection and clear coating. 

It's important to note that while it can be applied to some metal surfaces, it's not recommended for use on untreated, raw metal. Always test the product on a small area first to ensure compatibility with your specific materials. 


### How long does Fabric Mod Podge last once opened?

Once opened, Fabric Mod Podge should be used within 6 months for optimal performance. If you notice any separation, discoloration, or foul odor, it's best to discard the batch and use a fresh container. 

To extend its shelf life, store the unopened bottle in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Once opened, keep it tightly sealed when not in use and store in a refrigerator if you're unable to use it within the 6-month period.
