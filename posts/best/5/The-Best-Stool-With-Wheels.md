### How do wheels affect the stool's stability?

Wheels can impact stability, particularly in heavier or wider stools. Modern designs address this through larger, more robust wheels and improved base constructions. Look for stools with either four wheels for smooth rolling or two wheels and a support leg for added stability. 

Users with mobility concerns should consider stools with five wheels or a base design that includes both wheels and feet, providing better support while maintaining mobility.

### What maintenance requirements do wheel stools have?

Regular maintenance includes checking the wheels for proper rotation and ensuring the base remains clean and free of debris. This helps maintain optimal mobility and prevents damage to floors. 

Periodic inspection of the stool's structure and tightening of any loose screws can extend the product's lifespan and ensure continued safety and functionality. 


### Are wheel stools suitable for outdoor use?

Stools designed for outdoor use typically feature weather-resistant materials and protective coatings. Look for models with UV protection and water-resistant finishes to maintain the product's appearance and functionality in various environmental conditions. 

Wheels may collect debris or become less effective in outdoor settings, so consider models with larger wheels or those specifically designed for both indoor and outdoor use.

### How do wheels affect sound levels?

Modern wheel designs incorporate materials and technologies to minimize sound. This is particularly important in office environments where noise can be disruptive. Some popular solutions include rubber-tipped wheels and silent-bearing mechanisms.
