## Buyer's Guide

Artificial olive trees offer a practical and attractive alternative to live trees, combining the aesthetic appeal of natural foliage with the convenience of maintenance-free design. Understanding their key features and considerations will help you select the right tree for your space and needs. 


### Materials and Construction

The tree's structure typically consists of a plastic or metal stem with artificial branches. High-quality trees use durable ABS plastic branches that maintain their shape and flexibility. The leaves are usually made from polyethylene, offering realistic texture and color. Premium models incorporate additional elements like stone-like pots and water features for enhanced authenticity. 


### Sizing and Proportion

Measure your available space before selecting a tree. Standard heights range from 5 to 10 feet, with wider pots available for larger models. Consider how the tree will be positioned - corner placement requires taller trees, while hallway installations work better with narrower designs. The pot diameter should generally match the tree's height for proper balance. 


### Maintenance Requirements

While artificial olive trees require minimal care, regular inspection is essential. Check for loose branches, fading leaf color, or structural damage. Gently dust leaves with a microfiber cloth weekly to maintain the tree's appearance. If used as a centerpiece, keep the pot's drainage saucer clean to prevent water residue buildup. 


### Placement and Environment

Olive trees thrive in well-lit areas with indirect sunlight. Avoid placing the tree near heat sources like radiators or fireplaces. The appropriate temperature range is between 65-75°F (18-24°C). Humidity levels should remain between 40-60% to prevent leaf droppage. Using the tree indoors year-round may accelerate wear; consider seasonal placement outdoors during warmer months.
