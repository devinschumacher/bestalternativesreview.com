## Buyer's Guide

Aramis Cologne represents a sophisticated addition to any fragrance collection, combining lasting power with distinctive character. Understanding key aspects of these fragrances can help you select one that matches your style and preferences. 


### Intensity and Sillage

The concentration of fragrance in Aramis colognes ranges from light to robust, allowing consumers to choose based on their preferences for immediate impact versus gradual development. Sillage, or the trail left behind after application, varies between 0 (no detectable scent) and 4 (strongly noticeable), helping users determine how distinctive their fragrance should be in social settings. 


### Base Notes

Base notes form the lasting impression of an Aramis cologne, typically lasting 4-6 hours. Common base notes include woods (like oakmoss and sandalwood), musk, and vanilla. These ingredients provide depth and complexity that distinguishes Aramis fragrances from lighter colognes. 


### Top and Heart Notes

Top notes (active immediately after application) and heart notes (developing 15-30 minutes later) create the initial scent signature. Top notes often include citrus (orange, bergamot), mint, or green accords, while heart notes might feature spicy elements (cinnamon, clove), floral fragrances (jasmine, rose), or green notes. This combination sets the immediate character of the fragrance. 


### Long-term Maintenance

Aramis colognes generally retain their scent for 2-4 hours with proper application techniques. To maximize longevity, apply to pulse points (wrists, pulse, behind ears), avoid direct sunlight, and allow each application to fully absorb before layering additional products. Regular reapplication is recommended, especially after washing hands or sweating.
