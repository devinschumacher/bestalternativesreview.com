### What features should I look for in a cheap makeup vanity?

A budget-friendly makeup vanity should have a stable, well-lit mirror with adjustable height. The vanity table should offer generous tabletop space for applying makeup and storing products. 

Look for modern designs with clean lines and practical storage options like drawers or open shelves. Consider additional features like built-in tissue dispensers or wall-mounted mirrors for extra convenience. 


### How do I measure for a cheap makeup vanity?

Measure your available space, including the width, depth, and height of the area where the vanity will be placed. Add a few inches to each dimension to account for mounting hardware or wall fixtures. 

Consider the height of your existing furniture or flooring to ensure the vanity's base fits comfortably within the room's architectural elements.

### What materials are commonly used in cheap makeup vanity construction?

Most budget vanities feature particleboard cores with veneer finishes. Some may incorporate MDF (Medium Density Fiberboard) for increased stability. 

Look for vanities with metal legs or frames for added support, as these materials resist sagging and warping better than wood. 


### How do I check a cheap makeup vanity for stability?

Flip the vanity over and inspect the legs and frame for solid construction. The bottom should feel heavy and stable, with no wobbly components. 

Test the tabletop by pressing down forcefully in different areas. It should remain flat and even, with no noticeable compression or indentations.

### What types of vanity lights are available for cheap makeup vanities?

The most common options include clip-on fixtures, built-in LED strip lighting, and adjustable spotlights. Clip-on lights offer the most flexibility and can be added to nearly any vanity. 

LED strip lighting provides even illumination and is energy-efficient, while adjustable spotlights allow precise targeting of light where needed most. 


### How do I maintain a cheap makeup vanity?

Regularly wipe down the vanity surface with a damp cloth and mild soap solution to remove makeup residue and dust. Avoid harsh chemicals that can damage the finish. 

Check for loose hardware or shifting components monthly, and tighten as needed. Store makeup products properly to prevent spills and ensure easy maintenance of the vanity's integrity.
