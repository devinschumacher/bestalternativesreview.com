### How should I properly care for these slippers?

Follow the care instructions provided with your slippers. Machine wash the fabric slippers in a mesh bag using mild detergent and cold water. Air dry them flat to maintain their shape and prevent shrinkage. For pet slippers, spot clean regularly and wash thoroughly every two weeks. 

The foam soles may be dusted with a cloth weekly and cleaned with soap and water when needed. Pet slippers should be rotated between pairs to extend their lifespan, and replaced when the padding starts to flatten or lose its shape. 


### How do I determine the right size?

Measure your feet (including any toe room) and compare the measurements to the brand's sizing chart. These charts usually list the appropriate shoe size for each slipper size. Allow extra size for wearing socks or pet slippers in smaller sizes. 

Try on the slippers to ensure a snug fit without tightness. The heel should sit firmly in place, and the toes should have a small amount of room to move without dragging. Proper fit prevents slipping and provides better traction.

The plush fabric slippers offer decent insulation but may not be suitable for extremely cold temperatures. Look for products with added thermal padding or warm synthetic fill for colder climates. Pet slippers often include features like double-sole construction and thermal liners. Check the product specifications for details on material thickness and insulation level to ensure it matches your temperature needs. 


### How long do these slippers typically last?

With regular wear, most Mickey Mouse slippers last 6-12 months before showing significant signs of wear. Factors affecting durability include the quality of materials used, how often they're worn, and whether they're machine washed. Proper care extends their lifespan by reducing wear and tear.
