### Materials and Durability

The float's core is made from closed-cell polyethylene foam, which prevents water absorption and maintains its shape. The outer cover is usually made from UV-resistant polyester or PVC fabric. Look for floats with reinforced stitching at stress points and waterproof zippers if you plan to store them outdoors. 


### Drainage and Stability

Proper drainage is crucial to prevent water accumulation that could affect buoyancy. Ensure the float has multiple drain holes and a built-in bail system for quick and easy water removal. Stability is enhanced by the high center of gravity design, which helps keep the float upright in most swimming conditions.

### Care and Storage

Regular maintenance extends the float's lifespan. After each use, remove any debris, pump out excess water, and air dry the float in a shaded area before storage. The recommended storage environment is a dry, indoor space away from direct sunlight and extreme temperature changes.
