## FAQ


### What is the best way to maintain my couch's fabric when using a vacuum?

Use a brush attachment specifically designed for couches to prevent pulling fabric loops or damaging the surface. Start from the outside and move inward, paying extra attention to creases and corners where dirt tends to accumulate. 

For stubborn stains, avoid using water directly on the fabric. Instead, try a small amount of upholstery cleaner and gently blot the area, working from the outside inward to prevent spreading the stain. 


### How often should I clean my couch with a vacuum?

Regular maintenance requires vacuuming your couch at least once a week to remove surface debris and prevent buildup. Deep cleaning, using appropriate upholstery techniques, should be done monthly for pet owners and families with allergies, or as needed based on your living situation. 

Consider the type of fabric and how frequently the couch is used to determine cleaning frequency. Heavier traffic areas or outdoor fabrics may need more frequent attention than plush indoor upholstery. 


### What are the signs that I need to replace my vacuum's brush roll?

If your vacuum's brush roll becomes dirty with hair and debris, it's time to replace it. The brush roll ensures proper airflow and prevents clogging, so regular maintenance keeps your vacuum effective. 

Inspect the brush roll monthly, and replace it when it shows significant wear or when it's no longer able to effectively move hair and dirt away from the machine. 


### Can I use attachments to vacuum cushions separately?

Most couch vacuums come with attachments that can remove dust and debris from cushions effectively. Attachments like crevice tools and soft brushes work well on cushions without damaging the fabric. 

For deep cleaning cushions, consider removing them and washing them according to the manufacturer's instructions. The vacuum attachments can then be used to maintain the cushion's surface. 


### What should I check before buying a new couch vacuum?

Key features to consider include suction power, attachments for various cleaning needs, and brush roll design to prevent tangles. Look for vacuums with HEPA filtration for improved air quality and effective allergen removal. 

Consider the vacuum's weight, ease of use, and whether it's suitable for both plush and tight-weave fabrics. Ergonomic designs and lightweight construction can make regular cleaning more manageable. 


### How do I properly store my couch vacuum?

Store the vacuum in a cool, dry place when not in use. Remove all attachments and brushes, and wrap the hose around the body of the vacuum to keep it organized. 

Keep the vacuum's brushes and attachments clean and dry when not in use. Store the brushes in their designated slots or a protective cover to maintain their effectiveness for future use.
