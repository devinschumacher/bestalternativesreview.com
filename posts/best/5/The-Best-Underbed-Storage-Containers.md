## FAQ


### What is an underbed storage container?

Underbed storage containers are specialized boxes designed to fit beneath standard mattress foundations, providing discreet, organized storage for bedding, linens, or seasonal items. These containers typically feature-locking lids and come in various sizes to accommodate different bed frames and storage needs. 

Most containers measure between 40-72 inches in length to match standard bed frames, with depths ranging from 10-20 inches for optimal under-bed positioning. Width variations typically mirror common mattress dimensions, allowing for easy sliding under the bed frame. 


### How do you measure your underbed space?

To determine the appropriate container size, measure the available space beneath your bed frame, including any existing foundation or slats. Subtract the height of your mattress and foundation from the total under-bed height to ensure the container will fit comfortably. 

Measure the length and width of your bed frame's lowest point, accounting for any decorative moldings or raised edges. This measurement will help you select a container that fits snugly beneath your bed frame without obstruction. 


### What materials are underbed containers made from?

Most underbed storage containers are constructed from tough, weather-resistant materials like polypropylene or high-density polyethylene. These plastic variants resist scratches, cracks, and warping from seasonal temperature changes. 

Some premium models incorporate metal frames or reinforced corners for added durability, while others feature insulated walls to maintain consistent temperatures for food storage or winter bedding. The choice of material often depends on the intended use and environmental conditions. 


### How much weight can an underbed container hold?

Standard underbed containers are designed to support weights between 100-250 pounds, depending on the specific model and material construction. This capacity is adequate for storing linens, seasonal clothing, and small appliances like space heaters or humidifiers. 

For heavier items or specialized storage needs, consider containers with reinforced bases or additional support brackets. Always refer to the manufacturer's specifications to ensure the container meets your weight requirements for safe storage. 


### What types of locks are available for underbed containers?

The most common locking mechanisms for underbed containers include latching systems with magnetic closures, padlocks with locking levers, and combination locks with digital displays. These locks range from simple, single-point closures to multi-lock systems for enhanced security. 

Consider the lock type based on your storage needs and security concerns. Magnetic closures offer convenient, quick access, while padlocks provide superior protection for valuable or sensitive items. Digital locks combine convenience with security, making them ideal for shared spaces or professional storage solutions. 


### How do you clean and maintain underbed containers?

Regular maintenance helps preserve the integrity and appearance of your underbed container. Clean the exterior with mild soap and water, ensuring all parts are thoroughly dried before reassembly. For plastic containers, avoid harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners that may damage the surface. 

Check the interior and exterior for wear, cracks, or loose parts regularly. Replace seals or gaskets as needed to maintain a tight fit and prevent leakage. Store containers in a dry environment when not in use to prevent moisture damage and maintain optimal performance.
