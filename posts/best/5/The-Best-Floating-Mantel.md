## Buyer's Guide

A floating mantle adds an airy elegance to any living space while cleverly concealing cables and mounting equipment. This guide outlines key considerations to help you select the right floating mantle for your room. 


### Size and Proportions

Measure your wall space to determine the ideal mantle dimensions. The mantle width should complement your room size â€“ generally, 1-1.5 times the fireplace width looks balanced. Consider the height-to-width ratio, usually between 1:3 to 1:4, to maintain visual harmony.

### Construction Materials

Common materials include wood, composite, and metal. Wood offers natural beauty but requires regular maintenance. Composite materials combine durability with minimal upkeep. Metal provides a sleek alternative, though it may not suit all decorative styles. 


### Hidden Features

Look for mantles with internal channels or frames to conceal wires and hardware. Some designs include LED lighting channels or adjustable shelving. These features enhance functionality while maintaining a clean aesthetic.

### Installation Requirements

Check the mantle's weight capacity and mounting needs. While lightweight models can be wall-mounted, heavier units may require concrete anchors or wall studs. Consider the electrical requirements, such as built-in receptacles or USB ports, if desired.

### What is a floating mantle?

A floating mantle is a design technique that creates the visual effect of a fireplace mantel suspended in space, giving the appearance of floating or levitating. This architectural element can transform any room by adding depth and dimension. 

The floating effect is achieved through precise engineering that conceals the support structure behind the mantle, allowing the front-facing elements to appear unsupported and airy. This design approach requires careful consideration of load-bearing capacities and structural integrity. 


### How is a floating mantle constructed?

The construction of a floating mantle begins with determining the appropriate support structure, which may include steel beams, hidden brackets, or specialized mounting hardware. These support elements are carefully positioned and reinforced to ensure the structure can safely carry the weight of the mantle material.
