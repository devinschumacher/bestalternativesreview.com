Belgique cookware typically features heavy-gauge aluminum or stainless steel construction. Aluminum cores provide excellent heat conductivity, while stainless steel ensures durability and easy cleaning. Premium sets often include reinforced rims and stainless steel interiors for lasting quality. 


### Size and Compatibility

Standard Belgian cookware sets range from 7 to 11 pieces, including pots, pans, and utensils. Consider your kitchen space and storage capacity when selecting a size. Most sets have matching colors and designs, with common diameters from 10 to 14 inches. 


### Heat Distribution and Temperature Control

Well-designed Belgian cookware maintains consistent heat distribution with gradual temperature changes. Look for sets with even heating properties and appropriate thickness for your cooking needs. Cast iron inserts in some pieces help prevent hot spots and promote even cooking. 


### Handling and Maintenance

BELGique cookware requires minimal maintenance. Non-stick surfaces typically feature PTFE-free ceramic coating for safer cooking. Hand wash recommended, using mild detergent and soft sponges to preserve the cookware's condition. Regular seasoning and proper storage prevent warping and maintain optimal performance.
