## FAQ


### What are the main ingredients in granite cleaners and sealers?

Most granite cleaners contain water, mild surfactants, and added ingredients like enzymes to remove specific types of stains. The primary component in sealers is a penetrating material that coats the stone surface, preventing liquids from being absorbed. Common sealant types include silicones, silicones with organic modifiers, and water-based sealers. 

High-quality products often include additives like UV protectants and corrosion inhibitors. The specific formula can vary significantly between brands and product lines, so it's important to read the labels carefully to determine if the product contains ingredients suitable for your granite surface. 


### How do cleaners and sealers differ in their effects on granite?

Cleaners are designed to remove dirt, grime, and surface stains from granite without altering its appearance. They typically contain mild surfactants that break down soil and can be effective against common stains like water spots and light organic matter. 

Sealers protect granite by creating a barrier that prevents liquids from penetrating the surface. This protection helps maintain the stone's color integrity and makes it easier to clean, as oils and spills don't become permanently embedded. Both cleaners and sealers can be used together, but it's important to follow the manufacturer's recommendations for proper application and compatibility. 


### Can I use household cleaning products on granite?

While some household cleaners may work on granite, they're often too harsh and can damage the stone's protective seal or leave residue that attracts dust and dirt. Granite-specific cleaners are designed to be gentle while effectively removing soil and maintaining the stone's appearance. 

Always test any cleaner on an inconspicuous area first to ensure it doesn't etch, dull the surface, or remove the protective seal. The ideal product will clean effectively without requiring aggressive scrubbing or leaving any residue behind. 


### How often should I reapply granite sealant?

The frequency of reapplication depends on several factors, including the type of sealant used, traffic patterns, and exposure to liquids. Most manufacturers recommend reapplying sealant once or twice a year for high-traffic areas and yearly for areas with lower usage. 

Regular reapplication is particularly important in kitchens, bathrooms, and other spaces where large amounts of water and spills occur. Pay attention to signs that resealing may be needed, such as visible liquid beading on the surface or a dull appearance indicating the sealant has worn thin. 


### Is it safe to use natural stone cleaners on different types of stone?

Naturestone cleaners are generally safe for use on natural stone surfaces like granite, marble, and limestone when used as directed. These products contain mild detergents and are typically safe for common stone types unless specifically labeled as marble-specific or requiring a different formulation. 

Always check the product's label and compatibility information. Some natural stone cleaners contain acidic ingredients that can etch certain stone types if not used correctly. Natural stone retailers or manufacturers can provide guidance on the most suitable products for specific stone types. 


### What signs indicate a product isn't suitable for granite?

Signs that a product isn't suitable for granite include visible etching or dulling of the surface after use, excessive residue that attracts dust, and a noticeable change in the stone's appearance. If the product causes any of these issues, it's not appropriate for granite. 

Watch for specific warnings on the product label, such as instructions to avoid use on granite or marble, and look for certifications or recommendations from stone manufacturers. Products that contain strong acids, degreasers, or bleach should be avoided when cleaning granite.
