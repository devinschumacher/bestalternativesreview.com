## Buyer's Guide

Cleansing conditioners combine the benefits of a traditional conditioner with mild cleaning properties, leaving hair clean and nourished. They're particularly beneficial for those who wash their hair daily or use harsh styling products, as they help maintain hair health while removing residue. 


### Cleaning vs. Nourishing Ingredients

Look for cleansing conditioners that use mild surfactants as their cleaning agents, such as cocamidopropyl betaine or decyl glucoside. These are less harsh than sulfates but still effective at removing dirt and product buildup. Nourishing ingredients like argan oil, shea butter, or ceramides should make up the majority of the ingredient list to ensure your hair remains moisturized and healthy. 


### Texture and Application

These products typically have a thicker texture than regular conditioner, resembling a lightweight lotion. Apply it to wet hair, focusing on the ends and scalp, and massage gently. Unlike traditional conditioners, you'll need to leave the cleansing conditioner on for at least 2-3 minutes for the cleaning properties to take effect before rinsing thoroughly. 


### Compatibility with Hair Types

Cleansing conditioners work well for all hair types, though they may be especially beneficial for oily scalps or those with styling products that can leave residue. Those with very dry or damaged hair might find regular use too harsh and should consider alternating with a traditional conditioner once a week. 


### Environmental and Ethical Considerations
