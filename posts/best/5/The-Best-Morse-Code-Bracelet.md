Morse code bracelets can transmit a variety of signals, from simple letters and numbers to internationally recognized distress codes. ### Bracelet Materials

Patterns can range from simple letters and numbers to complex distress signals. Basic bracelets display common letters and numbers, while advanced models include detailed codes for quick reference. The pattern clarity affects readability and functionality, with higher-quality bracelets featuring durable, raised patterns that resist wear. ### Care and Maintenance

Morse code bracelets require minimal care to maintain their readability and condition. Regular cleaning with mild soap and water keeps the patterns clear, while storing the bracelet in a dry place prevents damage from moisture. The bracelet's material dictates specific maintenance requirements, with stainless steel needing occasional polish to retain its luster and silicone requiring protection from extreme temperatures. Proper care ensures the bracelet remains legible and functional for years to come.
