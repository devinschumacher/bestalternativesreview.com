## FAQ


### What is a dog car harness?

A dog car harness is a type of safety gear worn by dogs while traveling in vehicles. Unlike traditional collars, a car harness distributes stress across the dog's chest and shoulders, providing better support and preventing strain on the neck. 

It consists of a chest strap that sits across the dog's chest and shoulders, with optional attachments for the leash in the back. This design helps prevent choking, restricts movement, and keeps the dog safely seated during travel. 


### Why use a dog car harness?

A harness provides excellent control and safety for your dog during car rides. It's particularly important for small or pets with sensitive necks, as traditional collars can pose significant risks. 

During sudden stops or turns, a harness keeps your pet securely in place, reducing the risk of injury from movement. It's also essential for dogs who pull on their leash or have a tendency to jump out of the car. 


### How does a dog car harness differ from a collar?

The main difference lies in the points of contact - while collars apply pressure around the neck, harnesses distribute weight across the chest and shoulders. This reduces the risk of cervical strain and other neck-related injuries. 

Harsh collars can become caught on seatbelts or objects in the car, potentially causing severe choking hazards. Harnesses are specifically designed to work with seatbelts and prevent such accidents. 


### Can I use a regular dog harness for car travel?

While any harness is better than no harness, specialized dog car harnesses are designed for vehicle travel and may not provide adequate safety. Regular harnesses often lack the necessary back attachment points and may sit too high on the dog's neck. 

Professional car harnesses have specific features like wider chest straps, secure back attachments, and padded components to prevent pressure points. These specialized designs ensure optimal safety during travel. 


### How do I properly fit a dog car harness?

Measure your dog's chest, just behind the front legs, and select a harness that allows one or two fingers to fit between the strap and your dog's body. The harness should fit snugly but not be tight enough to cause discomfort. 

Adjust the back strap to prevent the harness from riding up on the dog's neck. The chest harness should sit level and not slip forward or backward. Ensure all straps are correctly positioned before use. 


### How long should I use a dog car harness?

Always use a harness for vehicle travel until your pet is completely comfortable and restrained. This typically takes 1-2 short trips to acclimate to the harness. 

Continue using the harness whenever traveling in a vehicle, even after your pet seems relaxed. This reinforces good travel habits and ensures your pet remains safely restrained during all trips.
