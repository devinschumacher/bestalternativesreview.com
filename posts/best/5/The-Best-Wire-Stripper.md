## FAQ

![Wire-Stripper-4](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Wire-Stripper-4/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What is a wire stripper and how does it work?

A wire stripper is a specialized tool used to remove the insulation from electrical wire ends. It features a cutting blade and insulator removal mechanism, allowing you to safely expose wire cores while preserving the wire's integrity. 

The tool works by positioning the wire in the appropriate groove, then applying pressure to cut through the insulation while keeping the blade away from the wire core. This design prevents damage to the wire while efficiently removing insulation. 


### What are the main types of wire strippers available?

There are two primary types of wire strippers: manual and power-operated. Manual strippers are handheld tools that require physical pressure to remove insulation, while power strippers use electricity to automate the cutting and stripping process. 

Manual strippers are available as fixed-blade, compound-action, and stripper/cutter combinations. Power strippers come in corded and cordless models, offering adjustable settings for different wire gauges. 

![Wire-Stripper-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Wire-Stripper-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What factors should I consider when choosing a wire stripper?

Key factors include wire-gauge capacity, cutting length options, blade material (usually stainless steel or aluminum), and tool design. Consider whether you need precision for small gauge wires or power for thick cable stripping. 

Additional features like insulated handles, ratcheting mechanisms, and wire-flattening channels can improve safety and efficiency. The size and weight of the tool also impact user comfort during extended use. 


### How do I properly maintain a wire stripper?

To maintain your wire stripper, regularly inspect the blades for dullness or wear. Clean the blades with a wire brush to remove any embedded insulation particles. For power strippers, check the motor and electrical connections according to the manufacturer's specifications. 

Avoid using incorrect gauges, which can damage the tool, and keep the blades lubricated with a suitable petroleum-based cleaner. Periodic maintenance ensures optimal performance and extends the tool's lifespan. 

![Wire-Stripper-6](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Wire-Stripper-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What safety precautions should I take when using a wire stripper?

Always use wire strippers with appropriate voltage ratings for your working environment. Wear protective gloves and eyewear to prevent cuts and electrical shock. Keep loose clothing and jewelry away from the blade area. 

Never strip wire while the tool is in your pocket or resting on a metal surface. Cut one wire at a time to maintain control, and store strippers securely to prevent injury from dropped tools. 


### How can I choose the right wire stripper size?

Wire gauge determines the correct stripper size. For standard household wiring (14-22 AWG), a compound-action stripper with 25-30mm cutting length is suitable. Industrial work may require specialized tools or larger capacity strippers. 

When in doubt, select a tool with adjustable settings that accommodate a range of wire sizes. This flexibility provides better control and prevents damage to expensive tools from attempting to strip incompatible wire gauges.
