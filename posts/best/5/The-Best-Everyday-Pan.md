## FAQ

### What makes a good everyday pan?

A quality everyday pan should combine durability with versatility, featuring thick walls and a heavy base for even heating. The material should resist warping and scratching, with non-stick options providing easy food release. Pans with stay-cool handles and oven-safe properties enhance both safety and convenience. 

Common materials include stainless steel, cast iron, and hard-anodized aluminum. Look for multi-ply construction for superior heat distribution and dishwasher-safe options for easy maintenance. The pan's size and shape should match your cooking needs, from small sauté pans to large saucepans. 


### Can I use metal utensils with an everyday pan?

Most everyday pans, especially non-stick and brushed stainless steel varieties, are designed to be used with metal utensils. Using metal is generally safe and can help prevent food from sticking. However, always check the manufacturer's guidelines to ensure compatibility with your specific pan model. 

For cast iron and some specialty cookware, it's recommended to use wood or silicone utensils to avoid damaging the surface. Always remove food from the pan before cleaning to prevent scratching. 

![Everyday-Pan-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Everyday-Pan-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Which sides of the stove work best with an everyday pan?

Everyday pans perform well on both gas and electric stoves due to their sturdy construction. The key is to match the pan's size with your cooktop's available space. For gas stoves, consider the pan's height to ensure proper flame clearance. On electric stoves, look for a flat-bottomed pan with good heat conductivity. 

When using an everyday pan on a cooktop, position it in the center of the burner for even heating. This helps prevent hot spots and ensures consistent cooking results. Always maintain a safe distance between the pan's handles and the cooktop edges. 


### How do I properly store an everyday pan?

Store everyday pans vertically in a kitchen cabinet to prevent warping and scratching. Use a pan rack or wrap each pan in a protective cloth to avoid direct contact with other cookware. If space allows, store smaller pans in a pan caddy or similar organizer for easy access. 

For pans with non-stick surfaces, avoid stacking or storing heavy items on top. This prevents damage to the cooking surface and ensures proper ventilation during storage. Consider using silicone protectors between stacked pans to add an extra layer of protection.

### What maintenance is required for an everyday pan?

Regular maintenance ensures your everyday pan maintains its performance and appearance. For non-stick pans, wash with gentle soap and a soft sponge, avoiding harsh abrasives that can damage the coating. For solid-surface pans like stainless steel and cast iron, simple dish soap and warm water usually suffice. 

After each use, hand wash the pan to remove food residue. For stubborn grime, soak the pan in warm, soapy water before cleaning. For cast iron, dry completely after each use and apply a thin layer of oil to prevent rust. Store pans properly to prevent oxidation and maintain their condition. 


### How do I choose the right size pan for my cooking needs?

Selecting the right size pan depends on your cooking volume and cooktop space. Common sizes range from 6 to 12 inches, with 7-inch pans ideal for single servings and 12-inch pans suitable for family meals. Consider the pan's heat capacity when choosing between small and large sizes. 

For sautéing or pan-frying, a 9-inch pan works well for most dishes. Larger pans excel at simmering sauces and boiling water, while smaller pans are space-efficient for everyday cooking. Always refer to recipe specifications and consider the pan's material properties when selecting the appropriate size.
