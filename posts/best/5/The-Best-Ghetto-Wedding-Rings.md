### What are ghetto wedding rings?

Ghetto wedding rings, also known as hip-hop jewelry, refer to a style of jewelry that gained popularity in the hip-hop culture during the 1990s and early 2000s. These rings are characterized by their large sizes (often over 20 carats), thick bands, and simple, bold designs. The term "ghetto" reflects their association with working-class African American culture. 

These distinctive rings can be made from various materials, including gold, platinum, and white gold, often featuring diamond accents or other precious stones. Their design typically includes square or rectangular settings with multiple stones or engravings.

The cultural significance also varies, with traditional bands representing marital commitment, while ghetto rings can symbolize wealth and status in certain communities.
