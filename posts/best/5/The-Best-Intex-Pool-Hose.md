## Buyer's Guide

Connecting your pool cleaning equipment to your pump requires a reliable Intex pool hose that can handle various water pressures and resist damage from UV rays and chemicals. When selecting the right hose, consider these key factors to find a durable solution. 


### Material Durability

Intex hoses are available in PVC and rubber materials. PVC hoses offer excellent chemical resistance and are less expensive, making them suitable for basic pool maintenance. Rubber hoses provide superior flexibility and durability but come at a higher cost. For heavy-duty use or environments with harsh chemicals, rubber hoses are recommended. 


### Length and Size Compatibility

Measure the distance between your pump and cleaner to determine the appropriate hose length. Most pools require 25-50 feet, but longer distances may be necessary for some installations. Check the hose's inner diameter to match your pump's specific requirements, typically ranging from 1.5 to 2 inches. Narrower diameters can increase water pressure but reduce flow rate. 


### Connector Types

Standard fittings include BSP (British Standard Pipe) and NPT (National Pipe Thread). Ensure the hose's connectors match your pump and cleaning equipment. Some modern hoses feature quick-connect adapters that allow easy attachment and detachment without manual tightening. 


### UV Resistance and Protection

Extended sun exposure degrades most materials, including PVC and rubber, through a process called photoaging. Look for hoses with built-in UV protection or protective sleeves to extend their lifespan. Hoses without adequate protection may need replacement after one summer season in direct sunlight.
