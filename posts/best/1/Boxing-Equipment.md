### What are the differences between a punching bag and a speed bag?

A punching bag and a speed bag are two distinct pieces of boxing equipment, each designed to serve a specific purpose. A punching bag is typically made of heavy-duty materials, like vinyl or leather, and can be hung from a stand, ceiling, or wall. Punching bags are designed to withstand repeated punches and kicks, making them ideal for practicing different techniques, including jabs, hooks, and uppercuts. 

On the other hand, a speed bag is a small, cylindrical bag that is generally 10-12 inches in length. Speed bags are typically made of leather or synthetic materials and are filled with air or sand. They are designed to swing quickly and return to the user's hand after being punched, allowing boxers to improve their hand speed and reflexes. Speed bags are usually used in conjunction with other boxing equipment, like focus mitts or heavy bags, to create a comprehensive training routine.
