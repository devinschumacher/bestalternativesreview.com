### How do I clean my black tea kettle?

Cleaning a black tea kettle is relatively simple and involves a few steps. First, unplug the kettle and let it cool down completely. Once it's safe to handle, fill the kettle with water, add a small amount of vinegar, and bring the solution to a boil. The vinegar will help to break down any mineral deposits and prevent future buildup. If there is residual buildup, you may need to use a soaking technique with a mixture of water and vinegar, allowing it to sit for a few hours before rinsing. 

After cleaning the kettle, rinse thoroughly with water to remove any residual vinegar. If you still notice any residue or staining, you can use a soft sponge or cloth dipped in warm soapy water to gently scrub the inside. It's also a good idea to dry the kettle thoroughly after cleaning to prevent rust formation. Follow the manufacturer's instructions for any specific cleaning recommendations. 


### Can I use a black tea kettle on gas stove?

Yes, you can use a black tea kettle on a gas stove. The cast iron material is designed to work on various types of heat sources, including gas stoves. However, if you plan to use your kettle on a gas stove, make sure to use a stove-top trivet or put the kettle on a level surface to prevent it from tipping over. Always follow the safety guidelines provided by the manufacturer and ensure proper ventilation when using your kettle on a gas stove. 

It's also important to inspect your kettle regularly for any signs of wear and tear, especially if you're using it on high heat for extended periods. Proper maintenance will help prolong your kettle's lifespan and ensure optimal performance every time you use it. Remember to always unplug or turn off the gas stove when you're finished using your kettle.

### What is the best way to store my black tea kettle?

Proper storage of your black tea kettle is crucial to maintain its quality and extend its lifespan. When not in use, make sure your kettle is completely dry, then let it cool down before storing it. If you live in a humid environment, consider storing your kettle in a cool, dry place, such as a closet or pantry, away from direct sunlight and heat sources.

The lifespan of a black tea kettle depends on various factors, such as the quality of the material, the frequency of use, and the level of maintenance.
