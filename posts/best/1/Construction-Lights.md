### What's the difference between a floodlight and a spotlight?

Floodlights and spotlights are both types of lighting fixtures, but they have distinct features and applications. The primary difference between them is the area they illuminate and the shape of the beam produced. Here's a brief summary of each light type and its characteristics: Floodlights create a wide, diffused light beam that covers a broad area. They're commonly used for illuminating open areas, such as streets, parking lots, and outdoor work zones. Spotlights distribute a more precisely controlled light beam with a narrower spread. They're ideal for accentuating specific areas, such as a piece of artwork or architecture, and they're often used in theaters, museums, and other display environments. 

Both floodlights and spotlights can come in different wattages, power sources, and materials, and their performance depends on the manufacturer and the specific model. When choosing between them, consider your specific lighting needs and preferences to find the right solution for your construction project.
