### What are dehumidifiers used for?

Dehumidifiers are designed to remove excess moisture from the air, making them ideal for use in spaces with high humidity levels. They are frequently used in basements, crawl spaces, and other areas with damp conditions. ### How do dehumidifiers work?

Dehumidifiers use one of two primary methods to remove moisture from the air: compression cooling or absorption refrigeration. Compression cooling works by compressing a refrigerant gas, which causes it to evaporate and absorb moisture from the air. This moist gas, now in its liquid state, is then condensed back into a liquid and drained from the unit. Absorption refrigeration, on the other hand, uses a desiccant or absorbent material to attract and hold moisture from the air. The process creates a pressure differential that drives the damp air through the dehumidifier, trapping excess moisture in the desiccant. Once the absorber becomes saturated, it's normally heated to release the moisture. 

Additionally, most dehumidifiers have a built-in fan that circulates air, improving the distribution of moisture removal throughout the room it's in. Some models also features a built-in humidistat, which allows for automatic shutoff once the desired humidity level is reached. Overall, dehumidifiers are designed to make it easier to maintain a comfortable and healthier indoor environment by reducing moisture levels.
