### What is an Air Hose?

An Air Hose is a flexible pipe engineered to transport compressed air from a compressor to instruments or equipment. These hoses play a vital role in various sectors, such as automotive, manufacturing, and construction, enabling tasks like painting, scrubbing, or operating pneumatic tools effectively and quickly. Compressed air is channeled through the hose, which is manufactured from durable materials, such as rubber or polyurethane, enabling it to withstand the pressures associated with moving compressed air.

Different lengths, diameters, and materials characterise Air Hoses, tailored to various applications, industries, and environmental conditions. They come equipped with quick-connect couplings that make connecting and disconnecting the hose to the compressor and tools easy and convenient. Choosing the ideal Air Hose for specific uses, like the operating distance, flow rate, and pressure, ensures optimal efficiency and performance.


### What are the different types of Air Hoses?

Air Hoses can be categorised primarily based on the materials they are made from, the application, and the pressure ratings. Commonly, they are classified into PVC, Polyurethane, and Rubber Air Hoses. PVC Air Hoses are lightweight, flexible, and cost-effective, making them suitable for general-purpose uses. Polyurethane Air Hoses exhibit greater resistance to abrasion, chemicals, and UV radiation, making them suitable for harsher conditions and industrial applications.

Air Hoses also vary by their pressure ratings, such as low, medium, and high-pressure Air Hoses. High-pressure Air Hoses can manage pressures up to 250 psi or more, while low-pressure Air Hoses are designed for pressures as minimal as 15 psi. The pressure rating significantly impacts the work's performance and the tools that the Air Hose can power efficiently. Properly understanding the specifications of the Air Hose is essential for selecting the right one for the job.
