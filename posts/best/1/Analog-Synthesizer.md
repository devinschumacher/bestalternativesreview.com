### Overview of Analog Synthesizers

Analog synthesizers are electronic musical instruments that use voltage-controlled oscillators (VCOs) and voltage-controlled filters (VCFs) to generate and manipulate sounds. They have been popular since the 1960s and 1970s and are still widely used today in various music genres, from electronic dance music to synthwave.

### Important Features of Analog Synthesizers

When considering an analog synthesizer, some key features to look for include the number of voices, oscillators, and filters the instrument has. The number of voices determines the maximum number of notes the synth can play simultaneously, with monophonic synths capable of only one note at a time and polyphonic synths capable of multiple notes. The number of oscillators and filters directly affect the sound's complexity and richness.

### What is an analog synthesizer?

An analog synthesizer is a type of electronic musical instrument that generates sound by synthesizing, or combining, various audio waveforms. It creates these waveforms by manipulating electrical signals using a combination of voltage-controlled oscillators, filters, and amplifiers. Unlike digital synthesizers, which rely on digital technology to generate and manipulate sound, analog synthesizers use analog circuits to create their unique sound. 

Popular analog synthesizers include the Moog Model D, ARP 2600, and Roland Jupiter-8.
