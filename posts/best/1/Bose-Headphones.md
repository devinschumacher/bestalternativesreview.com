### How does Bose's noise-canceling technology work?

Bose has developed a proprietary noise-canceling technology called Active Noise Reduction (ANR), which is implemented in most Bose headphones. ANR uses a combination of hardware and software algorithms to generate an opposing frequency of sound that effectively cancels out external noise. This is done by comparing sounds outside the headphone with sounds inside the ear and then utilizing electronics to generate a soundwave with an opposite amplitude but the same frequency as the external noise. 

The headphone's microphones pick up external sounds and send them to the ANR circuit, which then modifies and adds the opposing soundwave to the headphones. This cancels out the external noise without affecting the audio being played through the headphones. Bose's noise-canceling technology is one of the most advanced in the industry and is highly regarded for its ability to reduce environmental noise and improve the overall listening experience.
