### What is rosin, and why do I need it for cello strings?

Rosin is a natural resin that is applied to the bow hair to help the bow "grab" the cello strings and produce sound. It also helps to prevent the bow from slipping on the strings while you play. Without rosin, the bow hair would have difficulty generating friction and couldn't produce the necessary sound on the strings. 

Using rosin is essential for maintaining the proper contact between the bow and the strings. Choose a high-quality rosin that best suits your playing style and preferences. Some rosins are harder for a more focused tone, while others are softer for a more rounded sound. A qualified luthier can provide guidance on selecting the right rosin for your playing style and cello.
