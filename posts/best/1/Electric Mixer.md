### What is the difference between a stand mixer and a hand mixer?

The primary difference between a stand mixer and a hand mixer lies in their functionality and design. A stand mixer has a fixed position on the countertop, usually with a base that raises and lowers the mixing bowl. The motor is powered by either an electricity source or a battery. Stand mixers generally have more capacity, multiple attachments, and a wider range of speed settings that make them versatile for different tasks. 

Hand mixers, on the other hand, are lightweight and portable, with a motorized unit held by the user. They are corded or cordless devices, powered by batteries or plugged in. Hand mixers offer less capacity, fewer attachments, and fewer speed settings than stand mixers. However, they are suitable for smaller batches, portable use, and quick mixing tasks.
