### What is the difference between a mid-calf and a knee-high boot?

The primary distinction between a mid-calf and a knee-high boot lies in their height, but they also vary concerning styling and occasion. Mid-calf boots, also referred to as calf-length boots, reach up to the middle of the calf, generally stopping just below the knee. These boots serve as a mid-point between ankle boots and knee-high boots and are perfect for most casual and semi-formal events. 

In comparison, knee-high boots or over-the-knee boots, as the name suggests, extend up to or even over the knee. These boots are characterized by a more statement-making and edgy appearance, ideal for a night out or a special occasion.
