### What are essential oils?

Essential oils are concentrated liquids extracted from the seeds, bark, stems, flowers, and roots of plants. They contain the plant's natural fragrance and are used in aromatherapy, cosmetics, perfumes, and various other applications. These oils are volatile and easily evaporate, releasing their unique scents into the air. 

The most common essential oils include lavender, peppermint, eucalyptus, tea tree, rose, and citrus oils. They can be used for relaxation, pain relief, stress reduction, and boosting mood due to their uplifting aromas. To use essential oils, they can be diluted with a carrier oil and applied topically, diffused in the air using an essential oil diffuser, or added to bathwater. 


### How do essential oil smells differ from those of synthetic fragrances?

Essential oils have a natural aroma, while synthetic fragrances are man-made, typically derived from petrochemicals. The chemicals used in synthetic fragrances can often mimic the scent of essential oils, but they do not offer the same therapeutic benefits or have the same calming and soothing effects on the body and mind. 

Synthetic fragrances may have strong, overpowering scents that can irritate the skin, nose, or respiratory system. On the other hand, essential oils have a more subtle, delicate aroma that can be more pleasing to the senses and less likely to cause irritation. Additionally, essential oils may have varying intensity, depending on the quality of the oil and the methods used for extraction.
