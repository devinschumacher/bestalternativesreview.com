### What are bath bombs?

Bath bombs are compact, rounded objects created from sodium bicarbonate (baking soda), citric acid, and a variety of essential oils and fragrances. When dropped into a bath, they start to disintegrate, producing bubbles, foam, and an enticing scent. Some bath bombs even contain ingredients such as herbs, flower petals, or sparkling glitter to boost the bathing experience. 

Bath bombs have gained significant momentum in recent years due to their compelling combination of beauty, relaxation, and self-care. They come in various flavors, colors, and scents, making them an ideal choice for pampering sessions and special gifts. 


### Are bath bombs safe for all skin types?

Most bath bombs are safe for all skin types because they are formulated with natural ingredients and do not incorporate harsh chemicals or preservatives. Nevertheless, people with sensitive skin should read labels carefully and conduct a patch test beforehand to avoid potential allergic reactions or irritation. 

Certain bath bombs may include ingredients that could potentially dry out the skin due to their alkaline nature, such as sodium bicarbonate. In such instances, it is advisable to use a moisturizing bath bomb or apply a moisturizing lotion following the bath to preserve skin hydration and balance the pH levels.

### How long do bath bombs take to dissolve in the bath?

The dissolution time of bath bombs varies depending on factors such as the size of the bath bomb, the temperature of the water, and the concentration of ingredients. Typically, bath bombs will begin dissolving within a few minutes of being dropped into the bath, particularly when the water is warmer and the bath bomb has a higher concentration of dissolving agents. 

Larger bath bombs might take longer to fully dissolve, while smaller ones can dissolve rapidly and release their components all at once. Regardless of dissolution speed, it is crucial to allow the bath bomb to dissolve gradually in the bath for an optimal experience, enabling the fragrance, color, and ingredients to create the desired bath atmosphere. 


### Are there any environmental concerns with using bath bombs?

Bath bombs, especially those containing synthetic fragrances and colorants, may include ingredients that can negatively affect aquatic life if not properly disposed of after use. Some elements, such as phthalates (used in synthetic fragrances) and FD&C dyes (used for color), can release chemicals into the water and impact marine ecosystems. 

To reduce environmental concerns, it is recommended to use bath bombs formulated with natural fragrance and color ingredients, like essential oils and plant-based colorants. Always remember to rinse your bath thoroughly after using a bath bomb to avoid any residual chemicals that can impact water quality and harm aquatic life.
