### What are the differences between open and closed-back headphones?

Open-back and closed-back headphones differ in terms of their design, sound quality characteristics, and overall listening experience. Open-back headphones have an open-air design that allows sound to leak out, resulting in a more natural and immersive sound experience. They are typically more spacious and airy, providing users with a sense of depth and richness in their listening experience. 

Closed-back headphones, on the other hand, have a more enclosed design that helps to reduce sound leakage. They are known for their more controlled and precise sound reproduction, making them appealing to those who prioritize accuracy and consistency in their listening experience. While closed-back headphones may not offer the same level of openness and airiness as open-back options, they often excel in noise isolation and provide a more isolated listening experience.

It's a good idea to clean your audiophile headphones regularly, especially if you live in a dusty or humid environment.
