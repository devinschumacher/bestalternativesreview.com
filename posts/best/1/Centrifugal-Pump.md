### How does a centrifugal pump work?

A centrifugal pump works by utilizing a rotating impeller, which is a fan-like component situated inside the pump. As the impeller spins, it creates a pressure gradient that forces fluid to be drawn towards the impeller's center. This motion causes the fluid to accelerate and, as a result, it exerts a pressure on the fluid, pushing it through the impeller and out of the pump. The discharged fluid is typically forced out via an opening at the impeller's center, which is known as the eye of the impeller. The fluid is then routed to the desired location through an outlet or piping system. 

Centrifugal pumps are designed to operate within a specific flow range, which is determined by factors such as pump size, design, and efficiency.
