### How should I clean and maintain my candle stand?

Proper cleaning and maintenance of a candle stand are essential to ensure its longevity and appearance. For metal or glass stands, use a soft, damp cloth with a mild cleaning solution to gently remove any dirt, dust, or wax residue. Avoid using abrasive cleaners or scrubbing the surface too vigorously, as this can scratch or damage the finish. For wooden or other decorative stands, utilize a soft-bristled brush or a damp cloth to remove dirt and dust gently.

If the candle stand has any movable components, such as adjustable legs or a collapsible design, adhere to the manufacturer's instructions for proper assembly and disassembly. Proper maintenance, such as tightening screws or locking mechanisms, can help prolong the life of your candle stand and prevent accidents due to instability or loose parts.


### What safety precautions should I take when using a candle stand?

Safety should always be a top priority when using a candle stand. First and foremost, ensure that the stand is stable and secure and that the candle is the correct size and weight for the stand. Avoid overloading the stand with an excessive number of candles, and ensure there is enough clearance around the base of the stand to prevent accidentally tipping or damage to the stand or surrounding surfaces.

Place the candle stand in a safe location away from flammable materials, drafts, or children and pets. Always supervise the use of candles, especially if young children are present. If the candle does not have a protective glass or metal casing, consider using a candle ring or holder to prevent wax from spilling or dripping. Lastly, never leave a lit candle unattended and extinguish the candle when no longer needed or when the flame becomes too low.
