### What materials are Oxo squeegees made from?

Oxo squeegees typically feature a durable plastic frame with a rubber blade. The plastic parts are usually BPA-free and resistant to cracking and warping. The rubber blades come in various materials, with some featuring a 100% natural rubber for superior water absorption. 

Some models include metal components like spring clips for added durability, while others use ABS plastic for the entire frame. The rubber blades vary in density and stiffness, with some designed for tough grime and others for gentle cleaning. 


### How should I clean an Oxo squeegee?

Regular cleaning involves wiping the blade and frame with a damp cloth or sponge. For stubborn residue, you can use a small amount of mild dish soap followed by rinsing with clean water. Ensure the blade is completely dry before storage to prevent mildew growth. 

Always remove the blade for thorough cleaning, and check the frame for any signs of wear. The blade should be replaced if it becomes dull, develops cracks, or shows signs of deterioration. Regular maintenance extends the life of your squeegee and ensures effective cleaning performance.

### How do I install an Oxo squeegee?

Most Oxo squeegees feature a straightforward blade installation process. Start by aligning the rubber blade with the frame grooves. For models with magnetic blade retention, simply press the blade against the frame, and it should snap into place. Some models use spring clips that hold the blade securely. 

If your squeegee includes a blade release system, carefully remove the blade by pressing the release mechanism. Store the blade in a dry place to prevent damage. Always reattach the blade before use, ensuring it's properly seated in the frame for optimal performance. 


### How long do Oxo squeegees typically last?

With regular use, an Oxo squeegee blade can last between 6 months to a year before needing replacement, depending on usage frequency and cleaning surfaces. The plastic frame typically lasts longer, with proper maintenance and storage. 

Signs indicating blade replacement include dull cutting action, visible cracks, or persistent streaking. The frame shows wear when components become loose, the plastic develops yellowing, or the blade releases easily without pressing the release mechanism.
