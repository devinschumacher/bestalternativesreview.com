Performance in golf balls primarily depends on three factors: cover material, core technology, and dimple pattern. ### Distance vs. Control

Lower-handicappers typically benefit from balls with higher initial velocity and longer carry distances. Titleist offers increased distance through optimized core composition and faster cover materials. Higher-handicappers may prioritize control and consistency, where the ball's trajectory and spin characteristics become more crucial than raw distance. 


### Weather Conditions

The environment significantly affects golf ball performance. Titleist balls featuring Tour-Preferred Urethane (TPU) cover technology perform exceptionally well in various weather conditions, maintaining their distance and feel characteristics whether playing in rain, heat, or cold. Balls with softer urethane covers excel in cooler temperatures, while harder Surlyn covers perform better in hot and humid conditions.

### Ball Care and Durability

Maintenance affects the ball's performance and lifespan. Titleist recommends storing balls in a protective case to prevent scuffs and damage. Regularly cleaning the ball removes grass stains and debris that can affect performance. For maximum durability, avoid mixing balls of different types and replace them when they show signs of wear, typically after 30-50 rounds.
