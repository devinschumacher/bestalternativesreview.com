## Buyer's Guide

A USB-C docking station with dual monitor support combines multiple connectivity options into a single, convenient solution for modern workstations and creative setups. These versatile hubs enable users to expand their computing capabilities while maintaining clean desk organization. 


### USB-C Connectivity and Power Delivery

USB-C ports offer both data transfer and power delivery in a single, reversible connector. For dual monitor setups, ensure the docking station supports DisplayPort Alternate Mode over USB-C, allowing both video outputs and additional power delivery to connected displays. 


### Ethernet and USB Port Diversity

Networking capabilities are crucial for stable internet connections, with USB-C docks often offering Gigabit Ethernet ports. The availability and number of USB ports (Type-A and Type-C) determine how many devices can be simultaneously connected and powered. 


### Video Output Support

Modern USB-C docks can deliver 4K resolution to connected displays, though performance may vary with video encoding and display technology. Consider whether the docking station supports multi-stream video protocols like DisplayPort MST for enhanced video distribution. 


### Audio and Display Features

Integrated USB-C docks often include 3.5mm audio ports and HDMI outputs as alternative display options. For audio, ensure the dock supports both video and audio passthrough for connected displays, and check HDMI capabilities (2.0b for 4K support) if USB-C video solutions are limited.
