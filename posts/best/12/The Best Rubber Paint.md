## FAQ


### What is rubber paint?

Standard rubber paint is a type of water-based coating that contains elastomer compounds, which give it superior flexibility and durability compared to traditional paints. It can stretch up to twice its original length without cracking, making it highly resistant to deformation from temperature changes, impact, or movement. 

The paint forms a robust barrier against water, mold, and mildew, thanks to its elastic properties. This makes it particularly effective in areas exposed to moisture, such as bathrooms, kitchens, and around plumbing fixtures. 


### What makes rubber paint different from regular paint?

Rubber paint combines water-soluble polymer binders with elastomeric modifiers that create its distinctive stretchability. This molecular structure allows rubber paint to adhere more strongly to surfaces and resist cracking under stress, unlike conventional paints that may chip or peel. 

During application, rubber paint behaves similarly to regular paint but cures into a film that can extend and contract with the substrate without splitting or detaching. This unique property makes rubber paint suitable for irregular surfaces and areas prone to movement. 


### Where should I use rubber paint?

Due to its excellent water resistance and flexibility, rubber paint is ideal for interior applications in damp environments. It performs especially well in: bathrooms (showers, around sinks, tubs), kitchens (around dishwashers, counters), and utility rooms (laundry areas, storage spaces). 

While suitable for many indoor surfaces, rubber paint might not be appropriate for outdoor use due to exposure to UV light and variations in temperature. Always verify the product label before application. 


### How is rubber paint applied?

Rubber paint is applied much like traditional paint using brushes, rollers, or sprayers. The key to achieving a quality finish is ensuring proper surface preparation with sanding and priming before application. Standard drying times are similar to regular paints, typically 30 minutes between coats and 24 hours for full cure. 

After application, rubber paint forms a smooth, elastic film that maintains its flexibility and waterproof properties. Multiple thin coats generally provide better results than one thick coat, allowing for even curing and full film development. 


### Do I need special tools for rubber paint?

Most standard painting tools work well with rubber paint. High-quality brushes and rollers with stiff bristles or nap provide the best results, as they effectively distribute the thick, flexible paint. Tapes and masks can be used for edges and trim, but specific adhesives are not required for application. 

Proper cleaning tools include specialized rubber paint removers for difficult jobs, and standard wash brushes for daily maintenance. Always check manufacturer recommendations for specific tool requirements and cleaning agents. 


### How long does rubber paint last?

With proper application and maintenance, rubber paint typically lasts 10-15 years in ideal conditions, comparable to other quality interior paints. The flexible nature of the coating helps prevent cracking and peeling, even in areas with significant movement or temperature changes. 

Protection from harsh chemicals and UV exposure extends the lifespan of rubber paint. Routine maintenance such as cleaning and touch-ups help maintain the original appearance and performance of the painted surface.
