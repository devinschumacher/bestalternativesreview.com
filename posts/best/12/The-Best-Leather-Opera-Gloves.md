## FAQ


### What materials are used to make leather opera gloves?

Quality leather opera gloves are typically made from full-grain cowhide, which provides durability and a natural look. Some modern options may use premium sheepskin for better flexibility and warmth. 

The leather is often treated with protective coatings to prevent water damage and preserve the glove's shape. Glycerin-based treatments are popular for their effectiveness and minimal impact on the leather's natural feel. 


### How do I properly care for leather opera gloves?

Dry leather opera gloves by laying them flat or hanging them by the wrists. Avoid direct heat sources like radiators or hair dryers, as they can damage the leather. 

Apply a small amount of leather conditioner monthly using a clean cloth. Gently rub the conditioner into the leather, avoiding overly absorbent areas, and wipe off any excess with a soft cloth. 


### What sizing options are available for leather opera gloves?

Leather opera gloves typically offer three standard sizing options: small, medium, and large. Some brands provide additional half-sizes between medium and large. 

Proper fitting is crucial. The glove should feel snug without being tight, and the wrist strap should sit just below the wrist bone when you make a fist. 


### How do I determine my glove size?

Measure the circumference of your hand at the base of your fingers, around the thickest part of the palm. Add 1/2 inch for small, 1 inch for medium, and 1/2 to 1 inch for large, depending on your preference for snugness. 

Remember that leather gloves may stretch slightly with regular wear, so it's generally best to err on the side of a slightly smaller size if you have wide hands. 


### What occasions are leather opera gloves appropriate for?

Leather opera gloves are traditional attire for formal evening events, particularly classical music performances and theater productions. They add a touch of ceremonial regality to your outfit. 

While primarily traditional, they can also work for certain modern formal occasions or elegant everyday wear, especially in colder climates. 


### Are leather opera gloves machine washable?

Machine washing leather opera gloves is generally not recommended as it can damage the finish and stretch the material. Most manufacturers recommend hand washing with mild soap and warm water. 

Wash each glove separately to prevent tangling, and reshape them while slightly damp to maintain their form. Allow them to air dry completely before wearing.
