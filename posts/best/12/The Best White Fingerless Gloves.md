### How do I care for white fingerless gloves?

Hand washing is recommended to preserve the gloves' color and shape. Use mild detergent and cold water, avoiding bleach or harsh chemicals that can damage the fibers.

### Can I wear white fingerless gloves in the winter?

While primarily designed for mild weather, basic white fingerless gloves can provide adequate protection in cooler temperatures with light activities. Look for models with a thin layer of insulation or moisture-wicking properties for better cold-weather performance. 

For winter activities, consider pairing the gloves with gloves or mittens for additional warmth while maintaining finger dexterity. 


### How do I choose the right size?

Measure around your wrist where you'd typically wear the gloves, then add about 1-inch for a comfortable, non-restrictive fit. Most brands offer standard sizing, with medium usually fitting both men's and women's average wrist sizes. 

If you find the gloves are too loose, consider sizing down; if too tight, size up. It's also helpful to try them on if possible, as different brands can have varying amounts of stretch.

These gloves excel for tasks that require finger dexterity and hand protection, such as gardening, painting, or crafting. They're particularly useful when you need to maintain grip on tools or slippery surfaces while keeping your hands cool.
