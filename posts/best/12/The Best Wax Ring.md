## FAQ

![Wax-Ring-4](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Wax-Ring-4/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What is a wax ring?

A wax ring is a cylinder shaped wax gasket used to create an airtight seal between a toilet flange and the toilet base. The wax ring compresses slightly when the toilet is installed, forming a water-tight seal that prevents sewer gases from entering the home. 

This essential plumbing component is usually made from petroleum-based wax and comes in both standard and noise-reducing varieties. The size and type of wax ring needed depends on the specific toilet and flange configuration. 


### How do I install a wax ring?

To install a wax ring, first thoroughly clean both the toilet flange and the underside of the toilet. Place the wax ring directly over the flange, making sure it's centered. Then, carefully lower the toilet onto the wax ring, allowing it to sit evenly on the floor with about a 1/8-inch gap all around. 

Slightly rock the toilet back and forth while securing it with nuts and bolts, which will compress the wax ring and create an airtight seal. Avoid over-tightening, as this can damage the wax ring and the toilet flange. 

![Wax-Ring-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Wax-Ring-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### How long does a wax ring last?

A properly installed wax ring typically lasts 10-20 years under normal household conditions. Factors that can affect its longevity include water quality, toilet usage frequency, and the quality of the wax ring itself. 

Signs that a wax ring may need replacement include persistent toilet leaks, noisy toilet operation, or difficulty in moving the toilet back and forth. To check the wax ring, rock the toilet slightly - if water seeps around the base, the ring needs replacement. 


### What's the difference between standard and noise-reducing wax rings?

Standard wax rings are the most common type, providing basic water-tight sealing capabilities. They're suitable for most residential applications and typically cost less than their noise-reducing counterparts. 

Noise-reducing wax rings incorporate materials designed to dampen toilet movement and minimize flange rotation. They're particularly beneficial in homes with older or flexible toilet flanges, as well as in multi-story buildings where toilet movement can create noise. 

![Wax-Ring-6](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Wax-Ring-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Can I reuse a wax ring?

It's generally not recommended to reuse a wax ring, as it can become compressed, contaminated, or deteriorate over time. Additionally, even if the ring appears unchanged, its properties may have degraded during the toilet's previous installation. 

If you're reinstalling the same toilet in the same location, it's safe to use the original wax ring. However, for any other installation or if you're unsure, it's best to use a new wax ring to ensure proper sealing and longevity. 


### How do I remove a toilet to check the wax ring?

To check the wax ring, you'll need to first disconnect the toilet's water supply and flush it completely. Then, using a snake or plumber's friend, carefully work around the toilet's base to break the seal between the wax ring and flange. 

Once the toilet is loose enough, rock it back and forth slightly to break any remaining seal. If you notice water seeping around the base or between the wax ring and flange, it's time to replace the wax ring before reinstalling the toilet.
