### What's the difference between USB 2.0 and USB 3.0 cables?

USB 3.0 cables offer significantly faster data transfer speeds (up to 5 Gbps) compared to USB 2.0 (480 Mbps), making them ideal for faster charging and data-intensive operations. 

USB 3.0 cables are also more resilient to interference due to their improved signaling design, while USB 2.0 cables remain the standard for basic accessory connectivity. 


### How many USB ports does the Xbox One have?

The base Xbox One console features two USB 2.0 ports - one for system charging and one for accessory connectivity. Some newer models may include additional ports. 

USB ports support various accessories including gamepads, controllers, and external storage devices.

### What type of power adapter does Xbox One use?

The Xbox One requires a standard wall adapter with a voltage of 100-240V and 1.8A output, featuring a proprietary connector specifically designed for the console. 

No alternative power solutions are officially supported; using unauthorized adapters may damage the system. 


### How long do Xbox One power cables typically last?

Official Xbox One power cables are built to last, with a typical lifespan of 2-3 years under normal use. Regular use may shorten this period. 

Symptoms of cable failure include frequent power issues, poor charging, or system instability. Replace cables showing visible wear or malfunction.
