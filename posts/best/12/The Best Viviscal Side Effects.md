### Most Common Side Effects

The primary reported side effects of Viviscal include temporary digestive disturbances such as nausea, diarrhea, and stomach upset. These symptoms are usually mild and resolve on their own within a few days of use. Some users may also experience increased flatulence or changes in bowel habits.

### Rarer Side Effects to Monitor

Less common side effects of Viviscal may include skin rashes, itching, or hives. These allergic reactions are typically localized and subside when use of the product is discontinued. More serious side effects such as chest pain, difficulty breathing, or severe swelling should be evaluated by a healthcare provider immediately. 


### Interactions with Other Medications

Viviscal contains biotin, which can interact with certain blood sugar-lowering medications and reduce their effectiveness. People taking insulin or other diabetes medications should monitor their blood sugar levels closely while using Viviscal. Additionally, biotin may interfere with vitamin B12 absorption, so those with B12 deficiency should consult their doctor before use.

### Special Considerations for Pregnant or Nursing Women

Pregnant or nursing women should use Viviscal only under the guidance of a healthcare provider. While the supplement is generally considered safe for women who are planning to become pregnant, its effects on fetal development are not yet fully understood. The safety of Viviscal during nursing has not been extensively studied, and its components may pass into breast milk.
