## Buyer's Guide

Dinosaur balloon decorations can bring your party to life, but with so many options available, it can be tough to choose. This guide will help you find the perfect dinosaur balloons for your event, covering everything from sizing and material to inflation and display options. 


### Material and Construction

Most dinosaur balloons are made from PVC or latex. PVC balloons are more durable and float longer, while latex balloons offer brighter colors but require more frequent replacement. Look for balloons with reinforced tails and sealed ends for better stability. 

![Dinosaur-Balloons-2](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Dinosaur-Balloons-2/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Sizing and Quantity

Standard sizes range from about 12 inches to 30 inches in diameter. Consider how many guests you're inviting and the space you have available. As a general rule, aim for one balloon per 4-5 guests. For larger events, you might also want a few larger decorative balloons measuring 36 inches or more. 


### Inflation and Care

Proper inflation is crucial for both appearance and longevity. Use a pump with a gauge to get the right air pressure. Over-inflating can cause balloons to burst, while under-inflating results in sagging. Store balloons in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heaters to prevent premature deflation. 

![Dinosaur-Balloons-3](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Dinosaur-Balloons-3/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Display Options

Think about how you want to display your balloons before you buy. You can hang them individually or in clusters from ceilings, walls, or gates. Consider using balloon weights to keep them in place, especially outdoors. For added effect, pair your balloons with helium to create floating displays, but be aware of local regulations regarding helium use.
