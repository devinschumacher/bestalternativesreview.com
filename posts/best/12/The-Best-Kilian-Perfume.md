### Heart Notes and Their Role

Heart notes emerge as the top notes fade, usually becoming most prominent between 30 minutes and 2 hours after application. These mid-tones form the core of a fragrance's character. Common heart notes include floral scents (roses, jasmine, lilies), warm spices (vanilla, cinnamon), and fresh woods (cedar, sandalwood). Analyzing these can give you insight into a fragrance's overall personality and structure.

Base notes develop after several hours and provide long-term character to the fragrance. They're often rich, warm, and dry-down properties that reveal the final stages of a scent's development. Common base notes include musk, vanilla, amber, patchouli, and various woods. The strength and choice of base notes significantly influence how a fragrance ages and how it's perceived over time.
