### What are John Sarno books about?

John Sarno's books focus on his controversial theory that many chronic pain conditions are psychosomatic, meaning they result from unconscious emotional conflicts rather than physical pathology. His work emphasizes the mind-body connection and the psychological origins of physical symptoms. 

Key themes include the "Tension Myositis Syndrome, " which Sarno attributes to stress and repressed emotions. His books offer both explanations for pain and alternative approaches to treatment, challenging conventional medical thinking.

### What medical background does John Sarno have?

John Sarno held a Doctor of Medicine degree from New York University and practiced as a neurologist at New York University Medical Center from 1965 to 2003. His clinical experience provided the foundation for his theories on the mind-body connection in pain perception. 

While his medical practice focused on neurology, Sarno's approach evolved to incorporate psychological interpretations of physical symptoms, leading to the development of his distinctive treatment methods. 


### Are John Sarno's books widely recognized in the medical community?

John Sarno's books have generated significant controversy within the medical community. While some practitioners and patients find his theories valuable, many traditional medical professionals criticize his approach as unproven and potentially harmful. 

The scientific community generally lacks controlled studies supporting Sarno's psychosomatic theory, though his work has inspired further research into the mind-body connection in pain conditions.
