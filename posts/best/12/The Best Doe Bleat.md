The typical doe bleat is a high-pitched, rising-pitch vocalization that can vary in intensity and duration. These bleats function across different contexts, from simple greetings to alarm signals. ### Context-Specific Doe Bleats

Recognizing the context surrounding a bleat provides valuable information about its meaning. A contented doe might use a soothing, rhythmic bleat during social interactions, while a doe in estrus will produce distinct, repeated bleats to attract males. Bleats of alarm occur in response to perceived threats and are characterized by higher pitch and intensity. 


### Age and Individual Differences

Goats of different ages produce bleats with varying characteristics. Kids typically emit more frequent and higher-pitched bleats compared to adults. Additionally, each goat has its own "vocal fingerprint" influenced by genetics and experience, making individual recognition possible.
