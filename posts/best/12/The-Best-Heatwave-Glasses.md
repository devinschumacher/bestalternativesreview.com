### What are Heatwave glasses designed to do?

Heatwave glasses are specialized protective eyewear that shield your eyes from intense heat sources, such as arc welding, plasma cutting, and other industrial processes. They maintain clear visibility while providing essential thermal protection. 

The glasses combine a high-impact safety lens with advanced heat-resistant coatings to prevent heat transfer and reduce glare, ensuring optimal visibility in extreme environments. 


### Who should use Heatwave glasses?

Heatwave glasses are primarily recommended for professionals who work with high-heat sources, including welders, metal fabricators, and industrial maintenance workers. They are also suitable for anyone who performs tasks in extremely hot environments where standard protective eyewear is insufficient. 

The glasses are not recommended for everyday use outside of industrial settings, as they are optimized for specific heat protection applications.

### What makes Heatwave glasses different from regular safety glasses?

Unlike standard safety glasses, which focus on impact protection and basic UV filtration, Heatwave glasses incorporate specialized heat-resistant materials and coatings. This allows them to maintain clear visibility while protecting against temperatures that would damage conventional lenses. 

The glasses typically feature a dual-layer construction with a polycarbonate impact-resistant lens and an anti-reflective coating specifically designed to prevent heat penetration. 


### How do Heatwave glasses prevent heat damage?

Heatwave glasses prevent heat damage through a combination of protective features. The lens material can withstand extreme temperatures without warping or cracking, while thermal barrier coatings reflect radiant heat away from the lens surface. 

This dual approach of material resistance and surface coating creates a barrier that maintains clear visibility while protecting the wearer's eyes from heat damage.

### Can Heatwave glasses be worn indoors?

### How long do Heatwave glasses last?

Heatwave glasses typically have a lifespan of 2-3 years with regular use, depending on the frequency and intensity of exposure to heat sources. The protective coatings and lens materials can degrade over time with repeated use.
