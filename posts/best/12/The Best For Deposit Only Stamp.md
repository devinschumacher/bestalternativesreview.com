## FAQ


### What is a for deposit only stamp?

A for deposit only stamp, also known as FD stamp, is a specialized stamp used in financial transactions where funds need to be held in a dedicated account before being transferred to another party. This stamp indicates that the funds are held in trust until specific conditions are met. 

These stamps are commonly used in real estate transactions, escrow services, and other financial agreements where parties need to hold funds safely until all contractual obligations are fulfilled. 


### What are the legal requirements for using a for deposit only stamp?

The legal requirements vary by jurisdiction, but generally, the stamp must be applied when funds are held in trust or deposited according to specific terms of an agreement. The stamp's use must be clearly documented in relevant legal and financial contracts. 

Financial institutions and legal professionals are typically responsible for ensuring compliance with local regulations regarding for deposit only stamp usage and documentation. 


### How should a for deposit only stamp be applied?

The stamp should be affixed to relevant financial documents, deeds, or contracts where the funds are held in trust. It should cover crucial sections that detail the terms of the deposit and the conditions for release of funds. 

Proper placement of the stamp ensures its legibility and maintains the integrity of the financial document's content while clearly indicating the trust-based nature of the transaction. 


### Can a for deposit only stamp be reused?

Financial regulations generally prohibit the reuse of for deposit only stamps to prevent misrepresentation of financial transactions. Each use should be documented separately to avoid confusion and maintain proper accounting records. 

Financial institutions typically maintain strict policies regarding stamp usage and retention to ensure compliance with legal and regulatory standards. 


### What are the alternatives to using a for deposit only stamp?

Financial institutions and professionals may use alternative methods to indicate trust-based transactions, such as electronic documentation, notarization, or specific language in contracts that clearly defines the nature of the deposit. 

While stamps provide a physical record, digital signatures and electronic platforms are increasingly used to document financial transactions securely and efficiently. 


### How can one verify the authenticity of a for deposit only stamp?

Authenticity verification requires examining the stamp's design, size, and any unique identifiers specific to local regulations. Financial institutions typically maintain records of valid stamps and can assist with verification. 

Professional appraisers or legal experts can also authenticate stamps, ensuring their proper use in financial transactions.
