## FAQ


### What makes a ruby ring valuable?

Ruby value primarily depends on the "four Cs": color intensity, carat weight, cut quality, and clarity. The most valuable rubies have a deep, saturated red color without hints of purple or brown. Carat weight affects size, while cut determines how the stone's facets reflect light. Clarity refers to internal inclusions - fewer inclusions usually mean higher value. 

Additional factors include the ruby's origin (Burma rubies are historically most prized), treatments (unheated rubies command higher prices), and certification from reputable gemological institutions. Many professional jewelers can provide detailed reports explaining a specific ring's value. 


### How should I clean and care for a ruby ring?

Ruby is quite durable, ranking 9 out of 10 on the Mohs hardness scale. Daily care includes wiping the ring with a soft cloth after each wear to remove surface oils and dirt. For deeper cleaning, use mild soap and warm water, avoiding harsh chemicals and ultrasonic cleaners which can damage the setting. 

Store the ring separately to prevent scratches from other jewelry pieces. Harder materials like diamonds or sapphires should never touch rubies, even in settings designed for multiple stones. Proper storage reduces the risk of damage and keeps the ring looking its best. 


### What styles of settings complement ruby rings?

Rubies look especially striking in simple, classic settings that highlight their natural beauty. Prong settings with four or six prongs are particularly effective, allowing more light to reach the stone's surface. Bezel settings also protect the gem and maintain a sleek profile. 

Tutorial: For a modern look, consider straight prong settings with equal-length prongs. This style shows off the ruby's profile and pairs well with both solitaire and multi-stone rings. For traditional settings, six-prong configurations are slightly more secure and add a touch of vintage appeal. 


### What's the best way to choose a ruby ring size?

Accuracy is key when sizing a ruby ring. The ideal fit should be snug yet comfortable, allowing about a quarter size for daily wear and some movement within the setting. The ring should sit flat on the finger when not being worn, preventing wobbling or potential loss. 

Size guide: Most men find size 11 to 12 suitable, but consult with a jeweler who can measure your specific finger. Note that different bands may run slightly different sizes, so test multiple styles before finalizing your choice. 


### How do synthetic ruby rings compare to natural ones?

Synthetic rubies, created through intentional laboratory processes, replicate the chemical structure of natural rubies. While not as rare, they're generally more affordable and less susceptible to thermal stress from extreme temperature changes. Natural rubies form underground over millions of years, making each unique and valuable. 

Quality distinction: Lab-grown rubies can be of superior color quality due to controlled growth conditions, but natural specimens often have better internal clarity and less prominent inclusions. Both options are legitimate gemstone choices, with pricing reflecting their respective characteristics and market demand.
