## FAQ


### What is a hover board and how does it work?

A hover board is a personal transportation device featuring two inline wheels connected by a board. It uses gyroscopes and weight sensors to detect your movements, allowing you to glide in different directions by tilting your body. The board's motors then adjust the speed of the wheels to maintain balance and movement. 

Modern hover boards use lithium-ion batteries that provide power to both the motors and LED lights. Some models also incorporate Bluetooth connectivity for music streaming and app compatibility for additional features like augmented reality experiences. 


### How safe are hover boards?

 Hover boards can be unsafe if not used correctly. Falls are common, especially for beginners, so it's important to wear protective gear like helmets, knee pads, and elbow protectors. The risk of injury increases with poor maintenance or use of counterfeit batteries, which can overheat and pose fire hazards. 

To reduce risk, always use the hover board on a flat, dry surface and follow the manufacturer's guidelines for maintenance and usage. Avoid riding on uneven terrain or during rainy weather when the board's sensors may not function properly. 


### What's the average hover board runtime?

Most hover boards have a runtime of 8-20 minutes, depending on the model and weight of the rider. Lighter riders typically get a full charge, while heavier users may see reduced runtime. The board's LED display usually indicates remaining battery life, helping you plan your journey and find a convenient spot to recharge. 

Battery performance can vary based on environmental factors like temperature and the quality of the battery. Original equipment manufacturer (OEM) batteries generally provide more consistent performance than third-party replacement batteries. 


### How do you maintain and care for a hover board?

Regular maintenance includes checking battery connections, cleaning the wheels and board surface, and inspecting the gyroscope components. Store the board in a dry place when not in use, and avoid exposing it to extreme temperatures. Replace the battery after 3-5 years of regular use, depending on how often you ride. 

Follow the manufacturer's guidelines for cleaning and battery charging. Do not submerge the board in water or attempt to repair any electronic components. These steps can help extend the life of your hover board and ensure it operates safely. 


### What's the weight limit for hover boards?

Most hover boards have a weight limit between 110-220 pounds (50-100 kg), but this varies by model. Heavier riders may experience reduced performance or shorter ride times due to increased stress on the motors and battery. Always check your board's specifications before purchasing to ensure it can safely support your weight. 

Manufacturers determine weight limits based on safety standards and motor capacity. Riding above the recommended weight can damage the board and compromise your safety, so it's crucial to respect these guidelines. 


### Are hover boards suitable for children?

Most hover boards are not recommended for children under 12 years old due to the complex balance system and potential for falls. If you decide to let a child use a hover board, supervise their use and ensure they wear appropriate protective gear. The board should be well-maintained and used on a flat, safe surface. 

Some manufacturers offer child-specific models with lower weight limits and simplified controls, but these are still not ideal for very young children. Always consider the child's physical maturity and coordination before allowing them to use a hover board.
