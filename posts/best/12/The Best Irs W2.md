## FAQ

![Irs-W2-4](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Irs-W2-4/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What is an IRS W-2 form?

The IRS W-2 form is a wage and tax statement issued by employers to their employees each year. It provides detailed information about the employee's wages, taxes withheld, and other payroll-related data. 

This form is crucial for both employees and employers as it serves as a record-keeping tool for tax purposes, supporting accurate tax calculations and filings. 


### When are W-2 forms issued?

W-2 forms must be issued by the employer to each employee by January 31st of the following year. This form corresponds to the employee's earnings for the previous calendar year. 

The employer is required to file a copy of each W-2 with the Social Security Administration (SSA) by February 28th, unless an extension is granted. 

![Irs-W2-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Irs-W2-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What information is included in a W-2 form?

Each W-2 contains essential details such as the employee's name, social security number, address, and gross wages earned during the year. It also lists federal and state taxes withheld, as well as any other deductions or credits applied. 

The form provides a summary of the employee's income and withholdings for the previous year, helping both the employee and employer prepare for tax season. 


### How do I obtain a copy of my W-2?

Employees are typically provided with a copy of their W-2 at the end of January. If you need a replacement copy, contact your employer's HR department or payroll office. 

You can also request a replacement form electronically through the IRS website using an Online Request for Wage and Income Statement system (Request Form 4898). 

![Irs-W2-6](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Irs-W2-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What should I do with my W-2?

Keep your W-2 in your tax documents and store it securely for at least three years. It's important to maintain accurate copies of all tax-related documents for potential audits or tax filing purposes. 

Use the information provided to reconcile your records and complete your tax return accurately. The W-2 serves as a backup to your own records and helps verify your tax calculations. 


### What if my W-2 information is incorrect?

Notify your employer immediately if you notice any errors on your W-2. The employer is responsible for providing accurate information and may need to correct the form if mistakes are found. 

If the error affects your tax situation, you may need to file an amended return (Form 1040X) to correct the information. Keep copies of all correspondence with your employer regarding the correction process.
