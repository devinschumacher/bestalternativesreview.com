### Size and Fit

Proper fit is essential for both performance and protection. Measure your hand's length and circumference to find the right size. Consider wearing game socks to get an accurate feel for how the glove will sit on your hand. The thumb crotch should allow movement without bunching, and the heel should fit snugly to prevent slap. 


### Hand Position and Web Design

The web space between the thumb and forefinger should match your natural hand position for the most comfortable grip. Different web designs affect how the glove pockets the ball: - Single-web gloves provide better protection but may reduce accuracy

- Two-web gloves offer improved pocketing but less overall protection

- Quarter-web designs strike a balance between both options

### How do I break in a Wilson baseball glove?

Breaking in a Wilson baseball glove is crucial for discomfort-free performance. Start by soaking the glove in water for 10-15 minutes, then use your fingers to mold the pocket to your preferred shape. Continue to practice catches and stretch the glove daily until it conforms to your hand. 

Apply leather conditioner regularly to the glove's exterior to prevent drying and cracking. The conditioner also helps the glove maintain its shape after breaking it in. Avoid excessive stretching or excessive heat, which can damage the leather and affect the glove's structure.

Wilson recommends selecting a glove size based on your position and hand size. Catchers typically need gloves between 11-12.5 inches, while infielders usually prefer 11-12 inches. Outfielders generally use 12-13 inch gloves, providing the additional reach needed for fly balls.

### How can I maintain the condition of my Wilson baseball glove?

Proper maintenance extends the life of your Wilson baseball glove and keeps it performing at its best. After each use, clean the exterior with a soft-bristled brush and mild soap, then apply leather conditioner to the outside and break-in oil to the inside to maintain suppleness. 

Clean the interior with a toothbrush and mild soap, then air dry both surfaces away from direct heat sources. Store the glove in a protective cover when not in use and avoid stacking books or heavy items on it. The glove should be kept in a cool, dry place to prevent damage to the leather.
