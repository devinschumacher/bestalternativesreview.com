Common congestion medicines include decongestants, antihistamines, and saline nasal sprays. The choice of medication depends on the underlying cause of congestion and individual symptoms. 


### How do decongestants work?

Decongestants work by contracting blood vessels in the nasal passages, which reduces swelling and opens up the airways. This action helps alleviate nasal congestion and sinus pressure. 

These medications are available in both oral and nasal spray forms. It's important to follow the recommended dosage instructions to avoid side effects such as increased blood pressure and reduced urine output. 


### What are the side effects of congestion medicine?

The most common side effects of congestion medicine include increased heart rate, drying of the nasal passages, and insomnia. Some people may experience headaches, dizziness, or an upset stomach. 

Extended use of nasal decongestant sprays can lead to rebound congestion, where the nasal passages become more swollen than before treatment. It's important to follow the recommended usage guidelines and consult a healthcare provider if symptoms persist. 


### Who should avoid congestion medicine?

Individuals with high blood pressure, heart disease, thyroid disorders, or enlarged prostate should use congestion medicine with caution and under medical supervision. These conditions can be exacerbated by certain ingredients in these medications. 

Pregnant and breastfeeding women should also consult their healthcare provider before using congestion medicine, as some ingredients may affect fetal development or infant health.
