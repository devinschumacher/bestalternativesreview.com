## Buyer's Guide

Wilson Staff CB irons combine traditional design elements with modern technology for both experienced players and those transitioning from hybrids. These irons prioritize feel and workability while offering sufficient forgiveness. 


### Key Design Characteristics

Wilson's CB irons maintain a classic appearance with a slightly thinner topline and a more traditional heel-toe weighting compared to modern cavity-back designs. This construction helps players maintain their swing habits while still benefiting from increased ball speed through a combination of face angle and crown design.

### Technology Features

These irons incorporate a thin, forged face with a precision-milled design that enhances feel and control. The club features a pronounced reverse swept hosel that reduces weight in the center of the club, allowing for a higher moment of inertia and increased forgiveness on off-center hits. 


### Materials and Construction

The CB irons utilize a 1020 carbon steel face for optimal impact and sound, while the body construction combines stainless steel and 455 stainless steel for durability and weight distribution. The clubs employ a deep face design that increases the sweet spot size without compromising accuracy.

### What makes the Wilson Staff CB Irons unique?

The Wilson Staff CB Irons feature a distinct centerback design that combines a sleek appearance with optimized forgiveness. These irons utilize a compact, thin face construction with precision milling to enhance ball striking consistency. 

The club's unique shape is designed to reduce unnecessary weight, allowing the manufacturer to redistribute mass for improved forgiveness while maintaining a traditional iron look.

The CB Irons are available in four different iron sets: 3-9, 4-9, 5-9, and 6-9. Each club features a stainless steel face with carbon steel body construction. The lofts range from 20° to 60°, with matching gaps between each club for clean ball separation.

### How does the CB Iron's centerback design affect performance?

The centerback design reduces unnecessary face weight, allowing more material to be concentrated at the heel and toe for improved off-center hit forgiveness. This innovative approach allows for a thinner face profile while maintaining ample MOI (Moment of Inertia) for high-endurance ball striking. 

The precise milling patterns on the face optimize gear effect at impact points, resulting in higher launch angles and increased ball speed compared to traditional iron designs of similar size. 


### What are the main considerations when selecting a Wilson Staff CB Iron?

Players should consider their current swing speed, ball flight issues, and preferred iron set length when choosing between the different available sets. The 3-9 set is suitable for players with lower swing speeds or those seeking a complete set of irons, while shorter sets offer more specialized options for specific swing characteristics. 

Consider trying out different sets in a showroom or during a fitting to determine which length best matches your swing mechanics and playing style.
