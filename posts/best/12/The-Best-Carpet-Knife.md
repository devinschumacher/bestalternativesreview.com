## FAQ


### What is a carpet knife used for?

A carpet knife is specifically designed for cutting carpet, underlayment, and padding. Its primary uses include trimming carpet edges, creating precise cuts, and making notches for tacks or nails. 

The blade is typically rectangular with a dull edge, preventing accidental punctures. The handle provides excellent grip and control during cutting tasks. 


### How do I maintain a carpet knife?

Regular maintenance ensures your carpet knife remains sharp and safe to use. After each use, clean the blade with a dry cloth and store it in a secure sheath or holder to prevent damage. 

Sharpen the blade using a honing stone or metal plate when needed. The dull edge should be maintained for safety, so avoid aggressive sharpening that could create a dangerous point. 


### What's the difference between carpet knives and box knives?

While both have similar rectangular blades, carpet knives feature dull edges specifically for carpet cutting. Box knives have standard sharp blades, making them unsuitable for the specialized tasks required by carpet work. 

Carpet knives also typically have shorter blades and better grip designs, optimized for working with rolls and large pieces of carpet material. 


### Can I use a carpet knife for other cutting tasks?

Due to its specific design, a carpet knife is not recommended for general cutting tasks. The dull blade and shorter length make it less effective for materials like wood, metal, or thick paper. 

For versatility, consider using a box knife for general cutting and reserving the carpet knife for its intended applications. 


### How should I choose the right carpet knife?

The most important factor is blade length - typically between 3-4 inches for carpet work. Longer blades provide more cutting surface but less control, while shorter blades offer precision but require more frequent cutting. 

Handle design should provide a secure grip, with materials like plastic or rubber offering good comfort and durability. Consider the overall weight and balance of the knife for comfortable use. 


### What safety precautions should I take with a carpet knife?

Always handle a carpet knife with care, keeping the dull blade facing away from your body. Use a cutting mat or stable surface to prevent slipping and maintain control. 

When making cuts, apply even pressure and avoid sudden movements that could cause loss of control. Keep the blade in its sheath when not in use and store it out of reach of children and pets.
