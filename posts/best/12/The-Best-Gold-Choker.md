## FAQ

### What is a gold choker?

A gold choker is a narrow neckpiece that sits just below the neck's base, typically measuring between 14-20 inches in circumference. Unlike traditional necklaces, chokers hug the neck closely, creating an elegant and sophisticated look. 

When measuring for a gold choker, it's crucial to measure where you want the necklace to sit on your neck, as different designs may vary slightly in width and placement. 


### What materials are commonly used in gold chokers?

Most gold chokers are crafted from solid gold, 14k or 18k, with some featuring yellow, rose, or white gold finishes. The clasp typically matches the main metal piece and may include additional decorative elements like diamonds or enamel. 

A few contemporary designs incorporate alternative metals like sterling silver or platinum, combined with gold accents or alternative materials like pearls, gemstones, or acrylic. 

![Gold-Choker-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Gold-Choker-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### How should a gold choker sit on my neck?

A properly-fitted gold choker should sit flat against the skin just below the neck's natural curve, typically 3-4 inches above the clavicle bone. The chain should not sag but should still allow some movement when tilting your head. 

For double gold chokers, the second necklace should sit closely above the first, creating a layered effect that emphasizes the structure of the neck while maintaining balance. 


### What are the sizing options for gold chokers?

Most gold chokers come in fixed lengths ranging from 14 inches (1/2 inch wider than a standard collar) to 20 inches. Some manufacturers offer adjustable options with hidden clasps or extender chains that can be customized between 1/2 to 1 inch. 

When purchasing online, always provide your specific neck measurement to ensure the best fit, as sizing charts can vary between brands.

### How do you wear a gold choker with clothing?

Wear a gold choker with high-necked tops and dresses to keep the focus on the necklace. For lower necklines, pair with a simple turtleneck or a high-collar blouse that doesn't overpower the piece. 

If layering multiple necklaces, position the gold choker as the outermost piece, allowing it to catch light and draw attention naturally. 


### What maintenance is required for gold chokers?

Clean your gold choker weekly with a soft cloth and mild soap, avoiding abrasive materials that could scratch the surface. Store the necklace in a lined jewelry box or dry pouch to protect it from dust and tangling. 

Check the clasp regularly for wear, ensuring all joints move smoothly. If you notice any looseness or damage, have the piece repaired by a professional jeweler to maintain its integrity and extend its life.
