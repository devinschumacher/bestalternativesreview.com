## Buyer's Guide

When selecting a Rambo survival knife, several key factors determine its effectiveness for outdoor and survival situations. Understanding these features helps users choose a blade that matches their needs while ensuring safety and reliability. 


### Blade Design and Materials

Rambo knives typically feature durable, corrosion-resistant blades made from high-quality steel, such as 1095carbon or similar formulations. The blade design balances cutting efficiency with durability, often incorporating features like fuller blades for strength and saw teeth for quick cutting through thick materials.

### Housing and Construction

The knife's housing protects the blade and provides a secure grip. Look for knives with robust, non-slip handles made from materials like G10 composite, aluminum, or stainless steel. The housing should securely lock the blade in place when not in use, preventing accidents during transport. 


### Weight and Balance

Survival knives need to be practical for extended use. Heavier blades offer more cutting power but can be tiring to wield, especially in rough terrain. The ideal balance depends on personal preference, with most users preferring a knife that weighs between 1 and 2 pounds.

### Additional Features

Enhancements like a fire-starting steel strip, lanyard attachment point, or built-in whetstone improve a Rambo's functionality. These features can be crucial in survival situations, providing multiple tools in a single blade. The knife's overall length and clip design also affect usability, with shorter blades more convenient for quick access while clips help secure the knife on clothing.

### What maintenance requirements do Rambo survival knives have?

Regular maintenance is crucial for maintaining the blade's edge and overall functionality. Users should clean and dry the knife after each use, lubricate moving parts, and sharpen the blade when needed. 

Everbilt (Rambo's parent company) recommends professional blade sharpening every 6-12 months and replacing blades when they show excessive wear. 


### How do Rambo survival knives compare in durability to other outdoor knives?

Rambo knives are constructed with premium materials like 1075 high carbon steel and 420 stainless steel, offering superior edge retention and resistance to corrosion compared to many outdoor knives. 

However, their durable design comes at the cost of weight and bulk, making them less practical for casual outdoor use compared to lighter, more specialized knives.
