## FAQ


### What is a Teslong Borescope?

A Teslong borescope is a specialized inspection tool that allows users to visually inspect the interior of pipes, engines, and other small spaces. It consists of a thin, flexible tube with an embedded camera that transmits images to a display screen for real-time viewing. 

The system typically includes a main unit with a screen, a camera probe, and lighting. These portable devices are designed for quick and efficient inspection of industrial equipment, automotive parts, and medical devices where direct visual access is restricted. 


### How does a Teslong borescope work?

A Teslong borescope functions by inserting the camera probe into the target area. The camera captures images through its lens, which pass through a series of lenses and mirrors before being displayed on the attached screen. This optical system allows for magnified viewing of internal structures without physical obstruction. 

The system often features adjustable lighting options, including fiber optic illumination, to enhance visibility in dark or poorly lit environments. Some models include additional tools like magnetic attachments or measuring calipers for extended functionality. 


### What are the main components of a Teslong borescope?

The basic components of a Teslong borescope include the camera probe, main unit with display screen, and lighting system. The camera probe contains the imaging sensor and lens system, while the main unit processes the video feed and displays it on a screen. Additional components may include cleaning kits, adapters, and specialized attachments. 

The camera probe typically has a flexible shaft ranging from 0.5 to 1.5 meters in length, with diameters between 4 and 10 millimeters. The main unit usually incorporates controls for zoom, focus, and image capture, as well as storage capabilities for video and image files. 


### What are the key features to consider when selecting a Teslong borescope?

When choosing a Teslong borescope, several key features should be considered: the minimum and maximum viewing depth, image resolution, light source quality, and included accessories. The camera's field of view and probe diameter affect accessibility to different inspection areas. Some models offer wireless connectivity for video transmission, while others include built-in storage and measurement tools. 

The lighting system's versatility impacts image quality in various lighting conditions. Consider the borescope's durability, with options for waterproofing and dust resistance. Budget constraints, intended use case, and frequency of use also influence the selection process. 


### How do I maintain and care for a Teslong borescope?

Proper maintenance of a Teslong borescope involves regular cleaning of the camera probe and main unit to prevent dust and debris buildup. The optical components should be cleaned using appropriate solutions and soft cloths to avoid damaging the lenses. The lighting system requires periodic inspection of bulb function and cable integrity. 

Storage should be in a dry, dust-free environment when the borescope is not in use. The camera probe should be coiled properly to prevent kinking and stored with the main unit to maintain calibration. Regularly check the equipment for wear and tear, and address any issues promptly to prevent degradation of image quality and functionality.
