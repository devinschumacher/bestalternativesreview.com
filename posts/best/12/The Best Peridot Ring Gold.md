### Material Quality

Look for rings made from solid 14k or 18k gold, which provide better durability and value than gold-filled or plated alternatives. The peridot should display a rich green hue with good clarity and minimal inclusions for the highest quality stone. 


### Craftsmanship

Examine the setting for secure prongs and well-finished edges. The gold should be free of blemishes and show consistent coloring without plating. Professional inspection can help verify the metal content and stone characteristics.

### Insurance and Care

## FAQ


### What is peridot?

Peridot is a gemstone variety of olivine, known for its bright green color. It's often called the "gem of the sun" due to its warm hue. 

The stone ranges in color from light yellow-green to dark olive green. High-quality peridot should have an even, medium tone without being too dark or too pale. 


### What metals are peridot rings typically set in?

Peridot pairs well with yellow, white, and rose gold. It's also occasionally set in platinum or silver jewelry. 

Gold settings enhance the stone's natural hue, with yellow gold complementing lighter peridots and white gold or platinum bringing out deeper greens. 


### How should peridot rings be cared for?

Wear your peridot ring regularly, as this helps maintain its polish. Remove it when doing household chores or sports activities. 

When cleaning, use mild soap and warm water. Avoid ultrasonic cleaners and harsh chemicals that can damage the stone or setting. 


### What setting styles are suitable for peridot rings?

Peridot looks great in various settings, including solitaires, three-stone designs, and rings with other green gemstones like emerald. 

Prongs should be open to allow light to pass through the stone, while bezel settings can protect the peridot's corners and edges. 


### What are the most important factors to consider when buying a peridot ring?

Color intensity (the stronger the green, the more valuable), clarity (eye-clean gemstones are most valuable), and cut quality (ideal facets should maximize the stone's brightness). 

Look for a stone with a well-proportioned culet (the bottom point of the cut) and good symmetry. The ring's setting should secure the stone firmly without unnecessary metal exposure. 


### What is the history and significance of peridot in jewelry?

Peridot has been mined since ancient times and appears in the Bible as "gem of the night. " It was believed to protect against nightmares and evoke courage. 

During the Middle Ages, it was called the "evening emerald" due to its similarity in color to that gemstone. Today, it's recognized as the birthstone for August and a popular choice for gift-giving.
