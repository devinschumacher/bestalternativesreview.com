### What is a GPU riser?

A GPU riser is a small adapter that elevates graphics card connectors, allowing for installation in tight spaces where standard connectors wouldn't fit. They're particularly useful for older motherboards with shorter PCIe slots or systems requiring vertical GPU installation. 

GPU risers are available in different heights (1.25", 2", 2.25", etc. ) and support various card lengths. They're compatible with both single- and dual-GPU configurations and work with most modern graphics cards, including RTX and Radeon series. 


### How does a GPU riser work?

The riser board contains additional PCB traces that extend the length of the graphics card's connectors, effectively raising them 1.25" to 2.25" above the original position. This extended height allows compatibility with shorter motherboards or spaces where standard installation wouldn't fit. 

The graphics card's power connectors typically remain in the original position, while the video output connectors are raised through the riser board. This design maintains proper electrical contact while providing the necessary height clearance.
