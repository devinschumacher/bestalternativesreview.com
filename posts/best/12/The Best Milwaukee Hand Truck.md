Most Milwaukee hand trucks range from 200 to 400 pounds of capacity. Consider the maximum weight you need to transport and choose a truck that exceeds this by 20-30%. Compact models are ideal for tight spaces, while larger trucks provide more storage space.

The frame should be made from durable materials like steel for lasting performance. Wheels typically come in two sizes: 4" and 5". Smaller wheels are easier to maneuver in tight spaces, while larger wheels provide better load distribution and reduce ground pressure.

Look for trucks with padded, ergonomic handles that reduce strain during extended use. Some models incorporate adjustable or contoured grips to accommodate different hand sizes. The handle height should allow comfortable reaching without excessive bending. 


### Suspension and Load Distribution

Quality hand trucks feature suspension systems to absorb impact during transport. Single-axle trucks with leaf springs or rubber bumpers provide basic shock absorption, while dual-axle models with independent suspension offer smoother rides.
