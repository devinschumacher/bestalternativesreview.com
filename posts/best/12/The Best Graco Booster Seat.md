## Buyer's Guide

A Graco booster seat elevates your toddler or preschooler in vehicles, ensuring they're properly restrained until they reach the height and weight requirements for seat belts. These ergonomic seats come in highback and backless designs, each with unique features to consider. 


### Key Features

Most Graco booster seats include built-in headrests, shoulder harnesses, and adjustable waist belts. Highback models provide side impact protection and often include cushioning for comfort. Backless seats are lighter and more discreet but require proper lap and shoulder belt positioning. 


### Size and Growth Patterns

Measure your child's height and weight before selecting a booster seat. The seat should accommodate your child until they reach the manufacturer's maximum height and weight limits, typically between 4 and 8 years old and 40-100 pounds. 


### Safety Standards

Ensure the booster seat meets current safety standards, such as FMVSS 213 in the United States. Look for seats with the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) star rating system, with higher ratings indicating better safety performance. 


### Installation and Compatibility

Check the seat's compatibility with your vehicle's seat belts or LATCH system. Some models offer easier installation features like one-hand click-lock bases or tether attachments. Consider the seat's weight and whether it can be left in the vehicle or needs to be regularly transported. 


## FAQ


### What is a Graco booster seat, and how does it work?

Graco booster seats enhance the safety of your vehicle's seat belts by raising your child to a height where the seat belt fits correctly across their chest and lap. They typically feature a built-in harness system that transitions to seat belt use around 40 pounds or when the child reaches 4 years old, whichever comes first. 

The seat positions the lap belt low across the hips and the shoulder belt away from the neck and face, mimicking the fit of an adult seat belt. This helps distribute the force of impact more evenly, providing better protection in the event of a collision. 


### What are the key safety features of Graco booster seats?

Graco booster seats incorporate several safety features, including a five-point harness system for proper positioning, energy-absorbing materials in the seat structure, and padded surfaces to protect the child's head and body. Most models also include impact protection features like padded sides and a reinforced bottom structure to resist collapse during an accident. 

The seats are engineered to meet or exceed federal safety standards for child restraint systems. They undergo rigorous testing to ensure the harnesses, seat belts, and structural components can withstand the forces experienced in a real collision. 


### What types of Graco booster seats are available?

Graco offers a range of booster seat models, including high-backed boosters and backless boosters. High-back models provide head and neck support, while backless boosters are thinner and more discreet. Each model is designed for specific age and weight ranges, typically 40-100 pounds. The seats come in various designs and colors to match your vehicle's interior and personal style preferences. 


### How do I install a Graco booster seat in my vehicle?

Most Graco booster seats can be installed using the vehicle's seat belt system or Latch connectors. The seats come with installation instructions and may include a harness guide and clip system to help position the seat belt correctly. The seat should sit flat against the vehicle seat bottom with the seat back at the correct angle for proper belt positioning. 

Check that the seat moves no more than one inch forward or side-to-side after installation. Make sure the harnesses or seat belt are tight enough that you cannot pinch any fabric between your fingers at the shoulder and hip straps when the seat is installed correctly. 


### At what age and weight should children transition from a harness to a booster seat?

Children should transition from a harnessed car seat to a booster seat when they exceed the height and weight limits of their current harness system, typically around 40 pounds. Many Graco booster seats begin transitioning between 40-45 pounds or when the child is 4 years old, depending on the specific model. 

Children should remain in a harnessed car seat as long as possible, up to the highest weight and height limits allowed. This provides additional protection as they grow quickly in their early years.
