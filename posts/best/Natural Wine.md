### What defines Natural Wine?

Natural wine is a beverage crafted from organically-grown grapes using minimal human intervention possible. The term "bio-dynamic" or "raw" is often used to describe these wines. Fermentation occurs with natural yeasts, and sulfites are not added. The result is a wine offering a genuine and unaltered taste of the grape varietal and its terroir.

Unlike conventional wines, which may be rife with artificial additives, natural wine is believed to be more authenticate and environmentally friendly. Due to lack of chemical preservatives, it comes with a shorter shelf life, appealing to those who appreciate freshness and minimal intervention.


### How is Natural Wine distinct from other types of wine?

Natural wine sets itself apart with its minimal processing, including a lack of added chemicals. Typically, the grapes are organically grown without employing synthetic pesticides and fertilizers. As for the winemaking process, it relies on wild or natural yeasts and bacteria which can produce wines with unique flavors and aroma profiles.

Another significant difference lies in the fact that natural wine does not undergo fining or filtration, like conventional wine. Instead, it remains unfiltered, which means it might contain some sediment or particles, adding to its earthy or robust texture. The absence of filtration also results in a wine with higher levels of tannins and acidity, providing a more complex and intense taste experience.

### Is natural Wine always Organic?

Although many natural wines are organic, not all of them adhere to organic practices. Unfortunately, "natural wine" lacks standardized definition, and some winemakers use the term to describe wines crafted with organic grapes but with some chemical manipulation during production. To ensure your wine is truly organic, look for labels that state "organic" or "biodynamic" along with a recognized organic wine certification. Though not all winemakers choose certification due to cost and bureaucratic hassles, such transparency is continuously improving in the natural wine industry, allowing for informed purchasing decisions.


### How is Natural Wine stored?

The storage process for natural wine is similar to that of other wine types. Store your wine in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight, such as a wine cellar, pantry, or under the stairs. Ideal storage temperature ranges between 45-65°F (7-18°C) for white wines and 55-65°F (13-18°C) for red wines.

Avoid storing natural wine in areas with fluctuating temperature, humidity, or vibrations as these factors can impact the wine's quality and aging process. Keep the bottles lying horizontally to preserve the moist cork and prevent air ingression, which can lead to oxidation and spoilage. For optimal storage and preservation over a year, it's best to use a temperature-controlled wine cellar or storage room.

### How long do natural Wines last?

Natural wine has a shorter shelf life compared to conventional wines due to the lack of chemical preservatives. Unopened, natural wine can usually last for 1-3 years, depending on the grape varietal and winemaking process. However, this duration can vary greatly based on storage conditions and specific wine.

Once opened, the wine should be consumed within a few days to a week, depending on its freshness and storage conditions. If the wine starts to turn or develop off-flavors, it's best to discard it. White natural wines generally have a shorter shelf life than reds, and rosés typically fall somewhere in between.


### Which grape varietals are often used in Natural Wines?

Natural wines can come from a wide range of grape varietals, depending on the winemaker's philosophy and terroir. Popular grape varietals used in natural wines include but are not limited to Chenin Blanc, Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, Gamay, and Cabernet Franc. You may also find lesser-known varieties such as Malvasia, Txakoli, and Fiano in natural wine offerings.

The choice of grape varietal is not only determined by the winemaker's preference but also by the potential of the grape to express its unique character while highlighting the characteristics of the terroir. Natural wines made from organic grapes are often considered "terroir-driven," which means they offer a reflection of the soil, climate, and environment where the grapes were grown.
