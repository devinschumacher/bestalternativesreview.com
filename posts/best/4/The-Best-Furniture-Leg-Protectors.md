## FAQ

![Furniture-Leg-Protectors-4](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Furniture-Leg-Protectors-4/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What are furniture leg protectors?

These protective caps or pads cover the bottom of furniture legs to prevent damage from scratches, dents, and wear caused by floor surfaces and frequent movement. They extend the life of furniture while protecting floors from scuffs and dings. 

Protectors come in various materials including rubber, plastic, metal, and composite. The right type depends on your furniture style, room environment, and level of foot traffic. 


### What types of furniture need leg protectors?

Almost any piece of furniture with legs benefits from protectors. This includes dressers, tables, chairs, desks, bookcases, and entertainment centers. Any furniture that gets moved regularly or placed directly on floor surfaces should have protective caps. 

Protectors are particularly important for furniture with thin legs or delicate finishes, expensive flooring, hardwood floors, or homes with pets and children who might scratch or damage furniture legs. 

![Furniture-Leg-Protectors-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Furniture-Leg-Protectors-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### How do I measure furniture legs for protectors?

Measure the circumference of the furniture leg at its widest point. This measurement determines the size of the protector needed, as protectors typically come in standard diameters. Common sizes range from 1 to 2 inches in diameter. 

Check the protector's height to ensure it provides adequate floor protection. Most protectors add between 3/16 to 1/4 inch of height, allowing the furniture to sit level on the floor while protecting the leg. 


### How long do furniture protectors last?

Properly installed protectors typically last 3-5 years with regular use and maintenance. Environmental factors, furniture type, and protector quality affect their longevity. 

Check protectors regularly for signs of wear such as cracks, peeling, or compression. Replace them when damage occurs or when they no longer provide adequate protection to the furniture leg. 

![Furniture-Leg-Protectors-6](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Furniture-Leg-Protectors-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Can I install protectors myself?

Most protectors are easy to install using basic tools. Remove the furniture legs, apply adhesives if needed, and press the protectors firmly onto the legs' bottoms. 

Always follow the manufacturer's installation instructions, which may vary by protector type. Some protectors require special adhesives, while others snap or screw onto legs. 


### Are there protectors for uneven legs?

Yes, some protectors are designed for furniture with uneven legs. These often come in modular sets that allow you to adjust leg height and protect multiple leg diameters. 

Specialized protectors can level furniture with uneven legs by adding height to shorter legs or by attaching to the sides of legs for extra protection. These are particularly useful for antique furniture and pieces with damaged legs.
