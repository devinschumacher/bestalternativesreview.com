### Durability and Maintenance

Despite its natural beauty, acacia wood ranks between 2,200 and 2,600 on the Janka hardness scale, similar to oak. This makes it resistant to scratches and dents common with softer woods. ### Natural Characteristics

The wood's inherent qualities include small natural holes called "vital cells" and distinctive open grain patterns. These features, while noticeable, are considered natural and contribute to the wood's character. High-quality acacia furniture will preserve these features without excessive filling or sanding.

### What maintenance does acacia wood furniture require?

Regular maintenance of acacia wood furniture involves periodic cleaning with a mild soap solution and water. Avoid harsh chemicals as they can damage the wood's natural finish. 

For outdoor furniture, occasional oiling with a food-safe oil can help maintain the wood's appearance and protect it from environmental elements. 


### Is acacia wood furniture prone to scratches?

Acacia wood is more resistant to scratches than many other hardwoods due to its dense grain structure. However, sharp objects can still cause minor scratches. 

To minimize scratches, use felt pads under furniture legs and avoid dragging heavy items across the wood surface.

### How does acacia wood furniture compare to teak?

Both acacia and teak offer excellent resistance to decay and insect damage, making them suitable for outdoor furniture. However, acacia is typically more affordable and easier to work with. 

Teak has a more pronounced grain and richer color, while acacia tends to have a more uniform appearance. Both woods maintain their structural integrity well over time.
