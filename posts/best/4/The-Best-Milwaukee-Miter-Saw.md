### What is a Milwaukee Miter Saw?

A Milwaukee miter saw is a precision cutting tool that combines a miter gauge with a powerful blade. It enables users to make accurate cross-cuts and bevel cuts on wood and other materials. 

These saws feature Milwaukee's signature durability and performance, with adjustable blades and precise alignment systems for professional results. 


### What types of blades work with Milwaukee miter saws?

Milwaukee miter saws accept specific blade sizes and types, typically ranging from 10 to 12 inches. Common blade designs include crosscut, combination, and crosscut/bevel options for different materials. 

Always check the saw's specifications for compatible blade recommendations and safety requirements. 


### How do I adjust the miter gauge?

To adjust the miter gauge, use the locking mechanism located at the base of the gauge. Turn the wheel or handle to set the desired angle, then lock it in place for stable cuts. 

Regularly clean the gauge's slots to ensure precise measurements and smooth operation. 


### What maintenance do Milwaukee miter saws need?

Milwaukee miter saws require regular maintenance to maintain performance. Clean the blade and arbor regularly, check blade tension, and lubricate moving parts as recommended in the manual. 

Storage should be in a dry environment, and blades should be covered when not in use to prevent damage and rust. 


### How do I perform a basic calibration?

Place a straightedge along the cutting guide and run a test cut. If the cut deviates from the straightedge, adjust the blade height or miter angle until the cut aligns with the straightedge. 

For more accurate calibration, use a square or digital measuring tool to verify both blade height and miter angle settings. 


### What safety precautions should I follow?

Always wear protective gear, including safety glasses and ear protection when operating a Milwaukee miter saw. Keep hands and clothing away from the blade. 

Use push sticks or specialized guards when cutting small pieces to prevent fingers from contacting the blade.
