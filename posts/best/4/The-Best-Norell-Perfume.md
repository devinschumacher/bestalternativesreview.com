### Understanding Norell's Fragrance Families

The Norell collection spans various fragrance families, including floral, vanilla, creamy, and woody profiles. Each family offers distinct olfactory experiences, from the warm embrace of vanilla notes to the clean freshness of floral compositions. ### Top Notes vs Base Notes

When evaluating Norell perfumes, consider the interplay between top notes and base notes. Top notes are immediate and typically correspond to the fragrance's opening impression, while base notes form the foundation, contributing to the perfume's lasting character. This understanding helps in appreciating the scent's development over time. 


### Size Matters: Concentration and Lasting Power

Concentration affects both the intensity and longevity of Norell fragrances. Eau de Parfum typically offers the strongest impact, followed by Eau de Toilette and Eau de Cologne. Higher concentrations last longer but require more careful application to prevent overpowering others. Understanding these differences helps in selecting the right concentration for daily wear or special occasions. 


### Storage and Environmental Factors

Proper storage significantly affects a perfume's quality and longevity. Store Norell fragrances in cool, dry places away from direct sunlight and heat sources. Temperature fluctuations can alter the fragrance's chemical composition. Additionally, keeping the bottle tightly sealed prevents evaporation and contamination from air and moisture.
