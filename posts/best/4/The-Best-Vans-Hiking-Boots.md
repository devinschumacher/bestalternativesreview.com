### Fit Considerations

Proper fit is crucial for hiking boots. Start by measuring your foot, including any necessary arch support or orthotics. The boots should feel snug but not tight, with a half-inch of space between your longest toe and the end of the shoe.

Some boots feature gaiters that attach over the upper to protect against ankle-scratching vegetation and debris. Consider whether this feature aligns with your hiking style and trail conditions. 

The lacing system should allow for a custom fit, with multiple eyelets and appropriate gore panels to prevent bunching and hotspots.
