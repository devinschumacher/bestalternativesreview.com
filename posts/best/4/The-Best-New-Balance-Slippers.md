## Buyer's Guide

New Balance slippers combine comfort with brand recognition, making them a popular choice for casual wear at home. When selecting a pair, consider several key factors to ensure you find a style that meets your needs and preferences. 


### Material Quality

The outer material determines how your slippers hold up over time. Common materials include mesh for breathability, suede for durability, and synthetic fabrics for easy care. The inner lining should be soft and moisture-wicking to keep your feet dry during extended wear. 


### Arch Support and Fit

Effective arch support prevents foot fatigue and helps maintain proper foot alignment. Look for models with contoured insoles that match your arch type. The slippers should fit snugly but not be restrictive, allowing about a thumb's width between your longest toe and the end of the shoe. 


### Heel Design

The heel style impacts both comfort and stability. Slippers with a low, flat heel provide better balance and reduce strain on your Achilles tendon. Heavier heel designs can be more supportive but may feel less comfortable during extended wear. 


### Water Resistance and Cleaning

If you plan to wear your slippers in the bathroom or near water sources, choose a model with at least moderate water resistance. The materials should also be machine washable for easy cleaning and maintenance.

### How should I care for my New Balance slippers?
