### What are cat eyelash extensions?

Cat eyelash extensions are artificial lashes applied to the eyelid in a similar manner to human lash extensions. These extensions are made from synthetic materials that mimic natural human lashes in appearance and length. The term "cat" eyelash extensions specifically refers to extensions that are tailored to look more like cat whiskers, often featuring thinner, more delicate strands. 

These extensions can range from individual strands applied to specific areas of the eyelid for a subtle enhancement, to full sets that create a dramatic, cat-eye effect. Unlike human lash extensions, they are typically designed to be thinner and more flexible to achieve a natural cat-like appearance. 


### How long do cat eyelash extensions last?

Cat eyelash extensions usually last between 2 to 4 weeks with proper care. The actual longevity depends on factors such as frequency of touching the eyes, exposure to water, and the quality of the extension materials. 

Regular maintenance, including daily cleaning and avoiding touching or rubbing the eyes, can help extend their lifespan. Unlike natural eyelashes, these extensions do not shed naturally and must be removed or trimmed by a professional to maintain their look.

### How are cat eyelash extensions applied?

Cat eyelash extensions are applied using a two-step process. The first step involves measuring and selecting individual strands that match the natural lash length and thickness. The extensions are then carefully applied one by one using a special adhesive specifically designed for natural-looking results. 

Each extension is placed at the base of the natural lash, bonded individually to create a natural-looking fan effect. This process requires precise techniques and can take around 2-3 hours to complete for a full set. 


### Are cat eyelash extensions safe for everyone?

While generally safe, cat eyelash extensions may not be suitable for everyone. Individuals with allergies to adhesives, sensitive skin, or compromised immune systems should exercise caution and consider alternative options. 

Before undergoing any extension procedure, it's important to consult with a professional to ensure you have no underlying conditions that could affect the treatment's safety. Some people may also need to wait between 2-4 weeks after regular hair treatments like bleach or dye before considering extensions.

Care for cat eyelash extensions requires regular maintenance to keep them looking natural and clean. The recommended care routine includes daily cleaning with specialized products to remove makeup and prevent debris build-up.
