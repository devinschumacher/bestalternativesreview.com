## FAQ


### What is the difference between a dehumidifier and a regular air conditioner?

A dehumidifier and a regular air conditioner both help control indoor humidity, but they operate differently. While an air conditioner removes both heat and moisture from the air, a dehumidifier specializes in removing moisture without cooling the air as effectively. This makes dehumidifiers particularly useful in maintaining comfortable humidity levels during transitional seasons when the temperature isn't excessively high. 

Both appliances are effective in reducing indoor humidity, but they serve different temperature ranges. Air conditioners excel in cooling and dehumidifying spaces above 70°F, while dehumidifiers perform best between 60° to 80°F. For optimal results, consider your specific climate needs when choosing between these options.
