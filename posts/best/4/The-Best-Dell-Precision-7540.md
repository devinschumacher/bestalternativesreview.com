The Dell Precision 7540 surpasses consumer laptops in several key areas. It features professional-grade components like Intel Xeon processors, dedicated NVIDIA graphics for workflow acceleration, and robust I/O capabilities including multiple Thunderbolt 3 ports and dedicated graphics output.

### What kind of battery life can I expect?

While the 7540 prioritizes performance over extreme portability, it offers significant battery life for a workstation laptop. Real-world usage typically delivers 6-8 hours of battery life for general mobile work, which can extend to 9-12 hours with more conservative usage and settings. 

Note that battery life varies based on usage patterns, connected components (like graphics), and system settings. For extended mobility, the 7540 can optionally be configured with a second battery for up to 16 hours of runtime. 


### How does it handle high-resolution displays?

The Precision 7540 supports up to 5120x1440 resolution (32:9 aspect ratio) on its built-in display, and can simultaneously stream 4K video to an external 8K display via USB-C. It can also handle multiple external displays, with support for a maximum of four displays in various combinations of resolutions and refresh rates. 

The laptop's DisplayPort 1.4 and USB-C ports enable seamless connectivity to high-resolution displays, with support for DisplayPort 1.4a and USB4 enabling the latest Display streaming features.
