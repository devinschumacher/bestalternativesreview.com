## Buyer's Guide

Choosing the right girls trip shirt means finding a balance between comfort, style, and functionality. These tops should keep your crew cool and connected during adventures, whether you're exploring new cities or relaxing by the beach. 


### Material and Construction

Look for shirts made from breathable fabrics like cotton or moisture-wicking materials, particularly useful for active days exploring museums or walking tours. Premium shirts often feature reinforced seams and double-stitched stress points for lasting quality through frequent washing and regular wear. 


### Fit and Style

Assess how different neckline styles - from scoop to crew - feel against your body for all-day comfort. Short sleeves offer immediate breathability, while long sleeves provide essential sun protection and modesty options. Consider matching pairs for team outings or coordinating solids and prints for variety without overwhelming your wardrobe. 


### Pocket Options

Interior pockets keep phones secure and out of sight, while exterior pockets offer quick-access storage for snacks, maps, or camera equipment. Some thoughtful designs feature hidden pockets or zippered closures for added security of personal items. 


### Print Quality and Care

Invest in shirts with high-quality printing processes that prevent fading and bleeding in washes. Check for clear color registration and crisp details in logo or graphic artwork. Considercare instructions for fabric types - while cotton requires minimal maintenance, technical fabrics may need more careful handling to preserve their performance properties.

### How do I wash and care for my Girls Trip Shirts?

### How do I measure for a proper fit?

To measure for your shirt size, measure the fullest part of your chest, under the arms and over the shoulder blades. The measurement should be taken while wearing underwear but no jacket. 

For the waist, measure around your natural waistline, which is usually found at the narrowest part of your torso. For sleeves, measure from the center of your shoulder to where you'd like the sleeve to end, typically to the wrist or just past it.
