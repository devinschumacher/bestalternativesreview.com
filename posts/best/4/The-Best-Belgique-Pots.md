### What size pot should I choose for my plant?

Select a Belgique Pot that is 1-2 inches larger in diameter than your plant's root ball. This extra space allows for growth while providing adequate drainage. The pot's depth should accommodate the plant's roots with at least 1-2 inches of space between the soil surface and the pot's rim. 

Consider the plant's mature size when making your selection. Overly large pots can retain too much water, while pots that are too small will restrict growth and require frequent repotting. 


### How do I care for Belgique Pots?

Regular maintenance keeps your Belgique Pots looking their best. After each use, remove any plant debris and rinse the pots with water to prevent mineral buildup. For outdoor pots, ensure they are completely dry before storing to prevent mold and mildew growth. 

Every 6 months, check the pots for any signs of wear or damage. If you notice cracks, chips, or significant discoloration, it's time to replace the pot. The glazes may gradually develop a natural patina, which is normal and can enhance their appearance.
