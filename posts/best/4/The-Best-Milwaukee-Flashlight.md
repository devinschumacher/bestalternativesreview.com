## Buyer's Guide

Milwaukee flashlights combine durability, versatility, and illumination power in tools designed for professional use. Understanding their key features will help you select the right flashlight for your needs. 


### Output and Lumens

The light output, measured in lumens, determines how bright the flashlight is. For general-purpose use, 1,000-1,500 lumens provide adequate brightness, while 1,500-2,000 lumens offer enhanced visibility for longer distances. Professional-grade flashlights often exceed 2,000 lumens for extreme illumination requirements. 


### Battery Life and Power Options

Battery life varies based on output levels, with higher lumens requiring more power. Common battery options include AA, AAA, Lithium-ion, and rechargeable Nickel-Metal Hydride. Consider total runtime, activation methods (momentary or constant-on), and whether you need backup power sources. 


### Beam Pattern and Illumination Range

Beam pattern affects how light spreads, with spot beams providing intense central illumination and flood beams offering wider, more diffuse illumination. Illumination range, typically measured in feet, indicates how far the flashlight can effectively light an area. For detailed inspection, look for at least 300 feet range. 


### Physical Features and Build Quality

Water resistance rating, typically measured in IPX (Ingress Protection) standards, indicates how well the flashlight can handle moisture. Full IPX7 rating means it's fully waterproof, while IPX4 provides splash resistance. The body material should match your usage needs, with aluminum offering durability but weight, while plastic keeps the flashlight light but potentially less resistant to impacts.
