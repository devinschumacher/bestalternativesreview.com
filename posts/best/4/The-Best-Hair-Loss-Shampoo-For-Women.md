## FAQ


### What ingredients should I look for in a hair loss shampoo?

Look for shampoos containing proven hair growth stimulants such as minoxidil, biotin, or caffeine. Minoxidil, available in concentrations from 2% to 5%, is clinically proven to increase hair growth. Biotin helps strengthen hair follicles, while caffeine can improve blood circulation to the scalp. 

Avoid products with harsh chemicals like sulfates, which can dry out the scalp and worsen hair loss. Instead, choose gentle cleansers that maintain scalp health. 


### How often should I use a hair loss shampoo?

Use the shampoo as directed, typically 2 times per week. Some products need to be used daily, while others are applied once weekly. Consistency is key, but avoid overuse as it may cause scalp irritation. 

After the initial treatment period, you may reduce usage to maintenance levels, usually 1-2 times per week, to preserve hair growth results. 


### Will it work for all types of hair loss?

Most hair loss shampoos target female-pattern hair loss, also known as androgenetic alopecia. These shampoos work best for women with miniaturized hair follicles due to hormonal changes, genetics, or stress. 

For other causes of hair loss, such as alopecia areata or scalp infections, consult a dermatologist before using hair loss shampoo. These conditions may require specific treatments beyond scalp cleansing. 


### How long does it take to see results?

Most women notice improvements within 3-6 months of consistent use. Initial results may include reduced hair shedding and new growth at the edges of the affected area. 

Individual results vary based on the severity of hair loss, compliance with the regimen, and underlying causes. Some women may experience noticeable growth after 2 months, while others may need 6-12 months to see significant changes. 


### Can I use this with my regular shampoo?

Some women prefer alternating between a regular shampoo and a hair loss formula for added cleansing. This approach can help maintain scalp health while addressing hair loss concerns. 

For best results, use the hair loss shampoo as directed and allow your regular shampoo for occasional deep cleaning. Always follow the manufacturer's instructions to avoid disrupting your hair growth regimen. 


### Are these shampoos safe during pregnancy?

While minoxidil is generally considered safe during pregnancy when used on the scalp, other ingredients may not be appropriate. Always consult with your healthcare provider before using any hair loss product during pregnancy. 

Pregnant women should focus on maintaining overall scalp health through gentle cleansing and proper nutrition. Discuss hair loss concerns with your doctor to determine the safest course of action.
