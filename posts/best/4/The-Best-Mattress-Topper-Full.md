###  What is a mattress topper?

A mattress topper is a protective and comfort-enhancing layer that goes on top of your existing mattress. It provides an extra layer of support, improves sleep quality, and extends the life of your mattress. 

Mattress toppers come in various materials and thicknesses, offering different levels of support and comfort. Common options include foam, memory foam, latex, and hybrid combinations. 


###  Which mattress topper should I choose?

Select a mattress topper based on your sleep preferences and existing mattress type. Consider factors like temperature regulation, pressure relief, and allergen resistance. 

Common recommendations include memory foam for pressure relief, latex for natural resilience, and organic cotton for allergy sufferers. Always choose a topper that matches your mattress size (full, queen, king).

###  How thick should a mattress topper be?

A standard mattress topper thickness ranges from 1 to 4 inches, with each option offering distinct benefits. 

1-2 inch options are suitable for enhancing a firm mattress, while 3-4 inch models provide significant comfort and pressure relief for sleepers with back pain or other conditions. 


###  How do I measure my mattress for a topper?

Measure your mattress's length and width using a tape measure. Account for extra inches needed for installation, typically about 2 inches on all sides for most toppers. 

Ensure the topper's dimensions match your mattress's size exactly or allow for slight overhang (about 6 inches on each side) for a premium sleep surface.

Most mattress toppers are machine washable, but check the product label for specific care instructions.

###  How long does a mattress topper last?

Mattress toppers typically last 5-10 years with regular use.
