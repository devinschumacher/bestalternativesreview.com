## Buyer's Guide

Bonnie and Clyde sunglasses combine classic design elements with modern styling, making them a popular choice for both casual wear and special occasions. Understanding key features and considerations can help you select a pair that matches your personal style and lifestyle. 


### Frame Materials

Bonnie and Clyde offer frames in various materials, including metal, plastic, and acetate. Metal frames provide stability and durability, while plastic and acetate options can be more lightweight and flexible. Consider the material's weight, comfort, and ability to hold shape for your daily wear preferences. 


### Lens Types

Lens options range from clear for everyday wear to various shades of grey, brown, and purple for UV protection and visual enhancement. Photochromic lenses automatically adjust to changing light conditions, while polarized lenses reduce glare from surfaces like water and roads. Understanding your environmental needs will help you choose the right lens type. 


### Size and Fit

Bonnie and Clyde sunglasses come in standard sizes, but proper fit is crucial for comfort and functionality. Consider the size of your face and the frame's width when selecting a pair. Wraparound styles offer better protection from glare, while aviator shapes suit a wider face. Nose pads and temple design affect how the frames stay in place during various activities. 


### Care and Maintenance

Proper maintenance ensures your Bonnie and Clyde sunglasses remain in excellent condition. Store them in a protective case when not in use to prevent scratches and damage. Avoid exposure to extreme temperatures and chemicals. Regular cleaning with a soft cloth and mild soap helps maintain clarity and reduces fingerprint visibility.
