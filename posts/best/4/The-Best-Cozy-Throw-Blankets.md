## Buyer's Guide

Add warmth and texture to your living space with a cozy throw blanket. These versatile additions enhance your favorite chair, sofa, or bed, offering both comfort and style. When choosing the right throw blanket, consider the material, weight, and care requirements to find the perfect piece for your home. 


### Material Matters

The materials used in throw blankets significantly impact their feel and performance. Natural fibers like cotton, wool, and shearling provide excellent warmth and durability. Cotton blends are soft yet breathable, while wool offers superior insulation ideal for cooler months. Synthetic materials like polyester deliver affordability and easy care but may lack natural warmth. Consider your specific needs and preferences when selecting a material.

### Weight and Size Considerations

Throw blankets come in various weights, matching everything from lightweight summer pieces to heavy winter blankets. Consider the room's temperature and your personal warmth preferences when selecting a weight. Standard sizes include twin (60x80 inches) for beds, half-size (50x75 inches) for armchairs, and full-size (76x90 inches) for sofas. Measure your intended spot before purchasing to ensure the blanket fits properly. 


### Care and Maintenance

Proper care extends a throw blanket's lifespan and maintains its appearance. Machine washable options with delicates settings are convenient, while hand washing preserves the quality of delicate materials. Pay attention to drying instructions, as some materials require non-chafing surfaces. Avoid direct sunlight and high heat when drying to prevent color fading and shrinkage. Regularly removing dust and pet hair with a soft brush extends the blanket's cleanliness.

### What materials are commonly used in throw blankets?

Throw blankets can be made from a variety of materials, including cotton, polyester, and microfiber. Cotton blends offer natural warmth and breathability, polyester blankets provide durability and quick drying, while microfiber creates a soft, plush texture. 

The most common materials range from 100% cotton to polyester blends, with options for those seeking natural fibers or synthetic alternatives for extended use. 


### How should I measure for the right size throw blanket?

Measure your seating area diagonally from one corner to the opposite corner to determine the ideal blanket size. A standard living room sofa requires approximately 70-80 inches of blanket length. 

For beds, consider a blanket that measures 45 inches by 60 inches for single beds, 60 inches by 80 inches for queen beds, and 80 inches by 90 inches for king beds. 

![Cozy-Throw-Blankets-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Cozy-Throw-Blankets-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What is the difference between brushed and unbrushed microfiber?

Brushed microfiber features a soft, velvety texture created through specialized manufacturing processes. This treatment enhances the feel while maintaining water resistance. 

Unbrushed microfiber provides a smooth, sleek appearance with slightly less texture. It's equally water-resistant but offers a different aesthetic appeal. 


### How do machine washable options compare to those meant for dry cleaning?

Machine washable throw blankets typically feature detailed care instructions on the label. Following these guidelines ensures proper cleaning while maintaining the blanket's shape and texture. 

Blankets meant for dry cleaning generally require professional attention to preserve specific qualities like water resistance or color integrity, though they may cost more to maintain.

### What factors affect the warmth of a throw blanket?

Weight and thickness determine a blanket's warmth, with heavier options providing more insulation. Thefill material, such as down or synthetic fibers, also significantly impacts heat retention. 

Consider the blanket's construction, with tightly woven fabrics blocking cold drafts more effectively than loosely woven alternatives. 


### How can I care for my throw blanket to maintain its quality?

Most throw blankets require regular airing and spot cleaning to maintain appearance. Check the care label for specific recommendations, which may include regular washing for machine-washable options. 

Proper storage prevents creasing and damage. Fold rather than roll large blankets, and store them in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.
