### What exactly is a dehydrator, and how does it differ from an oven?

A dehydrator is kitchen equipment designed specifically for removing moisture from food, resulting in dried or jerky-like products. Unlike conventional ovens, which use both heat and direct air flow, dehydrators maintain low temperatures while circulating air through a vertical stack of trays, allowing for gentler, more uniform drying of food items. 

The controlled environment of a dehydrator prevents overheating, which can cause food to cook or caramelize rather than dry. This makes it particularly suitable for drying fruits, vegetables, meats, and even grains, preserving nutrients and extending shelf life.
