## FAQ


### What is a Cherry MX Blue switch?

The Cherry MX Blue switch is a mechanical keyboard switch that offers a distinct tactile feedback and audible click when actuated. This type of switch is popular among gamers and typists who prefer a visual and sensory confirmation of key presses. 

Blue switches feature actuation at 4mm, which provides both audible and tactile feedback. They require approximately 45cN of actuation force, making them more responsive than many other switch types while still offering enough travel distance for comfortable use. 


### How do Cherry MX Blue switches compare to other switch types?

Compared to linear switches, which provide a straight force feedback without the tactile bump, Blue switches offer a more distinct typing feel with the same actuation point. They differ from Speed switches, which have a shorter actuation distance of 2mm and less noticeable feedback. 

When compared to Tactile switches, which provide a more gentle bump at actuation, Blues offer a firmer tactile response and distinct click sound, making them more suitable for precise typing and gaming applications. 


### What makes Cherry MX switches reliable?

Cherry MX switches are well-known for their durability, thanks to their robust construction. Each switch undergoes rigorous testing to ensure consistent performance over millions of actuations. The switches feature high-quality materials and a standardized design that allows for interchangeable mechanical components. 

The internal mechanics of MX switches include a precise spring system and a metal actuator, which together provide consistent keystroke performance. This reliability extends to the switch's keycap mounting system, which helps prevent wear and tear common in other switch types. 


### Are Cherry MX Blue switches suitable for gaming?

Yes, Cherry MX Blue switches are excellent for gaming due to their responsive actuation point and audible feedback. The distinct click helps users identify key presses more clearly during fast-typing scenarios, making them particularly useful for applications requiring precise input. 

While the actuation force (45cN) might be considered higher than some gaming-friendly options, the trade-off provides more tactile confirmation of each keystroke. This feature can reduce errors in typing-intensive games, making the slightly higher actuation force beneficial for certain gaming applications. 


### How do I maintain Cherry MX Blue switches?

Regular maintenance of Cherry MX Blue switches can extend their lifespan and ensure optimal performance. Use a compressed air can to remove dust and debris from the switch mechanisms, and clean the surface with a soft, dry cloth after each use. 

For deeper cleaning, remove the keycaps and soak them in warm water with a mild cleaning solution. Avoid harsh chemicals or abrasive materials that could damage the switch components. Reassembling the keyboard with clean keycaps helps maintain optimal performance. 


### What's the difference between Cherry MX Blue switches and the newer Speed Silver version?

Cherry MX Blue switches feature a 4mm actuation point with both tactile feedback and an audible click, requiring 45cN of actuation force. In contrast, Speed Silver switches have a 2mm actuation point with less tactile feedback and slightly lower actuation force of 42cN. 

While both switches use the same SpeedSilver material for the actuator, the longer actuation distance of the Blue switch provides more noticeable feedback. This makes Blue switches more suitable for users who prefer a distinct tactile confirmation of each keystroke.
