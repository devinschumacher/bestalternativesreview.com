### Fiber Content and Quality

Authentic angora comes from rabbit fur and should contain at least 70% mohair-angora blend for durability. Look for softness ratings and fiber quality grades to ensure you receive high-quality mohair or rabbit angora strands. 


### Care Requirements

Angora requires special care to maintain its properties. Most angora yarns should be dry cleaned or hand washed in cool water with gentle detergents. Proper storage in a cool, dry place with ventilation prevents matting and tangling.

### What is Angora yarn?

Ancient and luxurious, Angora yarn is crafted from the soft, downy undercoat of Angora rabbits. This natural fiber produces yarn with an exceptionally high loft - the volume-to-weight ratio that gives it its characteristic lightness and warmth. 

The process of harvesting the fine hairs requires careful shearing, typically done once a year. The raw fibers are then blended with other materials like wool to create yarn that combines remarkable softness with practical durability. 


### What makes Angora yarn special?

Anger yarn's most distinctive feature is its incredibly fine diameter, often 15-20 microns compared to wool's 25-30 microns. This creates an incredibly soft texture that's gentle against the skin. 

While softer than most natural fibers, it's worth noting that raw Angora requires careful handling to prevent matting and tangling. The resulting fabric is extremely lightweight and warm, though less resistant to wear than pure wool garments. 

![Angora-Yarn-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Angora-Yarn-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### How should Angora yarn be cared for?

Angora requires gentle care to maintain its shape and texture. Most manufacturers recommend hand washing in cool water with a mild detergent, followed by laying flat to dry. 

For those who prefer machine washing, use a mesh bag on the gentle cycle with cold water and a minimal amount of gentle detergent. Avoid spin cycles as they can damage the delicate fibers. 


### What products can be made with Angora yarn?

Angora yarn's finest versions are often reserved for the softest baby garments and intimate wear, where their hypoallergenic properties are most beneficial. The coarser grades can be used for hats, gloves, and scarves. 

When blended with other fibers, it creates everything from warm sweaters to elegant shawls. The blend ratio determines the final product's suitability for different weather conditions and activities. 

![Angora-Yarn-6](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Angora-Yarn-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Is Angora yarn suitable for everyone?

While Angora's hypoallergenic properties and fine texture make it ideal for sensitive skin, it's important to note that some individuals may experience irritation. Allergies to Angora are rare but possible. 

Children and adults with known fiber sensitivities should perform a patch test before using Angora products. Those who sweat heavily might find that natural wool alternatives provide better moisture management.
