### What is spikenard essential oil?

Spikenard essential oil is derived from the roots of the spikenard plant (Nardostachys jatamansi), native to the Himalayas and western China. It's known for its distinctive earthy-sweet aroma and traditional use in Ayurvedic medicine. 

Botanically distinct from true vetiver, spikenard oil has a darker color and richer scent profile. The extraction process typically involves steam distillation of the root material. 


### What are the top benefits of spikenard essential oil?

Known for its grounding properties, spikenard oil helps promote relaxation and emotional balance. It's often used to reduce stress, anxiety, and insomnia due to its sedative effects on the nervous system. 

The oil also possesses anti-inflammatory and anti-tumor properties, making it useful for skin issues like eczema and psoriasis. Some studies suggest it may enhance cognitive function and memory retention.

### How should spikenard essential oil be diluted?

Due to its strength, spikenard oil should always be diluted before topical application. A common dilution ratio is 1-2 drops per teaspoon of carrier oil (like sweet almond or jojoba). 

If using internally, always follow dosing instructions from a qualified aromatherapist or healthcare provider, as spikenard oil is not recommended for internal use without professional guidance. 


### What's the best way to use spikenard essential oil?

The most effective methods include diffusing 2-3 drops in a room diffuser, adding 3-5 drops to a warm bath, or applying 5-10 drops to a cloth and inhaling deeply. 

For topical application, mix 1-2 drops with a carrier oil and massage into the skin or apply to pulse points. Avoid direct sunlight for 12 hours after applying as the oil may increase photosensitivity.

### Are there any potential side effects?

While generally safe, spikenard oil can cause skin irritation in some individuals, especially when applied undiluted. Discontinue use if you experience redness, itching, or rash. 

Pregnant women, nursing mothers, and those with liver conditions should avoid using spikenard oil without consulting a healthcare provider. It can interact with certain medications and may affect blood pressure. 


### How do I store spikenard essential oil?

Keep your spikenard oil in a dark glass bottle to protect it from light, which can degrade the oil's properties. Store in a cool, dry place away from direct heat sources. 

Avoid exposing the oil to air for extended periods as it can oxidize. Most oils remain effective for 1-2 years when properly stored. Check for any changes in color, consistency, or odor before use.
