### Filter Technology

Air purifiers use various filtration technologies to capture pollutants and allergens. The most efficient systems employ multiple-stage filtration, starting with a pre-filter to catch large particles, followed by activated carbon for odor removal, and ending with a HEPA filter to trap fine particles like pollen, smoke, and viruses. ### Airflow and Coverage

The purifier's airflow capacity determines how effectively it can clean the air in your space. Filtered airflow is typically measured in cubic meters per minute (m³/min), and you need enough capacity to turn over the room's air volume at least four times an hour. Calculate your room's volume and compare it with the purifier's stated coverage area to ensure adequate performance. 


### Sleep Mode and Quiet Operation

For bedrooms or quiet spaces, prioritize models with genuine sleep mode functionality that significantly reduces fan speed and operating noise. Effective sleep mode should quiet the purifier to below 25 dBA while maintaining sufficient filtration efficiency. Some advanced models use whisper-quiet AC motors specifically designed for low-noise operation during the night.

### Energy Consumption and Maintenance

Consider the purifier's power consumption as measured in watts. Effective air purifiers should balance clean air delivery rate (CADR) with energy efficiency.
