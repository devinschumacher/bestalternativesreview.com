## FAQ


### What makes a good windshield cleaner?

A quality windshield cleaner should effectively remove dirt, bugs, and grime while protecting your vehicle's finish. Look for products that use high-quality ingredients like pure water, methanol, and a blend of acids and bases that balance decontamination with protective properties. 

Effective cleaners also prevent streaking, which is crucial for maintaining clear visibility. Most products achieve this through careful formulation and sometimes by including surface tension modifiers that allow the cleaning solution to flow smoothly across the windshield. 


### How do I choose the right strength?

Most windshield cleaners come in different strength levels, indicated by star ratings (1-5 stars). Lightly soiled windscreens typically only need a 1-2 star product, while heavy grime or bug residue requires something stronger (3-5 star rating). 

Consider the local driving conditions and time of year. Products with higher strength ratings may be necessary in areas with harsh winters or high insect populations. 


### Can I use household cleaners instead?

While natural and organic cleaners are popular, they may not effectively remove tough road grime or leave your windshield with the protective properties of purpose-made cleaners. Household items like vinegar and water can damage your vehicle's paint and actually increase streaking. 

For best results, stick to windshield cleaner products specifically designed for automotive use. These are formulated to balance cleaning power with safety for your vehicle's surface. 


### How often do I need to use it?

Use your windshield cleaner whenever your view through the glass is obstructed by dirt, grime, or bugs. This could be daily in heavy usage situations or less frequently when conditions are clean. 

Consider keeping an extra bottle in your vehicle for quick cleanups and maintaining visibility during your commute or travels. 


### Can I mix different brands?

Mixing different brands of windshield cleaner can lead to chemical reactions that damage your vehicle's paint and glass. It's best to use the product recommended by your vehicle's manufacturer unless you're switching to an equally compatible brand. 

If you decide to change brands, thoroughly clean and refill your spray bottle to avoid residue from the old cleaner affecting the new product. 


### What's the recommended application method?

Shake the bottle well before use to mix the ingredients. Spraying directly onto a clean cloth and applying in a zigzag pattern from the outside corners towards the center helps distribute the cleaner evenly and prevents dripping onto your dashboard. 

Always use a fresh cloth for each application to prevent transferring dirt back onto your windshield. For tougher grime, allow the cleaner to sit for a minute before wiping.
