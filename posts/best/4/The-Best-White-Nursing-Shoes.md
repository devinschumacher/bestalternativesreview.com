Non-slip soles enhance safety during busy shifts, while cushioned insoles provide the necessary arch support for long periods of standing. Breathable fabrics keep feet cool and dry, especially important in hospital environments where shoes might be worn for extended periods. 

Anti-microbial properties help prevent odor and reduce the growth of bacteria in the shoes, maintaining a fresh and professional smell throughout the workday. Look for shoes that offer ample arch support and proper heel retention to prevent slipping out during movement. The width and depth of the shoe should accommodate various foot types, including those with wide feet or high arches. Proper fit is crucial for all-day comfort and prevents blisters or foot pain during long shifts. 

Many nursing shoes now come in multiple widths and lengths to accommodate different foot shapes. Consider trying on several pairs to find the most comfortable fit that still maintains the expected level of arch support and heel stability. 


### Maintenance and Durability

While white nursing shoes require regular cleaning to maintain their appearance, they should also be built to withstand frequent washing and sanitization. Look for shoes with removable insoles for easy cleaning and replacement when needed. 

The materials should be resistant to fading from frequent washing and the harsh chemicals often used in medical environments. The stitching and construction quality will impact how well the shoes hold up over time, especially in high-use areas like the soles and heel counters.
