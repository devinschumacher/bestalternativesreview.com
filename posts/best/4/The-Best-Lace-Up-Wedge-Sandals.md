### What is the difference between a wedge sandal and a flat sandal?

Wedge sandals feature a heel raised on a slanted block, giving the footwear a noticeable height while maintaining a flat surface. This design provides stability and elevation while walking. Flat sandals, on the other hand, have minimal or no heel, keeping the sole of the shoe completely level. 

The raised heel in wedge sandals can offer an extra inch or two of height, perfect for getting that little something extra while keeping the style simple and versatile. Flat sandals prioritize comfort and ease of movement, with some featuring heel loops or slight height variations for added style without compromising on flatness. 


### How do you properly measure for wedge sandals?

To get the right fit, measure your foot length from heel to longest toe while standing. Wedge sandals can typically be 1/4 to 1/2 inch smaller than your standard shoe size, as they offer moderate heel height and shaping for the foot. 

Consider your usual walking style and intended use. For everyday wear, a half-size down from your regular shoe size usually works well. If you plan to wear them for extended periods or for active activities, you may want to try a more relaxed fit.

### What types of closures are common in wedge sandals?

Most wedge sandals feature adjustable straps or webbing that wrap around the foot, providing both security and customizability to your fit. These closures allow you to distribute pressure across your foot and prevent slippage during walking.

### How do wedge sandals fit differently from flat sandals?

The raised heel and slanted profile of wedge sandals create a slightly different feel compared to flat sandals. The heel area experiences more pressure, and the foot may sit at an angle within the shoe. 

When trying on wedge sandals, pay attention to how they feel in both standing and walking positions. The heel should feel secure but not tight, while the straps should create a gentle, even pressure across your foot.
