## FAQ


### What types of cat toys are suitable for indoor cats?

Indoor cats thrive on toys that match their natural hunting instincts. Small mice-like toys with attached feathers or strings are particularly effective. Some cats enjoy interactive puzzle toys that dispense treats, while others prefer ball-shaped toys that roll across floors and carpets. 

Modern technology has also introduced LED light toys that move unpredictably, mimicking prey behavior. These toys can keep cats entertained for hours, especially during solitary moments when other household pets are sleeping. 


### How often should I change my cat's toys?

Change toys regularly to prevent boredom and keep your cat interested. A good rule of thumb is to rotate toys every 1-2 weeks. This keeps the play environment fresh and encourages your cat to explore different objects. 

Pay extra attention to toys with small parts or fillings that could come loose. These items pose choking hazards and should be replaced immediately if they show signs of wear. Always supervise your cat during playtime to ensure their safety. 


### Are all cat toys safe for scratching?

Scratching posts and pads provide essential exercise and help maintain your cat's claw health. Look for toys made from natural materials like sisal rope or cardboard, which are both safe and biodegradable. 

Avoid synthetic materials or toys with small attachments that can detach and be swallowed. The base of scratching toys should offer stability to prevent tipping and potential injuries. Consider vertical scratching options to give your cat a full-body workout. 


### Can I make my own cat toys?

Home-made toys can be a rewarding and budget-friendly option. Consider sewing small stuffed toys with safe, cat-friendly materials, or creating simple toys with cardboard boxes and string. 

Always check toys for loose threads or detachable parts before giving them to your cat. Homemade toys should be washable and resistant to chewing to ensure durability. 


### How do I choose the right size toy?

Toy size should match your cat's age and strength. Small toys are ideal for kittens and cats with weaker jaws, while larger, more robust toys suit adult cats and larger breeds. 

Ensure all toys are too big to be swallowed and have no small parts that could detach. The toy should be challenging but manageable for your cat to play with consistently. 


### What's the best way to introduce a new toy?

Introduce new toys gradually to avoid overwhelming your cat. Start by letting your cat examine the toy from a distance, then allow interactive play sessions of 5-10 minutes daily. 

If your cat shows interest but no play behavior, gently move the toy around to demonstrate how it works. Most cats will develop a natural interest through repetition and positive associations with playtime.
