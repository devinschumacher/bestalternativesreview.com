## FAQ


### What is a slow juicer machine?

A slow juicer machine, also known as a cold press juicer, uses gradual compression to extract juice from fruits and vegetables. This process applies even pressure through a mesh screen, minimizing heat generation and preserving the nutritional integrity of the produce. 

Unlike traditional juicers that use rapid spinning blades, slow juicers operate at speeds typically between 3,000 to 6,000 RPM, allowing more time for the juicer to break down tough plant cells and extract both juice and pulp effectively. 


### What are the benefits of using a slow juicer?

Slow juicers retain more nutrients than centrifugal juicers by operating at lower speeds and minimizing heat exposure. This preservation method locks in enzymes, vitamins, and antioxidants that might otherwise be damaged during faster juicing processes. 

The resulting juice contains less foam and more pulp, making it more filling and nutrient-dense. Additionally, the slower extraction process removes more of the vegetable juice, particularly from leafy greens, which can help reduce sugar content compared to other juicing methods. 


### Can I juice all fruits and vegetables with a slow juicer?

While slow juicers can process most fruits and vegetables, they excel particularly with dense, fibrous produce. Items like carrots, celery, and leafy greens yield the best results due to their ability to handle the extended juicing process. 

However, soft fruits like berries and delicate vegetables may require more frequent cleaning and maintenance. Always refer to the manufacturer's guidelines for specific recommendations on compatible ingredients. 


### How efficient is a slow juicer compared to other types?

Slow juicers typically produce between 30-60 ounces of juice per cycle, depending on the model and ingredients. While this may seem less efficient than centrifugal juicers which can produce up to 100 ounces per cycle, the retained nutrients make the yield more valuable. 

The slower process means more time spent juicing, with cycles lasting 5-20 minutes compared to the 2-3 minutes of centrifugal juicers. However, this added time results in fewer oxidation reactions and better nutrient preservation. 


### What maintenance requirements do slow juicers have?

Slow juicers need regular cleaning of the feed chute, collection jar, and pulp container following each use. The auger and mesh screen should be cleaned more thoroughly to remove lingering pulp that could affect future juice quality. 

Annual maintenance checks may be required for parts like the motor and housing, and some models have warranty requirements for regular servicing. Check the manufacturer's guidelines for specific maintenance schedules and recommendations. 


### How do slow juicers compare in noise level?

Slow juicers operate quietly, often producing less than 45 decibels of sound - about as loud as a soft conversation. The consistent hum of the motor and gentle grinding of ingredients makes them suitable for morning juicing sessions without disturbing others. 

This low noise level also benefits kitchen environments where sound sensitivity is important. However, the continuous nature of the juicing process means it's not ideal for quick morning juicing unless other family members are awake and present.
