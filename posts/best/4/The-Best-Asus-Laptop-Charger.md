## Buyer's Guide

When selecting an Asus laptop charger, several key factors will influence your choice. The charger's input voltage and current match your laptop's specifications, with typical requirements between 100-240V and 1.7A to 4.4A. Consider the connector type that matches your laptop's charging port - often USB-C for newer models, or the proprietary Micro-USB or USB-A ports for older laptops. USB Power Delivery (USB-PD) is also important for newer laptops that support higher charging speeds. 


### Power Output and Compatibility

The charger's power output must equal or exceed your laptop's rated power consumption. Check your laptop's manual for the specific wattage requirement, which is typically between 45W and 90W for modern laptops. Ensure the charger is compatible with both your laptop model and any power adapters you might use. 


### Physical Characteristics

Portability is crucial for travel, so look for a charger with a slim profile and lightweight construction. The included cables should be long enough to reach outlets comfortably, typically between 1.5m and 2m in length. The charger's build quality, including the cable's durability and the plug's grounding properties, affects both longevity and safety. 


### Safety Features

Modern chargers should include safety features like over-voltage protection, short-circuit protection, and thermal management. These prevent damage to both your laptop and the charger itself. Look for certifications from recognized testing labs, such as UL (Underwriters Laboratories) or CE (Conformité Européenne), which indicate meeting international safety standards. 


### Environmental Factors

Efficiency ratings determine how much energy is wasted as heat, with higher efficiency chargers being more environmentally friendly. Chargers that meet the EU's CoC (Coding of Control) Tier 2 or higher standards are more efficient. Additionally, consider chargers with built-in USB ports for quick device charging, which can be convenient for multitasking.

The wattage rating indicates the maximum power output of the charger. For most modern laptops, this ranges between 65W to 135W. If your charger is too weak, your laptop's battery charging speed may be slower than advertised.
