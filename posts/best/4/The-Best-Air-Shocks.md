### What are air shocks?

Air shocks, also known as air springs or pneumatic suspension, are vehicle suspension components that use compressed air to control ride height and damping. Unlike traditional mechanical shocks, air shocks contain an inflatable bladder that can be adjusted to alter the vehicle's ride height and suspension firmness. 

These shocks consist of an upper and lower mounting bracket, a pressure vessel containing air and a small pump, and a height control valve that allows for ride height adjustment. Some advanced systems also include electronic controls for precise height and damping regulation. 


### What benefits do air shocks provide?

Adjustable ride height: Air shocks enable raising or lowering the vehicle by inflating or deflating the air bladder, which helps with clearance in high-torque situations or when towing heavy loads. 

Better ride quality: The air bladder allows for more controlled compression and rebound, resulting in a smoother ride over rough terrain, while maintaining good performance on the highway.

### How do air shocks differ from conventional coil-over shocks?

Ride height adjustment: Coil-over shocks require mechanical adjustments to change ride height, while air shocks can be inflated or deflated to achieve the desired height instantly. 

Damping control: Coil-over shocks typically offer limited damping adjustability, whereas air shocks often include multiple levels of damping adjustment that can be changed independently of ride height. 


### Can air shocks be installed on any vehicle?

General compatibility: While many vehicles can accommodate air shocks, the specific fitment depends on the original suspension design and available mounting points.
