## FAQ


### What is a quartz cleaner?

A quartz cleaner is a specialized cleaning tool designed to remove built-up grime and mineral deposits from quartz surfaces. Unlike harsh chemical cleaners, these devices use ultrasonic waves or specialized cloths to break down stubborn stains without damaging the stone. 

These cleaning systems are particularly effective for countertops, sinks, and backsplash areas made from quartz, a durable but porous material that can accumulate soap scum, hard water marks, and other residue over time. 


### How does a quartz cleaner work?

Most quartz cleaning systems operate using ultrasonic technology or specialized cloths embedded with cleaning agents. ultrasonic cleaners immerse the quartz surface in a tank of water treated with cleaning solution, where high-frequency sound waves agitate the water molecules to loosen debris. 

For non-ultrasonic types, users typically apply a small amount of cleaning solution to the cloth, which is then activated by ultrasonic waves or a power source to release micro-vibrated cleaning particles that scrub away stains. 


### What surfaces are compatible with quartz cleaners?

 Quartz cleaners are specifically designed for use on quartz surfaces, which include artificial stone countertops, sink basins, and bathroom accessories. While some models may have attachments for other hard surfaces like granite or marble, always verify compatibility before purchasing. 

These cleaners should not be used on natural stone surfaces, as the ultrasonic vibration could damage the stone's finish. Additionally, avoid using them on glass, metal, or wood surfaces, as the cleaning tools may scratch these materials. 


### How often should I use a quartz cleaner?

For daily maintenance, a quartz cleaner should be used 1-2 times per week to remove soap scum, hard water marks, and daily residue. In heavily used areas or kitchens with multiple cooks, daily use may be necessary. 

Regular use of a quartz cleaner prevents mineral buildup and maintains the natural shine of quartz surfaces. However, for occasional deep cleaning or renovation work, less frequent use (every 1-2 weeks) is sufficient. 


### What are the benefits of using a quartz cleaner?

Using a quartz cleaner regularly extends the life of your quartz surfaces by preventing mineral buildup, removing tough stains, and maintaining the material's natural shine. These cleaning systems are more effective than standard household cleaners because they target the microscopic pores where grime accumulates. 

These devices save time compared to manual cleaning methods, particularly for difficult-to-reach areas like sink basins and countertop edges. They also reduce water usage and avoid harsh chemicals that can damage the stone over time. 


### How do I maintain my quartz cleaning system?

Always refer to the manufacturer's instructions, but generally, quartz cleaning systems require minimal maintenance. For ultrasonic models, empty the water tank and clean it after each use to prevent mineral buildup. For cloth-based systems, wash the cloths according to the guidelines after each use. 

Store the cleaner in a cool, dry place when not in use. For ultrasonic models, ensure the water tank is completely dry before storing. Check the cleaning solution applicator frequently and replace it when it shows signs of wear or reduced effectiveness.
