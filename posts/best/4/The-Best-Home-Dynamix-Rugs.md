### Material Quality

Look for durable materials like premium polypropylene or cotton blends that resist matting and retain color. The thickness (pile height) affects both comfort and noise reduction; thicker rugs absorb more sound but may feel less plush. 


### Design and Color

Consider how the rug's pattern and color will interact with your existing décor. Mixing neutral tones can create a clean, cohesive look, while bold patterns add visual interest. The rug's texture can also enhance its aesthetic appeal, with velvet or tufted designs offering additional depth.

### Maintenance Requirements

Easier-to-clean materials like PET or spill-resistant options can simplify maintenance. Regular vacuuming and occasional professional cleaning can extend a rug's lifespan. Consider the ease of replacement if damage occurs – some materials and designs may not be as practical for high-traffic areas.
