## FAQ


### What is a microwave air fryer?

A microwave air fryer combines the features of a microwave oven with those of an air fryer. This appliance uses hot air to cook food, circulating the heat around the ingredients to achieve crispy results while using less oil than traditional frying methods. 

Unlike conventional microwave cooking, which heats food from the inside out, air frying creates a more uniform cooking process that can produce restaurant-quality results at home. 


### How does it differ from traditional air fryers?

Microwave air fryers, also known as convection microwave ovens, combine the functions of a microwave and an air fryer. Traditional air fryers use a separate fan and heating element to circulate hot air around the food, while microwave air fryers rely on the oven's fan and heating elements. 

The dual functionality of microwave air fryers makes them more versatile, suitable for a wider range of cooking tasks beyond just air frying. 


### What types of food can I cook in a microwave air fryer?

Most microwave air fryers can cook a variety of foods, including chicken, fish, vegetables, and even baked goods. They're particularly effective for cooking small portions of food and reheating leftovers. 

For the best results, avoid cooking large items like whole turkeys or thick-cut steaks, as they may not cook evenly in these appliances. 


### How important is the cooking time?

Unlike conventional microwave cooking, microwave air fryers typically require precise cooking times and may vary slightly based on the model. Most recipes include recommended cooking times, but it's essential to monitor the food's progress and check for doneness. 

Food may take longer to cook than expected if the microwave air fryer isn't fully preheated, so allow extra time for the initial heating cycle. 


### How much oil do I need?

Microwave air fryers require significantly less oil than conventional frying methods – usually just 1-2 tablespoons for cooking vegetables and up to 2 tablespoons for chicken. This makes them a healthier alternative for cooking at home. 

For best results, place a small amount of oil in the bottom of the microwave air fryer basket before adding your food. 


### What maintenance is needed?

Regular maintenance includes cleaning the microwave air fryer after each use, wiping down the interior with a damp cloth, and cleaning the air filter according to the manufacturer's instructions. The heating elements should be free from debris. 

Air fryer baskets and trays should be hand washed with soap and water. For non-stick surfaces, avoid using abrasive cleaners or steel wool.
