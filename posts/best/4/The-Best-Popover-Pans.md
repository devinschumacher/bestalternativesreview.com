## Buyer's Guide

Popover pans create individual popover breads or muffins with their built-in molds. This guide helps you choose the right pan based on your baking needs and preferences. 


### Capacity and Size

Standard popover pans hold 6-12 mold cups. Consider your baking quantity needs: single-pan models are ideal for small batches or individual servings, while multi-pan varieties provide more flexibility for larger groups or multiple batches. 

![Popover-Pans-2](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Popover-Pans-2/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Material and Construction

Popular materials include metal (stainless steel and aluminum), ceramic, and non-stick coated aluminum. Metal pans conduct heat well for consistent baking but require regular seasoning. Non-stick pans make release easier but may scratch if utensils are too sharp. Ceramic pans offer a classic look and naturally non-stick surface. 


### Non-Stick Coatings

While non-stick coatings facilitate easy release, their durability can vary. Look for pans with PTFE or PFOA-free coatings that resist scratches and maintain their finish longer. If you prefer non-stick performance without coatings, opt for ceramic or specially treated metal pans. 

![Popover-Pans-3](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Popover-Pans-3/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Baking Considerations

These pans are versatile for both sweet and savory recipes, but baking results can vary. Metal pans require preheating to prevent sticking, while non-stick varieties may need less greasing. Consider the height of your batter – taller batters work best in metal pans, while non-stick varieties can accommodate slightly more liquid recipes.

### What exactly is a popover?

A popover is a rich, buttery English muffin-sized bread that puffs up dramatically during baking, creating a hollow center. The result is a light, crisp exterior surrounding a warm, tender interior - perfect for spreading butter or loading with savory fillings. 

While similar to muffins, popovers are distinct due to their delicate structure and impressive rise, achieved through the use of eggs, butter, and a gentle baking process that allows steam to trap air inside the batter.

### How do I store and reheat popovers?

After baking, popovers can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 24 hours or frozen for up to 3 months. For best quality, allow them to cool completely before storing. 

Reheat in a 350°F oven for 10-15 minutes or microwave for 20-30 seconds. To maintain their structure, avoid using microwave settings that cause liquids to separate, which can create a soggy texture. 


### What's the best way to prevent sticking?

Before each use, brush the pan thoroughly with softened butter, ensuring every nook and cranny is coated. For difficult releases, try a quick zap in the microwave before greasing - the heat helps melt the butter slightly, enhancing non-stick properties. 

For added insurance, line each cup with parchment paper before greasing, though this method requires more careful removal to preserve the popovers' shape during serving.
