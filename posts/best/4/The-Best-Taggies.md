## Buyer's Guide

Taggies, or pet identification tags, are crucial for ensuring your furry friend can be returned home if they become lost. These simple yet essential items come in various designs and materials to suit different needs and preferences. 


### Core Features of Taggies

Essential features include durable materials like stainless steel or nylon, as they resist wear and damage. Many tags now feature quick-response codes linking to pet registries or owner contact information. Some models also offer customizable options for adding photos or detailed pet identification. 


### Size and Attachment Considerations

When selecting tags, size compatibility with your pet's collar is crucial. The tag should be securely attached but still allow for easy removal. Common mounting options include split rings, swivel hooks, and snap clasps, with split rings providing the best combination of security and ease of attachment. 


### Environmental Factors

The environment where your pet wears their tag affects material choice. For outdoor pets, resistant materials like stainless steel resist rust and fading from exposure to elements. Indoor pets may use lighter materials like plastic or PVC, which are more affordable and easier to replace. 


### Legal and Safety Standards

Compliance with local pet identification laws ensures your pet meets legal requirements. Check if your area mandates specific information on tags. Additionally, verify the tag's durability meets local standards, as some jurisdictions require rustproofing for outdoor pets. 


## FAQ


### What are Taggies?

Taggies are portable chargers that combine Qi wireless charging with USB-C ports to power multiple devices. These compact chargers are designed for travel and on-the-go use, featuring durable builds and long-lasting batteries. 

Most Taggies support wireless charging for Qi-compatible devices while providing USB-C charging for older or non-wireless devices. The chargers typically include multiple USB ports for simultaneous charging and have built-in surge protection for your devices. 


### How does wireless charging work with Taggies?

Wireless charging with Taggies requires both the Taggie and your device to support Qi technology. Place your Qi-enabled phone or compatible device on the Taggie's charging surface to begin wireless charging. 

The Taggie's wireless charging port is usually located on the bottom, with charging power options ranging from 5W to 15W depending on the model. Compatible devices will charge automatically, while older devices can use the USB-C port for faster wired charging. 


### What should I consider when choosing a Taggie?

Selecting the right Taggie depends on your device's charging needs and travel habits. Consider the number of devices you need to charge, the total capacity of the battery, and the charging speeds available. 

Most Taggies come with a USB-C wall charger, which is essential for keeping the battery topped up. Additional features like LED indicators, multiple charging ports, and durable builds can also enhance your charging experience. 


### How long does it take to charge a Taggie?

Charging times for Taggies vary but typically range from 2 to 4 hours, depending on the model and charging source. The Taggie's built-in battery usually provides 10-20 charges for your devices before needing a full recharge. 

You can charge your Taggie using the included USB-C wall charger or through a compatible USB-C power bank. The Taggie's LED indicator light will show when charging is complete and how much battery life remains. 


### Are Taggies suitable for all devices?

Most Taggies support wireless charging for Qi-compatible devices, including smartphones, smartwatches, and compatible laptops. The wireless charging port is typically located on the bottom of the Taggie. 

USB-C charging is supported for all devices with a USB-C port, including older smartphones, tablets, and some laptops. The USB-C port can supply up to 18W of power, making it suitable for faster charging of compatible devices. 


### What maintenance is required for Taggies?

Regularly clean your Taggie with a soft, dry cloth to remove dust and fingerprints. The wireless charging surface should be wiped down after each use to prevent debris buildup. 

Store your Taggie in a cool, dry place when not in use. Avoid placing heavy objects on the wireless charging surface, and ensure the USB-C ports are dry before use to prevent damage.
