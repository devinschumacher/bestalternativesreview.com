## FAQ


### What is a bike GPS tracker?

A bike GPS tracker is a device or app that uses satellite technology to track the location, speed, and route of a bicycle in real-time. These tools help cyclists stay safe, monitor their rides, and share their adventures with others. 

Most bike GPS trackers are compact, waterproof, and mountable on bikes. They sync with smartphone apps that display detailed ride data such as distance traveled, elevation changes, and average speed. 


### How accurate are bike GPS trackers?

Bike GPS trackers typically offer an accuracy of within 5-10 meters in open terrain. Under ideal conditions, they can track movement as fine as 1-2 meters per second, making them suitable for both casual and competitive cyclists. 

The actual accuracy depends on factors like satellite visibility, interference from buildings or trees, and the specific technology used in the tracker. 


### What features should I look for in a bike GPS tracker?

Key features include battery life (at least 6 hours per charge), water resistance (IPX7 rating recommended), and storage capacity for offline maps. Optional features might include heart rate monitoring, voice assistant compatibility, or social sharing capabilities. 

Consider whether you need advanced cycling metrics, real-time navigation, or just basic tracking for short rides. 


### Are bike GPS trackers expensive?

Budget trackers start around $30-50 USD, offering essential location tracking and basic ride metrics. Mid-range options (around $60-100 USD) add features like detailed route mapping, heart rate monitoring, and longer battery life. 

High-end models (above $100 USD) typically include additional health metrics, longer battery life, and enhanced data analysis features. 


### How do I use a bike GPS tracker?

Most trackers pair with a smartphone app via Bluetooth or WiFi. During a ride, you typically start a new track, view your speed and route in real-time, and stop the track when finished. For analyzing your ride later, you sync the data to the app. 

To set up a new tracker, charge it fully, select your preferred app, and follow the on-screen instructions to connect and configure your settings. 


### Can I use a bike GPS tracker without a phone?

Some advanced bike GPS trackers offer basic standalone functionality, including turn-by-turn navigation and route storage. These devices often display information through a built-in touchscreen or connecting to compatible smartwatches. 

For real-time tracking and detailed data analysis, most users still need to pair the device with their smartphone through the accompanying app.
