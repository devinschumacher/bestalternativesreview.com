### What is the recommended tire pressure for Michelin all-season tires?

Michelin recommends checking tire pressure monthly and maintaining a pressure of 30-35 psi for most passenger vehicles, with specific recommendations listed on the tire's sidewall. Proper inflation ensures optimal performance and extends tire life. 

Always check pressure when the tires are cold – before driving or at least 3 hours after driving. Cold inflation pressure typically reads 4-5 psi lower than the pressure shown on the tire's sidewall. 


### How do Michelin's all-season tires compare in winter conditions?

While not designed for snow or ice, Michelin's all-season tires provide better performance in light snow than summer tires. The silica compound helps maintain grip on icy roads, and the closed shoulder design prevents snow buildup in the tread. 

For areas with significant winter weather, consider installing winter-specific tires for optimal safety and performance during harsh conditions. 


### What is the expected lifespan of these tires?

Michelin all-season tires typically last between 40,000 to 60,000 miles under normal driving conditions, depending on factors like driving habits, climate, and maintenance. Proper care and regular rotation can extend tire life. 

Signs indicating it's time to replace your tires include visible wear indicators, uneven wear patterns, and cracks in the sidewalls. Always inspect tires visually and check the tread depth (minimum 2/32nds of an inch remaining). 


### Are Michelin all-season tires compatible with all vehicle makes?

These tires are designed for most passenger vehicles, including cars, minivans, and light-duty SUVs. However, specific compatibility depends on your vehicle's manufacturer recommendations and tire size requirements.

### What maintenance is recommended for Michelin all-season tires?
