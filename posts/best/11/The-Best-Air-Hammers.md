### What is an air hammer?

An air hammer, also known as an impact air tool, combines an internal combustion engine with a reciprocating piston mechanism to deliver powerful, controlled impacts. This makes it particularly effective for breaking through concrete, removing nails, and performing demolition work. 

These tools typically operate using compressed air, which drives the internal mechanism. The resulting impacts create a rapid sequence of energy transfer, making them suitable for both professional and industrial applications. 


### What are the main benefits of using an air hammer?

Air hammers offer several advantages, including exceptional breaking power for materials like concrete and stone, reliable performance regardless of temperature conditions, and reduced physical strain compared to manual tools. They are particularly beneficial in environments where consistent, high-force impacts are required. 

These tools eliminate the need for electricity or special fuel, making them ideal for use in remote or hazardous locations. Additionally, they provide precise control over impact force and frequency through adjustable settings.

### How do I maintain an air hammer?

Regular maintenance includes checking air connections for leaks, inspecting the tool's wear parts, and cleaning debris from the working surface. The tool's piston and valves should be cleaned and lubricated according to the manufacturer's recommendations to maintain optimal performance.
