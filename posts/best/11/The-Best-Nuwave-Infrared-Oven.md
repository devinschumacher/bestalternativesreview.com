## FAQ


### What is an infrared oven?

Unlike traditional ovens that heat air around food, an infrared oven uses electromagnetic waves to heat food directly. This results in faster cooking times and more even heat distribution. 

These ovens are particularly effective for browning and crisping foods without added oils, making them popular for various cooking applications including baking, broiling, and roasting. 


### What types of food work well with infrared cooking?

Infrared ovens excel at cooking dense foods like meat and vegetables, giving them a nice char without additional seasoning or oil. They're especially good for foods that benefit from intense heat or quick cooking times. 

These ovens can be used for everything from baking bread and cooking steaks to grilling vegetables and making pizzas, providing versatility in meal preparation. 


### How does an infrared oven compare to a conventional oven?

Conventional ovens use hot air to cook food, while infrared ovens use electromagnetic waves. This difference results in faster cooking times and more efficient heat transfer, particularly for surface browning and crisping. 

Unlike conventional ovens, which can take time to heat up and cool down, infrared ovens reach cooking temperature quickly and maintain consistent heat throughout the cooking process. 


### What are the energy consumption benefits of an infrared oven?

Infrared ovens typically consume less energy than conventional ovens because they heat food more efficiently by targeting the food directly rather than heating an entire cooking chamber. 

However, overall energy usage can vary based on cooking frequency and the specific design of the oven. Always check the wattage and expected operating costs for your model. 


### Can you use cookware from a conventional oven in an infrared oven?

Many cookware items compatible with conventional ovens can be used in infrared ovens, including metal and glass cookware. Some specialized ceramic and cast iron cookware also works well. 

It's important to avoid using aluminum cookware in infrared ovens, as it can damage heating elements and affect cooking performance. Always check your oven's manual for specific cookware recommendations. 


### What maintenance requirements do infrared ovens have?

Infrared ovens generally require minimal maintenance. Regular cleaning of the interior, exterior, and venting system helps maintain optimal performance and safety. 

Check and replace cooking elements as needed based on manufacturer recommendations. Regularly inspect hinges, handles, and other components for wear and tear to ensure safe operation.
