## FAQ

### What is a bed board?

A bed board, also known as a bed base or foundation, is a supportive structure that connects to your mattress and frame. It helps distribute weight evenly and prevent sagging, extending the life of your mattress. 

Bed boards come in various materials like wood, metal, and engineered woods. Queen-size bed boards specifically support a standard 6-inch mattress thickness. 


### Why choose a bed board?

A bed board enhances your sleep experience by providing a stable foundation that prevents uneven settling and extends mattress lifespan. It's particularly beneficial if you're using an older or memory foam mattress. 

Choosing the right bed board size and material ensures proper support for your mattress and frame, contributing to better sleep quality. 

![Bed-Board-Queen-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Bed-Board-Queen-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What are the main types of bed boards?

Standard wood bed boards offer natural durability but can warp with moisture. Metal bed boards, like steel or aluminum, provide consistent support but have a shorter lifespan. 

Engineered wood bed boards combine strength with stability, resistant to warping and splintering, making them a popular choice for queen-size beds. 


### How do I measure for a bed board?

Measure the distance between your mattress frame's legs or slats. Add 2 inches to accommodate the bed board's edge clearance. For queen size, this typically requires a 60-inch span. 

Consider the total length and weight capacity of your mattress when selecting a bed board. Ensure it matches your frame's specifications for proper support. 

![Bed-Board-Queen-6](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Bed-Board-Queen-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### How do bed boards prevent sagging?

Bed boards distribute the weight of sleepers evenly across the mattress, transferring pressure from points of contact to the frame's surface. This even distribution prevents localized compression that can cause sagging. 

Avoid sagging by selecting a bed board that matches your mattress thickness. Standard queen boards support 6-inch mattresses, providing adequate spacing between the board and the floor. 


### How long do bed boards last?

With proper support and regular maintenance, wood bed boards can last 10-15 years. Metal bed boards generally last 7-10 years under similar conditions. 

Engineered wood bed boards, due to their construction, often last 15-20 years when properly maintained. Environmental factors like moisture exposure significantly impact their longevity.
