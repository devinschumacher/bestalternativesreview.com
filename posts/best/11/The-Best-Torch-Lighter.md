## Buyer's Guide

A torch lighter combines the convenience of a traditional lighter with the powerful illumination of a flashlight. This versatile tool uses butane as its fuel source, offering reliable ignition and extended runtime compared to conventional lighters. When selecting a torch lighter, consider factors such as fuel capacity, beam intensity, battery requirements, and overall durability to find a device that matches your specific needs. 


### Fuel Capacity and Runtime

Most torch lighters hold between 12 and 20 grams of butane, providing anywhere from 100 to 200 ignitions per fill, depending on the model. Premium models may offer larger fuel capacities or improved fuel efficiency, extending the number of usable ignitions significantly. Consider how frequently you'll need to use the lighter and choose a model with adequate fuel reserves for your typical usage pattern.

### Beam Intensity and Illumination Range

The core function of a torch lighter is its ability to produce intense light. Modern models typically offer multiple brightness settings, from a simple steady glow to high-intensity beams perfect for illuminating distant objects. Look for light distribution patterns that match your intended use—spot beams are excellent for targeting specific areas, while wide beams provide better visibility in larger spaces. 


### Battery Requirements and Operability

While many torch lighters run on butane, some models incorporate batteries for additional functionality. These dual-purpose lighters often feature LED illumination modes that consume minimal battery power, providing extended run times for non-friction igniting scenarios. When evaluating battery requirements, consider whether you prefer disposable AA batteries, rechargeable lithium-ion cells, or the convenience of built-in fuel canisters.

### Durability and Build Quality

The materials and construction quality of a torch lighter significantly impact its longevity and performance. Premium models typically feature machined aluminum bodies with protective coatings, while lower-cost options may use plastic casings with fewer protective features. Consider the drop resistance, water seal rating, and overall weight distribution when selecting a model, as these factors affect the tool's durability and ease of use in various conditions.

### What is a torch lighter?

A torch lighter combines the power of a traditional butane lighter with the reliability of a flashlight. These multi-functional devices typically produce a powerful, long-burning flame while also offering bright illumination through LED lights. 

Most torch lighters feature adjustable flame settings, allowing users to choose between a small, precise flame for detailed work and a high-intensity setting for quick heating tasks. The LED lights often include multiple brightness levels and colored options, making them ideal for various applications. 


### What's the difference between a torch lighter and a regular butane lighter?

While both use butane as a fuel source, torch lighters are designed with additional features. They typically offer adjustable flame settings and integrated LED illumination, whereas regular butane lighters provide a fixed flame height with basic on/off functionality.
