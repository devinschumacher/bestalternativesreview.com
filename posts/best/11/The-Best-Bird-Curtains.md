### What are bird curtains?

Bird curtains, also known as bird mesh or bird screens, are specialized window treatments designed to prevent birds from colliding with glass. These screens consist of fine mesh or netting installed between the window glass and an outer frame, allowing for clear visibility while creating an obstacle that birds can detect. 

When installed properly, bird curtains reduce window strikes by creating visual and physical barriers that guide birds away from collision points. The mesh patterns interrupt birds' flight paths and provide a tactile surface that birds recognize as obstacles. 


### Why choose bird curtains?

These window treatments offer several advantages over other bird safety solutions. They maintain clear visibility while providing robust protection, making them ideal for large windows or multi-story buildings. The mesh allows for natural light and views while preventing dangerous collisions. 

Bird curtains are compatible with various window types and styles, including French doors, sliding doors, and bay windows. They are also easy to install and maintain, requiring minimal fuss compared to alternative solutions like decals or films.

### How do bird curtains work?

The mesh panels create a physical barrier that interrupts birds' flight paths, making it clear to them where the window edge is. The tiny gaps in the mesh are too small for birds to safely navigate through, effectively protecting them while maintaining an unobstructed view for humans. 

The design also helps address false reflections in windows, which often deceive birds into thinking they can fly through glass. The texture and opacity of the mesh make these reflections ineffective, further reducing collision risks. 


### What maintenance is required for bird curtains?

Bird curtains typically need minimal cleaning compared to window glass. Wipe the mesh panels with a damp cloth when necessary to remove dust and bird droppings. For outdoor installations, the mesh can often be rinsed with a garden hose. 

Check the connections and frame regularly for any signs of wear or loose connections. Simple maintenance like tightening screws or adjusting the frame can extend the life of your curtains and ensure they function correctly.

While primarily designed for outdoor applications, bird curtains can be installed indoors in areas where additional bird safety is needed, such as near feeders or in bird-activity zones. Ensure proper lighting to avoid creating additional visibility issues.
