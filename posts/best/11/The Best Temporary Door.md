## FAQ

### What is a temporary door?

A temporary door is a portable door solution designed for short-term use, such as room partitions, stage blocking, or emergency access control. These doors are typically made from lightweight materials like fabric, plastic, or metal frameworks. 

Unlike permanent doors, temporary doors can be easily installed, removed, and repositioned as needed, making them ideal for events, home improvement projects, or commercial spaces where quick changes are required. 


### How do temporary doors work?

Most temporary doors feature a frame that can be anchored to the floor or walls using included hardware or existing fixtures. The door panel, which may be solid or translucent, is then slid into the frame. 

For fabric doors, tension rods or telescoping poles help maintain the shape and stability of the panel. Metal or plastic doors often include interlocking mechanisms to secure them in place. 

![Temporary-Door-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Temporary-Door-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### What are the main types of temporary doors?

The primary categories of temporary doors include fabric partitions, metal rolling doors, and plastic folding doors. Each type offers specific advantages in terms of weight, durability, and visibility. 

Fabric partitions are best for acoustic separation and can be printed with patterns or text. Metal rolling doors provide robust protection and are ideal for industrial applications. Plastic folding doors balance aesthetics with stability and are suitable for both indoor and outdoor use. 


### How do you measure for a temporary door?

To measure for a temporary door, determine the height and width of the opening. Consider adding an extra 2-3 inches to each measurement for proper installation, especially if using fabric or plastic panels. 

If your space has irregular angles or thresholds, take additional measurements to ensure the door will fit properly once installed.

### How long do temporary doors typically last?

With regular use and proper maintenance, most temporary doors can last between 6 months to 2 years, depending on the material and frequency of use. Fabric partitions typically have the shortest lifespan due to wear from frequent movement and exposure to sunlight. 

Metal and plastic doors generally provide more durability, though they may require occasional adjustments to maintain their shape and function, especially in environments with temperature changes. 


### How do you clean and maintain a temporary door?

Cleaning temporary doors requires minimal effort. For fabric partitions, dust and vacuum regularly, and spot-clean with mild soap and water. Avoid harsh chemicals that can damage the material or reduce visibility. 

For metal and plastic doors, use a soft cloth and mild detergent to remove dirt and debris. Regular inspection for loose connections or worn parts helps maintain optimal performance and prolongs the door's lifespan.
