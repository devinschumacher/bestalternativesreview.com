## FAQ

### What is a cube dresser?

A cube dresser combines a compact storage solution with a mirror, featuring a cubic cabinet design that utilizes vertical space efficiently. Each side can open to reveal multiple shelves for organizing personal items. 

The distinctive cube shape not only saves space but also doubles as a room decor element, making it a practical addition to small apartments, college dorms, or home offices. 


### What sizes are available for cube dressers?

Standard cube dressers measure approximately 20-24 inches on each side, providing around 40-50 inches of vertical storage space per unit. The depth typically ranges from 12-15 inches, making them suitable for even the smallest rooms. 

Some manufacturers offer larger versions with extra shelving options or compact models with fewer compartments for smaller spaces. 

![Cube-Dresser-5](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Cube-Dresser-5/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### How do I measure for a cube dresser?

Measure the available wall space where you plan to place the cube dresser, including any necessary clearances for opening doors. This measurement should reflect the total width and depth of the unit, not just its face dimension. 

Consider the height of existing furniture and the room's ceiling to ensure the dresser fits properly within the space. 


### What materials are cube dressers made from?

Most cube dressers are constructed from engineered wood, such as MDF or particle board, finished with durable paint or laminate surfaces. Higher-end models may use solid wood or metal frames for increased durability. 

The backs and sides often feature minimal material to reduce bulk, while the front panels typically carry the decorative elements and storage compartments. 

![Cube-Dresser-6](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Cube-Dresser-6/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### How much weight can a cube dresser hold?

The typical weight capacity for each shelf in a cube dresser ranges from 20-30 pounds, depending on the specific construction and shelf design. Manufacturers usually provide detailed weight capacity specifications based on the number of shelves and overall structure. 

Place heavier items at the bottom and distribute weight evenly across all shelves to maintain balance and stability. 


### What maintenance do cube dressers require?

Regular maintenance for a cube dresser includes periodic dusting of both surfaces and internal compartments. Use a soft cloth with mild soap and water for cleaning, avoiding harsh chemicals that can damage the finish. 

Check all hardware regularly for loose screws or worn parts, and apply lubricant to moving components to maintain smooth operation.
