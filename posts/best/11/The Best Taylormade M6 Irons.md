## FAQ


### What makes the Taylormade M6 Irons unique?

The Taylormade M6 Irons combine a thin topline design with an optimized center of gravity for increased distance and improved ball flight. The clubs feature a 460 aluminum crown that reduces weight, allowing for more substantial internal weighting and a lower center of gravity in the 3-7 iron set. 

The 7-9 irons incorporate an Inverted Cone Technology face structure, which focuses mass toward the heel and toe for enhanced flexibility and reduced ball rollback. This design innovation helps players of all skill levels achieve more consistent contact and longer ball flight. 


### How do the M6 irons compare to previous Taylormade models?

Updating their popular M5 series design, the M6 irons represent Taylormade's latest technology advancements. While maintaining the signature M series aesthetic, the M6 features refined internals and updated face compositions that optimize ball speed and launch characteristics across the set. 

The new M6 design builds upon established M5 strengths, particularly in terms of MOI (Moment of Inertia) and center of gravity positioning, but introduces subtle improvements that players and club-fitters have praised for their forgiving and accurate performance. 


### What are the key technical specifications of the M6 irons?

The M6 irons utilize a 455-gram design across the 3-7 iron set, with specific head configurations optimized for each club. The 3-iron houses 45 grams of tungsten, while the 7-iron uses 23 grams of tungsten for precise launch characteristics. The 8- and 9-irons maintain minimal offset for versatility across longer and shorter irons. 

The lofts range from 18° for the 3-iron to 64° for the 9-iron, with consistent progression throughout the set. All M6 irons feature a 1020 mild carbon steel face construction with a U-concavity design that promotes optimal ball flight. 


### Are the M6 irons suitable for all golfers?

The M6 irons are designed to be versatile enough for golfers of various skill levels, from mid- to high-handicappers. The combination of forgiving yet responsive design elements makes them suitable for players who struggle with consistency but want a reliable iron set. 

However, the advanced 460 aluminum crown technology and thinner topline might not suit very low-handicappers who prefer more traditional iron feel and appearance. The M6 strikes a balance between modern technology and traditional iron construction that differentiates it from other contemporary offerings. 


### What are the maintenance requirements for M6 irons?

For optimal performance and longevity, regular maintenance of the M6 irons is recommended. This includes proper care of the 1020 mild carbon steel faces, which should be protected with a consistent polish during play to maintain peak performance. The tungsten weighting in the clubs requires careful handling to prevent damage. 

Each iron should be thoroughly cleaned and inspected after every use to remove dirt, grass, and water. The grooves and face should be free from debris to maintain the proper interaction with the ball during impact. Taylormade recommends using a soft brush and appropriate cleaning solutions for maintenance purposes.
