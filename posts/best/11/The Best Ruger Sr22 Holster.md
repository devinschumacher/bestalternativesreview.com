## Buyer's Guide

The Ruger SR22 has become a popular choice for concealed carry due to its reliable operation and compact size. When selecting a holster for your SR22, several key factors should guide your decision to ensure both comfort and functionality. 


### Material Quality

High-quality holsters are typically made from durable materials like leather or Kydex. Leather holsters offer excellent conformability and breathability, making them a comfortable option for daily carry. Kydex holsters provide stable retention and are resistant to wear from exposure to the elements. 

![Ruger-SR22-Holster-2](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Ruger-SR22-Holster-2/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Retention Level

Choose a holster based on your retention preference. Magazine release retention requires you to depress the magazine release when removing the firearm, offering the most secure mounting option. Slide retention prevents the firearm from sliding out but requires less manipulation to remove. Consider lock retention for an in-between option that offers secure mounting with easier removal. 


### Retention Mechanism

Locking mechanisms add an extra layer of security by requiring a specific action to release the firearm. Positive-locking holsters ensure the gun stays securely in place while allowing quick draw. Spring-loaded retention systems provide consistent pressure without the need for manual adjustment. 

![Ruger-SR22-Holster-3](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Ruger-SR22-Holster-3/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Carry Position and Concealment

Common carry positions include waistband, inside the waistband (IWB), and appendix (fanny pack) carry. Waistband holsters are accessible and allow for quick draw, while IWB holsters offer better concealment and stability. Appendix holsters are ideal for those with smaller waists or who prefer concealed carry in tight spaces.
