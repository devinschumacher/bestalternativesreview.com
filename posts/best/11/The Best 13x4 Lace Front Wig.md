## FAQ


### What is a lace front wig?

A lace front wig features a natural-looking lace material at the hairline, creating a more realistic blend with your own hair. The synthetic hair is attached to this lace tape, which can be secured with clips or adhesives. 

The 13x4 size refers to the lace front measuring 13 inches from front to back and 4 inches wide, providing a moderate length and coverage. 


### Who should consider a lace front wig?

Anyone seeking a natural-looking hairline transition, particularly in front of the ears and forehead. It's ideal for those who prefer lightweight styling with minimal product. 

These wigs are suitable for daily wear, special occasions, or as a styling alternative to natural hair. 


### What maintenance is required for a lace front wig?

Use mild shampoo and cool water for regular cleaning. Avoid fabric softeners and hot water, as they can damage the lace or hair quality. 

Store the wig in a protective mesh bag when not in use and keep it away from direct sunlight and harsh chemicals. Minimal daily maintenance is needed compared to natural hair. 


### Can a lace front wig be worn with natural hair?

Yes, the lace material allows your natural hair to show through, creating a seamless blend. However, the lace tape should not touch your scalp to prevent irritation. 

Some users prefer to use extensions or clip-on hair pieces to create more volume or length. 


### What styles are available with a 13x4 lace front wig?

These wigs typically come in straight or slightly wavy styles and can be worn short to shoulder-length. The natural lace front allows precise layering and styling. 

Popular styles include bob cuts, pixie cuts, and layered bobs, though other styles may require additional shaping and styling product. 


### How does a lace front wig compare to a monofilament top?

Both offer a natural hairline transition, but a lace front wig's material is thinner and may show through fine natural hair more readily. 

A monofilament top provides a slightly more lifting quality and can be advantageous for finer hair types or those who prefer higher volume.
