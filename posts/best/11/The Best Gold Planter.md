### What materials are used in gold-plated planters?

Most gold-plated planters feature a metal core, typically made from zinc, Brass, or Bronze. The metal is plated with a thin layer of gold using either electroplating or immersion plating processes, providing both durability and aesthetic appeal. 

The thickness of the gold plating usually ranges from 1.5 to 3 microns, which is sufficient for decorative purposes but requires regular cleaning and protection from harsh chemicals to maintain its luster. 


### How should gold-plated planters be cleaned?

Use a soft cloth or brush to remove any dust or debris. For light cleaning, mix mild soap with warm water and wipe the surface. Avoid using abrasive cleaners or harsh chemicals, which can damage the gold plating. 

After cleaning, rinse the planter with clean water and gently dry it to prevent water marks. Regular cleaning helps maintain the appearance and extend the life of the gold plating.

### What sizes are available for gold-plated planters?

Sizes vary by manufacturer, but common diameters range from 4 to 12 inches, with heights typically between 6 and 18 inches. Some specialty manufacturers offer sizes up to 24 inches or more. 

Width can also vary, with rectangular planters providing increased base stability. Always measure your intended pot and confirm the planter's dimensions to ensure a proper fit. 


### Are gold-plated planters suitable for indoor or outdoor use?

While outdoor-rated planters resist corrosion better, most gold-plated indoor planters remain stable under normal household conditions. Outdoor use should be limited unless specifically noted as weather-resistant. 

Ensure the planter is placed on a stable, level surface to prevent tipping. If using outdoors, consider protection from extreme weather conditions and direct sunlight to maintain the gold finish.
