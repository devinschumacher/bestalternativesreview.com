## Buyer's Guide

Burgundy hair dye combines rich red and brown tones to create deep, vibrant hair color with hints of warmth. When selecting a burgundy dye, several factors influence the final result and longevity of your hair color. 


### Color Intensity and Saturation

Burgundy shades range from slightly brown-toned red to more vibrant red-purplish tones. Consider whether you prefer warmth (more brown) or coolness (more red-purple) in your shade. The dye's formula affects intensity, with some formulations lifting your natural hair color more than others.

### Shade Accuracy and Transfer Rate

High-quality dyes with better shade accuracy offer results closer to the color swatches, while poor accuracy can lead to mismatches. The transfer rate indicates how much of the dye color actually transfers to your hair - faster transfer means less color is lost during processing. 


### Texture and Conditioning

Some dye formulas contain conditioning agents that help prevent damage during coloring, while others may strip more natural oils. If you have chemically treated or damaged hair, seek ingredients that provide additional moisture and protection during the coloring process.

### Formulation and Application

Professional-grade dyes often require developer for full color development, whereas home dye kits typically use a single application process. The formulation's compatibility with your hair type and color is crucial for achieving consistent results across your hair strands.

### What type of hair dye is this?

Burgundy hair dye falls under the category of copper-based hair colors, which gives it its distinctive red undertones. It's designed to achieve rich, deep berry and ruby shades, though the exact results can depend on your natural hair color and previous dye history. 

Most burgundy dyes offer full coverage, with options available for both natural and previously colored hair. They range from single-toned formulas for natural brunettes to multi-shade mixes for more dramatic effects. 


### Can anyone use burgundy hair dye?

Burgundy dye is generally suitable for all hair types and skin tones, though as with any hair color, it's advisable to perform a patch test first. Those with naturally light hair may not achieve the same intensity as those with darker hair types. 

Women who are pregnant or nursing should avoid permanent hair dyes altogether, including burgundy, as the chemicals may not be safe during this time.

### How long does burgundy hair dye last?

Burgundy hair dye typically lasts between 4-6 weeks with proper maintenance. This depends on color maintenance, hair growth, and how frequently you swim or dye your hair. 

To extend the life of your burgundy color, use shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for colored hair, and limit your exposure to chlorine and harsh chemicals. 


### What tools are needed for at-home application?

Burgundy hair dye kits usually include all necessary tools for at-home application, including the dye itself, developer, gloves, and applicators. Always follow the manufacturer's instructions included with your kit. 

For best results, you'll need to section your hair, wear protective clothing, and have access to old towels or a drop cloth to protect your work surface.

### Are there natural alternatives to burgundy hair dye?

Natural alternatives to synthetic burgundy hair dye include henna-based treatments, which produce softer, more reddish-brown tones. However, henna can be unpredictable and may not cover gray hair effectively. 

Other natural options include homemade mixes using beetroot juice or red cabbage, which can give subtle bronze or red undertones to lighter hair. These alternatives offer a more eco-friendly choice but may not provide consistent results. 


### Is it safe to combine burgundy with other hair colors?

Burgundy can be combined with other hair colors for more dramatic effects, though results can vary depending on your natural color and previous dye history. For best results, consult a professional before mixing colors. 

The combination of burgundy with platinum or pastel shades can create striking icy reds or silver tones, while darker shades like black or brown can intensify the red undertones of burgundy.
