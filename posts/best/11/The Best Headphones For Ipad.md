## FAQ


### What is the difference between active and passive headphones?

Active headphones include built-in amplifiers and active noise cancellation. They deliver higher sound quality and can block out environmental noise, making them ideal for travel, work, or crowded environments. 

Passive headphones rely solely on the iPad's audio output. While they're simpler and less expensive, they typically provide basic sound reproduction without additional features like active noise cancellation. 


### Can I use any headphones with my iPad?

Most headphones will work with your iPad through the 3.5mm headphone jack or Bluetooth connection. However, certain features like active noise cancellation or equalizer settings may require headphones specifically designed for those capabilities. 

If your iPad has a USB-C port instead of a 3.5mm jack, you'll need a USB-C to 3.5mm adapter or Bluetooth headphones to connect. 


### How do I pair Bluetooth headphones with my iPad?

On your iPad, go to Settings > Bluetooth and make sure it's turned on. Then, press and hold the Bluetooth pairing button on your headphones until they appear in the list of available devices. 

Select your headphones from the list and follow any on-screen prompts to complete the pairing process. 


### What should I consider when choosing between wired and wireless headphones?

Wireless headphones offer greater freedom of movement, are untangled when you're on the go, and can cancel out environmental noise. They're especially useful for working or gaming in public spaces. 

Wired headphones are generally more affordable, less susceptible to interference, and provide a more stable connection. They're ideal for budget-conscious users or those who prioritize sound quality over convenience. 


### How long do Bluetooth headphones last on a single charge?

Standard Bluetooth headphones offer 5-10 hours of playback on a single charge before needing to be recharged. Some high-end models can provide up to 20 hours of battery life. 

Charging time varies from 1-4 hours depending on the model, with some newer headphones offering quick-charge features that provide several hours of playback with just 10-20 minutes of charging. 


### Can I use my iPad's headphones while it's in sleep mode?

Yes, you can continue listening to music or watch videos in the background while your iPad is in sleep mode. However, the sound will pause when the screen locks, and you'll need to unlock your device to resume playback. 

To prevent your iPad from automatically sleeping while playing music, go to Settings > Display & Brightness > Screen Timeout and set it to a longer duration, or use the Never option for unlimited playback.
