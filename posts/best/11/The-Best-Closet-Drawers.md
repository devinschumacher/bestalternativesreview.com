## Buyer's Guide

Closet drawers are a practical solution for organizing clothing and everyday items, offering both convenience and style. Understanding the key features and considerations will help you select the right drawers for your storage needs. 


### Size and Configuration

Measure your closet space to determine the appropriate drawer dimensions. Common sizes range from 12 to 24 inches wide and 12 to 30 inches deep. Consider vertical space as well, with drawers typically measuring 28 to 36 inches high. For multi-drawer configurations, opt for uniform depth to maximize organization. 

![Closet-Drawers-2](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Closet-Drawers-2/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Materials and Construction

Quality materials ensure longevity and smooth operation. Choose drawers with box construction for maximum durability, where the sides and back are attached to the front panel. The runners support the drawer's weight and ensure smooth movement - metal runners offer the most stability, followed by hardwood and plastic. 


### Storage Solutions

Dividers and organizers enhance functionality. Adjustable dividers allow flexible organization, while specialized containers protect smaller items. Consider adding built-in features like shoe organizers, hanging rods, or pullout baskets for increased storage versatility.

### How do I properly install closet drawers?

Start by securing the rail or support structure to the closet walls using appropriate hardware. Mark the center points for each drawer and drill pilot holes before mounting the drawer guides. 

Install the bottom rail first, attaching each guide to the rail. Slide the bottom rail into place and attach it to the floor or existing bottom rail. Install the top rail and slide the drawer frame into position, securing it with screws through the front rail.

### What maintenance is required for closet drawers?

Regular maintenance includes checking drawer guides for proper operation and lubricating metal glides annually. Dust and debris should be removed from the tracks and runners to maintain smooth movement. 

Inspect the back panel for warping or gaps, and replace any damaged or worn-out components. Apply a protective finish to the exterior of the drawers if they're exposed to moisture or frequent handling. 


### How do I select the right size for my closet?

Measure the total space available for drawers, including closet width and height. Allow 12-18 inches of clearance above and below the drawers for easy access. Consider the ratio of hanging space to drawer space based on your clothing needs. 

For deep closets, prioritize hanging space to maximize storage capacity. In shallower closets, focus on maximizing drawer storage. Factor in the width of your largest items to determine the minimum drawer size needed.
