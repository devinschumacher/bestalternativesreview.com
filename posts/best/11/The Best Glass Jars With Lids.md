## FAQ


### What is the difference between tempered and non-tempered glass jars?

Tempered glass jars are heat-treated to become four to five times stronger than non-tempered glass. This makes them much more resistant to impacts, temperature changes, and pressure. They also have the advantage of safely fragmenting into tiny round pieces rather than sharp shards when broken, enhancing safety. 

Non-tempered glass jars, while more cost-effective, lack this enhanced strength. They are suitable for applications where the risk of impact and temperature changes is minimal. 


### What closures work with glass jars?

Common closures for glass jars include screw caps, snap lids, and flip-top lids. Some jars feature specialized closures like airtight seals with rubber gaskets or specialized medical-grade lids with locking mechanisms. 

Always check the jar's mouth diameter and interior threading when selecting a closure to ensure proper fit and functionality. 


### How do I properly store glass jars?

To prevent breakage, store glass jars in a dry, cool location away from direct sunlight and significant temperature fluctuations. Use original closures when possible, as they may provide better sealing and protection against moisture. 

If stacking, ensure each jar supports the weight of those above it, and consider using protective cardboard or plastic between layers to prevent chipping. 


### Can glass jars be reused indefinitely?

While glass jars are reusable and environmentally friendly, their lifespan depends on usage and condition. Check for visible damage, cracks, or chips before reuse. Jars with scratches, especially on the inside, may trap bacteria more easily during food storage. 

Avoid reusing jars for acidic foods or for storing food longer than recommended, as the glass may react with certain foods. 


### How should I clean glass jars before reuse?

Wash glass jars with soap and warm water, ensuring all labels and residue are removed. For best results, use a soft brush to clean corners and rims. Rinse thoroughly with hot water. 

Sanitize jars by boiling them in water for 10 minutes, or running them through a dishwasher's hygiene cycle if dishwasher-safe. Always allow jars to air dry completely before use. 


### What conditions indicate a glass jar needs replacement?

Replace glass jars showing signs of physical damage, such as cracks or chips. Check lids for reliability and seals for proper closure. If a jar has been dropped, impacted, or used to store acidic or moisture-sensitive foods, consider replacing it to prevent potential contamination or breakage. 

A regular visual inspection can help identify jars that need replacement before they compromise food safety or integrity.
