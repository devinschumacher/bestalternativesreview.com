## Buyer's Guide

Tanning bed lotion plays a crucial role in your outdoor tanning experience, protecting your skin and enhancing your tan's appearance. Understanding the key features and considerations will help you choose the right product for your needs. 


### Key Ingredients & Their Benefits

Sunscreen protection: Look for lotions with broad-spectrum SPF to block both UVA and UVB rays. Higher SPF doesn't necessarily mean better protection, but it can be essential if you're tanning longer or have particularly sensitive skin. 

Hydrating properties: Ingredients like jojoba oil and aloe vera help lock in moisture, preventing skin dryness and cracking often associated with tanning sessions. 

Tan-enhancing agents: Some lotions contain DHA or EBA, which react with skin protein to create a natural-looking tan that doesn't wash off. These products typically require longer application and waiting times. 


### Application & Usage Instructions

Liquid consistency: Opt for a formula that's lightweight and spreads easily, avoiding heavy creams that may block pores or make you oily. Gels and mousses can be a good choice for men or those who prefer drier applications. 

Full-body coverage: Ensure the product you choose provides full-body coverage, as many salon-quality options target specific areas like the face and hands. 

Application technique: Proper technique is crucial. Apply in a thin, even layer, focusing on areas that receive the most sun exposure. Avoid applying to cuts, scratches, or irritated skin. 


### Customization Options

Scent preferences: Choose a formula with your preferred scent, noting that natural and fragrance-free options are available for those with sensitivities. 

Additional features: Some tanning lotions offer specialized formulas for specific skin types (dry, oily, sensitive) or concerns (aging, hyperpigmentation). 

Combination products: Consider lotions that also provide lip protection or feature SPF for everyday use, combining your tanning needs with general skin care. 


### Maintenance & Storage

Product shelf life: Check the expiration date and store your lotion properly (usually in a cool, dry place) to maintain its effectiveness. 

Reapplication guidelines: Frequency depends on your tanning duration and lotions' water-resistant properties. General guidelines recommend reapplication every 60-90 minutes during tanning sessions. 

Cleaning needs: Some lotions require washing off after tanning sessions, while others can be worn directly through clothing. Follow product instructions for proper removal. 


## FAQ


### Why use tanning bed lotion?

Special tanning bed lotion helps prevent skin dryness, enhances tan development, and protects your skin from UV damage. The ingredients in these lotions help create a more even tan while keeping your skin moisturized and healthy. 

Using lotion regularly can also prevent common side effects like itching, peeling, and burning, making your tanning experience more comfortable and enjoyable. 


### What ingredients should I look for in tanning bed lotion?

Look for lotions that contain humectants like glycerin or hyaluronic acid to lock in moisture. Also consider products with UV protectants like zinc oxide or titanium dioxide, which can help prevent sunburn while tanning. 

Avoid lotions with harsh chemicals or fragrances, as these can irritate sensitive skin. Ingredients like Aloe Vera can soothe and protect the skin during the tanning process. 


### How long before tanning should I apply lotion?

Apply tanning lotion 20-30 minutes before entering the tanning bed. This allows the products' ingredients to fully absorb into your skin and start working their protective and moisturizing effects. 

If you're using a product with active self-tanning ingredients, follow the manufacturer's instructions for application timing. Some products require longer wait times to develop an even color. 


### Can I use regular lotion instead of tanning bed lotion?

While regular lotion can help prevent dry skin, specialized tanning bed lotions contain specific ingredients that enhance tan development and protect your skin. Regular lotions may not provide the same level of UV protection or moisture retention. 

Specialized lotions are designed to resist breakdown from UV light and contain ingredients that help the tanning process, making them more effective than regular lotion for tanning purposes. 


### How much should I apply to my body?

Use enough lotion to cover your entire body, with a slightly heavier application to areas that tan more deeply, like the chest and back. Aim for a thin, even layer to avoid excessive oiliness and maintain a natural-looking tan. 

A common recommendation is to use about 1-2 ounces of lotion per body application. Be thorough in your application to ensure even coverage and optimal results. 


### Do I need to reapply lotion during my tanning session?

Reapply lotion if you notice areas becoming dry or if you're extending your session beyond the recommended time. This helps maintain even moisture levels and prevents the lotion from breaking down under UV light. 

For maximum protection and moisture, reapply a light layer of lotion mid-session, focusing on areas that may start to feel dry. This can also help maintain your tan's intensity throughout your session.
