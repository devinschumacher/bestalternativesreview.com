### Technical Specifications

When considering audio headphones, technical specifications play a crucial role. Look for details on frequency response, impedance, and sensitivity. For studio monitoring, a flat frequency response within a narrow range (like 20-20,000 Hz) helps maintain accurate sound reproduction. Impedance affects compatibility with different audio sources, while sensitivity influences how loud the headphones can play at a given signal level. 


### Build Quality and Durability

The construction materials and build methods impact both comfort and longevity. Premium headphones often feature robust cables, durable drivers, and comfortable over-ear pads made from breathable materials. Consider the weight distribution, as lighter models may be more tiring to wear during extended sessions. 


### Cable Design and Connectivity

The cable design should account for both practicality and durability. Look for headphones with single-ended cables for simpler connections, versus balanced XLR cables which offer better noise rejection. The cable length and stiffness affect mobility and tangle resistance. Connector quality is also crucial; look for durable gold-plated connections to prevent corrosion and maintain signal integrity. 


### Additional Features

Some headphones offer additional features that can enhance the listening experience. Noise isolation helps maintain focus by reducing ambient sounds, while some models include adjustable headbands or earcup rotation for personalized comfort. Consider whether you need these features based on your intended use and environment.
