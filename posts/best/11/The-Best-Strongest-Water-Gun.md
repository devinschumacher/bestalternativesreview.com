## FAQ


### What makes a water gun "strongest"?

The strength of a water gun is typically determined by its maximum water pressure and range. High-pressure guns can project water farther and with more force, making them more powerful. 

Additional factors include the gun's design for minimizing water leakage and its ability to maintain consistent pressure during use. 


### How do you measure water gun pressure?

Water gun pressure is usually measured in pounds per square inch (psi). Professional water guns often range from 1,000 to 1,500 psi. 

Most pressure measurement devices clip onto the water tank or gun body and display the reading directly. 


### What's the longest water gun range?

High-pressure water guns can achieve ranges up to 60 feet, while standard pressure guns typically hit targets between 20-40 feet. 

Range depends on pressure, nozzle design, and water source volume. Gravity-fed guns generally have shorter ranges than those with internal pumps. 


### Are there age restrictions for strong water guns?

Most strong water guns are appropriate for ages 8 and up due to their power and pressure. Always supervise young children when using these products. 

Check the manufacturer's recommendations for specific age guidelines and safety precautions. 


### What maintenance is needed for strong water guns?

Regularly check seals and o-rings for wear, and replace them as needed to maintain proper pressure and prevent leaks. 

After use, thoroughly dry the gun and store it in a cool, dry place to prevent rusting and maintain performance. 


### How do you tell if a water gun is too strong?

A water gun is too strong if it causes intentional injury, damage to property, or persistent complaints from others. The force should allow for fun play without danger. 

Soft surfaces like grass should be unaffected by play. If damage occurs, consider using a less powerful model.
