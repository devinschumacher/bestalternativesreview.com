## FAQ


### What makes Vans high tops different from regular Vans shoes?

Vans high tops feature an additional layer of rubber between the ankle strap and the upper part of the shoe. This design provides extra support and protects the feet from getting scuffed or worn at the ankle. 

The high top silhouette also offers improved stability and coverage, making these shoes particularly suitable for activities like skateboarding or as everyday footwear in colder weather. 


### What materials are used in Vans high tops?

The uppers of Vans high tops are typically made from durable canvas or leather, with rubber reinforcement around the heel and toe areas. The midsole uses a combination of foam and rubber for cushioning and traction. 

Modern versions may incorporate synthetic materials for added moisture resistance and quick-drying properties, while maintaining the classic Vans construction methods. 


### How do you clean Vans high tops?

Regular maintenance involves wiping the shoes down with a damp cloth to remove surface dirt and dust. For tougher stains, use a mixture of mild soap and water, avoiding abrasive cleaners that can damage the material. 

After cleaning, allow the shoes to air dry completely before wearing. Avoid using a hairdryer or direct heat sources, as they can affect the rubber components. 


### Can Vans high tops machine wash?

Machine washing is generally not recommended for canvas and leather Vans high tops, as it can stretch the rubber components and damage the material. Hand cleaning is the best method for maintaining their appearance and longevity. 

If you decide to machine wash your Vans high tops, use a gentle cycle with cold water and a mild detergent. Consider washing them inside out to protect the rubber details. 


### What sizing options are available for Vans high tops?

Vans typically offers three main width fits for their high top models: regular, wide, and extra wide. The sizing chart indicates how much extra width each style provides for comfort. 

Most Vans high tops run true to size, but some variants, like those with reinforced rubber, may feel slightly narrower. It's recommended to measure your feet and compare to their sizing chart for the best fit.
