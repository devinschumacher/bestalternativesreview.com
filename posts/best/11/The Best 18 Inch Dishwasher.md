## FAQ


### What is an 18-inch dishwasher?

An 18-inch dishwasher is a compact kitchen appliance that measures 18 inches in width, making it ideal for smaller kitchen spaces or apartments with limited counter and cabinet width. These dishwashers are typically 24 inches tall, matching standard counter heights. 

While smaller than full-sized models, 18-inch dishwashers offer complete cleaning cycles and are available in various styles, including under-mount, freestanding, and built-in options. They are compatible with both standard and European-sized plates and glasses. 


### How much counter space do I need for an 18-inch dishwasher?

For optimal installation, you'll need 18 inches of cabinet width and about 12 inches of clearance on either side. Additionally, ensure you have at least 3 inches of space above the dishwasher for proper ventilation and access. 

Consider the overall depth of your kitchen cabinets when installing an 18-inch dishwasher. Standard cabinet depths are 24 inches, allowing for proper door clearance when opening. 


### What are the main differences between European and American plate sizes?

European plates are typically 12 inches in diameter, while American plates measure 10 inches. As a result, European plates might not fit as well in American dishwashers, which are designed for smaller plates. 

Most 18-inch dishwashers can accommodate both plate sizes through their adjustable racks. However, European plates may require additional space, so consider this when planning your dish placement. 


### How efficient are 18-inch dishwashers in terms of water and energy consumption?

Modern 18-inch dishwashers typically use between 4 and 6 gallons of water per cycle, with energy consumption ranging from 0.9 to 1.7 kilowatt-hours per load. This is comparable to full-sized models when considering the smaller load capacity. 

Efficiency varies by model and cycle selection. Look for dishwashers with ENERGY STAR certification, which typically meet stricter water and energy consumption standards. 


### What maintenance requirements do 18-inch dishwashers have?

Regular maintenance includes checking the filter monthly for debris, ensuring proper door sealing, and maintaining good drainage. The dishwasher should be run with a cleaning cycle at least once a month to prevent mineral buildup. 

Check the dishwasher's water supply and drain lines annually for leaks or blockages. Proper ventilation behind the unit and regular cleaning of the exterior can also extend its lifespan. 


### Can 18-inch dishwashers accommodate all types of dishware?

These dishwashers can handle standard dinnerware, glasses, and utensils. However, larger serving pieces or deep cookware may not fit properly due to their smaller capacity. 

Most models include adjustable rack systems that can be customized to fit different dish sizes. Consider this when selecting a model to ensure it meets your specific dish storage needs.
