Choosing the right beach blanket can transform your sandside experience, keeping you cozy and dry while blocking out the sun. Our comprehensive guide covers the essential features to consider when selecting a beach blanket that prevents sand from seeping through. 


### Waterproof Layer

Look for a blanket with a waterproof backing that seals out moisture. This feature not only prevents sand penetration but also keeps you dry during unexpected rain showers. The waterproof layer should extend to the edges and corners for complete protection.

### Non-Slip Base

A non-slip bottom surface is crucial for keeping your blanket in place on sandy or grassy surfaces. The base material should grip firmly while remaining comfortable underfoot. Textured patterns or rubberized coatings provide the best traction.

Most beach blankets block harmful UV rays, but check the material's SPF rating. Higher SPF ensures better sun protection. Additionally, look for blankets with UPF (Ultraviolet Protection Factor) certification for added peace of mind.
