## FAQ


### What are Keto Coconut Clusters?

Keto Coconut Clusters are a keto-friendly snack that combines coconut flakes with other nutrient-dense ingredients like nuts and seeds. These clusters are typically low-carb, high-fat, and contain minimal sugar. 

These clusters serve as an ideal keto snack for individuals following a ketogenic diet, providing sustained energy and helping maintain ketosis. 


### What ingredients are commonly used in Keto Coconut Clusters?

The primary ingredients in Keto Coconut Clusters include coconut flakes, nuts (such as almonds or macadamia nuts), seeds (like pumpkin seeds or sunflower seeds), and a small amount of sweetener, usually erythritol or monk fruit extract. 

Some recipes may also include additional ingredients like cocoa powder, sea salt, or vanilla extract to enhance flavor while maintaining low-carb content. 


### How are Keto Coconut Clusters made?

Keto Coconut Clusters are typically made by combining coconut flakes with mixed nuts and seeds in a large bowl. The mixture is then pressed into a pan and left to firm up before being broken into clusters. 

Some recipes involve rolling the clusters in coconut flakes or other toppings before allowing them to set completely. 


### What are the nutritional benefits of Keto Coconut Clusters?

These clusters provide a good source of healthy fats from nuts and seeds, which are essential for a ketogenic diet. They also contain fiber from coconut and seeds, helping to promote satiety and digestive health. 

The low-carb profile makes them suitable for maintaining ketosis and managing blood sugar levels, while the combination of nuts and seeds offers vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. 


### How long do Keto Coconut Clusters last?

Once made and fully set, Keto Coconut Clusters will last for about 1-2 weeks when stored in an airtight container at room temperature. They should be kept away from moisture and heat to maintain their quality. 

For longer storage, clusters can be frozen for up to 3 months. When freezing, place them in an airtight container or freezer-safe bag to prevent freezer burn.
