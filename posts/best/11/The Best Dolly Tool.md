## FAQ

### What is a dolly tool?

A dolly tool is a specialized building accessory used for floor preparation, particularly in concrete and stone work. It consists of a metal frame with interchangeable attachments that allow for the creation, maintenance, and removal of flat, smooth surfaces. 

The tool works by using hydraulic pressure to press the attachment into the floor, effectively "doling" or leveling materials. This process helps achieve a consistent surface texture and proper slope for various flooring applications. 


### How does a dolly tool work?

The basic mechanism of a dolly tool involves hydraulic pressure that drives the attachment into the floor material. The user controls the depth and consistency of the surface created through precise pressure application and attachment selection. 

Common attachments include grinding wheels, polishing pads, and sealer application tools. The hydraulic system ensures even pressure distribution, making the tool suitable for both professionals and experienced DIY users.

### What are the main components of a dolly tool?

Essential components of a dolly tool include the hydraulic system, frame assembly, and interchangeable attachments. The hydraulic system controls pressure and flow, while the frame provides stability and mounting points for the attachments. 

Attachments typically consist of specialized wheels, pads, or applicators designed for specific surface treatments such as grinding, polishing, or sealer application. 


### What is the difference between dolly tool attachments?

Dolly tool attachments vary in material and design to suit different flooring applications. For example, grinding wheels are suitable for removing existing finishes and creating a clean surface, while polishing pads enhance surface smoothness and provide a professional finish. 

Sealer application tools specifically target the final stage of concrete or stone preparation, helping to protect the surface and enhance its appearance.

### How do I maintain a dolly tool?

Regular maintenance of a dolly tool includes checking the hydraulic system for leaks and ensuring all components are free from debris. The hydraulic fluid should be checked and changed as recommended by the manufacturer. 

Attachments should be inspected for wear and cleaned thoroughly after use. The frame and hydraulic cylinders should be lubricated according to the manufacturer's guidelines to maintain smooth operation. 


### What are the limitations of dolly tools?

While dolly tools are powerful for floor preparation, they require proper technique and operator skill to achieve optimal results. The hydraulic system demands regular maintenance to prevent leaks and ensure consistent performance. 

Attachment selection is critical, as using unsuitable tools can damage the floor surface or reduce the tool's effectiveness. The weight and size of the tool make it less suitable for overhead work or confined spaces.
