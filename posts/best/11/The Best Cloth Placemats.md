### What are cloth placemats primarily used for?

Cloth placemats protect tables from scuffs and scratches caused by tableware, especially helpful in preventing damage from hot dishes. They also add a decorative element to the dining table, coming in various sizes, patterns, and materials to match different table settings and occasions. 

These protective covers can measure anywhere from 14 to 24 inches in diameter, with some featuring extensions that can be used as coasters. The primary material options include cotton, linen, and polyester, each offering different levels of protection and style. 


### How do you properly care for cloth placemats?

Machine wash your cloth placemats using mild detergent and a gentle cycle. For best results, consider using a mesh bag to prevent tangling, especially with circular placemats. Air dry them completely before use, as hanging can cause stretching. 

Regular care maintains their appearance and functionality. Flip them weekly to ensure even wear and consider alternating between multiple sets to extend their lifespan.

### What sizes are available for cloth placemats?

Standard cloth placemat sizes typically range from 14 to 24 inches in diameter, with some manufacturers offering larger options for centerpiece placements. Common diameters include 16, 18, and 20 inches, while square placemats usually measure 15 by 15 inches. 

For extended table coverage, placemats with extensions or overlapping edges can double as coasters, combining protection with additional surface area. 


### What are the most practical material options?

Cotton, linen, and blended cotton-polyester are the most practical materials for cloth placemats due to their durability and moderate care requirements. These natural fabrics resist stains well and maintain their shape through regular use. 

Eco-friendly options like organic cotton and jute offer similar protection with sustainable benefits, though they may require more careful handling to prevent pilling and fraying.

### How do you choose the right number of placemats?

For proper protection and visual appeal, use one placemat per person plus one extra for any centerpiece or decorative placement. This ratio helps distribute pressure evenly and ensures adequate table coverage. 

When using multiple placemats, consider the cumulative size and how it affects your available surface area. Ensure there's enough space around each placemat for comfortable dining and movement.
