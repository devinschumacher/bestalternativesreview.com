## Buyer's Guide

Fabric transfer paper is essential for permanently transferring designs onto clothing and fabrics. Understanding its features and proper use is crucial for achieving professional-looking results. 


### Key Features of Fabric Transfer Paper

Fabric transfer paper comes in various types, including heat-sensitive and sublimation paper. Heat-sensitive paper requires ironing for transfer, while sublimation paper uses heat presses or specialized equipment. The paper thickness affects transfer quality, with thinner paper providing better detail but potentially curling during transfer. 

Image quality depends on paper characteristics like opacity and surface texture. Premium papers offer sharper details and richer colors, suitable for complex designs and photographic transfers. Consider the paper's environmental impact, as some materials contain PVC or other chemicals that can damage fabrics when not fully heat-set.

### What is fabric transfer paper?

Fabric transfer paper is a specialized printing medium designed to fix images onto fabric surfaces. It consists of a receptive coating on one side and a release liner on the other, allowing for precise image transfer through heat application. 

When placed between a printed design and the fabric, this paper enables heat press machines to bond the graphic permanently to various fabrics, including cotton, polyester, and blends. 


### How does fabric transfer paper work?

The paper's receptive coating contains microcapsules that break open when heated, releasing pigments directly onto the fabric. The combination of heat and pressure fuses the image permanently while the paper release liner is removed. 

Commonly used between 320-350°F (160-177°C), heat press machines apply the necessary temperature and pressure for 15-30 seconds to ensure proper bond and image transfer.
