## Buyer's Guide

Gamo offers a variety of airguns, each designed for specific purposes and skill levels. Understanding the differences between their models helps in selecting the right air rifle or air pistol for your needs. 


### Caliber and Power

The caliber determines the power and range of the airgun. Gamo's popular calibers include. 177, . 22, and. 25. Higher calibers deliver more energy and punch but require more air pressure. The power level affects how far the gun shoots and how much kinetic energy the pellet carries. 


### Gun Type

Gamo produces both rifles and pistols. Rifles typically offer higher power and longer ranges, making them suitable for hunting small game. Pistols are shorter and lighter, ideal for close-range practice or indoor shooting. 


### Trigger Mechanism

Modern Gamo guns feature progressive triggers or integrated cocking mechanisms. Progressive triggers reduce trigger pull weight gradually, improving accuracy. Cocking mechanisms like the PowerLever or PowerSystem help maintain consistent energy transfer from the power source to the pellet. 


### Scope Compatibility

Many Gamo rifles are compatible with various scope mounts, allowing you to customize your sight picture. Consider the maximum magnification and size of the scope compatible with your chosen gun.

The company offers a wide range of products, from single-shot air rifles and pistols to multi-shot systems, catering to both recreational users and competitive shooters.

Gamo focuses on delivering high-quality products at competitive prices, with many models featuring precision engineering and durable construction.

### What types of air guns are available in Gamo's lineup?

They offer a comprehensive selection including single-shot rifles and pistols, as well as multi-shot systems like their popular Gamo 5020 series.
