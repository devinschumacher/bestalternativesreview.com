## Buyer's Guide

rowing gloves enhance comfort, prevent blisters, and protect hands during workouts. They're especially recommended for indoor rowing machines where handles can be cold and rough. Here's what to consider when choosing the right pair. 


### Hand Protection

Look for gloves with padded palms and fingers to cushion your hands during high-intensity workouts. The padding should be durable enough to withstand repeated gripping motions. Fingerless designs are common for better grip on the handles, while full-fingered gloves offer additional thermal insulation and protection from sweat. 


### Cold Weather Considerations

When rowing in colder environments, select gloves with moisture-wicking fabrics and insulated inserts. Synthetic materials like neoprene provide warmth without adding excessive bulk. The gloves should allow for dexterity while keeping your hands warm and dry during intense sessions. 


### Rubberized Grip Technology

Griptastic rubberized surfaces on key areas like the palm and fingertips significantly reduce slippage, especially during powerful pulling strokes. The rubber should adhere well to various handle surfaces and maintain its grip integrity through repeated use. 


### Maintenance Requirements
