## Buyer's Guide

When your Roku remote stops working or becomes too old, replacing it might seem simple - but choosing the right one can make a big difference in your streaming experience. This guide helps you understand the key features to look for and consider when selecting a replacement Roku remote. 


### Basic Features

Modern Roku remotes offer improved usability with features like longer battery life, better build quality, and enhanced ergonomics. Look for a remote with a comfortable grip, durable buttons, and a reliable power source to prevent future failures. 


### Button Layout and Functionality

The button layout should match the Roku interface for intuitive control. Common buttons include power, volume, channel selection, and the home menu. Consider a remote with additional features like voice commands, dedicated app keys, or physical return/home buttons for easier navigation. 


### Battery Type and Power Source

Roku remotes typically use small coin cells (CR2032) that last several years with regular use. Some remotes offer replaceable batteries, while newer models may use non-removable lithium-ion cells. Consider the battery type and replacement options for long-term convenience. 


### Range and Connectivity

While most Roku remotes work well within a room, longer-range options are available for larger living spaces or apartments. Some remotes use proprietary wireless technology, while others may require line-of-sight for optimal performance. Consider your viewing habits and room size when selecting range capabilities.

### How much do Roku remote replacements cost?
