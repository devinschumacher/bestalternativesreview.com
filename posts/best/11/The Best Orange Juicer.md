## FAQ


### What is the difference between centrifugal and masticating orange juicers?

Centrifugal juicers separate pulp from juice using high-speed spinning, making them quick but less efficient for citrus. Masticating juicers crush and extract juice slowly, preserving more nutrients and producing less heat, making them better for citrus fruits. 

While centrifugal juicers are more affordable and convenient, masticating juicers typically yield a higher quality orange juice and are suitable for those prioritizing nutritional content. 


### How do I properly store freshly squeezed orange juice?

Store freshly squeezed orange juice in aseptic containers with as little air space as possible to minimize oxidation. Keep at room temperature for no more than 24 hours or refrigerate in a sealed container for 3-5 days. 

For extended storage, consider freezing the juice in ice cube trays or freezer-safe containers. Frozen orange juice can last up to 3 months when properly sealed. 


### What are the key factors to consider when purchasing an orange juicer?

Key factors include motor power, pulp extraction efficiency, ease of cleaning, and durability. Consider the size and space available, as well as any specific features like automatic pulp ejection or reversible speed control. 

For professional-grade performance, look for juicers with stainless steel components and commercial-grade motors. Entry-level models with plastic parts and basic features are suitable for occasional use. 


### How long does an orange juicer typically last?

With regular use and proper maintenance, an orange juicer can last 5-10 years. Factors affecting longevity include usage frequency, quality of construction, and adherence to cleaning instructions. 

Stainless steel and BPA-free materials generally provide longer-lasting performance compared to cheaper plastics. Regularly check for wear on blades and motor components to determine if maintenance or replacement is needed. 


### Can I use my orange juicer for other types of fruit?

Most orange juicers are designed specifically for citrus fruits and may not perform optimally with non-citrus varieties. Using them on non-citrus fruits can damage the appliance and affect juicing efficiency. 

If you need to juice other types of produce, consider purchasing a juicer specifically designed for the fruits you plan to use. This ensures optimal performance and longer appliance lifespan. 


### How should I clean my orange juicer?

After each use, detach all parts and thoroughly wash the juicer according to the manufacturer's instructions. Most parts can be hand-washed with mild soap and water, while some may require soaking or specialized brushes for stubborn residue removal. 

Reassemble the juicer when thoroughly dry, following the cleaning sequence in reverse. Regular maintenance includes checking for worn parts and performing deep cleaning every 2-3 months to prevent buildup and ensure optimal performance.
