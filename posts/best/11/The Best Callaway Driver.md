### What are the key technical specifications to consider?

Key specifications include head size, moment of inertia (MOI), and center of gravity (CG) position. Larger heads (460 cubic centimeters and above) generally offer more forgiveness, while lower CG designs promote straighter ball flights. 

The center of gravity should be positioned towards the rear for slightly higher starting balls, while forward CG designs promote more penetrating shots. 


### Are adjustable features worth the extra cost?

Adjustable settings allow customization of ball flight and launch angle, making the driver adaptable to different golf courses and conditions. These features are particularly beneficial for high-handicappers or golfers with variable swing characteristics. 

While the initial investment may be higher, adjustable drivers often provide greater value in terms of performance scalability and equipment longevity.

### What's the difference between men's and women's drivers?

Women's drivers typically feature smaller overall size, lighter weight, and more fade-bias designs to accommodate slower swing speeds and less consistent ball striking. They often have shallower faces and shorter, softer grips. 

Men's drivers offer a larger sweet spot, deeper face design for more ball speed, and adjustable features that can help golfers tune the ball flight to their specific swing characteristics. 


### How should I maintain my Callaway driver?

Regular maintenance includes replacing worn grips every 6-12 months, cleaning the clubface before and after each round, and checking the adjustable settings to ensure proper alignment. The crown should be protected from scratches with a club cover when not in use. 

A well-maintained driver can last 3-5 years with regular play, but signs of wear on the face or crown indicate it's time for an inspection or replacement.
