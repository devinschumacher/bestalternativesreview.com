## Buyer's Guide

A Displaylink docking station streamlines your work setup by extending your display options and connecting multiple devices through a single cable. Consider several key factors to find the right docking station for your needs. 


### USB-C or USB-A Compatibility

Select a docking station that matches your primary computer's USB type. USB-C options offer faster charging and data transfer but require devices with USB-C ports. USB-A stations work with older computers but have slower data transfer speeds. 

![DisplayLink-Docking-Station-2](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/DisplayLink-Docking-Station-2/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Display Output Options

Most docking stations provide at least two DisplayPort or HDMI outputs for external displays. Some advanced models offer additional outputs, including USB-C for daisy-chaining more devices. Consider how many monitors you need and their maximum supported resolution. 


### Integrated Ports

Docking stations typically include USB-A ports for connecting keyboards, mice, and other peripherals. Advanced models feature Thunderbolt 3 ports for even faster data transfer and power delivery. Wireless charging pads and SD card readers are convenient extras for some users. 

![DisplayLink-Docking-Station-3](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/DisplayLink-Docking-Station-3/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Power Delivery and Charging

Ensure the docking station provides sufficient power delivery for your devices, especially if you're connecting laptops or USB-C devices. Some stations offer pass-through charging, allowing you to charge connected devices while using them.

### What is a Displaylink docking station?

Displaylink docking stations enable you to connect multiple displays and peripheral devices to a laptop or ultrabook without requiring additional USB ports. They use Displaylink technology to deliver high-quality video and USB connectivity through a single USB-C or DisplayPort connection. 

The Displaylink platform supports up to three displays with resolutions up to 4K, as well as USB 3.0 and USB-C ports for charging and data transfer. This makes them ideal for professionals who need multiple display setups but have limited port availability on their laptops. 


### How do Displaylink docking stations compare to USB hubs?

Displaylink docking stations excel in multi-display capabilities compared to standard USB hubs. While USB hubs primarily focus on additional USB ports, Displaylink solutions offer video output through DisplayPort or USB-C, supporting up to three displays with high resolutions. 

USB hubs may also experience performance limitations when connecting multiple high-bandwidth devices, whereas Displaylink technology ensures stable performance for both display outputs and USB connections.

### What types of Displaylink docking stations are available?

Displaylink offers several models, including USB-C and DisplayPort variants. USB-C docking stations usually feature USB-C input and provide USB-C charging, while DisplayPort models use DisplayPort input for video output. 

These stations come in different form factors, from compact travel docks to all-in-one solutions with built-in power adapters. The choice depends on your specific needs for portability and power requirements. 


### Are Displaylink docking stations compatible with Windows and Mac?

Displaylink products support both Windows and Mac operating systems. They include drivers for Windows 10, 11, and macOS, allowing seamless integration with modern computers. Older operating systems may require specific drivers. 

The drivers enable full functionality, including multi-display capabilities and USB connectivity. Mac users will need to download and install the Displaylink software from the official website to access all features.

### What are the power requirements for Displaylink docking stations?

Most Displaylink docks powered by USB-C draw power through the USB-C port, requiring a minimum 15W USB-C power supply. Standalone power adapters are available for models that don't support USB-C input. 

Some advanced models include 65W power adapters, allowing simultaneous charging of up to two USB-C devices while providing DisplayPort video output. The power configuration depends on the specific model you choose. 


### How do Displaylink docking stations handle video syncing and display configuration?

Displaylink docking stations support multiple video syncing modes, including dual independent displays and single extended display setups. The configuration process requires drivers installation and display setup through Windows or Mac system settings. 

Once configured, the docking station automatically manages display scaling and orientation based on your computer's display settings. The Displaylink software also allows additional customization of display properties and USB settings.
