## FAQ


### What safety features should I look for in a children's playhouse?

Look for a playhouse with rounded edges and corners to prevent injury. The floor should be made of non-slip material, and all surfaces should be smooth to eliminate potential tripping hazards. Adequate ventilation is important to prevent heat buildup, especially if the playhouse doesn't have air vents or windows. 

Sturdy construction is crucial - check that the frame is made from high-quality materials and that all connections are secure. The playhouse should have weather-resistant finishes and durable hardware to withstand outdoor conditions. 


### How do I choose the right size playhouse for my child?

Consider your child's age and developmental stage. Younger children (ages 2-4) need smaller playhouses with simpler designs. Older children (ages 5-12) can handle larger structures with more features. Allow at least 36 inches of space per child for comfortable play. 

Check the playhouse dimensions against your available yard space. The total footprint should be no more than 10 feet by 10 feet for smaller playhouses, with clear access for entry and exit. 


### What materials are best for outdoor playhouses?

Pressure-treated wood and vinyl offer excellent durability and resistance to weathering. However, they can contain chemicals. Pine and cedar are natural alternatives that resist rot and insects but require regular maintenance. 

Plastic playground materials are increasingly popular due to their longevity and resistance to fading and cracking. Metal structures are also available but may not be as visually appealing and can be prone to rusting. 


### How do I properly maintain a children's playhouse?

Regularly inspect the playhouse for loose bolts, cracks, or damaged areas. Clean it quarterly with a mild soap solution and water to remove dirt and grime. Check weather stripping and seals to prevent water infiltration. 

Apply protective treatments like sealants or finishes yearly to maintain the playhouse's appearance and extend its lifespan. Trim surrounding vegetation to prevent overgrown branches that could damage the structure. 


### What is the average lifespan of a children's playhouse?

With proper maintenance, a well-built playhouse made from quality materials can last 10-20 years. Pressure-treated wood and vinyl structures typically have the longest lifespan, while metal and plastic versions can last 15-20 years. 

The actual lifespan depends on factors like local weather conditions, maintenance frequency, and how heavily the playhouse is used. Regular inspections can help identify potential issues before they become serious problems. 


### How much space do I need for a children's playhouse?

The playhouse itself should have a minimum of 3 feet by 3 feet for a seated child, with an additional 2 feet of space around it for movement. For larger playhouses with multiple levels, allow at least 4 feet between each level to prevent overcrowding. 

When siting the playhouse, consider the surrounding space. Children need room to run and play around the structure. A clear space of 10 feet in all directions around the playhouse is ideal for safety.
