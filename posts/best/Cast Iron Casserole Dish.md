## Buyer's Guide

When you're ready to dive into the world of cooking, cast iron casserole dishes might be the perfect addition to your kitchen arsenal. These versatile pots are built to last, making them an excellent investment for new and seasoned chefs alike. In this buyer's guide, we'll look at the key features you should consider when buying a cast iron casserole dish and offer some general advice on how to get the most out of yours. 


### Choose the Right Size

The size of your cast iron casserole dish can make a big difference when it comes to cooking. A larger dish will allow you to cook more significant portions for large gatherings, while smaller dishes are more suitable for smaller families. Consider the size of your usual cooking needs and choose accordingly. Most cast iron casserole dishes come in the following sizes: 7 inches, 10 inches, 12 inches, and even larger.

### Heat Capacity and Retention

One of the most significant benefits of cast iron casserole dishes is their ability to retain heat. This means that they maintain a steady temperature throughout the cooking process, which can be beneficial for dishes that require consistent heat (e. g. , slow cookers, soups, and stews). However, this also means that the cooking surface is relatively smooth and non-stick. When preparing dishes with sticky ingredients, it's wise to add some oil or butter to the pan. 


### Seasoning Your Cast Iron Casserole Dish

Cast iron casserole dishes require seasoning to ensure they remain non-stick and easy to clean.
