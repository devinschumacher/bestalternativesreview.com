### What sets apart the conventional and stone lamp?

Conventional lamps often stem from materials such as glass or metal, while stone lamps are typically crafted from stone or rock. This element gives stone lamps a rustic, natural appearance and makes them an excellent addition to outdoor settings or rustic-themed rooms. Moreover, stone lamps are usually more robust, making them a reliable choice over the long run.

Stone lamps are not merely functional elements; they also serve as decorative items. They can serve as a central focus in a space or as statement pieces, transforming the ambiance. Some stone lamps come equipped with built-in LED lights or tea lights, adding functionality to their aesthetics.


### Is it easy to maintain stone lamps?

Indeed, stone lamps are generally easy to clean. Most stone varieties are non-porous, meaning they do not absorb liquids and stains easily. To clean the lamp, use a soft damp cloth, gently wiping it. For more stubborn dirt or spots, a mild soap and water solution is recommended. After cleaning, ensure that the lamp is rinsed and dried thoroughly to avoid any residue buildup.

Avoid using abrasive or harsh chemicals, as they can damage the stone or its finish. Also, refrain from using extremely hot or boiling water, as it might damage the stone. Regular cleaning and maintenance can prolong the lamp's lifespan and keep it looking its best.
