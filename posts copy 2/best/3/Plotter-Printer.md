## FAQ


### What are plotter printers?

Plotter printers, also known as pen plotters or graphic plotters, are specialized printers that can produce detailed, high-quality vector graphics on paper. These printers are commonly used for technical drawings, architectural plans, engineering schematics, and digital art. They are typically more expensive than standard desktop printers and are tailored to meet the demands of professionals and enthusiasts who require precise and high-quality output. 

Plotter printers are different from inkjet printers, which are better suited for printing photos and text documents. While inkjet printers use liquid ink to create images, plotter printers use a pen or stylus to draw on the paper, offering distinct advantages when it comes to producing vector graphics. Plotter printers are particularly useful for large-scale output or detailed technical illustrations, where precise lines and accurate scaling are essential.

Proper maintenance and cleaning of a plotter printer are essential for ensuring consistent performance and longevity. Here are some tips for maintaining your plotter printer: 
1. Regularly empty the output tray and clear any paper jams;

3. Check for ink buildup on the pen or stylus and clean it if necessary;
