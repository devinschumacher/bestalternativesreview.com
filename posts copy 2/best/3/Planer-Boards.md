### What are planer boards?

Planer boards, also known as planed boards or dressed boards, are a type of engineered wood product made by heating, steaming, and flattening logs to reduce dimensional instability caused by natural wood movements such as warping, twisting, and cupping. These boards undergo a rigorous manufacturing process to achieve a consistent and stable thickness, making them ideal for various applications ranging from flooring to cabinetry. 

Planer boards are available in various sizes, thicknesses, and finishes to suit different design preferences and functional requirements. They come in a wide range of species, including hardwoods and softwoods, to cater to various markets and budgets. Some popular species used for planer boards are oak, maple, hickory, and pine. 


### How are planer boards made?

The process of creating planer boards involves several steps, including log selection, drying, and milling or planing. Firstly, logs are chosen based on their straightness, grain pattern, and moisture content. Next, the selected logs are dried to minimize the loss of moisture, which reduces the risk of warping or cracking during the manufacturing process. 

After drying, the logs are steamed at high temperatures for a few hours to soften them and make it easier to plane. The steaming process also helps to remove any sapwood or defects that might affect the board's appearance or strength. After steaming, the logs are cooled down and sent through a planer to achieve the desired thickness and smooth surface finish. Finally, the planed boards are processed and sorted according to their quality and properties, and their dimensions are rounded to the nearest fraction of an inch. 


### What are the advantages of using planer boards?

Planer boards offer several advantages over traditional solid wood products. Firstly, they have a consistent and uniform thickness, which eliminates the need for time-consuming milling or planing during the installation process. Secondly, planer boards are less prone to warping, cupping, or twisting due to their stabilized nature, making them ideal for flooring or other applications where dimensional stability is crucial. 

Additionally, planer boards are generally more cost-effective than solid wood products, as they utilize a mixture of various wood species in different proportions. This results in a more efficient use of timber resources and reduced manufacturing costs.
