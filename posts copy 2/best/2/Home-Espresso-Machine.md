Proper maintenance and cleaning are essential for the prolonged performance and longevity of your home espresso machine. Begin by consulting the manufacturer's manual for specific instructions tailored to your machine. General tips include cleaning the machine after each use, descaling it every 3-4 months to remove mineral buildup, and replacing worn or damaged parts as needed.

### What is the difference between single- and double-shot home espresso machines?

The primary distinction between single- and double-shot home espresso machines lies in the amount of coffee grounds used to brew a shot. Single-shot machines are designed to use one serving of coffee, while double-shot machines can handle two servings. This allows for either two single-shots or one double-shot of espresso to be produced.

Double-shot machines tend to be more versatile, offering convenience for those who frequently entertain or need to serve multiple people. Single-shot machines are generally more affordable and user-friendly, making them a suitable choice for those who consume or serve a single cup of espresso.
